## Attribution Notes

### Results where attribution is uncertain

1. **Born rule from Haar measure (Paper 1, App C):** Could be classified
   as P1 (the geometric reinterpretation of probability as e-density) or
   P4 (the uniqueness of Haar measure as a topological property of compact
   groups). Listed as P4 generative because the *uniqueness theorem* is
   the non-obvious step --- the geometric picture motivates the question
   but does not answer it.

2. **Omega_DM/Omega_b (Paper 2, App E):** Three patterns contribute
   (P3, P4, P6). The generative move is P6 (the hidden brane), but the
   quantitative prediction uses Casimir (P3) and orbifold rigidity (P4).
   The xi parameter from baryogenesis dynamics introduces model dependence.

3. **Theorem U* (Paper 7, frontier):** Uses Pattern 4 *inverted* ---
   topological rigidity as a ceiling, not a floor. All geometric inputs
   are O(1), so any derived R is bounded near l_P. The 10^30 gap to R_obs
   is unreachable by perturbative means. This is a genuine new use of
   Pattern 4 and is tagged as `[Pattern 4 inverted]` in the text.

### Results that do not fit the six patterns

No result was found that requires a seventh pattern. Every major
derivation in the series uses one or more of the six. The closest
candidate for a distinct move is the **BPHZ locality argument**
(Appendix K, §K.5.3), which uses the locality of counterterms to
establish factorization. This is a standard QFT technique rather than
a new reasoning pattern; it supports Pattern 5 but is not itself a
separate generative move.

### Most generative pattern across the series

**Pattern 4 (Topological Rigidity)** is the most frequently generative
pattern, appearing as the key insight in the spin-statistics theorem,
the generation count, the strong CP solution, the firewall resolution,
the GUT flux condition, and both Theorems U and U*. It is also the
pattern that distinguishes the framework most sharply from conventional
approaches: where other programs treat quantized results as empirical
inputs, this framework derives them as topological consequences of
the compact geometry.

**Pattern 6 (Projection Produces Apparent Pathology)** is the most
*philosophically* generative --- it is the meta-pattern that motivates
the entire framework. Without P6, there is no reason to look for a
fifth dimension. Once P6 is accepted, Patterns 1--5 become available.

**Pattern 5 (Zeta Regularization)** is the most *technically* generative
for the finiteness program. Without the structural zeros of Epstein
zeta functions, the framework would have UV problems like any other
approach to quantum gravity.
