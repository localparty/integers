Idea 1: The hidden brane Casimir energy as the EDE source.
The visible brane Casimir energy gives dark energy at z = 0. But the hidden brane at φ = π has its own Casimir energy — from the mirror sector fields. If the mirror sector had a different temperature history than the visible sector (which it would, since they're thermally decoupled), its Casimir energy contribution to the expansion could be different at early times. Specifically: if the mirror sector was hotter at z ~ 1000 (from a different reheating history), the mirror Casimir energy could be non-negligible and effectively contribute to an elevated expansion rate near recombination. This doesn't require the dilaton at all — it uses the existing hidden brane physics.

Idea 2: The KK graviton tower and the sound horizon directly.
The KK graviton tower contributes to the radiation energy density at temperatures above the KK mass scale (~10 meV, or T ~ 100 K). At recombination (T ~ 3000 K), all KK modes with mass < 3000 K ~ 0.26 eV are relativistic. The first ~30 KK modes (n = 1 to ~30) are below 0.26 eV and would be relativistic at recombination. If these modes are produced (even faintly) during the thermal history, they contribute to the relativistic energy density and expand the Hubble rate at recombination — which is exactly what's needed. The question is whether the production rate is large enough. The Vafa group's intra-tower analysis suggests the production is real but small. This needs quantification.

Idea 3: The Z₂ orbifold and a non-trivial cosmological phase transition.
The Z₂ orbifold has a phase transition: the moment when the e-circle transitions from a circle to an orbifold (or equivalently, when the Z₂ symmetry is spontaneously broken or restored). If this phase transition happened near recombination — which could be arranged if the critical temperature is T_c ~ 0.3 eV — it would release latent heat into the primordial plasma, briefly boosting the expansion rate. The latent heat of the orbifold transition is set by the tension of the orbifold fixed points (the brane tensions). This is speculative but geometrically motivated: the Z₂ orbifold is a topological structure that presumably formed at some temperature, and the transition energy could be cosmologically relevant.

Idea 1 (hidden brane Casimir) is the most physically motivated — it uses existing framework physics without new assumptions, and it's the kind of calculation that could actually be done with the same Casimir methods as Appendix W.

Idea 2 (KK graviton radiation) is already partially in the literature through the Vafa group's work. The question is quantitative — does the KK contribution shift H₀ enough? Their S8 paper (arXiv:2311.05318) is probably the place to start.

Idea 3 (orbifold phase transition) is the most speculative but potentially the most elegant — a geometric phase transition at recombination temperatures would be a genuinely novel mechanism. It needs more thought.
