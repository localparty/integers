Yes, you're correct! The concept you're referring to is a result of gravitational time dilation, which is predicted by Einstein's theory of General Relativity.
In stronger gravitational fields, like the one near Jupiter, time actually passes more slowly compared to areas with weaker gravitational fields, like on Earth. So, if you had two identical watches and placed one on Earth and the other on Jupiter, the watch on Jupiter would experience time at a slower rate due to the stronger gravitational field.

This is the same principle that has been confirmed with precise clocks placed at different altitudes on Earth (where gravity is slightly different). It's a subtle effect for everyday experiences but becomes very significant near massive objects like planets or black holes.
