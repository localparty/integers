# PROOF-CHAIN -- Goldbach Conjecture

*Research programme (not a proof chain). Every even integer >= 4 is the sum of two primes. No chain of links leading to a proof exists; the BC framework provides a natural home but no obvious new tools.*
*Status: programme only (~1/10) | Confidence: 1/10*

## Entry point
Primes generate the BC algebra as Hecke operators mu_p. Goldbach is an ADDITIVE statement about these MULTIPLICATIVE generators. The BC partition function Z(beta) = zeta(beta) encodes the full prime distribution.

## Key question
Can the interplay between additive and multiplicative structure within the BC framework (Q/Z characters, KMS states, Hecke semigroup) provide tools beyond classical additive number theory?

## What is known
- Under RH: Goldbach holds for sufficiently large even integers (Montgomery-Vaughan 1975)
- Helfgott 2013: ternary Goldbach proved (odd >= 7 is sum of 3 primes)
- Binary (even) case remains open
