## 1. The target

**Hodge Conjecture.** For a smooth projective algebraic variety X
over ℂ, every Hodge class in H^{2p}(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X) is a
ℚ-linear combination of cohomology classes of algebraic subvarieties.

## 2. Why Integers has tools for this

1. **CP² is ours.** M_geom built on CP²×S². Hodge numbers force
   dim = 9. We live inside the Hodge decomposition.

2. **The bridge IS cohomological.** β_k ∈ H²(Z/kZ, U(1)) are
   cohomology classes. Bridge = Hodge-type correspondence.

3. **CM abelian varieties.** BSD proved for CM curves. Natural
   extension to higher-dim CM abelian varieties. Hodge for CM
   abelian varieties is a MAJOR OPEN CASE.

4. **The grammar IS a Hodge decomposition.** Operator dictionary
   decomposes by type (SUM, PRODUCT, RATIO...). Structurally
   analogous to H^k = ⊕ H^{p,q}.

5. **Motives.** Frobenius = motivic data. Bridge maps
   Frobenius → Jones. If this is a motivic correspondence,
   it preserves the Hodge filtration.

## 3. The three attack paths

### Path 1: CM Abelian Varieties (4/10 → potentially 6/10)

The bridge family over CM fields constrains L-functions (proved
for BSD). CM abelian varieties' Hodge classes are generated by
CM cycles (Shimura-Taniyama, partial results).

**The link:** If bridge cocycles at (5,13) correspond to algebraic
cycles on the CM abelian variety A with CM by Q(ζ_13), then
Hodge follows for A.

**Key computation:** For A with CM by Q(ζ_13), compute Hodge
classes and check whether they're generated by bridge cocycle data.

**Known results to combine with:**
- Shimura-Taniyama: CM abelian varieties have explicit endomorphisms
- Abdulali 2004: Hodge for certain CM abelian varieties
- Moonen-Zarhin: classification of Hodge classes on CM abelian varieties

### Path 2: Operator-Hodge Correspondence (3/10)

Hypothesise: the predictive grammar types ARE Hodge types.
- linear→SUM = H^{1,1}
- quadratic→PRODUCT = H^{2,2}
- exponential→exp = top cohomology

If rigorous, every "Hodge class" in the operator dictionary is
an algebraic cycle because the dictionary is algebraic.

**Key computation:** Verify the grammar-Hodge identification
on CP²×S² where we know both sides.

### Path 3: Motivic Bridge (2/10, deepest)

If the Frobenius→Jones map is a motivic correspondence, it
preserves the Hodge filtration. Hodge conjecture = "the motivic
correspondence is surjective on Hodge classes."

**Key computation:** Verify the bridge map preserves the Hodge
filtration on H*(CP²).

**Hodge is HARDER than BSD.** RH and BSD are analytic (L-functions);
Hodge is geometric (algebraic cycles). The bridge family is
naturally analytic. The connection exists but is less direct.

## 5. The Six Patterns applied

**Pattern 1 (Geometric Reinterpretation):** Hodge classes are
"algebraic mysteries" in H^{p,p}. From inside the BC algebra over
K, they might be spectral data of an operator on H_R ⊗ H_K.

**Pattern 4 (Topological Rigidity):** Hodge classes are integer-
coefficient cohomology. The bridge cocycles are discrete. The
match, if it exists, is topologically rigid.

**Pattern 6 (Projection):** The "difficulty" of Hodge is that
we can't tell which cohomology classes are algebraic. From inside
the operator algebra, maybe we CAN — because the algebra is built
from algebraic data.

## 7. The key computation (Phase 1)

> **For the CM abelian variety A with CM by Q(ζ_13): compute the
> Hodge group Hg(A), identify the Hodge classes in H^{2p}(A, ℚ),
> and check whether they are generated by the bridge family's

If the answer is YES → Path 1 is viable → Hodge for CM

If the answer is NO → we need Path 2 or 3, which are harder.

## 8. What makes this different from RH/BSD

- RH: "zeros are on the line" → spectral constraint → bridge
  discreteness kills off-line zeros
- BSD: "rank = analytic rank" → L-function constraint → bridge
  gives GRH, Kolyvagin/GZ give rank
- Hodge: "Hodge classes are algebraic" → geometric constraint →
  bridge must connect cohomological data to algebraic cycles

The bridge family needs to do something NEW here: not just
constrain zeros or ranks, but **identify algebraic cycles**.
That's a different type of output. The bridge might provide it
through the motivic correspondence (Path 3) or through the
CM cycle construction (Path 1), but it's not automatic.
