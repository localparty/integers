## 2. Agent P1 Results: H^2 and H^4 of A

### H^2(A) cap H^{1,1}(A) — No exotic classes

By the **Lefschetz (1,1)-theorem** (unconditional):

    Hdg^1(A) = NS(A) tensor Q

dim NS(A) tensor Q = dim K^+ = phi(13)/2 = **6**.

The 6 degree-1 Hodge classes are the divisor classes from
CM endomorphisms: {(a, 13-a) : a in Phi}. All algebraic. No exotic classes in H^2.

### H^4(A) cap H^{2,2}(A) — 8 exotic classes found

Weight-zero analysis: a Hodge class in H^{2,2}(A) corresponds to a
4-element subset S of (Z/13Z)* with |S cap Phi| = 2, |S cap Phi_bar| = 2,
and sum(S) equiv 0 mod 13.

| Count | Description |
|:------|:------------|
| **23** | Total (2,2)-type weight-zero 4-subsets |
| **15** | Product classes: wedges of degree-1 Hodge classes, = C(6,2) |
| **8** | **EXOTIC classes**: not in the product subring |

### The 8 exotic classes (explicit)

| # | Phi part | Phi_bar part | Orbit structure |
|:--|:---------|:-------------|:----------------|
| 1 | {1, 4} | {10, 11} | O1, O3, O2, O2 |
| 2 | {1, 6} | {9, 10} | O1, O3, O3, O2 |
| 3 | {1, 8} | {7, 10} | O1, O1, O3, O2 |
| 4 | {2, 3} | {9, 12} | O2, O2, O3, O1 |
| 5 | {2, 8} | {7, 9} | O2, O1, O3, O3 |
| 6 | {3, 4} | {7, 12} | O2, O3, O3, O1 |
| 7 | {3, 6} | {5, 12} | O2, O3, O1, O1 |
| 8 | {4, 6} | {5, 11} | O3, O3, O1, O2 |

**Every exotic class is inter-orbit.** All 8 involve characters from
all three Frobenius orbits O_1, O_2, O_3. None is confined to a
single orbit. This is the bridge's Z/3Z symmetry at work.

## 4. Adversarial Review

### Attack on P1: exotic class count

- Weight-zero count verified: 23 total, 15 products, 8 exotic. **INTACT.**
- Product independence verified: 15 classes from C(6,2) distinct pairs. **INTACT.**
- Exotic verification: none of the 8 admits a pairing a <-> 13-a. **INTACT.**

### Attack on P1: "bridge-generated" vs "algebraic"

- The orbit projectors P_{O_i} are algebraic (CM endomorphisms). **INTACT.**
- But P_{O_i} generate only the PRODUCT classes in degree 2. **DAMAGED.**
- The 8 exotic classes are NOT generated by orbit projectors alone.
- beta_3 encodes inter-orbit structure but does NOT produce algebraic
  cycles on A (it lives in group cohomology, not variety cohomology). **DAMAGED.**

**Verdict on "bridge generates exotic classes": DAMAGED.**
The bridge ORGANISES the exotic classes (all are inter-orbit) but
does not CONSTRUCT them as algebraic cycles. The algebraicity comes
from Deligne's theorem (see below), not from the bridge directly.

### Attack on P3: CM lift and Hodge filtration

- For ordinary primes (p = 53): CM lift preserves everything. **INTACT.**
- For supersingular primes: potential issues, but not relevant here. **INTACT.**
- Surjectivity at totally split p: Tate count = Hodge count. **INTACT.**

**Verdict on Tate-Hodge pipeline: INTACT.**

### Critical adversarial finding: Deligne 1982

**Theorem (Deligne 1982).** For any abelian variety A over C, every
Hodge class on A is an absolute Hodge class. For CM abelian varieties,
absolute Hodge classes are algebraic.

**Corollary.** The Hodge conjecture holds for A^n for all n, when A
has CM. This includes our A with CM by Q(zeta_13).

**The 8 exotic classes are algebraic by Deligne 1982.**
This is a known theorem, not a new result. The computation in this
cycle identifies WHICH classes are exotic and shows their inter-orbit
structure, but their algebraicity was proved 44 years ago.
