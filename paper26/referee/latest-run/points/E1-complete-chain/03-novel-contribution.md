## Point E1(c): The Novel Contribution

**The question:**
What exactly is new?

**Finding:**
The novel contributions of Paper 26 are:

1. **GRH for Hecke L-functions over class-number-1 imaginary quadratic fields.** This is the central new result. It is achieved by extending the bridge family from Q to K = Q(i) and applying Baker's theorem.

2. **The observation that the bridge family is field-independent.** Theorem 4.6 shows the cocycle match holds over any number field with h_K = 1.

3. **The assembly of GRH + Kolyvagin + Gross-Zagier into BSD.** While this chain is "well-understood" conceptually (experts know that GRH would imply BSD for rank 0 and 1), the explicit assembly with all details verified is new.

What is NOT new:
- Kolyvagin's theorem (1990)
- Gross-Zagier formula (1986)
- Deuring's CM factorization (1953)
- Baker's theorem (1966)
- Ha-Paugam construction (2005)
- The observation that GRH implies BSD for rank 0+1 (folklore)

The genuine novelty is in item 1: unconditional (modulo CBB) GRH for Hecke L-functions. This is a significant result if the bridge construction is accepted.

**Verdict for this sub-question:**
SOUND -- The novel contribution is clearly identified: GRH for Hecke L-functions via the bridge family. The downstream assembly uses known results. This is appropriate scientific practice.
