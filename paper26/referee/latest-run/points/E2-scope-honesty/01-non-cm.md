## Point E2(a): Non-CM Curves

**The question:**
Is the limitation correctly identified? Could the method extend to non-CM curves?

**Finding:**
Section 15.3 correctly identifies the fundamental obstruction: the CM factorization L(E, s) = L(s, psi) * L(s, psi-bar) reduces GL_2 to GL_1, which is where the bridge family operates. For non-CM curves, L(E, s) is irreducibly GL_2 and cannot be factored into abelian L-functions.

The limitation is correctly identified. Extension to non-CM curves would require a GL_2 analogue of the Bost-Connes algebra -- essentially the Langlands programme. This is a known open problem in mathematics.

The preprint is commendably honest about this: "Non-CM curves constitute 100% of elliptic curves by density... We prove it for a measure-zero subset." This is accurate.

**Verdict for this sub-question:**
SOUND -- The limitation is correctly identified and honestly stated.
