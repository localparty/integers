## Point C1(a): Baker's Theorem Statement

**The question:**
Baker's theorem: if alpha_1, ..., alpha_n are non-zero algebraic numbers and log alpha_1, ..., log alpha_n are linearly independent over Q, then beta_1 log alpha_1 + ... + beta_n log alpha_n != 0 for any algebraic numbers beta_i not all zero. Verify that the Gaussian prime norms are algebraic numbers.

**Finding:**
Baker's theorem (Theorem 8.1 in the preprint) is correctly stated. The Gaussian prime norms are positive integers (2, 5, 9, 13, 17, ...), which are trivially algebraic. Baker's theorem applies to logarithms of algebraic numbers, so it applies to log(N(p)) for all Gaussian primes p.

The qualitative version used (Corollary 8.2: if alpha_1, ..., alpha_n are multiplicatively independent algebraic numbers, then log alpha_1, ..., log alpha_n are linearly independent over the algebraic numbers) is a standard consequence of Baker's theorem.

Baker's theorem is one of the most well-established results in transcendence theory, with a Fields Medal (1970) and extensive literature. There is no question about its validity or applicability.

**Verdict for this sub-question:**
SOUND -- Baker's theorem is correctly stated and applies to Gaussian prime norms (positive integers, hence algebraic).
