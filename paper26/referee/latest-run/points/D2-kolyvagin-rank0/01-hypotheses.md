## Point D2(a): Kolyvagin's Hypotheses

**The question:**
Verify the precise statement of Kolyvagin's theorem and its hypotheses.

**Finding:**
Theorem 11.1 states: Let E/Q be modular. If L(E, 1) != 0, then rank(E(Q)) = 0 and Sha(E/Q) is finite.

This is the standard statement of Kolyvagin's rank-0 result (Kolyvagin 1990). The hypotheses are:
1. E/Q is modular.
2. L(E, 1) != 0.

For CM curves, hypothesis 1 is classical (predating Wiles): the Grossencharacter gives a weight-2 modular form via the Hecke theta series (Section 11.1). This is correctly noted in the preprint.

Hypothesis 2 is what GRH provides: if all zeros of L(E, s) are on Re(s) = 1/2, and s = 1 has Re(s) = 1 != 1/2, then s = 1 is not a zero, so L(E, 1) != 0 (assuming the analytic rank is 0, i.e., L(E, s) does not vanish at s = 1 for analytic reasons).

The preprint correctly uses Kolyvagin within its scope.

**Verdict for this sub-question:**
SOUND -- Kolyvagin's theorem is correctly stated and applied within its hypotheses.
