# Abstract — Paper 10: Scheme-Independence of UV Finiteness in 5D KK Gravity

**Paper 10 in the QG5D Series**  
**Status:** Preprint draft

---

We address the open problem stated in Paper 1 (Appendix U §U.2c): whether the
vanishing of the Goroff-Sagnotti R³ counterterm in 5D linearized gravity on
M⁴ × S¹/Z₂ — established in Paper 1 within zeta regularization via the Universal
Epstein Vanishing theorem (Appendix K) — is scheme-independent.

We prove that the answer is yes for the one-loop sector, and yes unconditionally for
the two-loop Goroff-Sagnotti sector (Theorem 1, proved). Five independent routes are
developed, two of which yield outright proofs, three of which yield strong structural
demonstrations.

**Two proved results.** (i) The Seeley-DeWitt coefficients a₂ = 0 and a₄ = 0 of
the Lichnerowicz operator on the flat background M⁴ × S¹/Z₂ are intrinsic spectral
invariants, computed without reference to any regularization prescription. Their
vanishing establishes one-loop UV finiteness in every valid scheme (§2). (ii) The
4D Weyl anomaly coefficients a_total = c_total = 0 for the full KK graviton tower
are protected by the Wess-Zumino consistency condition: no finite local counterterm
can shift (a, c), and they vanish under zeta regularization, so they vanish in every
diffeomorphism-preserving scheme. This directly implies that the Goroff-Sagnotti
C³ counterterm is not generated by the KK tower in any such scheme (§4.4–§4.5).

**Three supporting demonstrations.** (iii) Z₂ parity forces an exact algebraic
cancellation between the even and odd KK mode contributions to the Goroff-Sagnotti
coefficient at each KK level n ≥ 1, covering dimensional regularization, symmetric
cutoff, Z₂-paired Pauli-Villars, and zeta regularization (§3). (iv) The Poisson
resummation exchange lemma shows that the dim-reg pole coefficient of the two-loop
effective action equals the zeta-regularized coefficient S₀ = 0; the two schemes
differ only by an exponentially suppressed finite residual of order e^{−2πRk},
introducing no new divergences (§4.1–§4.3). (v) The subleading Epstein zeros
E_L(−j; Q) = 0 for all j ≥ 1 are number-theoretically robust: they arise from
1/Γ(−j) = 0, a mathematical identity about the Gamma function independent of any
regularization choice (§3.7).

**Theorem 1 (proved).** The y-integral computation of the three-graviton vertex KK
decomposition (research memo `paper10/research/01-three-graviton-vertex.md`) is
complete, and all three assumptions A1–A3 are now proved (Lemmas A1–A3). The key
results are: (i) the diagonal coupling I_{+++}(n,n,n) = 0 for all n ≥ 1, so self-loop
contributions are absent; (ii) the triangle coupling I_{+++}(n₁, n₂, n₁+n₂) = πR/4
is a universal constant, fully mass-independent, for all n₁, n₂ ≥ 1; (iii) the GS
coefficient assembles as C_GS = [πR/4]² × J(0) × S₀² = 0 at leading order, with all
subleading corrections vanishing by Theorem K.1. Lemma A1 (UV power counting, Z₂
parity projection, Epstein backstop; §4.6, §5.2a) closes the de Donder gauge vertex
numerator. Lemma A2 (index structure and Z₂ selection rules; §5.2b) closes the
graviphoton/radion sector. Lemma A3 (method-of-images propagator on S¹/Z₂; §5.2c)
closes the internal KK momentum range. Theorem 1 is unconditional within linearized
5D gravity on flat M⁴ × S¹/Z₂.

**Forward.** Paper 10 closes the one-loop half of the open problem in Paper 1 §U.2c
rigorously, and closes the two-loop half unconditionally via Theorem 1 (proved) and
Lemmas A1–A3 (proved). It additionally establishes the Weyl anomaly of the full
orbifold KK tower — a_grand = 19/240 for the combined h_{μν} + A_μ + φ sectors — as
a real, non-zero, curved-background effect orthogonal to Theorem 1, and identifies it
as a separate open observation deserving further study (§5.3).
