# Quantum Geometry in 5D

**A Geometric Framework for Quantum Intuition**

## Overview

This project explores a geometric interpretation of quantum mechanics where reality is fundamentally 5-dimensional: three spatial dimensions (x, y, z), time (t), and a fifth dimension we call "e" - the entanglement or phase dimension.

Rather than treating quantum phenomena as mysterious or counter-intuitive, this framework shows how they emerge naturally from the geometry of 5D spacetime projecting onto our observable 4D experience.

## Core Insight

Just as a cube casts a 2D shadow that hides its depth, our 4D experience may be a projection of a richer 5D reality. Quantum "weirdness" - entanglement, superposition, interference - becomes ordinary geometry in this higher-dimensional space.

## Key Concepts

- **The Fifth Dimension (e)**: A real degree of freedom, as fundamental as x, y, z, and t
- **Entanglement**: Conservation law e₁ + e₂ = constant
- **Superposition**: Geometric extension in the e-dimension
- **Momentum**: Helical pitch through 5D space
- **Spin**: Chirality (handedness) of the helix
- **Wave Functions**: Literal geometric shapes in 5D
