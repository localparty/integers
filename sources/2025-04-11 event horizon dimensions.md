5 . A possible mental model
Think of spacetime as a loaf of bread you slice along the time direction:

Bottom line
Crossing the horizon doesn’t delete the particle’s proper time; it only looks frozen from afar.
The “surface smearing” is an information accounting principle rooted in black‑hole thermodynamics and holography, not a literal spreading of mass.
No mainstream theory requires or predicts a conversion of the time dimension into an extra spatial one at the horizon. What does happen is that, when we represent the bulk physics on the 2‑D horizon, temporal information gets re‑expressed as additional labels living on that surface.
Your intuition is brushing up against a real, fascinating theme in modern physics: dimensional reduction in holographic descriptions. The trick is to treat it as a mapping between descriptions rather than a metamorphosis of one fundamental dimension into another.

Putting it all together
The event horizon is three‑dimensional inside the four‑dimensional spacetime.
Two of those dimensions are the ordinary angular directions on a sphere—fully space‑like.
The third is null, generated by light rays that never leave the horizon. It supplies an ordering (“before/after”) for the horizon itself without introducing an intrinsic time‑like direction.
So the shorthand “(2 space) + (1 null)” is a concise way of saying:
“Inside the horizon’s own world, you can move around the sphere (2 space‑like directions) or slide along its light‑like generators (1 null direction), but you cannot find a vector that is both tangent to the horizon and time‑like.”
