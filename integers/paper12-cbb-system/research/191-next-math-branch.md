# 191 — The Next Math Branch: Operator-Algebraic Explicit Class Field Theory at Criticality

**Cycle 10 — creative survey.** The CBB system (BC C*-algebra at β=1 on H_R, Frobenius–Jones bridges via cyclotomic Brauer classes at (5,13), (3,13), (7,19), and the CP²×S² spectral-action vacuum coupled through the anti-hermitian V) now sits on a definite mathematical object. What branch of mathematics does it open?

## 1. Structural content

Three layers meet at a single Hilbert space:
- **Spectral**: BC algebra phase transition at β=1, log R̂, ζ Laurent data.
- **Bridge**: Frobenius–Jones isomorphisms realized as cyclotomic Brauer 2-cocycles on specific prime/level pairs.
- **Geometric**: spectral-action moduli on CP²×S², coupled via V.

The bridges are *explicit*: they are finite Brauer classes computed at named primes, not abstract existence statements.

## 2. Candidate programmes

- **A. BC–Brauer cohomology**: need a cohomology theory H*(BC, Br(Q(ζ_n))) with a spectral-action pairing; new: a cup product matching the V-coupling.
- **B. Operator-algebraic explicit CFT at criticality**: extend BC/CMR beyond Q to cyclotomic fields using the bridge primes as data; need reciprocity at β=1.
- **C. Categorification**: Frobenius–Jones as 1-morphisms in a 2-category of KMS sectors; need a coherence theorem matching the (5,3,7)/(13,13,19) pattern.
- **D. RH as CBB consistency**: RH ⇔ self-adjointness of V̂ on the critical fibre; need a Weil-positivity lift.

## 3. Chosen direction: **B**

Explicit class field theory is the one classical programme already known to speak to BC. Our bridge data upgrade it from Q to a finite tower of cyclotomic fields, *at criticality*, with operator-algebraic witnesses.

### Conjectures

1. **CBB Reciprocity.** For each bridge prime p ∈ {5,3,7} on level ℓ ∈ {13,13,19}, the Frobenius–Jones isomorphism induces the Artin map Gal(Q(ζ_ℓ)^ab/Q(ζ_ℓ)) → (BC_ℓ at β=1)_* intertwining KMS_1 states with Hecke characters.
2. **Brauer–KMS duality.** The cyclotomic Brauer class [β_{p,ℓ}] equals the obstruction to lifting the KMS_1 state on BC_ℓ to a tracial state on the V-coupled spectral-action algebra.
3. **Level-jump rigidity.** The jump 13 → 19 at p=7 is forced: no nontrivial Frobenius–Jones bridge exists for (7, ℓ<19).
4. **Spectral Kronecker–Weber.** Every abelian extension of Q(ζ_{13}) or Q(ζ_{19}) that embeds in the CBB Hilbert space arises from a finite product of the three named bridges.
5. **V-Hilbert 12th problem.** The anti-hermitian coupling V provides the explicit analytic generators for abelian extensions of the cyclotomic base, completing Hilbert's 12th for the CBB tower.

## 4. Audiences

- **Number theorists** (CFT, Iwasawa, Langlands): conjectures 1, 4, 5 are Hilbert-12 class.
- **Operator algebraists** (Connes, Marcolli, Bost school): BC at criticality, KMS–Brauer duality.

## 5. Verdict

Direction **B** is the richest because it is the only one where the CBB data are *already* the missing ingredient in a named open problem (Hilbert 12). A, C, D are natural but derivative; B re-frames the framework as an operator-algebraic solution to explicit CFT over cyclotomic fields, with RH (direction D) recovered as a corollary of conjecture 2. This is the follow-up programme.
