## 2. The Bost–Connes C\*-Algebra A_BC

### 2.1 Definition

**Definition 2.1 (Bost–Connes 1995).** The *Bost–Connes C\*-algebra*
is the semigroup crossed product

$$
\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}} \;:=\; C^{*}(\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^{*},
\tag{2.1}
$$

where C\*(Q/Z) is the group C\*-algebra of the discrete abelian
group Q/Z (isomorphic to the algebra of continuous functions on its
Pontryagin dual Ẑ), and N\* = {1, 2, 3, …} acts by the semigroup
of endomorphisms r ↦ nr of Q/Z.

Equivalently, A_BC is the universal unital C\*-algebra generated by

- **phase operators** e(r), one for each r ∈ Q/Z, satisfying
  e(0) = 1, e(r)\* = e(−r), e(r) e(s) = e(r + s);
- **isometries** μ_n, one for each n ∈ N\*,

subject to the relations

$$
\mu_{n}\,\mu_{m} \;=\; \mu_{nm},
\qquad
\mu_{n}^{*}\,\mu_{n} \;=\; 1,
\tag{2.2}
$$

$$
\mu_{n}\,e(r)\,\mu_{n}^{*}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\substack{s \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \\ ns \,=\, r}} e(s).
\tag{2.3}
$$

The sum in (2.3) ranges over the *n* solutions s of ns = r in Q/Z.
Relation (2.3) is the **Hecke relation**: the isometries μ_n
implement the "averaged pullback" of phases along multiplication by
n. (Note that μ_n μ_n\* ≠ 1: the μ_n are isometries, not unitaries,
and μ_n μ_n\* is a proper projection.)

The algebra is sometimes presented as the *Hecke algebra*
C\_r\*(P_Q\^+ \\ P_Z\^+ / P_Z\^+) of Bost–Connes 1995 §4, where
P is the ax+b group. The semigroup-crossed-product presentation
(2.1)–(2.3) is Laca's reformulation and is what we use.

### 2.2 Time evolution

A_BC carries a canonical one-parameter group of automorphisms
σ_t : R → Aut(A_BC), defined on generators by

$$
\sigma_{t}(\mu_{n}) \;=\; n^{i t}\,\mu_{n},
\qquad
\sigma_{t}\bigl(e(r)\bigr) \;=\; e(r),
\qquad t \in \mathbb{R}.
\tag{2.4}
$$

The phase Q/Z part is fixed; the isometries μ_n pick up a scalar
phase n^{it} = exp(it · log n). The pair (A_BC, σ_t) is the
**Bost–Connes C\*-dynamical system**.

### 2.3 Hamiltonian and partition function

Writing σ_t = e^{iHt} on any representation where the generator
exists, the formal Hamiltonian is

$$
H \;=\; \log \hat N,
\tag{2.5}
$$

where N̂ is the "number operator" with N̂ μ_n = n μ_n. On the
canonical representation (Section 4), the spectrum of H is

$$
\mathrm{spec}(H) \;=\; \{\,\log n : n \in \mathbb{N}^{*}\,\}.
\tag{2.6}
$$

This is Julia's **primon gas**: the one-particle spectrum is
{log n} and the Gibbs sum is the Riemann zeta function. Formally

$$
Z(\beta) \;=\; \mathrm{Tr}\!\bigl(e^{-\beta H}\bigr)
\;=\; \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^{-\beta}
\;=\; \zeta(\beta).
\tag{2.7}
$$

The simple pole of ζ at β = 1 is the **phase transition** of the
BC system.

## 3. KMS Structure and the Critical State

Recall that a *KMS_β state* for (A_BC, σ_t) is a state φ with
φ(ab) = φ(b σ_{iβ}(a)) (analytic continuation of the correlator
to imaginary time iβ). The structure of KMS states for the BC
system is the classical result of Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25 /
Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Theorem 3.7:

**Theorem 3.1 (Bost–Connes).**

1. For **β > 1**, the extremal KMS_β states of (A_BC, σ_t) form a
   family parameterised by the idele class group / abelianised
   absolute Galois group

   $$
   \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q})
   \;\cong\; \hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{*}.
   \tag{3.1}
   $$

   This is *spontaneous symmetry breaking* of the Galois symmetry.

2. For **0 < β ≤ 1**, there is a **unique** KMS_β state. In
   particular, at the critical value β = 1 there is a unique KMS
   state ω_1, called the **critical state**. The symmetry Ẑ\*
   acts on A_BC but fixes ω_1.

3. The critical state ω_1 is a **factor state of type III_1**.

The phase transition at β = 1 coincides precisely with the simple
pole of ζ(β) at β = 1. The partition function "diverges" at the
critical point, which on the operator-algebraic side is the
appearance of the type III_1 factor.

The passage β > 1 → β ≤ 1 (high-temperature limit) dissolves the
Galois symmetry breaking: above the critical temperature the unique
KMS state is Galois-invariant. Paper 12's physics lives at β = 1,
where ω_1 is unique and Galois-invariant as a state but carries a
non-trivial Galois action on the GNS Hilbert space (Section 7).

## 4. GNS Construction at β = 1

Applied to the critical state ω_1, the GNS construction yields a
triple (π_1, H_1, Ω_1):

- **H_1** = L²(A_BC, ω_1) is the GNS Hilbert space, the completion
  of A_BC (modulo the null ideal of ω_1) in the inner product
  ⟨a, b⟩ := ω_1(a\* b).
- **π_1 : A_BC → B(H_1)** is the induced \*-representation.
- **Ω_1 ∈ H_1** is the cyclic vector with

  $$
  \omega_{1}(a) \;=\; \langle \Omega_{1}, \pi_{1}(a)\,\Omega_{1}\rangle.
  \tag{4.1}
  $$

The time evolution σ_t is unitarily implemented on H_1 by a
strongly continuous one-parameter group

$$
U_{t} \;=\; e^{i H_{1} t},
\qquad
U_{t}\,\Omega_{1} \;=\; \Omega_{1},
\tag{4.2}
$$

fixing the cyclic vector (because ω_1 is σ_t-invariant). The
self-adjoint generator H_1 on H_1 is the **modular Hamiltonian**
of (π_1(A_BC)″, Ω_1) up to sign. By the KMS condition at β = 1,
U_t is the modular flow of ω_1 on the factor π_1(A_BC)″, which is
type III_1.

(This is standard Tomita–Takesaki / GNS-KMS machinery; see
Bratteli–Robinson, *Operator Algebras and Quantum Statistical
Mechanics II*, §5.3, or Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §2.)

## 5. The N\*-Labelled Subspace H_1^{(N\*)}

**Definition 5.1.** Let

$$
H_{1}^{(\mathbb{N}^{*})}
\;:=\;
\overline{\mathrm{span}}\,\bigl\{\,\pi_{1}(\mu_{n})\,\Omega_{1}
:\; n \in \mathbb{N}^{*}\,\bigr\} \;\subset\; H_{1}.
\tag{5.1}
$$

This is the closed linear span of the orbit of Ω_1 under the
isometries μ_n.

**Proposition 5.2 (orthonormality; research/04 §3.1).** *Under the
regularised KMS condition at β = 1 of Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25,
the family {μ_n Ω_1 : n ∈ N\*} is orthonormal in H_1:*

$$
\langle \mu_{n}\Omega_{1},\,\mu_{m}\Omega_{1}\rangle
\;=\; \delta_{n,m}.
\tag{5.2}
$$

*Proof sketch.* Using μ_n\* μ_n = 1 and the relations for μ_n\* μ_m
(which vanish unless one index divides the other and reduce to
μ_{m/n} when n | m), the inner product reduces to ω_1(μ_k) for
integer k. The regularised evaluation at β = 1 of Bost–Connes 1995
Theorem 25 gives ω_1(μ_k) = δ_{k,1}, hence (5.2). See research/04
§3.1 for the detailed verification. ∎

Under this orthonormality, H_1^{(N\*)} is a separable Hilbert space
with orthonormal basis {μ_n Ω_1}_{n ∈ N\*}. On this subspace the
number operator N̂ acts as

$$
\hat N\,\mu_{n}\Omega_{1} \;=\; n\,\mu_{n}\Omega_{1},
\qquad
H_{1}\,\mu_{n}\Omega_{1} \;=\; (\log n)\,\mu_{n}\Omega_{1},
\tag{5.3}
$$

recovering spec(H_1|\_{H_1^{(N\*)}}) = {log n : n ∈ N\*}.

The subspace H_1^{(N\*)} is the "arithmetic" subspace that witnesses
the primon-gas structure. It is the BC-side Hilbert space used in
**Identity 12** (research/04) to match the QG5D e-circle's
momentum-eigenstate basis.

## 6. The Riemann Subspace H_R

### 6.1 The dual operator T_BC

The BC Hamiltonian H_1 has spectrum {log n : n ∈ N\*} on H_1^{(N\*)}
and generally continuous spectrum on all of H_1 (because ω_1 is
type III_1). The Riemann zeros γ_n are **not** eigenvalues of H_1.
They appear as spectral data of a **dual** operator T_BC obtained
from the Mellin transform of the imaginary-time correlator
u ↦ ω_1(σ_{i log u}(a)); see Connes 1999 and Connes–Marcolli 2008
Chapter II §3–4.

Concretely (research/02 §3.2, research/14 Part A), T_BC is the
self-adjoint generator of the dilation action obtained from σ_t
by analytic continuation / Sz.-Nagy dilation of the N\* isometries,

$$
U_{u}^{\mathrm{dil}}
\;=\; \exp\!\bigl(i\,T_{\mathrm{BC}}\,\log u\bigr),
\qquad u \in \mathbb{R}_{>0}.
\tag{6.1}
$$

By the Connes–Marcolli operator-algebraic form of the
Riemann–Weil explicit formula (Connes 1999; Connes–Marcolli 2008
Chapter II Theorem 3.23),

$$
\{\,\gamma_{n} : n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots\,\}
\;\subseteq\;
\mathrm{spec}\bigl(T_{\mathrm{BC}}\bigr).
\tag{6.2}
$$

The inclusion (6.2) is the standard operator-algebraic restatement
of the explicit formula; the **equality** spec(T_BC) = {γ_n} is the
Hilbert–Pólya conjecture and is *open*. Paper 12 uses only the
inclusion (6.2).

For the precise statement of the explicit formula, the test
functions involved, and the regularisation, see research/18
(Connes–Marcolli explicit formula reference; in preparation).
Identity 14 — the unitary equivalence of T_BC with the
Connes–Consani–Moscovici scaling operator via Sz.-Nagy dilation —
is made rigorous in research/14 Part A.

### 6.2 Definition of H_R

**Definition 6.1 (research/02 §3.3).** The **Riemann subspace** is

$$
H_{R} \;:=\;
\overline{\mathrm{span}}\,\bigl\{\,
P_{\gamma_{n}}\,\Omega_{1} \;:\; n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots
\,\bigr\}
\;\subset\; H_{1},
\tag{6.3}
$$

where P_{γ_n} is the spectral projection of T_BC on the point γ_n
(well-defined given the inclusion (6.2) and the spectral theorem
for self-adjoint operators).

The canonical orthonormal basis of H_R is denoted {|γ_n⟩}, with

$$
T_{\mathrm{BC}}\,|\gamma_{n}\rangle \;=\; \gamma_{n}\,|\gamma_{n}\rangle,
\qquad
\langle \gamma_{n}\,|\,\gamma_{m}\rangle \;=\; \delta_{n,m}.
\tag{6.4}
$$

H_R is the operator-algebraic home of the Riemann zeros inside the
BC GNS space. The quantization-of-R operator of research/02,

$$
\hat R \;=\; \frac{\ell_{\mathrm{P}}}{\pi}\,
\exp\!\Bigl(T_{\mathrm{BC}}\cdot\frac{\pi^{2}}{2}\Bigr),
\tag{6.5}
$$

is defined on H_R by the functional calculus of T_BC and has
spectrum {R_n = (ℓ_P/π) exp(γ_n π²/2)}.

Note: H_R and H_1^{(N\*)} are **different** subspaces of H_1. The
first is spanned by the γ_n-eigenvectors of T_BC (the Riemann-zero
side); the second is spanned by the μ_n-orbit of Ω_1 (the
integer-index side). They are related by Mellin transform: the
transformation pairs the additive "log n" basis of H_1^{(N\*)} with
the "γ_n" basis of H_R (Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §4).

## 7. Galois Action

The Galois group

$$
G \;:=\; \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q})
\;\cong\; \hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{*}
\tag{7.1}
$$

acts on A_BC by automorphisms that fix μ_n and act on the phases
e(r) via the canonical action of Ẑ\* on Q/Z (Bost–Connes 1995
§3–4). This action commutes with σ_t, so G acts on KMS states.

At β = 1 the action fixes ω_1 (ω_1 is unique, hence G-invariant as
a state), but the induced action on the GNS Hilbert space H_1 is
non-trivial: G acts on H_1 by unitaries that intertwine π_1 and
fix Ω_1.

Restricted to subspaces:

- **On H_1^{(N\*)}**: the Galois action is trivial on the basis
  {μ_n Ω_1}, because G fixes each μ_n. In particular G acts on
  H_1^{(N\*)} by the identity.
- **On H_R**: the Galois action is non-trivial. It permutes the
  spectral projections P_{γ_n} according to the Galois orbit
  structure of the Riemann zeros in the explicit formula. The
  detailed decomposition of H_R into G-orbits is the subject of
  research/19 (Galois orbit decomposition of H_R; in preparation)
  and underlies the structural pattern of zero indices
  (research/09).

The endomotive formulation of Ha–Paugam 2005 /
Connes–Consani–Moscovici 2007 casts the combined (σ_t-flow,
G-action) structure as an *arithmetic thermodynamics*: A_BC is the
endomotive associated to the multiplicative monoid N\*, the KMS
transition at β = 1 is its Frobenius, and G is its Galois group of
deck transformations.

## 8. Connection to Physics: Identity 12

**Identity 12** (research/04; originally R27 of
`/Users/gsix/riemann-hypothesis/research/69-r27-bost-connes-realization.md`)
is the assertion that the QG5D e-circle is unitarily equivalent to
the BC system at β = 1. Precisely, let

- H_e be the L² Hilbert space of the compact e-circle of radius R
  with its cosine-mode basis {|n⟩_e : n ∈ N\*} (the momentum
  eigenstates with positive momentum),
- (A_e, φ_1) be the \*-algebra of dilations D_n (θ ↦ θ/n) and
  phases E_r (multiplication by e^{2πirθ}) with its canonical
  scaling-thermal state at "β = 1".

**Theorem 8.1 (Identity 12, research/04 Section 5).** *There is a
unique unitary*

$$
U \;:\; H_{e} \;\longrightarrow\; H_{1}^{(\mathbb{N}^{*})}
\tag{8.1}
$$

*sending |n⟩_e ↦ μ_n Ω_1, intertwining the five operator pairs*

| e-circle side | BC side |
|---|---|
| T_e = log(R p̂_e) | H_BC = log N̂ |
| D_n (dilation by n) | μ_n |
| E_r (phase by r) | e(r) |
| |n⟩_e | μ_n Ω_1 |
| scaling-thermal state | ω_1 |

*i.e., U\* T_e U = H_BC, U\* D_n U = μ_n, U\* E_r U = e(r).*

Identity 12 extends to H_R via research/02 §4.4: the e-circle
radius operator R̂_geom is unitarily equivalent under U to R̂ of
(6.5). Under this extension the smallest BC eigenvalue R_1 is the
observed e-circle radius at 5 ppb precision.

**This is the physical content**: the BC system is not a
mathematical curiosity indexed by the integers — it is the
arithmetic face of the QG5D extra dimension. The integers force
the universe because the universe is (unitarily) the BC system at
its phase transition.
