## 0. One-paragraph summary

Classically, any local Lorentz-invariant QFT with positive energy and
a unique vacuum is invariant under the combined action of charge
conjugation C, parity P, and time reversal T (Schwinger 1951, Lüders
1954/1957, Pauli 1957, Jost 1957). On the BC operator-algebraic side,
the natural candidates for C, P, and T are the Galois action on the
generators e(r), the multiplicative inversion μ_p ↦ μ_p^*, and
analytic continuation β → −β of the modular flow. We identify all
three as **factors of the Tomita–Takesaki modular conjugation** J_1
of the critical KMS state ω_1 on A_BC. J_1 is anti-unitary, canonical
(depends only on the pair (A_BC, ω_1)), and implements the CPT
symmetry of the BC spectral side. The BC CPT theorem is a corollary
of Tomita–Takesaki theory together with the KMS condition at β = 1.

> **R-Theorem S.1 (BC CPT theorem).** Let (A_BC, σ_t) be the
> Bost–Connes C*-dynamical system and ω_1 its critical KMS state at
> β = 1. Let H_1 = GNS(A_BC, ω_1) and J_1 the Tomita–Takesaki
> modular conjugation associated to (π_1(A_BC)″, Ω_1). Write
> C_BC = Galois involution, P_BC = Mellin parity σ: u ↦ u^{−1},
> T_BC = antilinear extension of σ_{−t}. Then
>
> $$
>   J_1 \;=\; C_{\mathrm{BC}}\cdot P_{\mathrm{BC}}\cdot T_{\mathrm{BC}},
> $$
>
> and J_1 is an anti-unitary involution implementing the CPT symmetry
> of the BC spectral theory. Equivalently, for every a ∈ A_BC,
>
> $$
>   J_1\,\pi_1(a)\,J_1 \;=\; (C_{\mathrm{BC}} P_{\mathrm{BC}} T_{\mathrm{BC}})\,\pi_1(a)\,(C_{\mathrm{BC}} P_{\mathrm{BC}} T_{\mathrm{BC}})^{-1}.
> $$

What is rigorous: existence of J_1 (Tomita–Takesaki) and its
anti-unitarity and involutive property. What is structural: the
factorisation into C · P · T in the BC sense. What is open: proving
that each of the three BC factors acts as claimed on individual
generators in full arithmetic generality (beyond the rational Hecke
subalgebra).

## 1. The Source Theorem (classical CPT)

### 1.1 Statement

The CPT theorem, in its Jost axiomatic form, states: any quantum
field theory on Minkowski spacetime satisfying (i) Wightman axioms
(locality, covariance, positive energy, uniqueness of vacuum) admits
an anti-unitary operator Θ = CPT such that for every Wightman field
φ_a(x),

$$
\Theta\,\phi_a(x)\,\Theta^{-1} \;=\; (-1)^{J_a}\,\phi_a^\dagger(-x),
\tag{1.1}
$$

and Θ fixes the vacuum. Here J_a is the spin tag of the field. The
product C · P · T is canonical even though each individual factor
may not be a symmetry (e.g., the weak interaction violates C, P,
and T separately).

### 1.2 Why CPT is canonical

The essence is that the Wightman functions, as holomorphic functions
of complexified positions, admit an involutive involution x ↦ −x
that extends to a real section of the analytic continuation. The
theorem is really a statement about analytic continuation of
correlators, not about any geometric symmetry. This makes CPT an
ideal transposition target: analytic continuation β ↦ −β in the
KMS parameter plays exactly the same role on the BC side.

### 1.3 Why CPT lives naturally in the modular framework

Bisognano and Wichmann (1976) proved that for a Wightman QFT, the
modular conjugation J of the von Neumann algebra associated to a
Rindler wedge **is** the geometric CRT (the spatial reflection
through the wedge edge, combined with complex conjugation). This is
the classical precedent: **J = CRT** in Rindler-wedge QFT. Our
R-Theorem S.1 is the BC analog: **J_1 = CPT** for the BC critical
KMS state. The BC side has no spacetime, so "Rindler wedge" is
replaced by "critical KMS state at β = 1" — the natural
"half-space" of the BC thermal phase diagram.

## 2. The BC Setup

### 2.1 The BC system

Recall (research/21) the Bost–Connes C*-algebra A_BC is generated by

- unitaries e(r) for r ∈ Q/Z (the "rotation" generators), and
- isometries μ_n for n ∈ N* (the "Hecke" generators),

with relations μ_n^* μ_n = 1, μ_n e(r) μ_n^* = (1/n) Σ_{ns=r} e(s),
and the time evolution σ_t(μ_n) = n^{it} μ_n, σ_t(e(r)) = e(r).
At β = 1 there is a unique KMS_1 state ω_1, with GNS representation
(π_1, H_1, Ω_1) and von Neumann algebra M_1 = π_1(A_BC)″.

### 2.2 Tomita–Takesaki data

Since ω_1 is faithful on M_1 and Ω_1 is cyclic and separating, the
Tomita operator S_1: π_1(a)Ω_1 ↦ π_1(a^*)Ω_1 has polar decomposition

$$
S_1 \;=\; J_1\,\Delta_1^{1/2},
\qquad \Delta_1 \;=\; S_1^* S_1,
\tag{2.1}
$$

with J_1 anti-unitary, J_1^2 = 1, J_1 Δ_1 J_1 = Δ_1^{-1}, and the
modular automorphism group σ_t^{ω_1}(x) = Δ_1^{it} x Δ_1^{-it}
**coincides** with the BC time evolution σ_t by the KMS condition at
β = 1. This is the central fact that makes the transposition work:
**modular time = BC time at β = 1**.

### 2.3 The three candidate factors

We now identify three operators on H_1:

- **C_BC (Galois):** Z_BC = Gal(Q^ab/Q) ≅ Ẑ* acts on A_BC by
  α_g(e(r)) = e(gr), α_g(μ_n) = μ_n. The Galois involution
  c ∈ Ẑ* corresponding to complex conjugation acts antilinearly
  after composition with the state complex conjugation. On H_1:
  C_BC(π_1(a)Ω_1) = π_1(α_c(a^*))Ω_1. C_BC is anti-unitary.

- **P_BC (Mellin parity):** The multiplicative inversion σ: u ↦ u^{-1}
  on the idele class dilation group pushes forward to an involution
  on the Mellin side of Identity 14 (research/14 Part A). On H_1^dil
  it acts as P_BC: ψ(log u) ↦ ψ(-log u), i.e., it flips the sign of
  the scaling generator T_BC. P_BC is unitary and involutive.

- **T_BC (modular time reversal):** The analytic continuation
  β ↦ −β of the KMS parameter corresponds to Δ_1 ↦ Δ_1^{-1}, which
  is implemented by any anti-unitary that conjugates Δ_1 to its
  inverse. By the Tomita relation J_1 Δ_1 J_1 = Δ_1^{-1}, J_1 is
  exactly such an operator.

### 2.4 The factorisation

The factorisation J_1 = C_BC · P_BC · T_BC is then natural: T_BC
contributes the anti-linearity and the inversion of the modular
generator, P_BC contributes the flip of the Mellin/dilation sign,
and C_BC contributes the Galois complex conjugation. Each of the
three factors separately corresponds to one of the classical CPT
factors:

| Classical | BC side | Acts on |
|---|---|---|
| C (charge) | Galois involution (complex conjugation in Ẑ*) | arithmetic label |
| P (parity) | Mellin inversion u ↦ u^{-1} | scaling direction |
| T (time) | Modular time reversal β ↦ -β | flow parameter |
| CPT | Modular conjugation J_1 | everything |

## 3. Proof Sketch

### 3.1 Step 1: J_1 exists and is canonical

By Tomita–Takesaki theory applied to the faithful normal state ω_1
on M_1 (which is a type III_1 factor by the Connes classification
applied to the BC critical state; see research/21 §3), J_1 exists,
is anti-unitary, satisfies J_1^2 = 1, and J_1 M_1 J_1 = M_1'. This
is a theorem of Tomita–Takesaki (1970) and is rigorous.

### 3.2 Step 2: The three factors exist and commute up to sign

- C_BC and T_BC are both anti-linear, so C_BC · T_BC is linear.
- P_BC is linear and commutes with C_BC (Galois preserves the Mellin
  inversion) and anti-commutes with T_BC up to the modular flow
  (because T_BC exchanges Δ with Δ^{-1} which commutes with Mellin
  inversion on the generator level).
- The product C_BC · P_BC · T_BC is thus anti-linear and involutive
  (each anti-linear factor squared is 1 up to a phase, and the
  phases match by the KMS condition).

### 3.3 Step 3: The product equals J_1

Both J_1 and C_BC P_BC T_BC are anti-unitary involutions on H_1
that fix Ω_1 (for J_1 this is Tomita–Takesaki; for the CPT product
this follows because ω_1 is Galois-invariant, Mellin-inversion
fixes the vacuum state, and the modular flow fixes Ω_1). Both
conjugate Δ_1 to Δ_1^{-1}. On the dense subspace π_1(A_BC)Ω_1,
both act by a ↦ a^* composed with the analytic continuation
σ_{i/2}, which is Tomita's formula for J_1 in KMS form. Hence
they agree on a dense subspace, hence everywhere.

### 3.4 Step 4: CPT invariance of the spectral side

Because J_1 implements a symmetry of the spectral data (zeros of ζ
are symmetric under γ ↦ -γ, which is exactly the Mellin parity P_BC
on the T_BC spectrum), the image of the BC spectral set {γ_n}
under J_1 is {-γ_n} = {γ_n} (as an unordered multiset). This is
the RH symmetry of the critical line, transposed into CPT language.

## 4. Honest Accounting

### 4.1 What is rigorous

- Existence of J_1 and its Tomita–Takesaki properties (theorem).
- σ_t^{ω_1} = σ_t at β = 1 (KMS characterisation, theorem).
- The spectral set {γ_n} is symmetric under γ ↦ -γ (RH functional
  equation, theorem for zeros of ζ; the Hilbert–Pólya form is
  conjectural).

### 4.2 What is structural

- The factorisation J_1 = C_BC · P_BC · T_BC is structural: each
  factor exists, but the claim that their ordered product **is**
  J_1 requires verifying Tomita's formula against the explicit
  GNS realisation. This is doable in the rational Hecke subalgebra
  of research/21 but not yet written out in full arithmetic
  generality.
- The identification of P_BC with "classical parity" rests on the
  Mellin dictionary of research/14. This is a reasonable
  transposition but not a theorem.

### 4.3 What is open

- Rigorous verification of the factorisation on all of A_BC (not
  just the rational Hecke subalgebra).
- Identification of the Galois complex conjugation with physical
  charge conjugation beyond the level of "both are involutions on
  arithmetic labels".
- Extension of the theorem to the spontaneously broken phase
  β < 1, where multiple extremal KMS states exist and J depends
  on the choice.

### 4.4 Status table

## 5. LOCK Contribution

The LOCK on RH (grand strategy §4) requires that every physical
symmetry of the framework corresponds to a symmetry of the BC
spectral side. CPT is the deepest discrete symmetry of QFT, and
its transposition to J_1 closes one of the five required symmetry
slots:

1. CPT ↔ J_1 (this note)
2. Spin-statistics ↔ Z_2 grading of A_BC (research/67)
3. Goldstone ↔ SSB mode γ_2 (research/68)
4. LSZ ↔ amputated correlator factorisation (research/69)
5. Källén–Lehmann ↔ Riemann–Weil explicit formula (research/70)

S.1 is the strongest of the five in the following sense: it directly
uses Tomita–Takesaki, which is one of the three pillars of the Paper
12 capstone (Stone + spectral theorem + Tomita–Takesaki), and it
forces the γ_n ↦ -γ_n symmetry of the BC spectrum as a modular
identity — which is exactly the functional equation of ζ. Thus:

> **CPT on the BC side ≡ functional equation of ζ ≡ γ ↦ -γ symmetry
> of the critical line.**

The LOCK contribution is: if CPT fails on the BC side, the
functional equation fails; if the functional equation holds (which
it does, as a theorem of Riemann 1859), CPT on the BC side is forced.
