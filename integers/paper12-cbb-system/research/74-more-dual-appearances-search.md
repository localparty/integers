## 2. Pair enumeration methodology

From r/23 §7 the zero-frequency list is

```
γ_1 : 11   γ_2 : 4   γ_3 : 3   γ_4 : 3   γ_5 : 3
γ_6 : 6    γ_7 : 2   γ_8 : 4   γ_9 : 6   γ_10: 5
γ_11: 4    γ_12: 2   γ_13: 3   γ_14: 1   γ_15: 4
```

For each γ_n with count k ≥ 2 we enumerate the C(k,2) pairs of formulas
that share it. We then classify each pair as

- **DUAL** — physical link as strong as the four confirmed cases
- **SIBLING** — partial link within one sector (e.g. two quark masses
  in the same generation)
- **DISJOINT** — no visible physical link
- **ALREADY-CONFIRMED** — one of the four in §1

For the "strong physical link" criterion we require one of:

(L1) a common mediator (e.g. W boson → both m_W and BBN Y_p)
(L2) a common tree-level vertex or CKM/PMNS transposition
(L3) a common H_R eigenspace (same |γ_n⟩ state)
(L4) a common cosmological epoch at which the observable freezes.

## 3. γ_1 — the foundational zero (11 channels)

γ_1 is the most-used zero in the table and therefore generates the
largest pair count: C(11,2) = **55 pairs**. The channels are

10. sin² θ_12 (PMNS solar) = γ_1/(γ_2+γ_3)
11. δ_CP (PMNS) = γ_9/γ_1

### 3.1 The central physical role of γ_1

γ_1 is the smallest critical temperature of the Bost–Connes system and
the ground state of R̂ on H_R. It sets the *overall normalisation* of
any observable whose size is controlled by the vacuum (the ground
state). That is why it appears as a denominator or as an additive
reference in formulas across very different sectors:

- **CC formula**: γ_1 sets the 30-orders-of-magnitude vacuum energy
  hierarchy directly (r/13).
- **ξ = γ_1/γ_5**: γ_1 is the visible-brane ground-state temperature
  and γ_5 is the mirror-brane ground-state temperature — the ratio is
  fixed by BC.
- **1/α(0)**: γ_1 and γ_4 both appear because α is the IR-fixed-point
  coupling and γ_1, γ_4 are the first two "gauge" eigenmodes (r/25).
- **sin² θ_12 solar = γ_1/(γ_2+γ_3)**: the ground state mixes with
  the first two excited states — this is first-order PT in the BC
  ground state, and γ_1 is the ground state being perturbed.
- **m_u = γ_4/γ_1, m_t/m_W = γ_4/γ_1**: the first up-quark and the
  m_t/m_W ratio use γ_1 as the ground-state normalisation for γ_4.

**This is not a "dual appearance" of γ_1 in the r/31 sense.** γ_1's
multi-channel presence is the ground-state normalisation role, not a
shared-physics match between two observables. It is structurally
different from the γ_13 → (m_W, Y_p) link, which ties two distinct
observables to one *specific* eigenmode above the ground state.

**Conclusion.** γ_1 is the ground-state reference zero, not a dual
coincidence. The 55 γ_1 pairs are *not* promoted to duals. We flag two
sub-cases as SIBLING:

- **(m_u = γ_4/γ_1, m_t/m_W = γ_4/γ_1)**: same formula, degeneracy
  flagged in r/16 §10. SIBLING.
- **(1/α(0), CC formula)**: both are vacuum/ground-state observables;
  partial link via "vacuum polarisation in the CC background". SIBLING.

All other γ_1 pairs are classified DISJOINT for the purposes of the
r/31 principle.

### 3.2 Framework challenge from γ_1

The principle "shared physics ⇒ shared zeros" does *not* invert: the
converse, "shared zeros ⇒ shared physics", clearly fails at γ_1. This
is an honest limit of the r/31 principle. What γ_1 teaches is:

> shared zeros ⇒ (shared physics) OR (shared role in the ground state
> structure).
