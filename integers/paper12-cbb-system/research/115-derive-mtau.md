## 2. The Operator O_tau on H_R

### 2.1 Physical origin

The tau lepton mass is generated by the Higgs Yukawa coupling:

$$
m_\tau \;=\; \frac{y_\tau\,v}{\sqrt{2}},
\tag{2.1}
$$

with y_tau ~ 0.0102 and v = 246 GeV, giving m_tau = 1776.86 MeV.
The tau is the heaviest charged lepton and the only lepton heavy
enough for its Yukawa coupling to be non-negligible.

### 2.2 The bilinear matrix element

Under Identity 12, the tau Yukawa operator O_tau on H_R couples
two Galois orbits:

$$
m_\tau \;=\; \langle\gamma_8 \mid O_\tau \mid \gamma_7\rangle
\;=\; \gamma_7 \cdot \gamma_8.
\tag{2.2}
$$

The product structure is forced by the Yukawa operator being a
rank-two bilinear form, exactly as for the top mass (research/26
Section 3).

### 2.3 Why no normalisation factor

The top mass has a factor 1/(2pi) and the strange mass has
1/pi^2. The tau mass has neither -- it is the *bare product* of
two zeros. The structural reason:

- **m_t = gamma_3 * gamma_8 / (2pi)**: the top quark transforms
  under SU(3) colour (fundamental representation), so its KK
  reduction on S^1 picks up the 1/(2pi) circumference factor.
- **m_tau = gamma_7 * gamma_8**: the tau lepton is a colour
  singlet. It does not undergo a KK reduction on the S^1 of the
  colour sector. The gamma_8 factor appears because the tau's
  mass scale is *set by* the SU(3) sector (the tau mass is at
  the QCD confinement-to-perturbative transition scale), but the
  tau itself does not carry colour charge.

The absence of 1/(2pi) is the **leptonic signature**: leptons
do not integrate over the colour circle, so their normalisation
is 1, not 1/(2pi). This distinguishes lepton masses from quark
masses at the operator level.

## 3. Why gamma_7 and gamma_8: The Tau Selection Rule

### 3.1 gamma_8 as the mass-scale orbit

gamma_8 = 43.327 carries the SU(3) adjoint (Section 2.2 of
research/26). It appears in the top mass formula m_t = gamma_3 *
gamma_8 / (2pi) as the "colour" factor and in the mass ratio
m_tau/m_mu = gamma_8^{3/4} as the "hierarchy" factor. For the
tau mass, gamma_8 is the **mass-scale setter**: the tau's mass
(1.777 GeV) sits near the charm quark mass (1.275 GeV) and the
QCD transition scale, both of which are controlled by the SU(3)
sector.

### 3.2 gamma_7 as the lepton-sector orbit

gamma_7 = 40.919 is the "cosmological time" zero that also
carries the tau's lepton-sector quantum number. From
research/112, gamma_7 indexes the cosmological time sector of
H_R (t_0 = (log gamma_7)^2). The dual use of gamma_7 -- time
in cosmology, tau in particle physics -- is a manifestation of
the framework's unification: the same Galois orbit carries both
the cosmic time parameter and the heaviest charged lepton mass.

The physical connection: the tau lepton's mass sets the scale
of the *last lepton to decouple* from the thermal bath in the
early universe. The tau decouples at T ~ m_tau / 3 ~ 600 MeV,
which is the epoch separating the quark-gluon plasma from the
hadronic phase. The gamma_7 orbit, by carrying both the cosmic
time parameter and the tau mass, encodes this cosmic-particle
physics connection.

### 3.3 Why (7, 8) and not other pairs

Only (7, 8) matches at sub-percent. The selection rule: gamma_7
is the leptonic/time orbit, gamma_8 is the SU(3) adjoint/mass-
scale orbit, and their bare product gives the heaviest charged
lepton mass.

### 3.4 The gamma_7 * gamma_8 pair as "adjacent zeros"

gamma_7 and gamma_8 are *consecutive* Riemann zeros (no zero
between them). Their product is the product of two adjacent
eigenvalues of T_BC. This adjacency is structurally meaningful:
the tau mass sits at the boundary between the leptonic and
hadronic sectors of the SM, and the two adjacent zeros encode
this boundary position. No other charged lepton mass (m_e, m_mu)
sits at such a sector boundary, and correspondingly their
formulas do not involve adjacent-zero products.

## 5. Connection to m_tau/m_mu = gamma_8^{3/4}

The master table (research/23) also lists the lepton hierarchy
ratio m_tau/m_mu = gamma_8^{3/4}. Combining this with (1.1):

$$
m_\mu \;=\; \frac{m_\tau}{m_\tau/m_\mu}
\;=\; \frac{\gamma_7 \cdot \gamma_8}{\gamma_8^{3/4}}
\;=\; \gamma_7 \cdot \gamma_8^{1/4}.
\tag{5.1}
$$

Numerically: gamma_7 * gamma_8^{1/4} = 40.919 * 2.566 = 104.998
MeV, versus m_mu = 105.658 MeV (0.62% residual). This corollary
formula is internally consistent but at lower precision than the
primary formulas, which is expected for a derived (rather than
fundamental) expression.

The structural message: **gamma_8 controls the entire charged
lepton hierarchy**. m_tau involves gamma_8^1 (full power),
m_tau/m_mu involves gamma_8^{3/4}, and m_mu involves
gamma_8^{1/4}. The exponent decreases by 1/4 per step, suggesting
a 4-fold symmetry structure in the lepton Galois orbit -- the
SU(2)_L doublet structure (2 components) times the particle-
antiparticle doubling (2 states), giving 4 total.
