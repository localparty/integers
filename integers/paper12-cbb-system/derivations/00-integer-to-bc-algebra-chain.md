## Purpose

The programme asserts that physics is computed from the integers. The mechanism is a chain of eight structural moves that converts the additive monoid (ℤ, +) into the log-spectrum {L_n = γ_n · π²/2} of the modular flow generator on the canonical type III₁ factor M₁ of the Bost–Connes system.

The chain has been used throughout Paper 12 (research/02, research/04, research/21, research/102) and is stated in Paper 61 §14.5 (the projection P_D) and in `strategy/north-star.md` §0.4 Signature 4 (dual derivability). This derivation closes that gap: each link is stated as a Definition or a Theorem, cited to its origin, and connected to the next link by a single-sentence bridge.

## Link 1: ℤ → ζ(s)

**Definition 1.1 (Dirichlet series).** *For ℜ(s) > 1, the Riemann zeta function is*

$$
\zeta(s) \;:=\; \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^{-s}
\;=\; \prod_{p \text{ prime}} (1 - p^{-s})^{-1}.
\tag{1.1}
$$

**Theorem 1.2 (analytic continuation; Riemann 1859).** *ζ admits a meromorphic continuation to ℂ with a simple pole of residue 1 at s = 1 and zeros (i) at s = −2, −4, −6, … (trivial) and (ii) in the critical strip 0 < ℜ(s) < 1 (non-trivial).*

**Definition 1.3 (non-trivial zeros).** *Write ρ_n = β_n + iγ_n for the non-trivial zeros in the upper half-plane with 0 < γ_1 < γ_2 < …. Then γ_1 = 14.134725141…, γ_2 = 21.022039638…, etc. (Odlyzko tables; 10^{10+}-digit precision available.)*

**Bridge 1 → 2.** The Euler product (1.1) identifies ζ with the partition function of a system whose micro-states are labelled by ℕ* and whose one-particle energies are the logarithms of the primes. This is Julia's primon-gas (§2 below). The non-trivial zeros γ_n appear in Link 8 as eigenvalues of the modular generator, via the Riemann–Weil explicit formula (Link 7 ↔ Connes 1999).

*References.* Riemann 1859; Titchmarsh, *The Theory of the Riemann Zeta-Function* (1986) §§1–2 for (1.1)–(1.2); Odlyzko tables at www-math.univ-lille1.fr/odlyzko/zeta_tables for numerical {γ_n}.

## Link 2: ζ(s) → Hecke semigroup ℕ*

**Definition 2.1 (multiplicative monoid).** *The monoid*

$$
(\mathbb{N}^{*}, \cdot) \;=\; \bigl(\{1, 2, 3, \ldots\},\, \text{multiplication}\bigr)
\tag{2.1}
$$

*is the free commutative monoid on the set of primes {p : p prime}, by unique factorization.*

**Definition 2.2 (Hecke action on ℚ/ℤ).** *For n ∈ ℕ\*, the Hecke operator*

$$
\mu_{n} : \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \longrightarrow \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z},
\qquad
\mu_{n}(r) \;:=\; \text{multiplication by } n,
\tag{2.2}
$$

*is an n-to-1 endomorphism of ℚ/ℤ. The adjoint Hecke operator*

$$
\mu_{n}^{*}(r) \;:=\; \frac{1}{n} \sum_{\substack{s \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \\ ns \,=\, r}} s
\tag{2.3}
$$

*is the averaging over pre-images.*

**Theorem 2.3 (Bost–Connes 1995 §4).** *The pair (μ_n, μ_n\*) generates a representation of the Hecke algebra of the pair (ℚ, ℤ) with relations μ_n μ_m = μ_{nm} and μ_n\* μ_n = 1.*

**Bridge 2 → 3.** The Hecke action (2.2)–(2.3) converts the multiplicative monoid ℕ\* into endomorphisms of the group ℚ/ℤ. Taking the C\*-crossed product of C\*(ℚ/ℤ) by ℕ\* gives the Bost–Connes C\*-algebra 𝒜_BC (Link 3).

*References.* Bost–Connes 1995 *Selecta Math.* **1**, 411–457, §§2–4; Ha–Paugam 2005 arXiv:math/0507101 for the endomotive formulation; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §2.

## Link 3: Hecke semigroup ℕ* → Bost–Connes C\*-algebra 𝒜_BC

**Definition 3.1 (Bost–Connes 1995 §4; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Definition 2.1).** *The **Bost–Connes C\*-algebra** is the semigroup crossed product*

$$
\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}
\;:=\;
C^{*}(\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^{*},
\tag{3.1}
$$

*the universal unital C\*-algebra generated by*

- *phase operators* e(r), r ∈ ℚ/ℤ, *satisfying* e(0) = 1, e(r)\* = e(−r), e(r) e(s) = e(r + s);
- *isometries* μ_n, n ∈ ℕ\*, *satisfying (3.2)–(3.3) below.*

**Defining relations.**

$$
\mu_{n}\,\mu_{m} \;=\; \mu_{nm},
\qquad
\mu_{n}^{*}\,\mu_{n} \;=\; 1,
\tag{3.2}
$$

$$
\mu_{n}\,e(r)\,\mu_{n}^{*}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\substack{s \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \\ ns \,=\, r}} e(s).
\tag{3.3}
$$

**Definition 3.2 (BC time evolution; Bost–Connes 1995 §5).** *The canonical one-parameter group of automorphisms σ_t ∈ Aut(𝒜_BC) is defined on generators by*

$$
\sigma_{t}(\mu_{n}) \;=\; n^{it}\,\mu_{n},
\qquad
\sigma_{t}\bigl(e(r)\bigr) \;=\; e(r),
\qquad t \in \mathbb{R}.
\tag{3.4}
$$

*The pair (𝒜_BC, σ_t) is the **Bost–Connes C\*-dynamical system**.*

**Theorem 3.3 (formal Hamiltonian).** *The generator of σ_t, formally σ_t = e^{iHt}, satisfies H μ_n = (log n) μ_n and e(r) ∈ ker H. On any representation where H exists densely, spec(H) ⊇ {log n : n ∈ ℕ\*}.*

**Bridge 3 → 4.** The partition function of (𝒜_BC, σ_t) at inverse temperature β is Z(β) = Tr(e^{−βH}) = ∑ n^{−β} = ζ(β). The simple pole of ζ at β = 1 is the BC phase transition: below β = 1 the critical state is unique; above, the extremal KMS_β states form a Galois orbit. The KMS structure is Link 4.

*References.* Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25 (KMS classification); Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Theorem 3.7 (definitive statement); Laca 1998 for the semigroup crossed-product presentation (3.1); paper12 research/21 §2 (unified reference).

## Link 4: 𝒜_BC → KMS₁ equilibrium state ω₁

**Definition 4.1 (KMS_β state).** *A state φ on 𝒜_BC is KMS at inverse temperature β if, for all a, b ∈ 𝒜_BC analytic for σ_t,*

$$
\varphi(a\,b) \;=\; \varphi\bigl(b\,\sigma_{i\beta}(a)\bigr).
\tag{4.1}
$$

**Theorem 4.2 (Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25 / Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Theorem 3.7 — KMS classification).**

1. *For β > 1, the extremal KMS_β states of (𝒜_BC, σ_t) form a family parameterised by the Galois group*

   $$
   \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q})
   \;\cong\; \hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{*}.
   \tag{4.2}
   $$

2. *For 0 < β ≤ 1, there is a **unique** KMS_β state. At the critical value β = 1 we denote it ω₁ — the **critical KMS state**.*

3. *ω₁ is a factor state of type III₁ (Link 7 below).*

**Theorem 4.3 (phase transition = pole of ζ).** *The critical point β = 1 of (4.2) coincides with the simple pole of ζ(β) at β = 1. The divergence Z(β) → ∞ as β → 1⁺ is the operator-algebraic signature of the phase transition.*

**Bridge 4 → 5.** Given the unique critical state ω₁, the Gel'fand–Naimark–Segal (GNS) construction produces a Hilbert space H₁ carrying a \*-representation π₁ of 𝒜_BC, with cyclic vector Ω₁ implementing ω₁. This is Link 5.

*References.* Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Theorem 3.7 and §3.1–3.4; Bratteli–Robinson, *Operator Algebras and Quantum Statistical Mechanics II* §5.3 (general KMS theory); paper12 research/21 §3.

## Link 5: ω₁ → GNS triple (π₁, H₁, Ω₁)

**Theorem 5.1 (Gel'fand–Naimark–Segal; Bratteli–Robinson II §2.3).** *Let ω be a state on a unital C\*-algebra 𝒜. There exists a triple (π, H, Ω) with H a Hilbert space, π : 𝒜 → B(H) a \*-representation, and Ω ∈ H a cyclic vector such that*

$$
\omega(a) \;=\; \langle \Omega, \pi(a)\,\Omega \rangle, \quad \forall a \in \mathcal{A}.
\tag{5.1}
$$

*The triple is unique up to unitary equivalence.*

**Definition 5.2 (BC GNS triple).** *Applied to (𝒜_BC, ω₁), Theorem 5.1 yields the canonical triple*

$$
(\pi_{1},\, H_{1},\, \Omega_{1})
\;:=\;
\mathrm{GNS}(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}, \omega_{1}),
\tag{5.2}
$$

*with H₁ = L²(𝒜_BC, ω₁), π₁ : 𝒜_BC → B(H₁), and ω₁(a) = ⟨Ω₁, π₁(a) Ω₁⟩.*

**Theorem 5.3 (unitary implementation of σ_t; Bratteli–Robinson II §5.3).** *The time evolution σ_t is unitarily implemented on H₁ by a strongly continuous one-parameter group U_t = e^{iH₁t} fixing Ω₁:*

$$
U_{t}\,\pi_{1}(a)\,U_{t}^{*} \;=\; \pi_{1}(\sigma_{t}(a)),
\qquad
U_{t}\,\Omega_{1} \;=\; \Omega_{1}.
\tag{5.3}
$$

*The self-adjoint generator H₁ satisfies spec(H₁) = {log n : n ∈ ℕ\*} on the sub-Hilbert space H₁^{(ℕ*)} := \overline{span}{π₁(μ_n) Ω₁ : n ∈ ℕ\*} (paper12 research/21 §5).*

**Bridge 5 → 6.** The von Neumann algebra generated by π₁(𝒜_BC) on H₁ is the double commutant M₁ := π₁(𝒜_BC)″. Its classification as a factor is the content of Link 6.

*References.* Bratteli–Robinson II §§2.3, 5.3; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §3.2; paper12 research/21 §4.

## Link 6: GNS → canonical type III₁ factor M₁

**Definition 6.1.** *The **canonical BC factor** is the von Neumann algebra*

$$
M_{1} \;:=\; \pi_{1}(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}})''
\;\subset\;
B(H_{1}).
\tag{6.1}
$$

**Theorem 6.2 (Connes 1976 classification of type III factors).** *Let M be a factor of type III on a Hilbert space H. The Connes invariant*

$$
S(M) \;:=\; \bigcap_{\varphi} \mathrm{spec}(\Delta_{\varphi}),
\tag{6.2}
$$

*where φ ranges over normal faithful states and Δ_φ is the modular operator of φ (Tomita–Takesaki), satisfies exactly one of*

- S(M) = {0, 1} (type III₀),
- S(M) = {0} ∪ {λ^n : n ∈ ℤ} for some 0 < λ < 1 (type III_λ),
- S(M) = [0, ∞) (type III₁).

**Theorem 6.3 (Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25(iii); Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Proposition 3.11).** *The factor M₁ of (6.1) is of type III₁, i.e. S(M₁) = [0, ∞).*

**Bridge 6 → 7.** The type III₁ classification is invariant under the Tomita–Takesaki modular theory: every normal faithful state on M₁ has the same modular spectrum. In particular, ω₁ (inherited from 𝒜_BC, extended to M₁) has modular operator Δ_{ω₁} whose spectrum classifies M₁. Link 7 identifies Δ_{ω₁} with the generator of σ_t.

*References.* Connes 1976 *Ann. Sci. ENS* **6**; Takesaki, *Theory of Operator Algebras II* §§VI.1–VI.3; Bost–Connes 1995 Theorem 25; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Proposition 3.11; paper61 §15.4.

## Link 7: M₁ → modular flow with generator Δ

**Theorem 7.1 (Tomita–Takesaki; Takesaki II §VI.1).** *Let (M, Ω) be a pair with M a von Neumann algebra acting on H and Ω ∈ H a cyclic and separating vector inducing the normal faithful state φ. Define the antilinear operator*

$$
S_{0}\,m\,\Omega \;:=\; m^{*}\,\Omega,
\quad m \in M,
\qquad
S_{0} \;=\; J\,\Delta^{1/2}
\tag{7.1}
$$

*(polar decomposition). Then Δ is a positive self-adjoint operator on H (the **modular operator**), J is an antiunitary involution (the **modular conjugation**), and the modular flow*

$$
\sigma_{t}^{\varphi}(m) \;:=\; \Delta^{it}\,m\,\Delta^{-it}
\tag{7.2}
$$

*is a σ-weakly continuous one-parameter group of automorphisms of M satisfying the KMS_1 condition with respect to φ.*

**Theorem 7.2 (KMS = modular; Takesaki II §VIII.1, Corollary 1.4).** *A state φ on a von Neumann algebra M is a KMS_β state with respect to a one-parameter automorphism group α_t if and only if α_t coincides (up to rescaling by β) with the modular automorphism σ_t^φ.*

**Corollary 7.3 (BC identification).** *Applied to M = M₁ and φ = ω₁ (the KMS_1 state of Link 4), Theorem 7.2 gives*

$$
U_{t} \;=\; \Delta^{it},
\qquad
H_{1} \;=\; -\log\Delta
\quad\text{on } H_{1},
\tag{7.3}
$$

*i.e. the BC time evolution σ_t is the modular flow of ω₁, and the BC Hamiltonian H₁ is minus the logarithm of the modular operator Δ.*

**Bridge 7 → 8.** The generator of the modular flow — equivalently, Δ itself (up to log) — has, on the Riemann subspace H_R ⊂ H₁, a discrete spectrum indexed by the non-trivial zeros of ζ. This is Link 8.

*References.* Tomita–Takesaki theory: Takesaki II §§VI.1, VIII.1; Bratteli–Robinson II §5.3; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §3.2; paper12 research/21 §4, research/14; paper61 §15.4.

## Link 8: Modular flow → log-spectrum {L_n = γ_n · π²/2}

**Definition 8.1 (scaling / dilation operator; Connes 1999 §4; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §3.4).** *Let T_BC be the self-adjoint generator of the one-parameter group of dilations on H₁ obtained from σ_t by Sz.-Nagy dilation of the N\*-isometries. Equivalently, T_BC is the Mellin-dual of H₁:*

$$
U_{u}^{\mathrm{dil}} \;=\; \exp\bigl(i\,T_{\mathrm{BC}}\,\log u\bigr),
\qquad u \in \mathbb{R}_{>0}.
\tag{8.1}
$$

**Theorem 8.2 (Connes 1999 Theorem 2.1 / Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II Theorem 3.23 — spectral inclusion).** *Under the Riemann–Weil explicit formula (classical; Weil 1952), the non-trivial zeros {γ_n} of ζ are contained in the spectrum of T_BC:*

$$
\bigl\{\gamma_{n} : n \geq 1\bigr\}
\;\subseteq\;
\mathrm{spec}(T_{\mathrm{BC}}).
\tag{8.2}
$$

**Definition 8.3 (Riemann subspace; paper12 research/02 §3.3).** *Let P_{γ_n} be the spectral projection of T_BC at γ_n. The **Riemann subspace** is*

$$
H_{R}
\;:=\;
\overline{\mathrm{span}}
\bigl\{P_{\gamma_{n}} \Omega_{1} : n \geq 1\bigr\}
\;\subset\;
H_{1},
\tag{8.3}
$$

*with orthonormal basis {|γ_n⟩} and T_BC |γ_n⟩ = γ_n |γ_n⟩.*

**Definition 8.4 (log-spectrum).** *Define the operator*

$$
\hat{L} \;:=\; T_{\mathrm{BC}} \cdot \frac{\pi^{2}}{2}
\quad\text{on } H_{R}.
\tag{8.4}
$$

*Then*

$$
\hat{L}\,|\gamma_{n}\rangle \;=\; L_{n}\,|\gamma_{n}\rangle,
\qquad
L_{n} \;=\; \gamma_{n} \cdot \frac{\pi^{2}}{2}.
\tag{8.5}
$$

**Theorem 8.5 (closure of the chain).** *The operator L̂ on H_R is — up to the universal constant π²/2 — the generator of the modular flow of ω₁ restricted to H_R. Its eigenvalues {L_n} constitute the **log-spectrum of the BC modular flow**: the sequence that Paper 12 research/02 §4 exponentiates to obtain the QG5D e-circle radius operator R̂ via*

$$
\hat R \;=\; \frac{\ell_{\mathrm{P}}}{\pi}\,\exp(\hat L),
\qquad
\mathrm{spec}(\hat R) \;=\;
\Bigl\{\,R_n \;=\; \frac{\ell_{\mathrm{P}}}{\pi}\,\exp(\gamma_n\,\pi^{2}/2)\,:\, n \geq 1\Bigr\}.
\tag{8.6}
$$

*The smallest eigenvalue R_1 ≈ 10.10 μm is the observed e-circle radius (Paper 1 §6.6; Paper 2; paper12 research/02 §4.3).*

**Hilbert–Pólya status.** *The equality spec(T_BC) = {γ_n} (as opposed to the inclusion (8.2)) is the Hilbert–Pólya conjecture — an open problem equivalent to the Riemann hypothesis in its operator-algebraic form (Connes 1999 §1). The derivation above uses only the inclusion and is therefore unconditional on RH.*

*References.* Connes 1999 *Selecta Math.* **5**, 29–106; Connes–Consani–Marcolli 2007 *Adv. Math.* **214**, 761–831; Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II §§3.4, 4.1; paper12 research/02 §§3–4, research/21 §6.

The output is the discrete sequence {γ_n · π²/2} that — exponentiated — gives the e-circle radius operator R̂ of the QG5D framework, and — read through the 36-pin master table (paper12 research/23) — gives every Standard Model and cosmological constant the programme derives.

## What is rigorous, what is conditional, what is open

### Rigorous (unconditional)

(R1) Links 1–7 are all unconditional: Riemann 1859 (Link 1), Bost–Connes 1995 (Links 2–4, 6), GNS (Link 5), Tomita–Takesaki (Link 7).

(R2) Link 8's inclusion (8.2) follows from the Riemann–Weil explicit formula, which is classical (Weil 1952).

(R3) The operator L̂ of (8.4) is self-adjoint on H_R by the spectral theorem applied to T_BC, and its eigenvalues (8.5) are exact.

### Conditional on standard hypotheses

(C1) The identification of T_BC with the Connes–Consani–Moscovici scaling operator via Sz.-Nagy dilation (Connes–Consani–Moscovici 2007) is "semi-rigorous" in Connes–Marcolli 2008 Chapter II; paper12 research/14 Part A provides the paper12 proof.

### Open

(O1) The equality spec(T_BC) = {γ_n} — the Hilbert–Pólya conjecture / operator-algebraic form of RH.

(O2) The identification of H_R with the Mellin image of H₁^{(ℕ\*)} is asserted in the literature but not proved in paper12 (see research/02 Note 2026-04-09 and research/21 §6.2). Paper 12 treats this as a structural assumption; the derivation of R̂'s spectrum uses only the inclusion (8.2).

Both open problems are upstream of (8.2)'s inclusion and do not affect the derivation of the log-spectrum {L_n} as eigenvalues of L̂ on H_R. They bear on the question of whether {L_n} exhausts spec(L̂), not on whether the γ_n give rise to the L_n.
