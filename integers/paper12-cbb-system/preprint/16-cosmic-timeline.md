Maybe the relation is:
```
log(M_Pl / M_n) = γ_n × π²/2 - log π
```

For γ_1: M_1 = M_Pl × exp(-68.6) ≈ M_Pl × 10⁻³⁰ ≈ 10⁻¹² GeV ≈ meV

For γ_2: M_2 = M_Pl × exp(-102.6) ≈ M_Pl × 10⁻⁴⁵ ≈ 10⁻²⁷ GeV

So the interpretation has to be cleaner.

### A different interpretation

Maybe the gauge transitions don't correspond to specific energy
scales but to specific RATIOS or PHASES of the BC system.

In the BC system:
- β > 1: ordered phase, KMS states multiple
- β = 1: phase transition (the pole of ζ)
- β < 1: disordered phase

The Riemann zeros at γ_n = imaginary parts of zeros at Re(s) = 1/2
correspond to β_n = 1 + 2iγ_n. These are COMPLEX temperatures,
not real ones. They represent oscillatory features of the partition
function NEAR the phase transition.

In this picture, "transitioning from γ_k to γ_j" doesn't mean moving
between energy scales — it means moving between OSCILLATORY MODES
of the BC system near β = 1.

The cosmic evolution might be the universe moving through these
oscillatory modes as it cools through the BC phase transition.
