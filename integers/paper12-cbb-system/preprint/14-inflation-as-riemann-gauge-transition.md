## Why This Could Be Real

### The discreteness of the Bost-Connes spectrum

The BC system has critical temperatures at the Riemann zeros:

```
β_n = 1 + 2iγ_n
```

These are DISCRETE values. The system can sit at one of these
critical points (a "stable phase") and transition between them.

**The hypothesis:** Cosmological evolution corresponds to the
universe traversing these discrete BC phases. Each phase has a
specific e-circle radius (set by γ_n × π²/2). Transitions happen
when the system "rolls" from one critical point to the next.

### Inflation as a single discrete jump

In standard inflation, the inflaton field rolls slowly down a
potential, providing a continuous expansion. The number 60 e-folds
is set by the potential shape and initial conditions — not by any
fundamental principle.

In the Riemann gauge picture, inflation is a SINGLE DISCRETE
transition between specific BC critical points. The e-fold count
is determined by which gauges the transition connects.

If the universe transitions γ_5 → γ_2, then N = 58.79 e-folds
EXACTLY (in this framework). No tuning, no initial conditions
required.

### Why specifically γ_5 → γ_2

This is the open question. Possible reasons:
- γ_5 corresponds to the framework's "initial vacuum state"
- γ_2 corresponds to the "thermalised post-inflation state"
- The transition is set by topological/symmetry constraints
- Or it's selected by the framework's other dynamics

The selection rule is currently empirical (we observe ~60 e-folds,
so the transition is the one giving that number).


**Possible resolutions:**
