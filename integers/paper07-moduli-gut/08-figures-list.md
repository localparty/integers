## Figure 1: The Two Regimes

**Description:** Schematic showing the two stabilization regimes of the
internal manifold. Left: S1 (large, super-Planckian radius R ~ 10^{-4} m)
stabilized by the perturbative Casimir potential. Right: CP2 x S2 (tiny,
sub-Planckian radii r ~ 10^{-18} m) stabilized by non-perturbative G4
flux. The 38-order-of-magnitude gap between the two scales is visualized
as a logarithmic separation, emphasizing that one geometry produces two
completely different stabilization mechanisms.

**Section:** Section 1 (Introduction)

**Type:** Schematic / conceptual diagram

## Figure 2: The Moduli Potential V(rho)

**Description:** Plot of the flux-generated potential V_flux(r2, r3) as a
function of the radius ratio rho = r2/r3, with r2 the CP2 radius and r3
the S2 radius. The potential shows a minimum at rho = sqrt(3)/2
corresponding to the GUT flux solution (n1 = 9, n2 = -17). Axes labeled
with V in Planck units (vertical) and rho (horizontal). The minimum is
marked with the GUT condition alpha_2 = alpha_3.

**Section:** Section 3 (Moduli Minimum and GUT Condition)

**Type:** Function plot

## Figure 3: The GUT Flux Lattice

**Description:** Integer lattice diagram in the (n1, n2) plane. Each
lattice point represents an allowed flux pair. The GUT unification
condition n2/n1 = -17/9 is drawn as a line through the origin. The
minimal coprime integer solution (9, -17) is highlighted with a filled
circle. Nearby lattice points are shown as open circles to emphasize the
discreteness of the flux choice. The tadpole bound |n1| + |n2| <= chi/24
is indicated as a boundary region.

**Section:** Section 3 (Moduli Minimum and GUT Condition)

**Type:** Lattice / discrete mathematics diagram

## Figure 4: The Hilltop Inflaton

**Description:** The cosine potential V = mu^4 [1 - cos(a_G / f)] with
f = M_Pl / sqrt(3), plotted as V/mu^4 vs a_G/f from 0 to 2*pi. The
hilltop at a_G = pi*f is labeled. The inflationary slow-roll region
near the hilltop is shaded, with an arrow indicating the field rolling
toward the reheating minimum at a_G = 0 (mod 2*pi*f). The number of
e-folds N ~ 60 is indicated along the slow-roll region.

**Section:** Section 5 (Inflaton from G4 Axion)

**Type:** Function plot with annotations

## Figure 5: The n_s-r Plane

**Description:** Standard inflation observables plot with spectral index
n_s on the horizontal axis and tensor-to-scalar ratio r on the vertical
axis (log scale). Shown: Planck 2018 1-sigma and 2-sigma contours (blue
shaded), the G4 axion hilltop prediction (n_s = 0.967, r ~ 0.001) as a
filled point with error bars, the CMB-S4 sensitivity band sigma(r) ~ 0.003
as a horizontal dashed line, and the Starobinsky/R^2 attractor prediction
for comparison. The G4 prediction sits within the Planck-allowed region
and above the CMB-S4 detection threshold.

**Section:** Section 5 (Inflaton from G4 Axion)

**Type:** Data comparison / observational constraints plot
