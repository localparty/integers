## §5.2 Vertex Mass-Independence: Status and Assumptions

**Theorem 1 (proved).** In the two-loop sunset diagram for the GS counterterm in
5D linearized gravity on M⁴ × S¹/Z₂, the three-graviton vertex y-integral coupling
I_{+++}(n₁, n₂, n₁+n₂) = πR/4 is a universal constant independent of the KK levels
n₁, n₂ ≥ 1. Combined with S₀² = 0 (leading) and Theorem K.1 (subleading), this gives
C_GS = 0 at all orders. All three assumptions A1–A3 are proved (Lemmas A1–A3); the
result is unconditional within linearized 5D gravity on flat M⁴ × S¹/Z₂; see §4.6
for the full proof chain.

Source: research memo `integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/01-three-graviton-vertex.md`.

**Status.** The y-integral computation is complete and unconditional. All three
assumptions have been proved: A1 (Lemma A1; research memo
`integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/02-de-donder-gauge-vertex.md`), A2 (Lemma A2; research memo
`integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/03-a2-graviphoton-radion-sector.md`), and A3 (Lemma A3; research

## §5.2a Assumption A1: de Donder gauge vertex numerator — **Proved (Lemma A1)**

**What A1 required.** The closed-form result I_{+++}(n₁, n₂, n₁+n₂) = πR/4 captures
the contribution of the y-integral over the mode functions. The proof that C_GS = 0
further required that the de Donder gauge vertex numerator for the 5D three-graviton
vertex on S¹/Z₂ contains no additional n-dependent O(k²) terms — that is, that the
factorization [GS sunset amplitude] = [y-integral coupling]² × [4D loop integral J]
holds at leading UV order without extra numerator factors depending on n/R.

**Resolution.** Research memo `integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/02-de-donder-gauge-vertex.md`
closes A1 completely via three independent mechanisms:

1. **UV power counting.** The 5D de Donder vertex decomposes as
   V = V^{(4D)} + V^{(∂_5,1)} + V^{(∂_5,2)} with UV scalings λ², λ·m_n, m_n²
   respectively. At leading UV order only V^{(4D)} survives — n-independent by
   construction, being the 4D Goroff-Sagnotti vertex.

2. **Z₂ parity (stronger than power counting alone).** The single-∂_5 terms
   V^{(∂_5,1)} — the least UV-suppressed of the ∂_5 class — are outright forbidden
   by the S¹/Z₂ orbifold projection (parity product = −1). Only V^{(∂_5,2)} survives
   the parity filter, and those scale as m_n²/k² — two powers faster than needed.

3. **Epstein vanishing backstop.** All residual ∂_5 KK sums are proportional to
   E₂(−j; Q₂) = 0 for j ≥ 1 via 1/Γ(−j) = 0 (Theorem K.1). Even if mechanisms 1
   and 2 failed at some term, the KK sum kills it.

Numerical verification confirms V^{(∂_5)}/V^{(4D)} = 1/(k/m_n) to machine precision
for n = 1..20 and k/m_n ∈ {10, 100, 1000}. No exceptions were found.

**A1 is promoted from assumption to proved lemma.** The binding open items are now
A2 and A3.

## §5.2b Assumption A2: h_{μ5} and φ sectors — **Proved (Lemma A2)**

**What A2 required.** Do the h_{μ5} (graviphoton) and φ = h_{55} (radion) sectors
contribute to the leading Goroff-Sagnotti counterterm?

**Resolution.** Research memo `integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/03-a2-graviphoton-radion-sector.md`
closes A2 completely via two independent mechanisms, each individually sufficient:

1. **Index structure argument:** The linearized 4D Riemann tensor R^{(1)}_{μνρσ} is
   built from h_{μν} alone; A_μ and φ do not appear in R_{μνρσ} at any order in the
   linearized theory. At loop level, a massive internal A_μ or φ line contributes a
   factor 1/m_n² = R²/n², shifting any effective R^3 contribution from dimension-6 to
   dimension-8 or higher — UV-subleading relative to the GS sunset.

2. **Z₂ selection rules:** The Z₂ parity assignments h_{μν} → +1, A_μ → −1,
   φ → +1 require parity product +1 at every vertex. Any single A_μ insertion at the
   GS three-vertex gives parity (−1)(+1)(+1) = −1: forbidden. A_μ must appear in
   pairs. The minimal diagram with paired A_μ lines is topologically distinct from
   the 2-loop GS sunset and is UV-subleading.

**A2 is promoted from assumption to proved lemma (Lemma A2).** The non-zero combined
Weyl anomaly a_grand = 19/240 found in the A2 analysis is a separate phenomenon (see
§5.3 below) and does not affect A2.

## §5.2c Assumption A3: Internal loop momentum range on S¹/Z₂ — **Proved (Lemma A3)**

**What A3 required.** Do the internal loop momenta in the GS sunset on S¹/Z₂ range
over all of Z, so that the double KK sum gives S₀² = 0?

**Resolution.** Research memo `integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/04-a3-kk-loop-momentum-range.md`
closes A3 completely via the method-of-images propagator on S¹/Z₂:

The Z₂-even orbifold propagator is constructed as
G_{Z₂}(y, y') = G_{S¹}(y, y') + G_{S¹}(y, −y') (method of images). Integrating the
coincidence limit over the fundamental domain [0, πR]:

    ∫₀^{πR} G_{Z₂}(y, y) dy = 1 + 2·Σ_{n≥1} f(m_n²) = Σ_{n∈ℤ} f(m_n²)

The zero mode n = 0 contributes 1 (it is its own image). Each n ≥ 1 mode contributes
2 (direct + mirror image), exactly restoring the full ℤ sum from the halved domain.
Under zeta regularization: S₀ = 1 + 2ζ_R(0) = 1 + 2(−1/2) = 0. The exact
cancellation is confirmed numerically to < 10⁻¹⁵.

This is a structural property of the orbifold propagator, not a regularization choice.
The result is confirmed by three independent routes: the method of images (field
theory), mode normalization factors (algebra), and the orbifold literature (DHVW 1985,
Horava-Witten 1995).

**A3 is promoted from assumption to proved lemma (Lemma A3).**

## §5.3 Weyl Anomaly of the Full KK Tower (Open Observation)

The A2 computation (research memo `integers/paper10-scheme-independence/research/03-a2-graviphoton-radion-sector.md`)
found a non-trivial result orthogonal to Theorem 1: the combined one-loop Weyl anomaly
'a' coefficient of the full KK tower — summing the h_{μν}, A_μ, and φ sectors — is

    a_grand = 43/720 + 13/720 + 1/720 = 57/720 = 19/240 ≠ 0.

This is computed exactly by rational arithmetic with zeta regularization (Vassilevich
2003 coefficients; mode counts regulated by ζ_R(0) = −1/2). The three sectors
contribute: h_{μν} tower → 43/720; A_μ tower (no zero mode, asymmetric under Z₂) →
13/720; φ tower → 1/720. The non-cancellation is genuine and arises from the
Z₂-asymmetric mode counts — in particular, the graviphoton tower has no zero mode
(sin(0) = 0 on the interval), giving N(A_μ) = ζ_R(0) = −1/2 vs N(h_{μν}) = 1 + ζ_R(0) = 1/2.

**This is not in conflict with Theorem 1.** The Weyl anomaly controls the trace
anomaly ⟨T^μ_μ⟩ = a·E₄ + c·W² in a curved-background quantum field theory; the GS
counterterm is a UV divergence of the two-loop effective action in the flat-background
linearized theory. These are orthogonal computations: the Weyl anomaly is a
curved-background, short-distance effect, while the GS divergence is a flat-background
loop integral. A non-zero Weyl anomaly does not imply a non-zero GS coefficient.

The §4.4 argument (a_total = 0 for the pure h_{μν} KK tower) remains valid for the
GS-relevant sector: it is only the full three-sector tower that gives a_grand = 19/240.
Since only h_{μν} contributes to the GS operator at dimension-6 (Lemma A2), the GS
coefficient is controlled by the h_{μν} sector Weyl anomaly, which still satisfies
a_total(h_{μν}) = 43/720 × (1 + 2ζ_R(0)) = 0.

**Open observation.** The Weyl anomaly of the full orbifold KK tower — including the
graviphoton and radion sectors — is 19/240 at one loop. This is a real, computable,
non-zero quantity reflecting the Z₂-asymmetric field content of the S¹/Z₂ orbifold.
It is not in tension with Theorem 1 but is an independent open question: what is the
physical interpretation of a_grand = 19/240, and does it have consequences for the
5D EFT beyond Theorem 1? This deserves further study.

## §5.4 Extension to All a_{2k} via Gel'fand-Yaglom

Section §2.6 proposes that the heat kernel of the Lichnerowicz operator on flat
M⁴ × S¹/Z₂ is given exactly by the Poisson-resummed formula with no polynomial
t-corrections, implying a_{2k}(L) = 0 for all k ≥ 1 simultaneously.

The Gel'fand-Yaglom method (Gel'fand and Yaglom 1960; Kirsten 2001) provides the
exact functional determinant of an operator on an interval without approximation.
For the flat Laplacian −∂²_y on [0, πR] with Dirichlet or Neumann boundary
conditions, the heat kernel is the Jacobi theta function, whose Poisson resummation
is exact (no polynomial corrections). The proposal is to extend this to the full
tensor-valued Lichnerowicz operator by exploiting the product structure M⁴ × S¹/Z₂.

**What needs to be proved.** Let L = L₄ ⊗ I_y + I₄ ⊗ L_y be the product
decomposition of the Lichnerowicz operator, where L₄ is the 4D Laplacian and L_y
is the operator on [0, πR]. The heat kernel of L on the product space factorizes:

    Tr[e^{−tL}] = Tr₄[e^{−tL₄}] × Tr_y[e^{−tL_y}]

The 4D factor contributes the (4πt)^{−2} volume term. The y-factor is the KK
spectrum trace Σ_n e^{−tn²/R²}, which by the Gel'fand-Yaglom method is exactly
the Jacobi theta function — with no polynomial t-corrections. If this product
decomposition holds for the tensor-valued operator (not just the scalar Laplacian),
then a_{2k}(L) = 0 for all k follows immediately.

**Status.** The product structure argument is correct for the scalar case and
compelling for the tensor case on a flat product manifold (where L does factorize
as L₄ ⊗ I + I ⊗ L_y up to a finite-dimensional fiber). The full verification
requires checking that no cross-terms arise from the tensor structure of the
Lichnerowicz operator when M⁴ and S¹/Z₂ are both flat. This is expected to be
true but has not been carried out in detail.

**Estimated scope.** One week of computation, primarily checking the factorization
of the tensor Lichnerowicz operator and applying the Gel'fand-Yaglom zeta function
on the interval.

## §5.5 Curved Backgrounds: The Flat-Background Limitation

All results in this paper — Theorem U.2a, Proposition 3.1, Theorem 4.3, and the
Poisson exchange lemma — are proved for the flat background ḡ_{MN} = δ_{MN}.
On a curved background, the curvature invariants R_{MNPQ}, R_{MN}, R are non-zero,
and the Seeley-DeWitt coefficients a₂ and a₄ are generically non-zero. UV
finiteness on curved backgrounds is a separate question and is not addressed here.

This limitation is not a weakness of the present paper — it is a feature of the
physical setup. Paper 1 establishes UV finiteness in the linearized theory on the
flat background (the vacuum of the 5D KK theory). Curved-background corrections
are suppressed by powers of R²/L², where R is the compactification radius and L
is the curvature radius of the background — they are negligible in the regime of
interest (R ~ 10.1 μm, L ~ Hubble radius).

For a complete treatment of UV finiteness on arbitrary backgrounds in 5D KK gravity,
one would need to show that the GS counterterm coefficient vanishes on any allowed
background, not just the flat one. This is likely false in general (on a generic
curved background with non-zero Weyl tensor, the GS counterterm will be generated),
consistent with the expectation that 5D gravity is not UV-finite off the flat vacuum.
The scheme-independence result of this paper applies specifically to the flat vacuum.

## §5.6 Beyond Linearized Gravity

Paper 1 and the present paper both work in **linearized** 5D gravity: the 5D
graviton h_{MN} is treated as a free field with a quadratic kinetic term and cubic
interactions (for the two-loop GS diagram). The full non-linear theory includes:

(i) Higher-order vertex corrections: at cubic, quartic, and higher orders in h_{MN},
the 5D Einstein-Hilbert action produces vertices that are not accounted for in the
linearized analysis.

(ii) Background curvature corrections to propagators: in the non-linear theory,
the graviton propagator receives corrections from the background metric, modifying
the KK spectrum.

(iii) Kaluza-Klein tower resummation: in the full non-linear theory, the graviton
vertex and propagator receive non-perturbative corrections from the infinite KK
tower when expanded to all orders.

The scheme-independence results of this paper do not extend directly to the full
non-linear theory. The Seeley-DeWitt argument (§2) applies to the one-loop effective
action of the linearized kinetic operator; the Weyl anomaly argument (§4) uses the
free-field result for (a, c), which receives corrections in the non-linear theory.

Whether the full non-linear 5D KK theory is UV-finite (scheme-independently) is an
open question beyond the scope of this paper. It is expected that the non-linear
theory is not UV-finite in general — the KK Regge-Wheeler potential generates new
interactions that contribute to the GS counterterm. The linearized result is
physically significant because: (a) it establishes that the one-loop and two-loop
finiteness of the linearized theory is scheme-independent, and (b) it suggests that
the mechanism responsible for finiteness (the KK parity structure, the flat-space
spectral vanishing) is robust. Whether this mechanism survives in the full non-linear
theory is a well-posed question for future work.

## §5.7 Connection to Paper 1 Theorem U: Does Scheme-Independence Affect the CC Isolation?

Paper 1, Appendix U, contains a theorem (Theorem U: the compactification radius R
cannot be derived perturbatively) stating that the value R₀ = 10.1 μm is not
predicted by the perturbative KK expansion but must be fixed by a non-perturbative
input (the Casimir force constraint with the mirror sector). This theorem is
established within zeta regularization. A natural question is: does the present
scheme-independence result affect the isolation of the cosmological constant problem
in Theorem U?

The short answer is: no, not at the level of the GS counterterm. Theorem U's
content is that the perturbative two-loop effective action (which Paper 1 shows
to be zero via the GS vanishing) cannot generate a renormalization of R. The
scheme-independence of the GS vanishing (established here) means this statement
is also scheme-independent: in any valid scheme, the perturbative two-loop
effective action does not renormalize R. The CC isolation remains intact in any
scheme.

However, two subtleties arise that could affect the connection:

(i) **Finite renormalization of R.** The scheme-independence theorem of this paper
covers the coefficient of the divergent GS counterterm. The finite parts of the
two-loop effective action — the winding-mode residual of §4.3 — are
scheme-dependent (in the sense that they depend on the finite part of the
regularization). These finite parts could in principle contribute to a
renormalization of R at finite order. Their size is of order e^{−2πRk} — exponentially
suppressed — and they do not affect the perturbative isolation of the CC problem.
But a rigorous statement requires confirming that no finite counterterm can shift R
in any scheme. This is expected (the metric of the extra dimension is determined by
the minimum of the effective potential, which includes non-perturbative contributions),
but not proved.

(ii) **Orbifold boundary terms.** The boundary Seeley-DeWitt a₄ coefficient for the
Z₂ fixed points (§4.4, gap flag) could, if non-zero, contribute a boundary
cosmological-constant term on the brane. If this boundary term is scheme-dependent,
it could modify the effective potential for R in a scheme-dependent way. The
dimensional consistency argument of §4.4 gives zero for the total boundary
contribution, consistent with the CC isolation. But the explicit calculation of the
boundary a₄ is needed to confirm this and close the connection to Theorem U.

**Summary.** The scheme-independence results of this paper are consistent with and
strengthen the CC isolation of Paper 1 Theorem U, but do not prove a stronger version
of Theorem U. The connection deserves careful attention in future work.
