# 7. BBN and Mirror BBN

## 7.1 Standard BBN on the Visible Brane

At `T ~ 1 MeV` (`t ~ 1 s`), the dilaton is frozen at its
post-inflationary value R₀ by Hubble friction. The e-circle is at its
initial value set by the conditions of compactification.
Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis proceeds on the visible brane
with:

- `η_B = 6 × 10⁻¹⁰` (from §5 or Paper 2)
- `N_eff = 3.04 + ΔN_eff` where `ΔN_eff ≈ 0.3` from the mirror sector
- Standard nuclear reaction rates

The framework's BBN is identical to ΛCDM except for the additional
dark radiation from the mirror sector, which increases `N_eff` and
slightly accelerates the expansion during BBN — shifting the
helium fraction `Y_p` upward by `~0.003`.

## 7.2 Mirror BBN on the Hidden Brane

At the same cosmic time, the hidden brane is at
`T' = ξT ~ 0.49 MeV` — just below the electron mass
`m_e = 0.511 MeV`. Mirror BBN proceeds with:

- The same nuclear physics (Z₂ symmetry)
- A lower temperature: `T'_{BBN} = ξ × 1 MeV ≈ 0.49 MeV`

The lower temperature means mirror BBN occurs LATER in the
expansion history (at lower density), producing:
- A higher mirror helium fraction `Y'_p > Y_p` (more neutrons
  survive because the weak freeze-out is earlier in mirror time)
- Different light element abundances (mirror D/H, mirror Li)

## 7.3 Mirror e± Annihilation

At `T' ≈ 0.43 MeV` (when `T_vis ≈ 0.88 MeV`), the mirror
electron-positron pairs annihilate:

    e'⁺ e'⁻ → γ' γ'

This heats the mirror photon bath relative to the mirror neutrinos,
producing the same (11/4)^{1/3} factor as in the visible sector
— but at a different cosmic time.

The mirror sector contribution to `N_eff` after mirror e± annihilation:

    ΔN_eff^{mirror} = 6.14 × ξ⁴ × f(ξ)

where `f(ξ)` accounts for the Boltzmann suppression of the mirror
e± at BBN (Paper 2, abstract):

    f(ξ) = 0.49 × (for ξ = 0.47, from the time-varying mirror e± contribution)

The total: `N_eff ≈ 3.04 + 0.30 = 3.34` at recombination (Paper 2).

## 7.4 The N_eff Prediction

The framework predicts `N_eff = 3.31–3.39` depending on the
scenario (Paper 2). This is:
- Consistent with the SM prediction (3.044) within 2σ of current data
- In 3–4σ tension with ACT DR6 (2.86 ± 0.13), though ACT is itself
  1.9σ below the SM
- **Definitively testable by CMB-S4** (`σ(N_eff) ≈ 0.03`) at 9σ

Mirror BBN proceeds at T_mirror ≈ ξ × T_visible ≈ 0.43–0.47 MeV
(for ξ = 0.49), which is the epoch at which N_eff^{BBN} ≈ 3.55–3.65
(Paper 2). The mirror helium yield Y_p^{mirror} is set by this
BBN-epoch value. The lower value N_eff^{recomb} = 3.31–3.39 quoted
in Paper 2's CMB predictions reflects mirror e± annihilation occurring
after mirror BBN, which reheats the mirror photon sector relative to
neutrinos — the same process that reduces N_eff between BBN and
recombination in the visible sector. The distinction between
N_eff^{BBN} and N_eff^{recomb} is therefore physically meaningful
and must be tracked separately when comparing this framework against
light-element abundance measurements versus CMB angular power spectrum
constraints.
