# 13. Conclusion

The universe's thermal history — from `10⁻³⁶` seconds to `10¹⁰`
years — is the trajectory of one field through one potential.

The dilaton `φ`, the modulus of the e-circle radius, has the potential
`V(R) = +c/R⁴` — the Casimir energy, exact to all perturbative orders
(no Goldberger-Wise or higher-loop corrections; Theorem K.1, Paper 1
Appendix K; §2.1). The Casimir energy is positive (fermionic bulk field
content dominates; §2.1a). The dilaton is not the inflaton (ε = 32/3 ≫ 1;
§3.1, Appendix A). It freezes at its post-inflationary value by Hubble
friction — the dimensionless fractional drift per Hubble time is
ΔR/R₀ ~ H₀R₀ ~ 3 × 10⁻³⁰ (Appendix A, Proposition A.1) — giving
`w = −1` to precision (ΔR/R₀)² ~ 10⁻⁵⁹: a true cosmological constant,
not quintessence.

**Four results are unique to this paper:**

1. **The reheating temperature** `T_reh ≈ 5 × 10⁹ GeV` — computed
   from the gravitational decay width of the G₄ flux axion inflaton
   (§4). Both the dilaton and the G₄ axion give the same T_reh at the
   parametric level because both couple gravitationally with the same
   functional form. Below M_N, this forces a non-thermal leptogenesis
   pathway.

2. **Non-thermal leptogenesis** — the inflaton decays to bulk neutrino
   pairs through off-shell parametric resonance (q ~ 58; §5.2), which
   then produce the baryon asymmetry `η_B ≈ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰` via the Z₃
   CP phases and resonant enhancement (factor ~80; §5.5), with strong
   washout K = 50 (efficiency η_L = 0.26; §5.6). This resolves the
   `T_reh < M_N` tension in Paper 2.

3. **The origin of ξ ~ 0.5 ± 0.2** — the brane temperature asymmetry
   is set at reheating by the inflaton's differential coupling to the
   two branes (warp factor suppression) plus gravitational
   thermalization plus entropy from bulk neutrino decays (§6). The
   precise value ξ = 0.49 (Paper 2) emerges from the full thermal
   history; this paper predicts the mechanism and the O(40%) range.
   The dark matter abundance `Ω_DM/Ω_b = 1/ξ² ≈ 5` is determined at
   the birth of the thermal universe.

4. **The frozen dilaton and w = −1** — the perturbative Casimir
   potential V = +c/R⁴ is exact to all orders (Epstein zeta zeros,
   §2.1). The dimensionless fractional drift is ΔR/R₀ ~ 3 × 10⁻³⁰
   per Hubble time — the ratio of the Hubble scale to the KK scale.
   The dilaton is frozen. The "cosmological constant" IS a constant
   — the Casimir energy at the frozen radius. The frozen point is
   perturbatively stable with Hubble-damped oscillations (§A.6). If
   DESI measures `w ≠ −1` at the percent level, non-perturbative
   modifications to the dilaton potential are required.

The framework provides a complete, self-consistent account of
cosmic evolution from the initial compactification to the distant
future. Every epoch is governed by the same geometry. Every
transition is driven by the same field or its geometric partners.
Every prediction is quantitative and falsifiable.

The universe began as a quantum fluctuation of the e-circle. Within
the perturbative framework, it approaches quasi-stable de Sitter
indefinitely — the frozen dilaton is stable against small perturbations
with Hubble damping (§11.4, Appendix A §A.6). Non-perturbative effects
at exponentially long timescales remain an open question.

One compact dimension. One field. One history.
