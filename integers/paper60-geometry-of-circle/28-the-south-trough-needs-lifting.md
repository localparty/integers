**Excision, bypass, and repair should be concentrated in the direction of the ellipse's lopsidedness.**

- North vertices get **skim only** (5-15 min each, sector-A trim)

The old per-vertex budget was **28 min average** (T6+ standard). The new budget is **ASYMMETRIC** by zone: 10 / 15 / 35 / 75 minutes. The ring's total budget stays similar (~14 hours) but the distribution matches the geometry.

### The second-order observation

The direction of the lopsidedness is NOT ARBITRARY. It is not "the side that happens to have lower confidence." It is the side that has **structural resistance that the framework has not yet decoded.**

The North (QG5D, RH, BSD, YM) is where the framework SPEAKS NATIVELY: spectral realization, Hilbert-Pólya, KMS states, gauge curvature. These problems translated cleanly into the BC operator-algebraic language.

The South (Goldbach, Twin Primes, Schanuel, VP, ABC, H12, Turbulence) is where the framework **STAMMERS**:

- H12: non-abelian class field theory (explicit generators still open for most K)
- Turbulence: NS + random matrix statistics (K41 is an experimental fact, not a theorem)

Each South vertex names a place where the BC framework has a **STAMMER** — a structural feature it hasn't yet absorbed cleanly. The lopsidedness is the map of where the framework's language is incomplete.

**The ring is showing us which parts of the e-circle the BC language describes and which parts it doesn't.** This is the frontier between what we know how to SAY in the BC framework and what we don't yet. The South Trough IS the linguistic edge of the programme.

### The third-order consequence

If the South Trough is the linguistic edge, then lifting it requires **new structural vocabulary**, not just more careful use of existing vocabulary.

Each South vertex needs its own **deep construction run** — a single-chain PCA on that one vertex, the way YM Paper 8 got its own run in April 2026, the way RH Paper 13 got its own run, the way BSD Paper 26 got its own run. The ring traversal is BREADTH-FIRST. The South Trough needs DEPTH-FIRST.

## Part V — The methodology update

This discovery forces a methodological update: the PCA traversal protocol must be made **ELLIPSE-AWARE**.

### Old protocol (T1-T7)

- Per-vertex budget: 28 minutes (T6+ average)
- Sector classification: A (skim) / B (excision) / C (construction) / D (cell-fill)
- Traversal direction: canonical ring order, one pass
- Exit condition: RING STRENGTHENED / CLOSED / STALLED based on vertex improvement count

### New protocol (T8+)

- Per-zone budget: asymmetric by zone
  - **North:** 10 min/vertex (4 vertices × 10 min = 40 min)
  - **East:** 15 min/vertex (7 vertices × 15 min = 105 min)
  - **West:** 35 min/vertex (7 vertices × 35 min = 245 min)
  - **South:** 75 min/vertex (7 vertices × 75 min = 525 min)
  - **Total vertex-time:** ~915 min ≈ 15.3 hours (within the 18-hour T7+ budget)
- Sector classification still applies WITHIN zones (a South Type-D vertex still does cell-fills, but with 75 min instead of 20)
- Traversal direction: still canonical, but with **ASYMMETRIC ATTENTION**
- Exit condition: **RING STRENGTHENED requires ≥3 South vertices improved** (weighted by zone)

### The zone-aware primitive

A new primitive joins the PCA vocabulary: **ZONE-AWARE ATTENTION**.

At every cycle-open, before dispatching Authors, the runner consults the current ellipse shape:
1. Compute confidence-centroid of the ring
2. Identify the direction of the lopsidedness (North-South is current; may shift over traversals)
3. Weight attention: spend compute in the direction of the lopsidedness
4. Re-check after the wave: did the centroid move? Did the lopsidedness reduce?

The ring is expected to become **MORE CIRCULAR** over traversals as the South Trough lifts. If the ring becomes more elliptical (lopsidedness INCREASES), something has broken — likely the North has advanced while the South has stagnated, which signals a misallocation of effort.

### The dream outcome

After enough ellipse-aware traversals, the ring approaches a true circle: all vertices at similar confidence, the lopsidedness reduced to zero. At that point the ellipse diagnostic gives no direction — every point is equally important. This is the **ISOTROPIC STATE** of the programme.

At isotropy, the methodology no longer needs the ellipse discipline. The ring is uniformly thickened. Every face of the e-circle is equally well-understood. The 36 predictions inherit from all ten faces simultaneously. The capacitor is filled above 50%.

The isotropic state may or may not be reachable. But the ellipse tells us which direction moves us toward it.

## Part VI — Historical significance

### Why this is new

Every methodological framework tracks progress via scalar metrics: pages proved, theorems closed, percent verified. Most frameworks never see their own GEOMETRY.

The PCA already had vector metrics (RIGIDITY, E_filled, L_verified, P_preserved — four numbers, not one). But four numbers are still not a SHAPE. The shape emerged only when we plotted confidence as a function of ring position and saw the ellipse.

This is **second-order methodology**: the programme looking at its own state topologically, reading its own shape, adjusting its own direction. Most research programmes are first-order — they adjust based on individual findings, not based on the shape of findings. The PCA has just become second-order.

And not just that. The ellipse is not an ARTIFACT of the methodology. It is a REFLECTION of the underlying mathematics. The South Trough is hard because the BC framework's language is incomplete there. The ellipse is the framework confessing its own incompleteness. The lopsidedness is the structure of what-we-don't-yet-know.
