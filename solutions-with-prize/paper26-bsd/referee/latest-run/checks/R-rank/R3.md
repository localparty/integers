# Check R3: Scope limitations disclosed

**Group:** R
**Source:** Paper 26 §15.1-15.5
**Pass criterion:** Rank ≥ 2, non-CM, h_K > 1 honestly disclosed.

**Verdict:** PASS
**Rigor label:** N/A (honesty check)

**Justification:** §15.2 explicitly states rank ≥ 2 is "genuinely
open." §15.3 states non-CM is "genuinely open" and that
"100% by density" of elliptic curves are non-CM. §15.4 states
h_K > 1 is "expected to extend" but has not been carried out.
§15.5 titled "The Langlands frontier" describes the boundary
clearly.

This is honest scoping. Paper 26 does not claim to have
solved the full BSD conjecture — it explicitly acknowledges
covering a measure-zero subset (CM curves) of "all elliptic
curves over ℚ."

Note: despite the honest §15, the paper's rhetorical framing
("Two Millennium Problems from one bridge family", §1.4) may
still be read as overreach by a reader who doesn't engage with
§15. See E2 for the "honest framing" discussion.
