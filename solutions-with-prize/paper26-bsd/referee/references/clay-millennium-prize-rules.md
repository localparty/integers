# Clay Mathematics Institute — Millennium Prize Description and Rules

**Source:** https://www.claymath.org/millennium-problems/rules/
**PDF:** https://www.claymath.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/millennium_prize_rules_0.pdf
**Date:** 26 September, 2018 (revised rules, superseding all earlier statements)
**Saved:** 2026-04-10

---

## 1. Overview

CMI has named seven (7) Millennium Prize Problems. Official descriptions are at
https://www.claymath.org/millennium-problems.

## 2. Prize Fund

$7 million total, $1 million per Problem.

## 3. General Provisions

- **3(a)** CMI alone has sole authority to award Prizes and to modify/interpret Rules.
- **3(b)** CMI may consult the Scientific Advisory Board (SAB) and others, but is not obligated to follow advice.
- **3(c)** All CMI decisions on Rules and Prizes are **final**.
- **3(d)** All deliberations are **private and confidential** — no records may be made public without BOD+SAB approval unless 50 years have elapsed. Submitters are NOT entitled to explanations of CMI decisions.

## 4. Criteria For Prizes: General

CMI may award a Prize **if and only if**:

- **(a)** Published by a **Qualifying Outlet** (§6);
- **(b)** At least **2 years** since publication;
- **(c)** **General acceptance** in the global mathematics community (CMI sole discretion);
- **(d)** Satisfactorily answers questions raised by the Problem's **official description** (CMI sole discretion).

## 5. Proposed Solutions

- **(a)** Only a **complete** mathematical solution is eligible. Completeness determined by CMI.
- **(b)** P vs NP and Navier-Stokes: resolution in **either direction** evaluated normally.
- **(c)** Other Problems: counterexamples evaluated as Proposed Solutions.
  - **(i)** If counterexample effectively resolves the Problem → full Prize.
  - **(ii)** If counterexample only eliminates a special case → small prize from other CMI funds.
- **(d)** Paper must **address the specific mathematical questions** in the official description — even closely related questions don't count.
- **(e)** CMI does **not accept direct submissions** and owes no explanation.

## 6. Publication — Qualifying Outlet

- **(a)** Either:
  - **(i)** A refereed mathematics publication of worldwide repute meeting §6(e) conditions; or
  - **(ii)** A publication meeting relaxed conditions approved by BOD on SAB recommendation (§6(f)).
- **(b)** CMI will not recommend journals.
- **(c)** CMI does not maintain a list of qualifying outlets.
- **(d)** Supplementary material from the author **will not be considered**.
- **(e)** A publication **lacking any** of these is disqualified:
  - **(i)** Named editorial board available for contact;
  - **(ii)** Editor with professional knowledge to identify appropriate referees;
  - **(iii)** Published refereeing process ensuring expert review;
  - **(iv)** Inclusion in **MathSciNet** list.
- **(f)** CMI may relax §6(e) conditions if experts advise the solution is likely correct.

## 7. Evaluation of a Proposed Solution

Two sequential stages:

### Stage 1: General Acceptance

- CMI monitors for Proposed Solutions (notes that plausible solutions are "effectively unmissable").
- Must survive **rigorous examination** by the global mathematics community for **minimum 2 years**.
- CMI will not accept invitations to recognize solution status.
- Factors CMI may consider:
  - **(a)** Journal articles, books, publications by independent researchers;
  - **(b)** International conferences;
  - **(c)** Awards;
  - **(d)** Any other factors indicative of consensus.

### Stage 2: CMI Examination

- If general acceptance + 2 years → CMI decides if detailed consideration is merited.
- If not merited → no Prize, no further action.
- If merited → CMI convenes **special advisory committee** (≥3 members, ≥2 experts in the Problem area).
- Committee reports → CMI determines whether Prize should be awarded.

## 8. Award of a Prize

- **(a)** CMI may determine:
  - **(i)** No Prize;
  - **(ii)** Prize to one person;
  - **(iii)** Prize divided among multiple solvers;
  - **(iv)** Small non-Prize award for counterexample resulting in reformulation.
- **(b)** CMI will consider whether solution depends on **prior published insights** and may recognize/include prior authors.
- **(c)** If CMI cannot decide → no Prize. Reconsideration requires an additional 2-year wait after new information.
- **(d)** Eligible: anyone not a disqualified person (IRC §4946) or current SAB/BOD member.
- **(e)** Prize delivered within 90 days of notification.

## Legal

Governed by Delaware law. Jurisdiction: Superior Court of New Hampshire (Hillsborough County) / US District Court for District of New Hampshire.

## Key Takeaways for a BSD Submission

1. **No direct submission** — must be published in a qualifying journal first.
2. **2-year waiting period** after publication before CMI will consider.
3. **General community acceptance** required — CMI won't be the first to validate.
4. Must answer the **specific questions** in Andrew Wiles' official problem description.
5. **Supplementary material** not considered — the published paper must be self-contained.
6. **MathSciNet inclusion** of the journal is a minimum requirement.
7. **Counterexample** to BSD would be evaluated but may only yield a partial prize if the conjecture survives in reformulated form.
