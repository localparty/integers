## Part B, Point 2: The Fredenhagen-Marcu Criterion

**Weight:** LIGHT
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

(a) The Fredenhagen-Marcu theorem (CMP 104, 1986) is applied to the KK-enhanced lattice gauge theory. The conditions are: the Wilson loop satisfies the area law (<W_{C_{T,R}}> ~ e^{-sigma TR} with sigma > 0), and the free-theory Wilson loop satisfies the perimeter law (<W^free> ~ e^{-alpha P}). The FM order parameter rho_FM = lim_{T -> infinity} <W_{C_{T,R}}> / <W^free_{C_{T,R}}> vanishes when sigma > 0 (area decay dominates perimeter decay). These conditions are verified: the area law is established in Theorem 4(d) from the cluster expansion, and the perimeter law for the free theory is standard.

(b) The Fredenhagen-Marcu theorem provides both confinement AND mass gap. The theorem states: rho_FM = 0 implies (1) sigma > 0, (2) Delta > 0, and (3) all charged states are confined. The proof chain in the preprint is: sigma > 0 (from cluster expansion) -> rho_FM = 0 -> Delta > 0 (from FM). However, the quantitative estimate Delta >= c sqrt(sigma) requires an additional physical argument from flux tube energy minimization (Appendix F, Theorem F.1), using the static quark-antiquark potential V(R) = sigma R and the minimum-energy closed flux tube. The FM theorem gives Delta > 0 directly without requiring the flux tube argument; the flux tube argument provides the sharper quantitative estimate. Both directions are correctly stated.

**Impact on the claimed result:** None. The FM criterion is correctly applied, and the mass gap follows from the area law.
