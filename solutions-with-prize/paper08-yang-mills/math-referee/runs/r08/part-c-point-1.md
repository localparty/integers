## Part C, Point 1: Balaban UV Stability — Precise Scope

**Weight:** HEAVY
**Verdict:** CLOSABLE GAP

**Finding:**

(a) The effective action decomposition $S_k = (1/g_k^2) S_{YM} + \sum_n c_n^{(k)} O_n + E_k$ is NOT stated as a theorem in exactly this form by Balaban. Balaban works with polymer activities $K_k(X,V)$, yielding $S_k[V] = (1/g_k^2) S_{YM}[V] + \sum_X K_k(X,V)$. The translation from polymer activities to the local operator decomposition with coefficient bounds $|c_n^{(k)}| \leq C_n g_k^{d_n-4}$ requires an "operator extraction lemma" — expanding the polymer activities in a basis of local operators and bounding the coefficients by the polymer decay $e^{-\kappa|X|}$. This operator extraction is detailed in Appendix I (Section I.1) and is a standard procedure in constructive field theory (Brydges 1986), but it is NOT done by Balaban himself nor by Dimock. It is new to this paper. The extraction is straightforward given the polymer expansion bounds, but it should be explicitly acknowledged as a new (if standard) step.

(b) Balaban's published program is primarily for SU(2). The preprint claims results for general SU(N). The structural features of the construction — block-spin averaging, polymer expansion, axial gauge fixing — are group-independent. However, specific bounds (propagator estimates, kernel analyticity) use properties of the Lie algebra. For SU(N): the covariant Laplacian $D^2[V]$ depends polynomially on $V_\ell$ through the adjoint representation, with Lipschitz constants proportional to dim(adj) = N^2-1. The propagator bounds generalize with constants scaling polynomially in N (the adjoint Casimir is proportional to N). Balaban's inductive hypotheses (CMP 109, Section 3) are stated in terms of group-independent quantities ($\kappa$, $m_W$, $C_D$), but the numerical values of these constants depend on N. For each fixed N, the extension is straightforward; the paper should state this more explicitly. **This is a closable gap requiring a paragraph of N-dependence tracking.**

(c) The remainder bound $\|E_k\| \leq C g_k^4$ per unit volume follows from the polymer expansion convergence. The norm is the $L^\infty$ norm on gauge-invariant functionals (supremum over configurations in the small-field region), which is the appropriate norm for the effective action. Balaban's bound is $|K_k(X,V)| \leq e^{-\kappa|X|}$ per polymer; summing over all polymers containing a given site gives the per-volume bound. The constant $C$ depends on $\kappa$ (the polymer decay rate), $d$ (dimension), and the gauge group through combinatorial factors, all of which are N-dependent but finite for each fixed N.

(d) The running coupling $g_{k+1}^2 = g_k^2 - b_0 g_k^4 \ln 2 + O(g_k^6)$ with $b_0 = 11N/(48\pi^2)$ is established by Balaban perturbatively with controlled remainder. The accumulated higher-order corrections are controlled because the sum $\sum g_k^4 \sim \sum 1/(b_0 k \ln 2)^2 < \infty$ (Basel series). The infinite product $\prod(1 + O(g_j^4))$ converges absolutely by comparison with $\exp(\sum g_j^4) < \infty$. The non-perturbative $\beta$-function is controlled within the small-field domain; outside (large-field configurations), the contributions are exponentially suppressed. This is standard asymptotic freedom machinery and is correctly applied.

(e) The boundary between "what Balaban proved" and "what this paper proves" is precisely drawn in Section 5.1.5 ("What Balaban Does NOT Prove") and Section 5.1.6 ("What We Need Beyond Balaban"). Balaban proved UV stability but not: the mass gap, confinement, the continuum limit as a QFT, or the spectral response of the transfer matrix to the perturbation $E_k$. The paper proves: the mass gap at the starting scale (Sections 2-4 via KK cluster expansion), the IR equivalence (Theorem 5), and the form factor bound controlling how $E_k$ affects the spectral gap (the stability of deviation order argument, Section 5.6). No unstated assumptions about Balaban's construction are identified.

**Impact on the claimed result:** The gap in (a) is a presentation issue, not a mathematical gap: the operator extraction from polymer activities is standard constructive QFT technique. The gap in (b) is closable with explicit N-dependence tracking (1 paragraph per estimate). Neither affects the logical soundness of the proof for any fixed N >= 2.
