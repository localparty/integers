## Part D, Point 3: The Spectral Lemma

**Weight:** MEDIUM
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

(a) The deviation extraction (Step 1) correctly distinguishes between $n_j = m$ (diagonal: factor = 0), $n_j = 0$ or $n_j = m \neq 0$ (vacuum intermediate: factor $\leq C\hat{\Delta}$), and $n_j \geq 2$ (higher: factor <= 1). The proof claims that at least $p$ of the extracted factors contribute $O(\hat{\Delta})$ -- this is guaranteed by the definition of dev >= p. Specifically, the factorization in Definition D.2 ensures that for each channel $(\alpha, \mathbf{n})$, the weight contains at least $p$ factors of $(e^{E_m - E_{n_j}} - 1)$. Each such factor is bounded by $C\hat{\Delta}$ via the estimate (S1.2). The factors with $n_j \geq 2$ contribute at most 1 (not $C\hat{\Delta}$), but by the definition of dev, there must be at least $p$ factors total -- the sum of those contributing $O(\hat{\Delta})$ and those contributing $O(1)$ counts at least $p$. Since the O(1) factors are at most 1, extracting $p$ factors gives at most $(C\hat{\Delta})^q \cdot 1^{p-q}$ where $q$ is the number contributing $O(\hat{\Delta})$. But this gives $(C\hat{\Delta})^q$, which could be less than $(C\hat{\Delta})^p$ if $q < p$. HOWEVER, the definition guarantees that the factored form has the deviation factors explicitly present as multiplicative factors in the weight -- so extracting them gives exactly $(C\hat{\Delta})^p$ contribution from the $p$ factors, regardless of how many are O(Delta) vs O(1). In fact the bound S1.2 gives $|e^{E_m - E_{n_j}} - 1| \leq C_0 \hat{\Delta}$ for ALL cases (diagonal gives 0 <= C_0 Delta, vacuum gives Delta(1+O(Delta)) <= C_0 Delta, higher gives <= 1 <= C_0 Delta when C_0 >= 1/Delta). So each extracted factor is bounded by $C_0\hat{\Delta}$. The proof is correct.

(b) The bound on $\zeta = \sum_{n \geq 1} e^{-(E_n - E_1)}$ requires $E_2 - E_1 > 0$. The preprint provides a rigorous justification: in Balaban's small-field domain, the Hamiltonian has the form $H_k = H_k^{(0)} + V_k$ with free spectrum $\{0, \hat{\Delta}_k, 2\hat{\Delta}_k, ...\}$ and interaction $\|V_k\| \leq C g_k^2$. By Kato-Rellich, the two-particle spectrum is perturbed from $2\hat{\Delta}_k$ with binding energy $\epsilon_B \leq C_B g_k^4 \hat{\Delta}_k$. This gives $E_2 - E_1 \geq \hat{\Delta}_k(1 - C_B g_k^4) \geq \hat{\Delta}_k/2$ for $g_k^2 \leq 1/(2C_B)$, satisfied for all $k \geq k_0$ on the AF trajectory. The finitely many initial steps contribute a bounded constant. The perturbative estimate IS valid non-perturbatively in Balaban's small-field domain because the interaction is bounded by $C g_k^2$ (not merely computed to some perturbative order) -- it is a rigorous bound from the polymer expansion, not a loop expansion. No deeply bound state can form because the interaction is weak relative to the kinetic energy scale.

(c) The Combes-Thomas estimate controls the volume independence of $\zeta$. The support radius $R_0$ of the operator $\hat{A}^{(s)}$ is bounded uniformly in $k$ because: (i) at step $k$, the effective action $E_k(x)$ is supported in a ball of radius $R_0 = O(1)$ block lattice spacings around $x$; (ii) the block-spin transformation maps local operators to local operators with radius growing by at most the blocking factor $L = 2$; (iii) after $k$ steps, the total support in original lattice units is $R_0 \cdot 2^k$, but in the current (step-$k$) lattice units it remains $R_0 = O(1)$ because the lattice spacing has also coarsened by $2^k$. So $R_0$ is $O(1)$ in lattice units at each scale, bounded uniformly in $k$. The Combes-Thomas bound does not degrade.

**Impact on the claimed result:** None. The spectral lemma is correctly proved with all constants bounded uniformly in $k$ and the lattice volume.
