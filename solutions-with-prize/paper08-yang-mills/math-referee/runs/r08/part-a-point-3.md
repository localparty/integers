## Part A, Point 3: Bogomolny Bound on the Lattice

**Weight:** LIGHT
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

(a) The lattice topological charge is defined via Lüscher's geometrical construction (Lüscher, CMP 85, 1982). For configurations in the small-field region Ω_s = {|F_{μν}| < p(g_k)}, the lattice topological charge Q_L is an integer that coincides with the continuum c_2 up to corrections of O(a^2 ||F||^2). Rough configurations in the large-field region have ill-defined topological charge but are exponentially suppressed by O(e^{-c/g_k^{2ε}}) (Balaban, CMP 119). The preprint addresses "dislocations" or rough lattice configurations: these are precisely the large-field configurations whose contribution is controlled by Balaban's large-field estimates. The Corollary following Theorem 4 quantifies the topological sector contributions: non-trivial sectors (c_2 ≠ 0) give Z_k/Z_0 ≤ C_k e^{-8π^2|k|β/N}, and the correction to the string tension is bounded by 1 - C e^{-4π^2β/N}, strictly positive for all β > 0.

(b) The restriction to c_2 = 0 is preserved under the dynamics by the Bogomolny energy barrier: tunneling between topological sectors requires energy E ≥ (8π^2/g^2)|c_2|, with transition amplitudes suppressed by O(e^{-8π^2/g^2}) at weak coupling. The CP^{N-1} connections are restricted to c_2 = 0 at each lattice site, and the lattice dynamics cannot change c_2 continuously. The non-zero topological sectors contribute corrections that are exponentially suppressed and explicitly bounded in the Corollary to Theorem 4. The combined theory (all sectors) retains σ > 0 and Δ > 0.

**Impact on the claimed result:** None. The Bogomolny bound is correctly implemented on the lattice using Lüscher's construction, and the topological sector restriction is dynamically preserved with explicitly bounded corrections.
