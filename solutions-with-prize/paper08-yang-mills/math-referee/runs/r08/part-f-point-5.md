## Part F, Point 5: OS Reconstruction to Wightman Theory

**Weight:** HEAVY
**Verdict:** CLOSABLE GAP

**Finding:**

(a) The preprint uses the corrected 1975 OS reconstruction theorem (with the OS0' linear growth condition). OS0' is explicitly verified: $|S_n(f)| \leq n! C_0^n \|f\|_{L^1} \leq n! C_0^n C_{4n} p_{4n+1}(f)$. This satisfies the required bound with Schwartz seminorm $p_{4n+1}$. SOUND.

(b) The Hilbert space issue for gauge theories is bypassed by constructing Schwinger functions from gauge-invariant observables (Wilson loops, plaquette traces) rather than from fundamental gauge fields $A_\mu^a(x)$. The GNS construction applied to these gauge-invariant Schwinger functions produces a positive-definite Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$ consisting of color-singlet states (glueballs, flux tubes). There are no negative-norm states because gauge-invariant observables have positive-definite two-point functions (by construction from the positive-definite lattice measure). The "field operators" in the Wightman theory are the continuum limits of gauge-invariant composite operators. The preprint states this explicitly (Section 5.7(f), Remark on Field operators). SOUND — this is the correct way to handle gauge theories in the Wightman framework.

(c) Non-triviality is established by three independent signatures: (i) $\sigma > 0$ (a free theory has $\sigma = 0$); (ii) non-vanishing connected 4-point functions (a free/Gaussian theory has all connected $n$-point functions with $n \geq 3$ vanishing); (iii) asymptotic freedom ($b_0 > 0$; a free theory has $b_0 = 0$). These are stated in Section 5.7(f), Remark on Non-triviality. **However, the argument could be more rigorous.** The string tension $\sigma > 0$ is proved for the lattice theory; its persistence in the continuum limit requires the area law to survive $a \to 0$. The preprint establishes this through the mass gap: $\Delta_\infty > 0$ implies confinement in the continuum theory (by Fredenhagen-Marcu), which implies $\sigma_\infty > 0$. The connected 4-point function argument is less rigorous — it asserts that the polymer expansion generates nonzero connected $n$-point functions without proving they survive the continuum limit. **This is a closable gap**: the non-triviality of the continuum theory follows most cleanly from $\Delta_\infty > 0$ combined with the non-vanishing two-point function of the plaquette operator (which is guaranteed by the mass gap and normalization). Closable with 1 paragraph.

(d) The Yang-Mills equations of motion are addressed through Ward identities. The lattice theory satisfies exact gauge Ward identities at each spacing $a > 0$ (by Haar measure invariance). The Schwinger-Dyson equations converge to continuum Ward-Takahashi identities as $a \to 0$ (Section 5.7(f), Remark on Ward identities), confirming the limiting theory is a gauge theory with gauge group SU(N). This does not prove the full YM equations of motion in the operator sense, but it establishes the gauge content of the theory. For the Clay prize, the theory is constructed from the YM lattice action and its gauge structure is verified — this is the standard notion of "Yang-Mills theory" in the Jaffe-Witten context.

**Impact on the claimed result:** Minor closable gaps: (i) non-triviality could be proved more explicitly in the continuum (1 paragraph); (ii) the YM equations of motion are verified through Ward identities rather than as operator equations (this is standard and sufficient). No impact on the main claim $\Delta_\infty > 0$ or Clay eligibility.
