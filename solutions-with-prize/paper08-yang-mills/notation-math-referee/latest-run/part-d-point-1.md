## Part D, Point 1: Exhaustiveness of the Dimension-6 Classification

**Weight:** MEDIUM
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

The claim is that every $\mathcal{C}$-even, gauge-invariant, local operator of engineering dimension 6 on the $d = 4$ hypercubic lattice falls into one of four classes, all with $\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$.

**(a) The Lüscher–Weisz basis and lattice operators.** The classification in Section 5.6, Part III.3 identifies four classes:

- **Class (I): Zero-derivative operators** — $\mathrm{Tr}(F^3)$ and $d^{abc}F^3$. Both are $\mathcal{C}$-odd. Their coefficients in any $\mathcal{C}$-invariant effective action vanish identically. Elimination is exact and non-perturbative. For SU(2), these operators vanish identically (no cubic Casimir).
- **Class (II): One-derivative operators** — absent entirely in the $\mathcal{C}$-even sector, since gauge-invariant operators require even numbers of field-strength factors.
- **Class (III): Two-derivative operators** — $\mathrm{Tr}(D_\rho F_{\mu\nu})^2$ and $\mathrm{Tr}(D_\mu F^{\mu\rho} D_\nu F^\nu{}_\rho)$. Both have $\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$.
- **Class (IV): Three-or-more-derivative operators** — $\mathrm{dev} \geq 3 > 2$.

The lattice-specific Symanzik classification is given in Appendix J, which provides the explicit basis on the $d = 4$ hypercubic lattice. The standard Symanzik argument (1983) that lattice artifacts are redundant by equations of motion is stated for the specific lattice action used (Wilson plaquette action). The paper notes that "operators vanishing by equations of motion" are not generated by the block-spin integration because the block-spin averages over all configurations, not just on-shell ones — but their matrix elements in physical states are captured by the remaining operators.

**(b) The second two-derivative operator.** The operator $\mathrm{Tr}(D_\mu F^{\mu\rho} D_\nu F^\nu{}_\rho)$ has $\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$ by two independent arguments (Section 5.6, Part III.3): (a) reduction via Yang–Mills EOM and Bianchi identity to $\mathrm{Tr}(DF)^2$ plus $\mathcal{C}$-odd commutator terms (which vanish in the $\mathcal{C}$-invariant action) plus dimension-8 terms; (b) explicit spectral verification showing the product of two temporal-derivative insertions gives the squared deviation factor $(e^{E_m - E_n} - 1)^2$. Both arguments confirm $\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$ for this operator, closing the gap flagged by the r05 referee.

**(c) Lattice artifacts at dimension 6.** The Wilson action contains $O(a^2)$ lattice artifacts, which after Balaban's block-spin integration propagate as dimension-6 operators in the Symanzik expansion. These are cleanly separated: the dimension-4 component is uniquely $S_{\mathrm{YM}}$ (verified in PROOF-CHAIN IV.3), and the subtraction $E_k = S_k - (1/g_k^2) S_{\mathrm{YM}} - \mathcal{E}_0 V$ is exact because $S_{\mathrm{YM}}$ is the unique dimension-4 gauge-invariant $\mathcal{C}$-even operator on the hypercubic lattice. Operator mixing between dimension 4 and dimension 6 is prevented by this uniqueness.

**Impact on the claimed result:**
No impact. The classification is exhaustive and correct. The treatment of the second two-derivative operator closes a previously flagged gap.
