## Part B, Point 2: The Fredenhagen–Marcu Criterion

**Weight:** LIGHT
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

The claim is that $\sigma > 0$ (string tension) implies $\Delta > 0$ (mass gap) via the Fredenhagen–Marcu theorem.

**(a) The precise conditions.** The paper explicitly clarifies (Remark 4.1 and Remark 4.2) that the Fredenhagen–Marcu criterion is used as a **consistency check**, not as a step in the rigorous mass gap proof. The rigorous mass gap bound is $\Delta_0 \geq \alpha/a > 0$ from the cluster expansion rate combined with reflection positivity and the spectral theorem (Theorem 4, part (e)). The Fredenhagen–Marcu diagnostic $\rho_{\mathrm{FM}} = 0$ certifies confinement (absence of charged finite-energy states) but is downstream of the established mass gap, not the source of it.

**(b) The direction of implication.** Fredenhagen–Marcu (CMP 92, 1986) relates the Wilson loop/Polyakov loop behavior to the absence of charged states in the physical Hilbert space. For gauge theories with center symmetry (like SU($N$) for $N \geq 2$), $\sigma > 0$ implies confinement, which in turn is consistent with $\Delta > 0$ but does not directly prove it. The paper correctly avoids using this implication as a proof step.

The heuristic flux-tube estimate $\Delta_0 \gtrsim 2\sqrt{\pi\sigma/3}$ from Appendix F.3 is also explicitly labeled as a physical consistency check, not a theorem. The paper is commendably transparent about the distinction between rigorous bounds and physical heuristics.

**Impact on the claimed result:**
No impact. Fredenhagen–Marcu is used only as a consistency check. The rigorous mass gap bound comes from the cluster expansion and spectral theory.
