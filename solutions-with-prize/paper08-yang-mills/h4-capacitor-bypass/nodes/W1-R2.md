## §2 Research

### 2.1 What the resurgent transseries framework says (general)

A resurgent transseries for an asymptotically free QFT observable $F(g^2)$ has the general form (Écalle; Mariño review arXiv:resurgence-course; Dunne CERN 2024 lectures arXiv:2511.15528):

$$F(g^2) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n g^{2n} + e^{-A/g^2} \sum_{m=0}^\infty b_m g^{2m} + e^{-2A/g^2} \sum_{k=0}^\infty c_k g^{2k} + \ldots$$

where the building blocks are $\{g^2,\, e^{-A/g^2},\, \log g^2\}$ (Dunne, CERN 2024 §1). In theories with renormalons, the singularities of the Borel transform of $\sum a_n g^{2n}$ at positions determined by $\beta_0$ encode the non-perturbative power corrections. The ambiguity in crossing the Borel-plane cut at position $u = u_*$ (in the variable $u = -\beta_0 t$ where $t$ is the Borel parameter) is of the form $e^{-2\pi u_*/(\beta_0 g^2)} \cdot (\text{Stokes constant})$, and this ambiguity must cancel against the explicit non-perturbative sector at that order.

The **median summation** resolves the ambiguity along Stokes directions: the non-perturbative sectors with Stokes constants $S_{u_*}$ combine with the lateral Borel sums to produce an unambiguous real result (Aniceto-Basar-Schiappa 2013; Mariño-Reis 2022 context).

### 2.2 IR renormalon structure of 4D Yang-Mills: what is established

The IR renormalon structure of the 4D SU(N) YM perturbative series is well-characterized in the large-$\beta_0$ / large-$N_f$ approximation (Beneke review, Phys. Rep. 1998, hep-ph/9807443):

- **Position $u = 2$** (in $u = -\beta_0 t$ convention): the leading IR renormalon, associated with the dimension-4 gluon condensate $\langle \alpha_s F^{\mu\nu} F_{\mu\nu} \rangle / \pi$ in the OPE. This is a single pole (distinguished from higher renormalons which are double poles).
- **Position $u = 3$**: next IR renormalon, associated with dimension-6 condensates.
- For the scalar gluonium correlator $\Pi(Q^2) = \int d^4x\, e^{iQx}\langle T\, \mathrm{Tr}F^2(x)\, \mathrm{Tr}F^2(0)\rangle$: the short-distance OPE is (Chetyrkin-Dominguez-Pivovarov-Schilcher; arXiv:1202.1169):

$$\Pi(Q^2) \sim \frac{N^2-1}{2\pi^6}\, Q^4 \log Q^2/\mu^2 \cdot \bigl[1 + O(\alpha_s)\bigr] + \frac{c_4}{Q^4}\langle \alpha_s F^2 \rangle + \ldots$$

The leading perturbative coefficient $\propto (N^2-1)/\pi^6$ (which fixes $C_N$) arises from the perturbative part, not from the OPE power corrections. The IR renormalon at $u=2$ produces the **ambiguity** in the Wilson coefficient that is cancelled by the **ambiguity** in the gluon condensate matrix element — it does not produce the Wilson coefficient's value.

This is the crucial structural distinction:

> **The renormalon at $u=2$ fixes the functional form of the power correction (dimension-4) and ensures ambiguity-cancellation; it does NOT fix the value of the perturbative short-distance coefficient $C_N$.**

The gluon condensate connection (Beneke 1998 §3; Zakharov 1992; Beneke-Zakharov 1993 PRL 69) shows: the renormalon ambiguity $\delta\Pi \sim \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^4/Q^4$ matches the ambiguity in $\langle\alpha_s F^2\rangle/Q^4$, ensuring the OPE combination is well-defined. But the *coefficient* $C_N = 2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$ of the leading $Q^4(\log Q^2)^{-2}$ term is purely perturbative (one-loop exact in the asymptotically free regime, up to $\alpha_s$ corrections from running) — it is set by the free-field propagator and the gauge group normalization, not by non-perturbative condensate data.

### 2.3 Does the full resurgent transseries for pure 4D YM exist?

**Finding: the full resurgent transseries for pure 4D SU(N) Yang-Mills has NOT been constructed. This is an open problem.**

Evidence from the literature:

1. **Dunne-Ünsal CERN 2024 (arXiv:2511.15528)**: lectures treat QM toy models, Heisenberg-Euler effective action (QED), and 2D integrable theories. The lectures explicitly state that "4D QFT cases remain an active field of current research" without identifying 4D pure YM as a solved case. No explicit 4D YM transseries construction appears.

2. **Argyres-Ünsal 2012 (arXiv:1206.1890)**: studies 4D gauge theories with adjoint fermions on $\mathbb{R}^3 \times S^1$ (compactified). The bion-anti-bion singularity in the compactified theory is *conjectured* to be continuously related to the 4D IR renormalon as the compactification radius is changed. This is a conjecture, explicitly stated as such. The uncompactified 4D pure YM case is not handled.

3. **Itou-Misumi-Sugino 2017 (arXiv:1704.05852)**: goes from 4D YM to 2D $\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}$ via compactification on $\mathbb{R} \times T^3$ with twisted boundary conditions. The setup eliminates IR zero modes "which could spoil the trans-series expansion for physical observables" — but this is precisely a workaround for the fact that the uncompactified 4D theory does NOT have a controlled trans-series expansion.

4. **Mariño-Miravitllas-Reis 2022 (arXiv:2111.11951, JHEP)**: discovers new classes of IR renormalons in 2D integrable, asymptotically free field theories via Bethe ansatz. Scope is explicitly 2D. Finding: "large-N estimates of renormalon location might be misleading" — this suggests the naive extrapolation from large-$N$ or large-$\beta_0$ arguments to the actual Borel singularity positions in 4D YM may be wrong.

5. **Shifman 2021 (arXiv:2107.12287)**: discusses limits of applicability of perturbative expansions in YM theories with asymptotic freedom. Title explicitly "Yang-Mills at Strong vs. Weak Coupling: Renormalons, OPE And All That." The paper is critical of naive renormalon-OPE correspondence in non-SUSY theories.

6. **Dunne-Shifman-Ünsal 2015 (arXiv:1502.06680, PRL 114)**: establishes the IR-renormalon vs. OPE conspiracy in **supersymmetric** Yang-Mills. For N=1 SYM: the renormalon-condensate cancellation structure is precisely mapped. For **pure non-SUSY YM**: the paper acknowledges "peculiar features that make [renormalons in N=1 SYM] different from renormalons in QCD," and the non-SUSY case remains harder. The Stokes constants for non-SUSY 4D YM renormalons are NOT computed in this paper.

7. **Gradient-flow OPE (arXiv:2510.12193, Oct 2025)**: uses gradient-flow regularization to produce a renormalon-free OPE for the Adler function in QCD (with quarks). This is perturbative OPE with gradient-flow regularization — a significant technical advance for renormalon subtraction, but explicitly NOT a non-perturbative matching. The paper works in QCD with $n_f = 3$ quarks, not pure YM. The method gives a cleaner Wilson coefficient but does not produce $C_N$ from the renormalon structure; rather, it subtracts the renormalon ambiguity from the perturbative coefficient to get a well-defined scheme.

### 2.4 The Parisi-SVZ connection: what it does and does NOT give

The Parisi-SVZ (SVZ = Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov 1979) / OPE-renormalon dictionary states:

> **The IR renormalon at position $u = d/2$ in the Borel plane (with appropriate conventions) signals the existence of an OPE power correction of dimension $d$.**

This is a structural dictionary: a renormalon at $u=2$ signals a dimension-4 condensate; at $u=3$, a dimension-6 condensate; and so forth. What the dictionary gives:
- The **form** of the power correction: $\langle O_d \rangle / Q^d$
- The **consistency** of the perturbative + non-perturbative combination (ambiguity cancellation)
- In large-$\beta_0$ approximation: the **imaginary part** of the Borel sum, hence the ambiguity magnitude $\sim \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^4/Q^4$

What the dictionary does NOT give:
- The **absolute value** of the Wilson coefficient $C_N$ at leading order (the free-field / one-loop answer)
- The **exact value** of the condensate $\langle F^2 \rangle$ (this requires non-perturbative input — lattice, sum rules, or instanton liquid models)
- The **Stokes constant** at the $u=2$ singularity for pure 4D SU(N) YM (not computed; would require going beyond large-$\beta_0$)

The leading perturbative coefficient $C_N = 2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$ of the $\mathrm{Tr}F^2$ correlator OPE is set by the free-field (zero-coupling) computation — it is the one-loop vacuum polarization bubble with gauge-group factors. The renormalon structure encodes how this coefficient is corrected by higher-order perturbative terms and how the ambiguity in resumming them is cancelled by the condensate. But the **leading-order value** $C_N$ itself is perturbative and is not produced by the renormalon machinery.

More precisely: $C_N|x|^{-8}(\log 1/|x|\Lambda)^{-2}$ with $C_N = 2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$ is the coefficient of the identity operator in the OPE of $[\mathrm{Tr}F^2]_R(x)[\mathrm{Tr}F^2]_R(0)$. This is the perturbative contribution, computable at one loop. The renormalon at $u=2$ is the leading correction showing that higher-loop contributions to this coefficient are factorially divergent — but the coefficient itself, at leading order, is a one-loop Feynman diagram result, not extractable from renormalon positions.

### 2.5 Step 5.5 Self-Suspicion

Three failure modes assessed:

**Failure mode 1: the transseries argument secretly reduces to pert-matching (LOCK-adjacent)**

Assessment: this failure mode IS triggered for naive versions of the argument. If one argues "the transseries $F^{\mathrm{pert}}(t)$ matches $F(t)$ at the level of Taylor coefficients via the transseries matching," that is exactly Route A (identity-theorem Taylor-coefficient identification), LOCK-bound. The transseries angle avoids this only if one argues at the level of the Stokes constants — i.e., reading $C_N$ from the Borel residue, not from perturbative coefficient matching. However, as established in §2.4, the renormalon Borel residue does NOT encode $C_N$ at leading order; it encodes the ambiguity magnitude at subleading order. The argument therefore cannot avoid pert-matching for the leading term.

**Verdict: Failure mode 1 is confirmed. The transseries does not bypass pert-matching for the $C_N$ determination.**

**Failure mode 2: renormalon cancellation exists for QM / 2D models but NOT for 4D YM specifically**

Assessment: this failure mode is CONFIRMED by the literature scan. The full resurgent transseries for pure 4D SU(N) YM has not been constructed. The renormalon cancellation mechanism (Stokes constant determination) is established in:
- QM toy models: yes, rigorously (see Mariño lectures; exact transseries for double-well quantum mechanics)
- 2D integrable models (CP(N-1), O(N) sigma model): yes, in many cases, often analytically via Bethe ansatz
- N=1 SUSY YM: partially, Dunne-Shifman-Ünsal 2015 provides structural results
- Pure non-SUSY 4D YM: **NO construction exists**. The bion-anti-bion / 4D IR renormalon relation is conjectural (Argyres-Ünsal 2012). The 4D pure YM Stokes constants are not computed.

**Verdict: Failure mode 2 is confirmed. The 4D YM case is genuinely open, not a port of existing results.**

**Failure mode 3: Parisi-SVZ statement requires assumptions not valid at the AF short-distance limit**

Assessment: this failure mode is also confirmed, in a subtler form. The SVZ sum rules are typically applied in the deep Euclidean region $Q^2 \gg \Lambda^2$ (intermediate to large $Q^2$), where both the perturbative series and the power corrections $\langle O_d\rangle/Q^d$ are individually well-defined (though the former is asymptotic). At the AF short-distance limit $|x| \to 0$ (or $Q \to \infty$), the power corrections are suppressed and the OPE is dominated by the perturbative coefficient $C_N(\log Q/\Lambda)^{-2}$. In this strict UV limit, the renormalon-condensate cancellation is a sub-leading effect — the leading $C_N$ coefficient is perturbative (one-loop exact, with $\alpha_s$ corrections). The Parisi-SVZ sum rules give $C_N$ only in the sense that the perturbative computation gives it; the renormalon gives information about the power-suppressed corrections, not the leading $(\log)^{-2}$ coefficient.

**Verdict: Failure mode 3 is confirmed. SVZ does not give $C_N$ from renormalon structure; it gives consistency of the power corrections.**

## §4 Bypass Assessment

**Verdict: CONJECTURED-NEGATIVE** for the transseries-level closure of H4.

### Detailed assessment

**Can the renormalon cancellation structure produce $C_N$ directly at the transseries level?**

No, for three independent reasons:

**Reason 1 (structural)**: The leading term $C_N|x|^{-8}(\log 1/|x|\Lambda)^{-2}$ is the Wilson coefficient of the identity operator in the $[\mathrm{Tr}F^2]_R \times [\mathrm{Tr}F^2]_R$ OPE. At short distances, this coefficient is the one-loop perturbative result: $C_N = 2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$ from the free-field contraction of the two operators. IR renormalons encode the ambiguity in higher-loop perturbative corrections to this coefficient and in the power-suppressed condensate terms. The renormalon Borel residue at $u=2$ gives the *magnitude of the ambiguity* (proportional to $\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^4$), not the *leading-order value* of $C_N$. These are different quantities at different orders in the $1/Q$ expansion.

**Reason 2 (existence)**: The full resurgent transseries for pure 4D SU(N) Yang-Mills has not been constructed. The Stokes constants at the IR-renormalon singularities are not known for pure non-SUSY 4D YM. Without Stokes constants, median summation cannot be performed, and the transseries cannot be used as a non-perturbative definition of $F(t)$. The available literature treats 2D integrable models (Mariño programme), compactified or SUSY 4D theories (Argyres-Ünsal, Dunne-Shifman-Ünsal), or large-$\beta_0$ / large-$N_f$ approximations. Pure 4D SU(N) YM is explicitly an open case.

**Reason 3 (LOCK-adjacency of any completion)**: To use the transseries to establish H4, one would need to argue that the lateral Borel sum of $F^{\mathrm{pert}}(t)$ (via the transseries completion) in a specific sector equals $F(t)$ in a way that the Taylor coefficients match — which routes back through Taylor-coefficient identification. The transseries machinery resolves the ambiguity on the $\mathbb{R}_+$ real axis but does not remove the need to compare the transseries sum with the non-perturbative $F(t)$. Any such comparison at the Taylor-coefficient level re-enters the LOCK.

### Partial bypass structure

A partial structure that IS consistent with the transseries angle:

- **Step 1**: Construct the full resurgent transseries for pure 4D SU(N) YM [OPEN — requires Stokes constant determination; no known method at present]
- **Step 2**: Show that the median summation of the transseries produces a well-defined non-perturbative object $\hat{F}(t)$ in some Borel-Gevrey class [OPEN — requires Step 1]
- **Step 3**: If $\hat{F}(t)$ is analytic in a Gevrey-class sense, then W1-R1's lateral-Borel angle takes over (identity theorem on $\hat{F}$ vs $F$) — but this is W1-R1's problem, not W1-R2's
- **Step 4**: To get $C_N$ from the transseries data, one would additionally need to show that the renormalon Stokes constant at $u=2$ is related to $C_N$ by an exact formula [CONJECTURED-NEGATIVE — the Stokes constant encodes the ambiguity magnitude, which is $\sim \Lambda^4$-scale, not the leading perturbative coefficient which is scheme-independent and one-loop exact]

**Conclusion**: the transseries angle provides complementary infrastructure (renormalon-OPE dictionary) to W1-R1's attack but does NOT independently close H4, and does NOT read $C_N$ from the transseries structure.

## §5 New Kill Documented

**K-8 (W1-R2 contribution): Transseries-reads-C_N trap.**

Pattern: structural-conflation — conflating the renormalon Borel residue (which encodes ambiguity magnitude $\sim\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^4/Q^4$) with the leading Wilson coefficient $C_N$ (which is the one-loop perturbative coefficient, gauge-group-trace-normalized, $\propto (N^2-1)/\pi^6$). These are different quantities:

- Borel residue at $u=2$: $\sim \pi \beta_0^2 e^{-4\pi u_*/(\beta_0 g^2)} \cdot (\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}/Q)^4$ — infrared, power-suppressed, non-perturbative
- $C_N$: $2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$ — ultraviolet, power-leading, one-loop perturbative

An approach claiming to derive $C_N$ from the transseries renormalon data without performing a perturbative computation has conflated these two quantities and is dead.

Re-entry gate: an exact theorem connecting the $u=2$ Stokes constant for pure 4D SU(N) YM to $C_N$ would be required; no such theorem is in the literature; the structural argument above makes it unlikely (different IR/UV origin of the two quantities).
