## Research

### R.1 What H4 actually asks (exact scope)

Preprint §L.7.1 verbatim:

> "The renormalised non-perturbative Schwinger function $S_2^{\mathrm{ren}}(x,y)$ constructed in Appendix N, §N.5 (Lemma L.3.8) admits a short-distance asymptotic expansion whose leading term coincides with the perturbative prediction: $S_2^{\mathrm{ren}}(x,y) = \dfrac{C_N}{|x-y|^8}\bigl(\ln\tfrac{1}{|x-y|\Lambda}\bigr)^{-2}\bigl[1 + O((\log)^{-1})\bigr]$ with $C_N = 2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$."

Preprint §L.7.3 mildest form: F(t) := S₂,ₜᶜ(x,y)/c₁(t)² is Lipschitz, the Cauchy estimate gives a convergent radius $r_t > 0$ (Lemma L.3.1), and H4 is then "whether the Taylor coefficients $F^{(k)}(0)$ are computable by Feynman-diagrammatic perturbative rules."

Note: the ambient manifold is $\mathbb{R}^4$. No compactification. No twist. H4 is an $\mathbb{R}^4$ statement.

### R.2 Why $\mathbb{R}_+$ Borel fails (LOCK-adjacent, do not revisit)

For 4D non-abelian gauge theory, the perturbative coefficients of F^pert(t) are asymptotic with Gevrey-1 factorial growth $n! \beta_0^n$ driven by IR renormalons. The Borel transform $B[F](u) = \sum F_n u^n / n!$ has singularities at $u = 2\pi k/\beta_0$ ($k \geq 1$) on the positive real axis of the Borel $u$-plane ('t Hooft 1977; Mueller 1984; Beneke 1999 review arXiv:hep-ph/9807443). The standard Borel integral $\int_0^\infty e^{-u/t} B[F](u)\,du$ is not defined because the contour runs through poles. This is what Delta-Critic tested and killed.

### R.3 The lateral Borel direction (Écalle 1981, Dunne-Ünsal 2012–)

**Mechanism (verbatim from web search of Dunne 2024 CERN lectures + Mariño resurgence corpus):**

> "Borel summation and its extension, Borel-Écalle summation, is a method of converting formal asymptotic expansions to an integral representation that can encode the full analytic structure of the function under consideration." (Dunne CERN 2024, arXiv:2511.15528)

> "By making use of transseries and Écalle's resurgence theory... a non-perturbative continuum definition of a class of field theories which admit semi-classical expansions may be possible." (Dunne-Ünsal 2012, arXiv:1210.2423, abstract)

> "An ambiguity of (lateral) Borel resummations of the perturbative expansion is precisely canceled by contributions from non-perturbative sectors." (Dunne-Ünsal CP(N-1) programme, JHEP11 (2012) 170)

**Mathematical structure.** Replace the divergent $\mathbb{R}_+$ contour by lateral contours $\mathcal{S}_{\theta^\pm}[F](t) = \int_0^{\infty e^{i\theta^\pm}} e^{-u/t} B[F](u)\,du$ along rays $\arg u = \theta^\pm$ that go *around* the IR-renormalon cut on the positive real axis. The Stokes jump is
$$(\mathcal{S}_{\theta^+} - \mathcal{S}_{\theta^-})[F](t) = 2\pi i \cdot \text{Disc}_{u \in \mathrm{cut}} B[F](u),$$
and Écalle's alien derivative $\dot\Delta_\omega$ at the singularity $\omega$ encodes the discontinuity. The ambiguity is cancelled by adding non-perturbative trans-series sectors — bion-anti-bion (or in 4D: instanton/anti-instanton) molecules whose semi-classical action matches the renormalon location. **Median summation** (Candelpergher-Nosmas-Pham; Écalle 1981) averages $\mathcal{S}_{\theta^+}$ and $\mathcal{S}_{\theta^-}$ to produce a real, unambiguous resummation.

The key theoretical point: **in a lateral sector** $\arg t \in (0, \theta^+) \cup (-\theta^-, 0)$ (not along $\mathbb{R}_+$), $F^{\mathrm{lateral}}(t) := \mathcal{S}_\theta[F^{\mathrm{pert}}](t) + \sum_k e^{-kS_I/g^2}\,\Phi_k(t)$ is genuinely analytic. The identity-theorem objection on $\mathbb{R}_+$ (R.A.1 Wrong-space, formal-series-vs-analytic-function) does not apply in the lateral sector because $F^{\mathrm{lateral}}$ is an analytic function there.

### R.5 Two-step assessment for the H4 bypass

**Step 1 (optimistic): does gradient-flow help?** YES, partially. The gradient-flow framework (Lüscher 2010; Narayanan-Neuberger 2006; used as the scaffold for F(t) in preprint §L.3) suppresses UV renormalons for the flowed action density E(t). The flow-time Taylor coefficients $F^{(k)}(0)$ are therefore cleaner than raw perturbative QCD coefficients — the only asymptotic growth is from IR renormalons at $u = 2, 3, \ldots$ (gluon-condensate-type).

**Step 2 (does it close H4?): NO — three gaps remain.**

**Gap 1 — lateral Borel for F(t) on $\mathbb{R}^4$ is CONJECTURED, not proven.** The Dunne-Ünsal programme on semiclassical realization of renormalons (the "complex bion" mechanism) is rigorously established only for: (a) QM toy models, (b) 2D CP(N-1), (c) 4D YM on $\mathbb{R}\times T^3$ with twisted BC. It is NOT rigorously established for uncompactified 4D YM on $\mathbb{R}^4$. The extension is explicitly called "speculative" by Yamazaki-Yonekura. Dunne CERN 2024 characterises the QFT case as "the general case is an active field of current research." No theorem exists for $\mathbb{R}^4$ 4D YM.

**Gap 2 — even if lateral Borel produced an analytic $F^{\mathrm{lateral}}(t)$ in a complex sector, matching it to the physical $F(t)$ on $\mathbb{R}_+$ requires a Watson-type theorem.** The physical Schwinger function lives on Euclidean $\mathbb{R}^4$ with real positive $t$. The Borel-Écalle machinery produces an analytic $F^{\mathrm{lateral}}$ in a cone $\arg t \in (-\theta, \theta)$ away from the positive real axis. To get H4, one needs $\lim_{\arg t \to 0^\pm} F^{\mathrm{lateral}}(t) = F^{\mathrm{physical}}(t)$ in the sense of distributional boundary values, plus uniqueness (some Watson-type theorem in the Gevrey-1 class). This boundary-value matching is not automatic; for 4D YM on $\mathbb{R}^4$ it is not established.

**Gap 3 — $C_N = 2(N^2-1)/\pi^6$ is a Feynman-diagram computation.** Even granting lateral analyticity and boundary-value matching, the AF coefficient $C_N$ is set by one-loop Feynman rules at short distances. Showing that $F^{\mathrm{lateral}}(0) \cdot |x|^{-8}$ carries the right $C_N$ requires computing the short-distance limit of the resurgent transseries and confirming it reduces to the standard one-loop answer — at which point one has re-derived what H4 asserts directly rather than bypassed it. **This is the LOCK re-entry trap (Step 5.5 self-suspect item 2).** The "cancellation" proposed is exactly pert-matching at a different level.
