# 02. The Mixed 't Hooft Anomaly: Explicit Derivation for CP²

## I. Review: What Is a 't Hooft Anomaly?

### I.1 Definition

A 't Hooft anomaly for a global symmetry $G$ is an obstruction
to gauging $G$ while preserving all other symmetries. If a theory
has a 't Hooft anomaly:

**(a)** The anomaly is RG-invariant (it matches between UV and IR).

**(b)** The anomaly is an obstruction to a trivially gapped,
symmetric vacuum. The IR must realize one of:
  - Spontaneous symmetry breaking
  - Gapless excitations (CFT)
  - A topological field theory (TQFT)

**(c)** The anomaly is classified by $H^{d+1}(BG, U(1))$ for a
$d$-dimensional theory (the group cohomology classification of
SPT phases one dimension higher).

### I.2 For discrete symmetries in 2D

For a 2D theory with $G = \mathbb{Z}_N^{(1)} \times
\mathbb{Z}_N^{(2)}$, the anomaly is classified by:
$$H^3(B(\mathbb{Z}_N \times \mathbb{Z}_N), U(1))
  = \mathbb{Z}_N$$

The anomaly is labelled by an integer $p \in \mathbb{Z}_N$ (mod $N$).
It manifests as a phase in the algebra of symmetry operators:
$$\hat{U}_1 \hat{U}_2 = e^{2\pi i p/N} \hat{U}_2 \hat{U}_1$$

**STATUS: This is standard 't Hooft anomaly theory. [ESTABLISHED,
Gaiotto-Kapustin-Seiberg-Willett 2015; Tachikawa 2017]**

## II. The Anomaly for $\mathbb{CP}^2$: Three Derivations

### II.1 Derivation 1: Operator algebra (most direct)

**Setup.** The $\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted $\mathbb{CP}^2$ model on
$\mathbb{R} \times S^1_L$. The two $\mathbb{Z}_3$ symmetries are:
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$: center symmetry, generated by $\hat{C}$
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$: discrete chiral symmetry, generated
  by $\hat{\chi}$

**Step 1: Identify the operators.**

In the low-energy effective theory (valid at small $L$ by
asymptotic freedom), the degrees of freedom are the dual photon
$\sigma \in [0, 2\pi)$ and its conjugate momentum $\hat{p}_\sigma$
(the electric flux through $S^1$).

The operators are:
$$\hat{C} = e^{i \hat{p}_\sigma \cdot 2\pi/3}
  \quad\text{(shifts electric flux by 1 mod 3)}$$
$$\hat{\chi} = e^{i \hat{\sigma} / 1}
  \quad\text{(shifts } \sigma \text{ by } 2\pi/3\text{)}$$

Wait -- let me be more careful. The correct identification uses
the $N$-component structure.

**Corrected Step 1.** In the $\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted theory, the
holonomy around $S^1$ is constrained to lie near the center
element $\Omega = \text{diag}(1, \omega, \omega^2)$. The
fluctuations around this saddle are parametrized by $N-1 = 2$
dual photons $\sigma_1, \sigma_2$, but the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ center
symmetry acts as the cyclic permutation $\sigma_a \mapsto
\sigma_{a+1 \bmod 3}$ (with the constraint
$\sigma_1 + \sigma_2 + \sigma_3 = 0$).

For the anomaly, it suffices to work with a single effective
angle $\phi$ that parametrizes the orbit of $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$.
The center symmetry acts on the conjugate variable.

**The canonical commutation relation:**
$$[\hat{\phi}, \hat{n}] = i$$

where $\hat{n} \in \mathbb{Z}$ is the conjugate integer momentum
(electric flux quantum number).

The symmetry generators are:
$$\hat{C}: \quad \hat{n} \mapsto \hat{n} + 1
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \hat{C} = e^{i \hat{\phi}}$$

(No -- $\hat{C}$ shifts the electric flux. Let me write this
correctly using the clock-and-shift algebra.)

**The clock-and-shift formulation.**

On the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ Hilbert space with basis $|0\rangle,
|1\rangle, |2\rangle$, define:
$$\hat{C} |k\rangle = |k+1 \bmod 3\rangle
  \qquad\text{(shift / clock)}$$
$$\hat{\chi} |k\rangle = \omega^k |k\rangle
  \qquad\text{(phase / shift)}$$

Then:
$$\hat{C} \hat{\chi} |k\rangle = \hat{C} (\omega^k |k\rangle)
  = \omega^k |k+1\rangle$$
$$\hat{\chi} \hat{C} |k\rangle = \hat{\chi} |k+1\rangle
  = \omega^{k+1} |k+1\rangle$$

Therefore:
$$\boxed{\hat{C} \hat{\chi} = \omega^{-1} \hat{\chi} \hat{C}
  = e^{-2\pi i/3} \, \hat{\chi} \hat{C}}$$

or equivalently:
$$\hat{C} \hat{\chi} \hat{C}^{-1} = \omega^{-1} \hat{\chi}$$

**The anomaly coefficient is $p = -1 \bmod 3 \equiv 2 \bmod 3$.**

(Convention: $p = 1$ and $p = 2$ are related by $C \leftrightarrow
\chi$ exchange and complex conjugation. The sign is a convention;
what matters is $p \neq 0 \bmod 3$.)

**CLAIM [DERIVED].** The mixed 't Hooft anomaly between
$\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ in the
$\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted $\mathbb{CP}^2$ model has anomaly
coefficient $p = 1$ (i.e., it is non-trivial, the unique
non-trivial class up to conjugation in
$H^3(B\mathbb{Z}_3 \times B\mathbb{Z}_3, U(1)) = \mathbb{Z}_3$).


### II.2 Derivation 2: Background gauge fields

This is the standard modern derivation (Gaiotto-Kapustin-
Komargodski-Seiberg 2017, adapted to the $\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}$
context by Tanizaki-Sulejmanpasic 2018).

**Step 1: Couple to background gauge fields.**

Promote $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ to a background gauge field
$B^{(C)} \in H^1(M, \mathbb{Z}_3)$ and $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$
to $B^{(\chi)} \in H^1(M, \mathbb{Z}_3)$, where $M$ is the
2D spacetime.

**Step 2: The partition function with backgrounds.**

The partition function $Z[B^{(C)}, B^{(\chi)}]$ should be
gauge-invariant under both background gauge transformations.
If it can be made simultaneously invariant, there is no anomaly.

**Step 3: The obstruction.**

Under a $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ gauge transformation $B^{(C)}
\mapsto B^{(C)} + d\lambda^{(C)}$, the partition function
transforms as:
$$Z[B^{(C)} + d\lambda^{(C)}, B^{(\chi)}]
  = Z[B^{(C)}, B^{(\chi)}] \times
  \exp\left(\frac{2\pi i}{3} \int_M \lambda^{(C)}
  \cup B^{(\chi)}\right)$$

The cup product $\lambda^{(C)} \cup B^{(\chi)}$ is a well-defined
element of $H^2(M, \mathbb{Z}_3)$. The integral
$\int_M \lambda^{(C)} \cup B^{(\chi)} \in \mathbb{Z}_3$
is non-zero in general.

**This phase cannot be removed** by adding any local counterterm
to the action. A candidate counterterm would be:
$$S_{\text{ct}} = \frac{2\pi i \, q}{3} \int_M B^{(C)}
  \cup B^{(\chi)}$$

for some $q \in \mathbb{Z}_3$. Under the gauge transformation,
this shifts by:
$$\frac{2\pi i \, q}{3} \int_M d\lambda^{(C)} \cup B^{(\chi)}
  = \frac{2\pi i \, q}{3} \int_M \lambda^{(C)} \cup dB^{(\chi)}
  = 0$$

(since $dB^{(\chi)} = 0$ for a flat background). The counterterm
does NOT cancel the anomaly phase. Therefore the anomaly is
genuine.

**CLAIM [DERIVED].** The obstruction is:
$$\mathcal{A} = \exp\left(\frac{2\pi i}{3} \int_M B^{(C)}
  \cup B^{(\chi)}\right) \in H^3(B\mathbb{Z}_3 \times
  B\mathbb{Z}_3, U(1)) = \mathbb{Z}_3$$

This is the mixed 't Hooft anomaly.


### II.3 Derivation 3: Anomaly inflow from 3D

The anomaly of the 2D theory can be described as the boundary
anomaly of a 3D SPT (symmetry-protected topological) phase.

**The 3D SPT action.** The bulk is 3-dimensional with action:
$$S_{\text{3D}} = \frac{2\pi}{3} \int_{M_3} B^{(C)}
  \cup B^{(\chi)} \cup B^{(\chi)}$$

No -- for $\mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_3$ in 3D, the correct
SPT is classified by $H^3(B\mathbb{Z}_3 \times B\mathbb{Z}_3,
U(1)) = \mathbb{Z}_3$, with the generating cocycle:
$$\omega_3(a, b) = \exp\left(\frac{2\pi i}{3} \,
  a_1 \cup b_2\right)$$

where $a \in H^1(M_3, \mathbb{Z}_3)$ and $b \in H^2(M_3,
\mathbb{Z}_3)$, or equivalently (via the Kunneth decomposition):
$$S_{\text{3D}} = \frac{2\pi i \, p}{3} \int_{M_3}
  A^{(C)} \cup \delta A^{(\chi)}$$

with $p = 1$ the anomaly coefficient.

The 2D theory lives on $\partial M_3$. The non-gauge-invariance
of the 2D partition function is exactly cancelled by the variation
of the 3D bulk action. This is anomaly inflow.

**STATUS: This is the standard anomaly inflow picture. The
classification $H^3(B\mathbb{Z}_3 \times B\mathbb{Z}_3, U(1))
= \mathbb{Z}_3$ is a standard group cohomology computation.
[ESTABLISHED]**

## III. The Physical Content of the Anomaly

### III.1 What the anomaly says

**Theorem ('t Hooft anomaly matching).** [ESTABLISHED]

*The IR phase of the $\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted $\mathbb{CP}^2$ model
must have the same anomaly $p = 1$ as the UV.*

This constrains the IR to be one of:

**(a) $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ preserved, $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$
preserved.** Both symmetries unbroken. The anomaly is matched
by non-trivial dynamics (a gapped, non-degenerate vacuum with
the anomaly carried by excitations). This is the CONFINING
scenario.

**(b) $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ spontaneously broken.** The vacuum
is 3-fold degenerate. The anomaly is matched by the permutation
action on the degenerate vacua. This is the DECONFINING scenario.

**(c) $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ spontaneously broken.** The vacuum
is 3-fold degenerate under the chiral symmetry. The anomaly is
matched similarly.

**(d) Both broken.** 9-fold degeneracy (or some sub-pattern).

**(e) Gapless (CFT).** The anomaly is matched by the conformal
symmetry algebra.

**(f) TQFT.** A topological field theory can carry the anomaly
through its ground-state degeneracy on non-trivial manifolds.

### III.2 The key limitation

**CLAIM [CRITICAL].** The anomaly $p = 1 \in \mathbb{Z}_3$
is consistent with BOTH scenario (a) and scenario (b).

**Proof that (b) matches the anomaly.** If $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$
is spontaneously broken, there are three degenerate vacua
$|0\rangle, |1\rangle, |2\rangle$ related by $\hat{C}$. The
chiral symmetry $\hat{\chi}$ acts as a phase on these vacua:
$\hat{\chi}|k\rangle = \omega^k |k\rangle$. The commutation
relation $\hat{C}\hat{\chi} = \omega^{-1} \hat{\chi}\hat{C}$
is automatically satisfied by this representation. The anomaly
is matched by the symmetry-breaking pattern itself.

**Proof that (a) matches the anomaly.** If both symmetries are
preserved, the unique vacuum $|0\rangle$ satisfies
$\hat{C}|0\rangle = |0\rangle$ and $\hat{\chi}|0\rangle
= |0\rangle$. But then $\hat{C}\hat{\chi}|0\rangle
= |0\rangle$ while $\omega^{-1}\hat{\chi}\hat{C}|0\rangle
= \omega^{-1}|0\rangle$, which gives $1 = \omega^{-1}$,
a contradiction.

**Wait.** This seems to say that scenario (a) is RULED OUT.
Let me re-examine.

If BOTH $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ are
preserved, the vacuum is invariant under both: $\hat{C}|\Omega
\rangle = |\Omega\rangle$ and $\hat{\chi}|\Omega\rangle
= |\Omega\rangle$. Then:
$$\hat{C}\hat{\chi}|\Omega\rangle = |\Omega\rangle$$
$$\omega^{-1}\hat{\chi}\hat{C}|\Omega\rangle
  = \omega^{-1}|\Omega\rangle$$

These are inconsistent: $|\Omega\rangle = \omega^{-1}|\Omega
\rangle$ implies $\omega = 1$, contradiction.

**Therefore: the anomaly FORBIDS the simultaneous preservation
of both $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$.**

This is the standard consequence of a mixed 't Hooft anomaly
between two $\mathbb{Z}_N$ symmetries: at least one must be
broken (or the theory is gapless/topological).


### III.3 Implications for adiabatic continuity

The question is whether $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ is preserved at
all $L$. The anomaly says:

**At each $L$**, at least one of $\{\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)},
\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}\}$ must be broken (or the vacuum is
gapless/topological).

The UV (small $L$) phase has:
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$: **preserved**
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$: **broken** (by the fractional
  instanton potential, which generates $N = 3$ degenerate
  vacua in the $\sigma$ direction)
- Anomaly matched by the chiral symmetry breaking

For adiabatic continuity, we want:
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$: **preserved at all $L$**
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$: **broken at all $L$**

But the anomaly alone allows the SWAP:
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$: **broken** at some $L_*$
- $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$: **restored** at $L_*$

This swap would be a phase transition -- exactly what we want
to rule out.

## IV. Summary

The mixed 't Hooft anomaly between $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and
$\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ in the $\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted
$\mathbb{CP}^2$ model:

1. **Exists** and has anomaly coefficient $p = 1 \in \mathbb{Z}_3$
   [DERIVED by three methods]

2. **Forbids** the simultaneous preservation of both symmetries
   in a gapped phase [PROVED]

3. **Does NOT by itself** determine WHICH symmetry is broken
   [CRITICAL LIMITATION]

4. **Is consistent with** both the confining phase ($C$ preserved,
   $\chi$ broken) and the deconfining phase ($C$ broken,
   $\chi$ preserved) [PROVED in III.2]

The anomaly provides a necessary condition (one symmetry must
break) but not a sufficient condition for adiabatic continuity
(it does not select which one breaks).
