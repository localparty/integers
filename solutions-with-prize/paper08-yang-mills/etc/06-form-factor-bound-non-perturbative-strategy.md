There are three realistic paths to closing the form factor gap, ranked by  feasibility:   

## Path 1: Extend Balaban to three-point functions. 
Your file 49 identifies this       correctly. Balaban's multi-scale cluster expansion controls the partition function (two-point). Extending it to the three-point function $\langle \phi(t) , O(0) ,     \phi(-s) \rangle_c$ would give the form factor bound non-perturbatively. This uses existing technology (Balaban 1985-89, Dimock 2011-13) applied to a new observable. This is the most promising mathematical path.
## Path 2: A lattice spectral perturbation theorem. 
Instead of bounding the form       factor directly, prove that the transfer matrix spectral gap responds to irrelevant  perturbations with the dimensional analysis scaling. This is the "one spectral     estimate" that file 46 identifies. The key insight: you need a perturbation theorem  for the spectral gap that's sensitive to the dimension of the perturbation operator, not just its norm.
## Path 3: Restructure the paper around what IS proved. 
Present the lattice mass gap   as Theorem 1 (fully proved). Present the continuum limit as Theorem 2 (conditional on the form factor bound, with the bound proved perturbatively and the     non-perturbative extension identified as the remaining problem). This is honest, publishable, and frames the contribution correctly.
