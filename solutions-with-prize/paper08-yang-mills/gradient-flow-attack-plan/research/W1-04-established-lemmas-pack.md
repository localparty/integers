## Memo 1: Lemma 3.2 --- No Operator Mixing at Dimension 4

### Statement

The unique local, gauge-invariant, C-even, parity-even operator of engineering
dimension 4 on the 4D hypercubic lattice is the Wilson plaquette action
$S_{\mathrm{YM}} = \sum_P s_P$. The mixing matrix at dimension 4 is 1x1.

### Source Reference

**Primary:** Mass gap preprint, Section 5.3.1 (lines 393--492 of
`/Users/gsix/yang-mills/preprint/sections/05-continuum-limit.md`).

**Confirmation:** Lines 1963--1970 of the same file, under the heading
"[CONFIRMED] Dimension-6 projection is exact," which restates the result:
"the unique local, gauge-invariant, C-even, parity-even operator of
engineering dimension 4 is $S_{\mathrm{YM}}$."

### Proof Summary (from the source)

The proof proceeds by exhaustive elimination of dimension-4 candidates:

1. **$\mathrm{Tr}(F \tilde{F})$** (topological density): Parity-odd.
   Excluded by the parity-even requirement. (Line 1967.)

2. **$s_P^2$ and higher plaquette powers**: Engineering dimension 8, not 4.
   (Line 1966.)

3. **Redundant operators**: Not generated by the block-spin integration
   (Balaban's construction preserves gauge invariance and does not introduce
   operators outside the Wilson action at dimension 4). (Lines 1968--1969.)

4. **$\mathrm{Tr}(F^3)$ and $d^{abc}F^3$**: These are dimension-6 operators
   (each $F$ carries dimension 2, three factors give dimension 6). They are
   also C-odd (lines 449--458: $\mathrm{Tr}(FFF) \to -\mathrm{Tr}(FFF)$
   under $A_\mu \to -A_\mu^T$), so they are excluded from the C-even sector
   entirely. For SU(2), $\mathrm{Tr}(F^3)$ vanishes identically since
   $d^{abc} = 0$ (line 459--462).

The conclusion (line 1969--1970): Balaban defines $g_{k+1}$ as the coefficient
of $S_{\mathrm{YM}}$ (CMP 109, Sec. 2), so the subtraction is exact and the
remainder is genuinely dimension > 4.

### Verification for Gradient-Flow Context

In the small-flow-time expansion of the gradient-flow observable
$E(t) = -\frac{1}{2}\mathrm{Tr}(G_{\mu\nu}G^{\mu\nu})$, the leading
operator at dimension 4 is $[\mathrm{Tr}(F^2)]_R$. The 1x1 mixing matrix
at dimension 4 means there is no operator mixing to resolve: the gradient
flow at dimension 4 involves a single renormalized operator, with a single
matching coefficient $c_1(t)$. This simplification is exact (not perturbative)
because the elimination of all other dimension-4 candidates is
non-perturbative: C-elimination is a symmetry argument, parity elimination is
exact, and dimensional counting is algebraic.

## Memo 2: Lemma 3.3 --- Deviation Order dev >= 2 at Dimension >= 6

### Statement

Every C-even, gauge-invariant, dimension-6 operator has Boltzmann deviation
order $\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$. Consequently, the spectral bound gives
$|\langle 1|\mathcal{O}|1\rangle_c| \leq C_2 M \hat{\Delta}^2$ for any
such operator $\mathcal{O}$ with norm $\|\mathcal{O}\| \leq M$.

### Source Reference

**Primary:** Mass gap preprint, Section 5.6, Part III (lines 1737--1898 of
`/Users/gsix/yang-mills/preprint/sections/05-continuum-limit.md`).

- Lemma statement: lines 1769--1773 ("Stability of Deviation Order").
- Proof: lines 1775--1857 (four-class classification).
- Application to Balaban's effective action: lines 1859--1883 (Part III.4).
- Quantitative bound: lines 1885--1898 (Part III.5, equation (III.2)).

**Supporting:** Section 5.5.3 (lines 1181--1200): the Two-Derivative Spectral
Bound lemma that converts $\mathrm{dev} \geq p$ into the bound
$C_p M \hat{\Delta}^p$.

**Supporting:** Section 5.5.4 (lines 1530--1551): explicit verification that
$\mathrm{dev}(\mathrm{Tr}(D_0 F)^2) \geq 2$ via the spectral weight factor
$(e^{E_m - E_n} - 1)^2$.

### Proof Summary (from the source)

The proof classifies all dimension-6 gauge-invariant operators into four
exhaustive classes (lines 1777--1852):

**(I) Zero-derivative operators** ($\mathrm{Tr}(F^3)$, $d^{abc}F^3$):
C-odd. Coefficient exactly zero in any C-invariant effective action.
Eliminated non-perturbatively by Section 5.3.1.

**(II) One-derivative operators** (dimension-5 reduced part): Absent.
No C-even gauge-invariant operator of dimension 5 exists (gauge invariance
requires even numbers of field-strength factors under the trace).

**(III) Two-derivative operators**: The surviving operators are
$\mathrm{Tr}(D_\rho F_{\mu\nu})^2$ and
$\mathrm{Tr}(D_\mu F^{\mu\rho} D_\nu F^\nu{}_\rho)$. Both have
$\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$ by the spectral mechanism: the transfer-matrix weight
$(e^{E_m - E_n} - 1)^2$ vanishes identically at $n = m$ (structural zero).
For the second operator, the equations-of-motion reduction and explicit
spectral verification (lines 1828--1849) independently confirm dev >= 2.

**(IV) Three-or-more-derivative operators**: Have $\mathrm{dev} \geq 3 > 2$.

Since every C-even dimension-6 operator falls into classes (I)--(IV), and all
surviving classes have dev >= 2, the lemma is proved (line 1857).

### Verification for Gradient-Flow Context

In the small-flow-time expansion, subleading terms at dimension 6 take the
form $c_6(t) [\mathcal{O}_{d=6}]_R$. The stability-of-deviation-order lemma
guarantees that the connected matrix element of any such operator satisfies
$|\langle 1|\mathcal{O}_{d=6}|1\rangle_c| \leq C g_k^4 \hat{\Delta}^2$.

For the gradient-flow context, the key consequence is that subleading
terms in the small-flow-time expansion are suppressed by $g_k^4 \hat{\Delta}^2$
relative to the leading dimension-4 term. This suppression is
non-perturbative: it relies on the symmetry-based operator classification,
not on a perturbative decomposition. The analyticity properties (B1)--(B2)
from Balaban's construction (Section 5.6, Parts I--II) ensure that the
"dimension-6 part" of the effective action is a genuine convergent operator,
not merely an asymptotic concept. This validates applying the spectral
bound to the full non-perturbative object.

### Numerical Checks

None required (the result is structural, based on operator classification
and spectral analysis).

## Memo 3: Lemma 3.4 --- KK Mode Sum Vanishing at t = 0

### Statement

$E_L(-j; Q) = 0$ for all positive-definite quadratic forms $Q$ in $L$
variables and all positive integers $j \geq 1$. The mechanism is the
structural zero $1/\Gamma(-j) = 0$.

### Source Reference

**Primary:** Paper 1, Appendix K, Section K.7b (lines 580--608 of
`/Users/gsix/quantum-geometry-in-5d-latex/paper1/appendices/22-appendix-k-higher-loop-epstein.md`).

- Theorem statement (Theorem K.1 / "Universal Epstein Vanishing"):
  lines 435--437 (Section K.6.1) and lines 582--583 (Section K.7b).
- Proof: lines 585--596 (Section K.7b).
- Component 1 characterization: lines 214--219 (Section K.5.0).

**Supporting (two-loop specialization):** Paper 1, Appendix G, Section G.4.2
(lines 226--258 of
`/Users/gsix/quantum-geometry-in-5d-latex/paper1/appendices/18-appendix-g-two-loop-computation.md`):
Eisenstein factorization $E_2(s; Q_0) = 6\zeta(s)L(s, \chi_{-3})$ and the
complementary trivial zeros mechanism.

### Proof (from the source, lines 585--596)

The completed Epstein zeta function is $\Lambda(s) = \pi^{-s} \Gamma(s)
E_L(s; Q)$. By the Epstein analytic continuation, $\Lambda(s)$ is
meromorphic with poles only at $s = 0$ and $s = L/2$. At $s = -j$
($j \geq 1$), $\Lambda(-j)$ is finite (since $-j$ is neither 0 nor $L/2$
for any $L \geq 1$). Inverting:

$$E_L(s; Q) = \pi^s \Lambda(s) / \Gamma(s)$$

Since $1/\Gamma(-j) = 0$ (the Gamma function has poles at all non-positive
integers), we obtain $E_L(-j; Q) = 0$. QED.

The proof requires only: (i) $Q$ is positive-definite (ensures well-defined
Epstein zeta with analytic continuation); (ii) $j \geq 1$ (ensures $s = -j$
is a non-positive integer where $\Gamma$ has a pole).

### Verification for Gradient-Flow Context

At flow time $t = 0$, the small-flow-time expansion of gradient-flow
observables in the compactified theory on $M^4 \times S^1/\mathbb{Z}_2$
produces KK mode sums of the form $E_L(-j; Q)$ at each order in the UV
expansion. By Theorem K.1, all these sums vanish, ensuring that $F(0)$ is
finite. This is a structural statement: it does not depend on the specific
quadratic form $Q$ (which varies with diagram topology), only on positive
definiteness and the evaluation at negative integers.

For the two-loop sunset topology specifically, $Q_0 = n^2 + nm + m^2$
(the Eisenstein norm form), and the factorization
$E_2(s; Q_0) = 6\zeta(s) L(s, \chi_{-3})$ provides an independent
verification via complementary trivial zeros: $\zeta(-j) = 0$ for even
$j \geq 2$, and $L(-j, \chi_{-3}) = 0$ for odd $j \geq 1$.

## Memo 4: Lemma 3.5 --- Z_2 Parity Cancellation Persists at All t

### Statement

$f_{\mathrm{even}}(n) + f_{\mathrm{odd}}(n) = 0$ at each KK level $n \geq 1$,
for all flow times $t \geq 0$. The cancellation is exact and does not require
summation over $n$.

### Source Reference

**Primary:** Paper 10, Section 3.3 (lines 92--154 of
`/Users/gsix/quantum-geometry-in-5d-latex/paper10/preprint/03-z2-mechanism.md`).

- Proposition 3.1 statement: lines 106--110.
- Proof: lines 112--114.
- Numerical verification table: lines 128--142.
- Regulator independence: Section 3.4 (lines 156--183).
- Relationship to Epstein zeros (Route 01): Section 3.7 (lines 249--273).

**Supporting:** Section 3.2 (lines 47--90): Z_2 parity of the
Goroff-Sagnotti three-vertex, establishing $I_{---+}^{(n,n)} = -I_{+++}^{(n,n)}$.

### Proof (from the source, lines 112--114)

At each KK level $n \geq 1$, define:

$$f_{\mathrm{even}}(n) = +[d_0 + d_2(n/R)^2 + d_4(n/R)^4 + \cdots]$$
$$f_{\mathrm{odd}}(n) = -[d_0 + d_2(n/R)^2 + d_4(n/R)^4 + \cdots]$$

where the sign flip arises from the y-integral structure: the Z_2-odd modes
(sine functions) integrate to opposite sign relative to Z_2-even modes
(cosine functions) at the momentum-conserving KK level. Direct substitution:

$$f_{\mathrm{even}}(n) + f_{\mathrm{odd}}(n) = [d_0 + d_2(n/R)^2 + \cdots]
  + [-(d_0 + d_2(n/R)^2 + \cdots)] = 0$$

The sign flip applies to the *entire* contribution at level $n$ (all
mass-dependent corrections), because the sign is inherited from the
y-integral $I_{ijk}$ which multiplies the full integrand at each order
(lines 99--104).

### Verification for Gradient-Flow Context

The cancellation mechanism is *independent of the 4D loop momentum structure*.
The sign flip $f_{\mathrm{odd}}(n) = -f_{\mathrm{even}}(n)$ arises from the
y-integral on $S^1/\mathbb{Z}_2$, which factorizes from the 4D momentum
integration. For gradient-flow observables at flow time $t > 0$, the 4D
propagators acquire a flow-time regulator $e^{-t k^2}$, but this modifies
only the 4D loop momentum integral, not the y-integral that produces the
sign. Therefore:

1. The Z_2 parity cancellation persists at all $t \geq 0$.
2. It holds at $t = 0$ as well (no flow-time dependence in the sign).
3. The cancellation is independent of regularization scheme (Section 3.4
   confirms: dimensional regularization, symmetric cutoff, Z_2-paired
   Pauli-Villars, and zeta regularization all preserve the cancellation).

**Note on open sub-problems:** The source identifies two open gaps in the
Z_2 parity route: the DOF asymmetry gap (Section 3.5, lines 185--214) and
the mixed-sector vertex gap (Section 3.6, lines 216--247). These are gaps
in the Z_2 parity *route* specifically; they do not affect the validity of
Proposition 3.1 for the pure-sector cancellation, and they are separately
addressed by Theorem 1 (Lemma 3.6), which proves C_GS = 0 unconditionally
via the vertex mass-independence argument.

### Numerical Checks

From the source (lines 128--142): at every truncation $N$, the even and odd
partial sums cancel exactly (to machine precision), both with and without
mass-dependent weights. The cancellation is algebraic, not numerical.

## Memo 5: Lemma 3.6 --- Goroff-Sagnotti Coefficient Vanishes in All Schemes

### Statement

### Proof Chain (from the source)

**Step 1 (y-integral, lines 283--292):** Product-to-sum identities give the
closed form $I_{+++}(n_1, n_2, n_1+n_2) = \pi R/4$ for all $n_1, n_2 \geq 1$.
The coupling $g(n,m) = I_{+++}(n,m,n+m)/(\pi R)^{3/2} = 1/(4\sqrt{\pi R})$
is independent of $n$ and $m$. This is exact, not an approximation.

**Step 2 (leading UV coefficient, lines 294--302):** Under Lemma A1
(vertex mass-independence), the leading UV coefficient is $n,m$-independent.
The KK-summed pole coefficient is:

$$C_{\mathrm{GS}}^{\mathrm{leading}} = [1/(4\sqrt{\pi R})]^2 \cdot J(0,0)
  \cdot S_0^2$$

where $S_0 = 1 + 2\zeta_R(0) = 0$, giving $C_{\mathrm{GS}}^{\mathrm{leading}} = 0$.

**Step 3 (subleading corrections, lines 304--307):** Each subleading term
contributes a factor $E_2(-j; Q_2)$ for $j \geq 1$. By Theorem K.1
(Universal Epstein Vanishing), $E_2(-j; Q_2) = 0$ for all $j \geq 1$.
Therefore all subleading corrections vanish as well.

**Conclusion (lines 309--310):** $C_{\mathrm{GS}} = 0$ to all orders in
the UV expansion, for all KK levels, in any regularization scheme preserving
the Epstein structure.

**Supporting lemmas (all proved):**

- **Lemma A1** (lines 312--317): In de Donder gauge, the ~~5D~~ M⁵<!-- legacy 2026-04-15 Intervention 8b §0.10: bare "5D" → "M⁵" --> three-graviton
  vertex numerator introduces no $n$-dependent terms at leading UV order.
  $\partial_5$ contributions are either Z_2-forbidden, mass-suppressed, or
  killed by Epstein vanishing.

- **Lemma A2** (lines 241--256): Graviphoton $A_\mu^{(n)}$ and radion
  $\phi^{(n)}$ contribute only at dimension 8 or higher (by index structure
  and Z_2 selection rules).

- **Lemma A3** (lines 258--272): The internal KK loop sum on $S^1/\mathbb{Z}_2$
  runs over all $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ (by method of images), giving
  $S_0 = 1 + 2\zeta_R(0) = 0$.

### Verification for Gradient-Flow Context

Two-loop UV finiteness at $t = 0$ is scheme-independent. The gradient-flow
regularization introduces the flow-time parameter $t$ as a UV regulator.
The key point is:

1. At $t = 0$, the gradient-flow regulator is absent, and the UV structure
   reduces to the standard field-theoretic computation. The vanishing
   $C_{\mathrm{GS}} = 0$ then holds by Theorem 1.

2. At $t > 0$, the flow-time regulator $e^{-tk^2}$ suppresses UV modes
   exponentially, making the theory manifestly finite. The gradient flow
   does not introduce new UV divergences.

3. The scheme-independence of $C_{\mathrm{GS}} = 0$ (proved via the Weyl
   anomaly route in Section 4.5 and the Poisson bridge in Section 4.1--4.3)
   ensures that the result holds whether one uses zeta regularization,
   dimensional regularization, or gradient-flow regularization.

The Weyl anomaly argument (Section 4.4--4.5, Theorem 4.3) provides the
deepest route to scheme-independence: since the total Weyl anomaly
coefficients $a_{\mathrm{total}} = c_{\mathrm{total}} = 0$ are
cohomologically protected by the Wess-Zumino consistency condition, they
cannot be shifted by any finite local counterterm. This extends the
vanishing to all schemes preserving 4D diffeomorphism invariance and the
UV operator product algebra.
