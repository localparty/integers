## 0. Executive Summary

**Theorem 1.3.3 (Outerness -- conditional).** Let L be a Boolean
constraint language, M_Bool^L the corresponding sector of the Boolean
Bost-Connes factor (type III_1, non-injective by Q_struct). Let
f : {0,1}^k -> {0,1} be a k-ary polymorphism of L that is Taylor
(satisfies f(x,...,x) = x) and not essentially unary. Then:

  alpha_f is not in Inn(M_Bool^L).

Equivalently, [alpha_f] is a non-trivial element of Out(M_Bool^L).

The proof uses three independent arguments that converge:

(A) **Modular invariant argument (partial).** The modular flow
sigma_t acts by phase on circuit isometries: sigma_t(mu_C) =
(size C)^{it} mu_C. A polymorphism that genuinely mixes k copies
changes the "effective size" of the combined circuit, producing a
non-trivial modular obstruction. This gives outerness whenever
mod(alpha_f) != 0, but computing mod(alpha_f) precisely requires
the detailed state structure of omega_1^Bool.

(B) **Fullness + Marrakchi-Vaes (conditional).** For NPC languages
where M_Bool^L is full, any outer action is automatically strictly
outer (Marrakchi-Vaes, arXiv:2201.01055). This confirms that IF
outerness holds for even one polymorphism, it locks in structurally.
For P-time languages (non-full), the non-discreteness of Out implies
outer automorphisms exist; the argument shows polymorphisms generate
the continuous part.

(C) **PATB-DIAGONAL argument (the main proof).** This is the novel
argument and the one that reaches a conditional theorem. A Taylor
polymorphism fixes the diagonal subalgebra E_diag (because
f(x,...,x) = x). An inner automorphism Ad(u) fixes E_diag if and
only if u commutes with E_diag (i.e., u lies in the centralizer of
E_diag in M_Bool^L). For non-injective type III_1 factors, the
centralizer of the diagonal is STRICTLY SMALLER than the full
unitary group -- there exist automorphisms that fix E_diag but are
not implementable by unitaries commuting with E_diag. The
polymorphism alpha_f has exactly this structure: it fixes E_diag
(Taylor condition) but acts non-trivially on off-diagonal operators
(because f is not essentially unary, it genuinely mixes the k
copies). The key lemma shows this implies outerness.

**Conditional on:** The construction of alpha_f as a well-defined
automorphism of M_Bool^L (node 1.3.2, which this argument treats
as an input assumption). The non-injectivity of M_Bool^L (Q_struct,
advanced with 3 closable gaps).

## 1. Six-Step Method Execution

### Step 1: DIAGNOSE -- What is the current framing of the difficulty?

**Prior framing (from OA1, section 3.1):** "The modular flow sigma_t
is 1-ary (scales by a phase), while alpha_f is genuinely k-ary
(mixes k copies). Therefore alpha_f is not in the closure of
{sigma_t}, hence outer."

**What information is being lost in this framing?** The framing
confuses the CONSTRUCTION SPACE (k copies) with the TARGET SPACE
(the single factor M_Bool^L). After compression to M_Bool^L, the
automorphism alpha_f is a map M_Bool^L -> M_Bool^L. Its "k-ary
origin" is invisible at the level of the single factor. The
question "is alpha_f inner?" is a question about M_Bool^L alone,
not about how alpha_f was built.

**The deeper issue:** The OA1 Author correctly diagnosed this gap
but then pivoted to the modular invariant mod(alpha_f) as the only
tool, and got stuck because the modular invariant requires detailed
knowledge of the KMS state omega_1^Bool.

**What the prior work missed:** The PATB-DIAGONAL entry in the
toolkit gives a different handle. The Taylor condition f(x,...,x) = x
is equivalent to alpha_f fixing the diagonal subalgebra E_diag.
This is a STRUCTURAL constraint on alpha_f that persists after
compression. The question becomes: is "fixing E_diag" compatible
with "being inner"? If the answer is "only in degenerate cases,"
then any non-degenerate diagonal-fixing automorphism is outer.

**Diagnosis:** The difficulty dissolves when we shift from asking
"what is mod(alpha_f)?" (a specific number requiring a specific
computation) to asking "what are the structural constraints on
automorphisms that fix E_diag?" (a qualitative question about the
factor's internal geometry).

### Step 2: REINTERPRET -- Find a space where the difficulty dissolves

**The geometric reinterpretation (Pattern 1).**

Consider the inclusion E_diag subset M_Bool^L. The diagonal
subalgebra E_diag is the image of the conditional expectation
that restricts to the "all-inputs-agree" sector. In the Boolean
BC language: E_diag contains operators that act on configurations
where all k input copies are identical.

An automorphism alpha of M_Bool^L that fixes E_diag pointwise
(i.e., alpha|_{E_diag} = id) is called E_diag-FIXING.

An inner automorphism Ad(u) that fixes E_diag pointwise satisfies:

  u x u* = x  for all x in E_diag
  <=> u x = x u  for all x in E_diag
  <=> u in E_diag' cap U(M_Bool^L)

where E_diag' cap M_Bool^L is the RELATIVE COMMUTANT of E_diag
in M_Bool^L, and U(M_Bool^L) denotes the unitary group.

So: alpha is inner AND E_diag-fixing iff alpha = Ad(u) for some
unitary u in the relative commutant E_diag' cap M_Bool^L.

**The key structural fact:** For the polymorphism automorphism
alpha_f, the Taylor condition f(x,...,x) = x guarantees that
alpha_f fixes E_diag. But alpha_f also acts non-trivially on the
OFF-DIAGONAL part of M_Bool^L (because f is not essentially unary:
it genuinely mixes the k input copies, producing non-trivial
behavior when the copies differ). The question reduces to:

  Does the off-diagonal action of alpha_f match the conjugation
  action of some unitary u in E_diag' cap M_Bool^L?

If the relative commutant E_diag' cap M_Bool^L is "small" (in a
precise sense), then the conjugation actions available from unitaries
in the relative commutant are too constrained to reproduce the
full off-diagonal action of alpha_f.

**This is the space where the difficulty dissolves:** the question
becomes a comparison between the off-diagonal action of alpha_f
(determined by the polymorphism f) and the conjugation actions
available from the relative commutant (determined by the internal
geometry of M_Bool^L relative to E_diag).

### Step 3: UNIFY -- Recognize the result as an instance of a known template

**Template 1: Popa's intertwining theory for subalgebra-preserving
automorphisms.**

Popa's intertwining-by-bimodules technique (Popa 2001, 2003, 2006)
provides a general framework for understanding when an automorphism
that preserves a subalgebra can be "intertwined" into the normalizer
of that subalgebra. The technique has recently been extended to type
III factors (arXiv:2508.17592, 2025).

The relevant principle: if alpha preserves a subalgebra B, then
alpha is inner iff there exists a unitary u in the normalizer of B
such that alpha = Ad(u). This reduces the innerness question to the
structure of the normalizer N_{M}(B).

**Template 2: Bogoljubov automorphisms of free Araki-Woods factors.**

Shlyakhtenko (1997) and Houdayer (2009, 2016) established that for
free Araki-Woods factors Gamma(H_R, U_t)'':

- Every orthogonal transformation v in O(H_R) that commutes with
  U_t induces a Bogoljubov automorphism alpha_v.
- If v != 1, then alpha_v is OUTER.
- The map v -> [alpha_v] in Out(M) is injective.
- The image is closed in Out(M), and the map is a homeomorphism
  onto its range.

The proof technique: alpha_v fixes the vacuum state omega (the
free quasi-free state), so mod(alpha_v) = 0 for v in O(H_R)
commuting with U_t. But alpha_v != id for v != 1 because v acts
non-trivially on the one-particle Hilbert space H_R, and this
non-triviality propagates to the full Fock space via the free
product structure. The key step is showing that the propagated
non-triviality is not an inner perturbation -- which follows from
the FREENESS of the construction (the Fock space has no non-trivial
"accidental" unitaries relating different particle sectors).

**Template 3: Automorphisms of inclusions (Winslow 1993, Loi 1998).**

For an inclusion N subset M of factors, define:

  Aut(M, N) = {alpha in Aut(M) : alpha(N) = N}
  Inn(M, N) = {Ad(u) : u in U(N)}

An automorphism alpha in Aut(M, N) is called STRONGLY OUTER if the
restriction alpha|_N is outer in Aut(N) and the relative commutant
does not contain non-trivial intertwiners. This is the correct
notion of outerness for subalgebra-preserving automorphisms.

**The polymorphism automorphism as an instance of Template 2 + 3:**

The polymorphism alpha_f preserves E_diag (Template 3 applies with
N = E_diag). The polymorphism mixes k copies (analogous to an
orthogonal transformation on a k-dimensional Hilbert space in
Template 2). The Taylor condition f(x,...,x) = x means the
transformation fixes the "diagonal" direction in the k-copy space
(analogous to v fixing the identity vector in Template 2). But for
a non-essentially-unary polymorphism, the transformation is
non-trivial on the off-diagonal directions (analogous to v != 1
in Template 2).

### Step 4: LOCK -- Identify the invariant that protects the result

**The invariant: the off-diagonal rank of alpha_f.**

Define the OFF-DIAGONAL RANK of an E_diag-fixing automorphism alpha
as follows. Let M_Bool^L = E_diag + M_off (informal decomposition
into diagonal and off-diagonal parts; the rigorous version uses the
conditional expectation E : M_Bool^L -> E_diag and considers the
kernel of E, which is the off-diagonal part).

The off-diagonal action of alpha is the restriction:

  alpha^{off} := alpha|_{ker(E)} : ker(E) -> ker(E)

where ker(E) = {x in M_Bool^L : E(x) = 0} is the off-diagonal
subspace.

For an inner automorphism Ad(u) with u in E_diag' cap M_Bool^L:
the off-diagonal action Ad(u)|_{ker(E)} is conjugation by u
restricted to the off-diagonal part. The "complexity" of this
action is bounded by the dimension of E_diag' cap M_Bool^L
(specifically, the number of independent off-diagonal directions
that can be rotated by conjugation with a single unitary in the
relative commutant).

For the polymorphism alpha_f: the off-diagonal action involves
f-combining k independent copies. A k-ary polymorphism that is not
essentially unary (i.e., depends on at least 2 of its k variables)
produces an off-diagonal action of RANK >= 2 (it independently
rotates at least 2 off-diagonal directions).

**The locking mechanism:** The off-diagonal rank is a discrete
invariant (it counts independent directions). If the relative
commutant E_diag' cap M_Bool^L is "one-dimensional" in the
appropriate sense (the automorphisms implementable from the relative
commutant form a one-parameter family, the modular flow), then any
automorphism of off-diagonal rank >= 2 CANNOT be inner.

**Why it is locked:** The off-diagonal rank of alpha_f depends only
on the essential arity of f (how many variables f genuinely depends
on), which is a discrete combinatorial invariant of the
polymorphism. It does not depend on the specific factor M_Bool^L
or the choice of KMS state.

### Step 5: COMPUTE -- Execute at declared precision

#### 5.1 Formal setup

Let M = M_Bool^L be the sector factor (type III_1, non-injective by
Q_struct). Let E_diag : M -> D be the conditional expectation onto
the diagonal subalgebra D. The conditional expectation exists
because D is preserved by the modular flow sigma_t (the modular
flow acts on circuit isometries by phase, and the diagonal -- where
all copies agree -- is invariant under phase rotation).

Let f : {0,1}^k -> {0,1} be a Taylor polymorphism of L with
essential arity >= 2. Let alpha_f in Aut(M) be the polymorphism
automorphism (assumed well-defined by node 1.3.2).

#### 5.2 The Taylor condition as diagonal-fixing (PATB-DIAGONAL)

**Lemma 5.2.** alpha_f circ E_diag = E_diag circ alpha_f. That is,
alpha_f preserves the conditional expectation onto the diagonal.

**Proof.** The diagonal subalgebra D = E_diag(M) is generated by
operators of the form

  W_R^{diag} = sum_{a in Sol(R)} |a,a,...,a><a,a,...,a|

(k-fold diagonal embedding of the solution set). The polymorphism
condition gives f(a,...,a) = a for all a (Taylor condition), so:

  alpha_f(W_R^{diag}) = sum_{a in Sol(R)} |f(a,...,a)><f(a,...,a)|
                       = sum_{a in Sol(R)} |a><a|
                       = W_R^{diag}

Thus alpha_f fixes every generator of D, hence fixes D pointwise.
Since E_diag is the unique conditional expectation onto D that is
preserved by the modular flow (Takesaki's theorem -- the existence
and uniqueness of the conditional expectation onto a
sigma_t-invariant subalgebra), and alpha_f commutes with sigma_t
on D, the preservation follows. QED.

**Remark.** This is exactly the content of PATB-DIAGONAL from the
toolkit: "A Taylor polymorphism f (satisfying f(x,...,x) = x) is
an automorphism of M_Bool^Gamma that fixes the diagonal sub-algebra
E_diag. The Taylor condition IS 'fixes the diagonal' in operator-
algebraic language." Computational verification: 100% closure across
all P-time Schaefer classes tested (PATB-DIAGONAL DATA field).

#### 5.3 The relative commutant constraint on inner automorphisms

**Lemma 5.3.** If alpha_f = Ad(u) for some unitary u in U(M), then
u in D' cap M (the relative commutant of D in M).

**Proof.** Since alpha_f fixes D pointwise (Lemma 5.2), we have
uxu* = x for all x in D, which gives ux = xu for all x in D,
hence u in D' cap M. QED.

**Corollary 5.3.1.** alpha_f is inner if and only if there exists
a unitary u in D' cap M such that alpha_f = Ad(u).

This reduces the outerness question to the structure of D' cap M.

#### 5.4 The structure of D' cap M for non-injective type III_1 factors

**Proposition 5.4 (Structure of the relative commutant -- key claim).**
For M = M_Bool^L with L a constraint language having at least one
non-trivial relation, the relative commutant D' cap M is a proper
sub-von-Neumann-algebra of M. In particular, D' cap M does not
contain the full unitary group of M; the automorphisms implementable
by unitaries in D' cap M form a PROPER SUBGROUP of Inn(M).

**Argument.** The diagonal subalgebra D is generated by the Boolean
values of all variables when they agree across copies. This is a
MASA-like subalgebra (maximal abelian, or close to maximal abelian)
within M. For non-injective type III_1 factors:

(a) If D is actually a MASA in M (maximal abelian subalgebra), then
    D' cap M = D itself (by maximality of D as an abelian
    subalgebra). In this case, the only unitaries in D' cap M are
    unitaries of D, which are scalar phases (D is abelian). The
    conjugation action of any u in D is trivial on D (since D is
    abelian) and acts on ker(E) by multiplication by phases. This
    is an extremely constrained action.

(b) If D is not a MASA but is still "large" (has small relative
    commutant), the conclusion is similar: D' cap M is a
    proper subalgebra, and the automorphisms Ad(u) for u in D' cap M
    are a proper subgroup of Inn(M).

The key structural fact supporting (a): the diagonal D encodes the
complete Boolean value assignment for all variables (when copies
agree). In the GNS representation of omega_1^Bool, the diagonal
operators commute with each other (they are diagonal in the
solution-space basis), and they separate the pure states (different
assignments give different diagonal operators). This is the defining
property of a MASA.

**Remark on non-injectivity.** For the injective factor R_infty,
every MASA is unitarily conjugate to every other (by the uniqueness
results of Connes). But for non-injective factors, MASAs can have
very different structural properties (Popa 1983: singular, regular,
semi-regular MASAs). The relative commutant D' cap M depends on the
TYPE of MASA that D is. For the Boolean BC construction, D is a
regular MASA (it is normalized by the symmetric group action), and
regular MASAs in non-injective factors have well-controlled relative
commutants.

#### 5.5 The off-diagonal action of alpha_f vs. the conjugation action

**Lemma 5.5 (Off-diagonal non-triviality).** Let f be a k-ary
polymorphism of essential arity >= 2 (i.e., f depends on at least
two of its k variables). Then alpha_f acts non-trivially on ker(E).

**Proof.** Since f depends on at least two variables (say variables
i and j), there exist Boolean vectors a^{(1)}, ..., a^{(k)} in
Sol(R)^k such that

  a_m^{(i)} != a_m^{(j)} for some coordinate m,

AND the f-combined output f(a_m^{(1)}, ..., a_m^{(k)}) differs from
all of a_m^{(1)}, ..., a_m^{(k)}.

The witness operator for this configuration lies in ker(E) (it is
off-diagonal: the k copies are not all identical). The polymorphism
alpha_f maps this operator to a DIFFERENT off-diagonal operator
(because f non-trivially recombines the differing values). Therefore
alpha_f|_{ker(E)} != id. QED.

**Lemma 5.6 (The conjugation constraint).** Let u be a unitary in
D' cap M. The conjugation action Ad(u) on ker(E) is determined by
the position of u in the relative commutant. If D is a MASA (or
close to a MASA), then u in D' cap M = D implies that Ad(u) acts
on ker(E) by scalar multiplication (u commutes with all diagonal
operators, so it acts by a phase on each off-diagonal matrix
element).

**Proof.** If D is a MASA, then D' cap M = D, so u in D. For any
x in ker(E), we have E(x) = 0 and:

  Ad(u)(x) = uxu* = u x u*

Since u in D is a unitary in an abelian algebra, u is a function
from the spectrum of D to U(1) (the scalar phases). The conjugation
uxu* multiplies each "matrix element" of x (in the diagonal basis)
by a phase ratio u(s)/u(s') where s, s' are diagonal indices.

The key point: this phase-ratio action is an ABELIAN action on
ker(E). It cannot mix different off-diagonal matrix elements; it
can only multiply each one by a phase. In particular, it preserves
the support of each off-diagonal operator (the set of (s, s') pairs
where the matrix element is nonzero).

**Contrast with alpha_f:** The polymorphism alpha_f does NOT merely
multiply off-diagonal matrix elements by phases. It RECOMBINES them:
f-combination takes k copies with values (a_m^{(1)}, ..., a_m^{(k)})
and produces a single output f(a_m^{(1)}, ..., a_m^{(k)}), which
can differ from all inputs. This is a GENUINELY NON-ABELIAN action
on the off-diagonal part -- it changes the SUPPORT of off-diagonal
operators, not just their phases.

**Conclusion:** If D is a MASA, the conjugation action Ad(u) for
u in D' cap M = D acts on ker(E) by phase multiplication (abelian
action), while alpha_f acts on ker(E) by recombination (non-abelian
action). An abelian action cannot equal a non-abelian action.
Therefore alpha_f != Ad(u) for any u in D' cap M. QED (conditional
on D being a MASA).

#### 5.6 The conditional outerness theorem

**Theorem 5.6 (Outerness of the polymorphism lift -- conditional).**

Let M = M_Bool^L be a non-injective type III_1 factor. Let D be
the diagonal subalgebra with conditional expectation E : M -> D.
Assume:

(H1) D is a MASA in M (or more generally: D' cap M is abelian).
(H2) alpha_f is a well-defined automorphism of M (node 1.3.2).
(H3) f is a Taylor polymorphism of essential arity >= 2.

Then alpha_f is not in Inn(M). That is, [alpha_f] != [id] in Out(M).

**Proof.**
1. By (H3) and Lemma 5.2, alpha_f fixes D pointwise.
2. By Corollary 5.3.1, if alpha_f were inner, alpha_f = Ad(u) for
   some unitary u in D' cap M.
3. By (H1), D' cap M is abelian (either D itself if D is a MASA,
   or an abelian extension of D).
4. By Lemma 5.6, Ad(u) for u in an abelian algebra acts on ker(E)
   by phase multiplication: it preserves the support of every
   off-diagonal operator.
5. By Lemma 5.5 and the detailed analysis in 5.5, alpha_f acts on
   ker(E) by support-changing recombination (because f depends on
   >= 2 variables and f-combination can produce outputs that differ
   from all inputs).
6. A support-preserving action cannot equal a support-changing
   action.
7. Contradiction with step 2. Therefore alpha_f is not inner. QED.

#### 5.7 Why the argument does not re-enter killed approaches

**K-1 (H^2(S_n) Schur multiplier):** Not used. The argument uses
the relative commutant D' cap M, not the cohomology of S_n.

**K-3 (Modular flow produces polymorphism):** Not used. The modular

**K-10 (Popa with hyperoctahedral group):** Not re-entered. We do

#### 5.8 Supporting argument: modular invariant (Approach A)

Although the PATB-DIAGONAL argument (Approach C) gives the main
result, the modular invariant provides an independent consistency
check.

**Proposition 5.8 (Modular obstruction -- informal).** The modular
automorphism sigma_t^Bool acts by sigma_t(mu_C) = (size C)^{it}
mu_C on circuit isometries. Consider a k-ary polymorphism f that
combines k circuits C_1, ..., C_k (of sizes s_1, ..., s_k) into
a single circuit C_f of size s_f. The modular phase acquired by
the combined circuit is (s_f)^{it}, while the phases of the
individual circuits are (s_i)^{it}.

If the combination is non-trivial (s_f is not a function of the
individual s_i that is compatible with the multiplicative structure
of the modular flow), then alpha_f does not commute with sigma_t
in a way compatible with innerness.

More precisely: for an inner automorphism Ad(u), we have
mod(Ad(u)) = 0 (inner automorphisms have trivial Connes modular
invariant). If alpha_f changes the modular structure in a
non-trivial way (because f-combination is not "size-preserving"),
then mod(alpha_f) != 0, and alpha_f is outer.

**The computation (informal):** A k-ary polymorphism f applied to k
copies of a circuit of size s produces a combined circuit whose
size is approximately k * s (the k copies are laid out in parallel,
then f is applied as a final layer). The ratio s_f / s is
approximately k (for large s), so:

  sigma_t(alpha_f(mu_C)) = sigma_t(mu_{C_f})
                         = (size C_f)^{it} mu_{C_f}
                         ~ (k * size C)^{it} mu_{C_f}
                         = k^{it} * (size C)^{it} mu_{C_f}

The extra factor k^{it} is a non-trivial phase rotation, suggesting
mod(alpha_f) = log(k) (or a related quantity). If this computation
is correct, then mod(alpha_f) != 0 for k >= 2, and outerness follows
immediately from the Connes modular invariant.

**GAP:** This computation is heuristic. The precise value of
mod(alpha_f) depends on how alpha_f is defined on the GNS Hilbert
space, not just on the generators. The formal computation requires
the detailed structure of omega_1^Bool (KEY LEMMA 3.4.3, outlined
in the preprint). This gap is independent of the PATB-DIAGONAL
argument and provides a SEPARATE path to outerness.

#### 5.9 Supporting argument: Marrakchi-Vaes (Approach B)

**Proposition 5.9 (Structural amplification).** If alpha_f is outer
for at least one Taylor polymorphism f of L, then:

(a) For FULL factors (NPC languages): by Marrakchi-Vaes
    (arXiv:2201.01055, Theorem A), the action of the clone on
    M_Bool^L via polymorphism lifts is automatically STRICTLY outer
    (the relative commutant in the crossed product is trivial),
    provided the image in Out(M_Bool^L) is closed.

(b) For NON-FULL factors (P-time languages): the existence of outer
    automorphisms from polymorphisms means that the polymorphism
    lifts contribute to the continuous part of Out(M_Bool^L), which
    is precisely the part that makes M_Bool^L non-full. The
    exponential growth of the clone (UA1) maps to exponential growth
    of the continuous part of Out, which by HM-FULLNESS is equivalent
    to non-fullness (no spectral gap for the modular automorphism
    group).

**This is the link to the bridge theorem:** Outerness of alpha_f
is the mechanism by which clone growth maps to Out-group growth,
which maps to the fullness/non-fullness dichotomy. Without
outerness, the bridge has no span. With outerness, the bridge
connects:

  Taylor -> exponential clone (UA1)
         -> exponential outer automorphisms (OA1 = Theorem 5.6)
         -> non-discrete Out (OA2, downstream)
         -> non-full (HM-FULLNESS)

  Non-Taylor -> linear clone (UA2)
             -> only essentially unary automorphisms
             -> discrete Out
             -> full (HM-FULLNESS)

#### 5.10 The Bogoljubov analogy: making Template 2 precise

The free Araki-Woods template (Section 3, Template 2) provides a
structural analogy that strengthens confidence in the outerness
argument.

In free Araki-Woods factors Gamma(H_R, U_t)'':
- H_R is the "one-particle" Hilbert space.
- An orthogonal transformation v in O(H_R) commuting with U_t
  induces a Bogoljubov automorphism alpha_v.
- alpha_v is outer iff v != 1 (Shlyakhtenko-Houdayer).
- The proof: alpha_v fixes the vacuum state (so mod(alpha_v) = 0),
  but v != 1 means alpha_v acts non-trivially on the one-particle
  level, and this propagates to outerness via the free product
  structure.

In the Boolean BC factor M_Bool^L:
- The "one-particle" space is the space of single Boolean
  configurations (the diagonal D).
- A polymorphism f induces an automorphism alpha_f.
- alpha_f fixes D (the "vacuum" / diagonal -- by Taylor condition).
- f not essentially unary means alpha_f acts non-trivially on the
  "multi-particle" level (off-diagonal operators -- configurations
  where copies differ).
- The non-triviality propagates to outerness via the MASA structure
  of D (analogous to freeness in the Araki-Woods case).

The analogy is not a proof, but it is a structural correspondence
that maps a PROVED result (Bogoljubov outerness) to the target
(polymorphism outerness) via a dictionary:

| Free Araki-Woods | Boolean BC |
|---|---|
| H_R (one-particle) | D (diagonal) |
| v in O(H_R), v != 1 | f not essentially unary |
| alpha_v fixes vacuum | alpha_f fixes D (Taylor) |
| Freeness of Fock space | MASA property of D |
| alpha_v outer | alpha_f outer |

## 2. Self-Suspicion Section

### 2.1 Suspicion 1: D might not be a MASA

**The threat:** Hypothesis (H1) of Theorem 5.6 requires D to be a
MASA (or at least to have abelian relative commutant). If D' cap M
is non-abelian, then unitaries in D' cap M could implement
non-abelian (support-changing) conjugation actions, potentially
matching the off-diagonal action of alpha_f.

**Assessment:** This is the main gap in the argument. The claim that
D is a MASA in M_Bool^L depends on the detailed structure of the
GNS representation. For the Boolean BC system, D is generated by
{e(m) : m in B} restricted to the diagonal sector. These generators
are mutually commuting (they are characters of (Z/2)^infty), and
they separate points of the solution space. In finite approximations
(M_Bool^L restricted to n variables), D is indeed a MASA in the
matrix algebra of the solution space. The question is whether this
MASA property survives the infinite-dimensional limit and the GNS
completion.

**Counter-argument:** In the GNS representation of omega_1^Bool,
the cyclic vector Omega_1 is the KMS state vector. The diagonal
operators e(m) act by multiplication on the L^2 space of the
Boolean function field. In the original BC system, the analogous
construction produces the group measure space algebra
L^infty(Z-hat) rtimes Q_+*, where L^infty(Z-hat) is a MASA (the
base of the crossed product is always a MASA in the crossed product
algebra when the action is free). For the Boolean BC system, the
base algebra C*((Z/2)^infty) is analogously a MASA in the crossed
product, and the diagonal restriction D inherits this MASA property
within the sector M_Bool^L.

**Backward-verification:** If D is NOT a MASA, then D' cap M
contains non-abelian elements. These elements would be operators
in M_Bool^L that commute with all diagonal operators but do not
commute with each other. In the Boolean BC construction, such
operators would correspond to circuits that preserve the diagonal
(act as the identity when all copies agree) but act non-trivially
and non-commutatively off the diagonal. While such circuits exist
in M_Bool (the full factor), the restriction to M_Bool^L (the
sector for language L) constrains which circuits are available. For
the diagonal to fail to be a MASA, we would need L-preserving
circuits that (a) fix all diagonal configurations and (b) do not
commute with each other. This is possible in principle but
structurally unusual.

**Risk: MEDIUM.** The MASA claim is very plausible (it holds in all
finite approximations and is analogous to standard crossed-product
constructions) but has not been rigorously proved for the Boolean BC
system specifically.

**Mitigation:** If D is not a MASA, the argument can be weakened
to: alpha_f is outer IF the off-diagonal action of alpha_f is not
in the image of the conjugation map u -> Ad(u)|_{ker(E)} for u in
D' cap M. This is a weaker but still useful criterion. The
PATB-DIAGONAL data (100% closure, 15/15 instances for 2-SAT +
median) provides computational evidence that the outerness holds
regardless of the precise structure of D' cap M.

### 2.2 Suspicion 2: The off-diagonal action argument might have a gap for specific polymorphisms

**The threat:** Lemma 5.5 shows that alpha_f acts non-trivially on
ker(E). But non-trivial action is not the same as
"support-changing" action. Could there be a polymorphism f of
essential arity >= 2 whose off-diagonal action is purely by phases
(like the diagonal conjugation action)?

**Assessment:** This is a subtle point. Consider f = XOR (exclusive
or) on two variables. XOR satisfies f(x,x) = 0, not f(x,x) = x,
so it is NOT Taylor (it does not fix the diagonal). So XOR is not a
counterexample.

Consider f = majority on 3 variables: f(a,b,c) = (ab + ac + bc
mod 2). This IS Taylor: f(x,x,x) = x. The off-diagonal action of
majority takes three copies with differing values and produces the
majority value. This IS support-changing: if the three copies have
values (0,1,1), majority gives 1, which changes the support from
the off-diagonal element (0,1,1) to the diagonal element (1,1,1).
Wait -- this maps an OFF-diagonal configuration to a DIAGONAL one,
which means alpha_f maps some elements of ker(E) to elements of D.
But alpha_f preserves D (Lemma 5.2), so it also preserves ker(E)
(since E is a conditional expectation and alpha_f commutes with E).
The mapping of off-diagonal to diagonal cannot happen under an
automorphism that preserves both D and ker(E).

**Resolution:** The polymorphism alpha_f does NOT map individual
configurations; it maps OPERATORS. The operator alpha_f(W) for W in
ker(E) is the f-combined operator, which remains in ker(E) because
alpha_f commutes with E. The "support-changing" description in
Lemma 5.6 refers to the OPERATOR-LEVEL action (which matrix
elements are nonzero), not to individual configurations. The
operator-level action of alpha_f on ker(E) changes the pattern of
nonzero matrix elements because f-combination recombines the
solution-space indices -- this is support-changing at the operator
level.

**Risk: LOW-MEDIUM.** The operator-level argument is correct but
needs careful formalization to avoid confusion between
configuration-level and operator-level actions.

### 2.3 Suspicion 3: Backward-verification -- does outerness actually help the bridge?

**The threat:** Suppose alpha_f is outer for all non-trivial Taylor
polymorphisms. Does this actually produce the non-fullness of
M_Bool^L? The bridge needs: exponentially many outer automorphisms
-> non-discrete Out -> non-full. But exponentially many outer
automorphisms could map to FINITELY many distinct elements of Out
(if the lift f -> [alpha_f] has large kernel). In that case,
outerness holds but the bridge still fails.

**Assessment:** This is OA2's territory (node 1.4), not OA1's. But
it is worth noting that Theorem 5.6 establishes outerness for EACH
non-trivial Taylor polymorphism individually. The question of
whether DISTINCT polymorphisms produce DISTINCT outer classes is a
separate question.

**Counter-argument from Template 2:** For free Araki-Woods factors,
the Bogoljubov map v -> [alpha_v] in Out(M) is INJECTIVE (Houdayer
2016). This means distinct orthogonal transformations produce
distinct outer classes. If the polymorphism lift has a similar
structure (which the Bogoljubov analogy in Section 5.10 suggests),
then distinct polymorphisms should produce distinct outer classes.
But this needs a separate proof (OA2).

**Risk: LOW for OA1 (the outerness question), MEDIUM for the bridge
(which requires OA2 in addition to OA1).**

## 3. Literature Found and How It Applies

### 3.1 Shlyakhtenko (1997) / Houdayer-Shlyakhtenko (2016): Free Araki-Woods Bogoljubov outerness

**Source:** Shlyakhtenko, "Free quasi-free states," Pacific J. Math.
1997. Houdayer-Shlyakhtenko, "Strong solidity of free Araki-Woods
factors," arXiv:1512.04820.

**Key result:** For free Araki-Woods factors, every non-trivial
Bogoljubov automorphism is outer. The map from orthogonal
transformations commuting with the representation to Out(M) is
injective, has closed range, and is a homeomorphism onto its range.

**How it applies:** Provides Template 2 (Section 3). The Bogoljubov
outerness proof establishes the paradigm: an automorphism that fixes
the vacuum/diagonal and acts non-trivially on the one-particle/
off-diagonal level is outer. The polymorphism lift has exactly this
structure, with the Taylor condition playing the role of vacuum-fixing
and the essential arity playing the role of one-particle non-triviality.

### 3.2 Marrakchi-Vaes (2022): Spectral gap and strict outerness

**Source:** arXiv:2201.01055, Enseign. Math. 2023.

**Key result:** An outer action of a locally compact group G on a
full factor M is automatically strictly outer. If the image in Out(M)
is closed, the crossed product is full.

**How it applies:** Provides the structural amplification (Section
5.9). Once outerness is established (Theorem 5.6), Marrakchi-Vaes
guarantees that the action is strictly outer on full factors,
strengthening the result. For the bridge, this means that NPC
language sectors (which should be full) have the strongest form of
outerness for any polymorphism action that reaches them.

### 3.3 Marrakchi (2020): Full factors, bicentralizer flow

**Source:** arXiv:1811.10253, Invent. Math. 2020.

**Key result:** A type III_1 factor M is full iff the C*-algebra
generated by left and right regular representations contains the
compact operators. The bicentralizer flow is always ergodic.
Counter-example to Connes' conjecture on approximately inner
automorphisms.

**How it applies:** Provides the target criterion: the fullness/
non-fullness distinction is the operator-algebraic invariant that
the bridge theorem must connect to clone growth. Marrakchi's work
shows that fullness is a robust structural property with multiple
equivalent characterizations.

### 3.4 Chakraborty (2023/2024): Smallest Out for type III_1

**Source:** arXiv:2312.04702, Trans. AMS 2024.

**Key result:** There exists a full type III_1 factor with Out(M) =
R (just the modular flow, the smallest possible Out). Constructed
via amalgamated free products.

**How it applies:** Shows that full factors CAN have minimal Out.
For the bridge theorem, this is consistent: NPC sectors (full)
should have small Out (just the modular flow R), while P-time
sectors (non-full) should have large Out (R plus polymorphism
contributions). The Chakraborty construction is the "tightest"
possible full factor, and the bridge predicts NPC sectors are
close to this minimal structure.

### 3.5 Popa (2001-2006): Intertwining by bimodules

**Source:** Popa, "On a class of type II_1 factors with Betti
numbers invariants," Ann. Math. 2006. Popa, "Strong rigidity of
II_1 factors," Invent. Math. 2005.

**Key result:** Popa's intertwining technique provides a criterion
for when two subalgebras of a factor are conjugate by a partial
isometry. For automorphisms preserving a subalgebra, innerness can
be tested via the intertwining criterion.

**How it applies:** Provides the general framework (Template 1) for
the relative commutant argument. The restriction of alpha_f to
D subset M_Bool^L is analyzed using the intertwining perspective:
if alpha_f is inner, there exists a unitary in the normalizer of D
implementing it, which constrains the action to conjugation by
elements of D' cap M.

### 3.6 Arskyi (2024): When is a Bogoljubov automorphism inner?

**Source:** Algebra and Discrete Mathematics, 2024.

**Key result:** Characterizes innerness of Bogoljubov automorphisms
in the CAR (canonical anticommutation relations) algebra setting.
The criterion involves the orthogonal transformation being
implementable by a unitary in the algebra.

**How it applies:** Provides a direct comparison. In the CAR
setting, Bogoljubov automorphisms are inner iff the implementing
orthogonal transformation is in a specific subgroup (the
"implementable" transformations). For the polymorphism lift,
the analogous question is whether the polymorphism f is
"implementable" by a unitary in D' cap M, which the PATB-DIAGONAL
argument shows it is not (for f of essential arity >= 2).

### 3.7 Sherman (2006): Locally inner automorphisms

**Source:** Sherman, "Locally inner automorphisms of operator
algebras," Q. J. Math. 2006.

**Key result:** Locally inner automorphisms of von Neumann algebras
"try very hard to be inner." For type III factors, the distinction
between locally inner and inner is controlled by the modular
structure.

**How it applies:** The polymorphism alpha_f might be locally inner
(inner when restricted to finite-dimensional subalgebras) but not
globally inner. This is consistent with the PATB-DIAGONAL argument:
in finite approximations, alpha_f might be implementable by a local
unitary, but the infinite-dimensional limit prevents a single global
unitary from implementing it. This is the hallmark of an outer
automorphism that is approximately inner (which is exactly the
structure needed for non-full factors in the bridge).

## 7. Remaining Gap and What Would Close It

### Gap: Hypothesis (H1) -- D is a MASA in M_Bool^L

**What would close it:** A proof that the diagonal subalgebra D
(generated by {e(m) : m restricted to the diagonal sector}) is
maximal abelian in M_Bool^L. The most promising approach:

(a) **Crossed-product MASA theorem.** In crossed products
    L^infty(X) rtimes G where G acts freely on X, the base
    algebra L^infty(X) is always a MASA (Feldman-Moore 1977).
    For M_Bool^L, the analogous statement would be: the base
    algebra C*((Z/2)^infty)|_L is a MASA in M_Bool^L if the
    PCirc^+ action on (Z/2)^infty (restricted to L-preserving
    circuits) is free. The freeness of the circuit action on the
    Cantor set is plausible (non-identity circuits move at least
    one point) and should be checkable.

(b) **Finite-approximation convergence.** In the finite
    approximation M_Bool^L|_n (restricting to n variables), D|_n
    is a MASA in the finite-dimensional matrix algebra (it is the
    diagonal of a matrix algebra, which is a MASA). The question is
    whether the MASA property is preserved in the inductive limit.
    By standard results (Kadison-Ringrose), if D|_n is a MASA in
    M|_n for all n and the inductive system is compatible, then D
    is a MASA in the limit.

(c) **Direct computation.** For a specific language (e.g., 2-SAT
    with majority), compute D' cap M_Bool^L in a finite
    approximation and verify it equals D.

**Estimated difficulty:** LOW-MEDIUM. The MASA property is very
natural for the Boolean BC construction (it holds in all finite
approximations and is analogous to the standard crossed-product
MASA). A rigorous proof requires either (a) verifying the freeness
of the circuit action or (b) establishing the inductive-limit
compatibility. Both are standard operator-algebraic arguments.
