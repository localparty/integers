## 2. Route A: CAT(0) Cube Complex Action -- BLOCKED

### 2.1 The idea

Construct a finite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complex on which G_L acts
properly. If such a complex exists, G_L would be bi-exact by
Chatterji-Niblo (2004) / Ozawa (2004, Theorem 15).

The constraint structure of L (clause-variable incidence graph, solution
space hypergraph) provides natural candidates for such a complex.

### 2.2 The obstruction (Genevois 2018)

**Theorem (Genevois, J. Group Theory 2019, arXiv:1804.01791).**
Thompson's group V has property (FW_infty): every isometric action of V
on a FINITE-DIMENSIONAL CAT(0) cube complex has a global fixed point.

In particular, V does NOT act properly on any finite-dimensional CAT(0)
cube complex. (It does act properly on an infinite-dimensional one --
this is Farley's 2003 result.)

**Why this blocks Route A.** V is a subgroup of G_Bool (Q_STRUCT Claim
5.1). If G_L contained V (which it may, for some NPC languages), then
G_L would also have property (FW_infty) and could not act properly on a
finite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complex.

Even if G_L does not contain V, the Genevois result shows that the
"natural" route to bi-exactness via finite-dimensional CAT(0) cube
complexes is dangerous for groups related to homeomorphisms of the
Cantor set. G_L acts on the Cantor set {0,1}^infty by polynomial-time
homeomorphisms, which puts it in the same geometric family as V.

**More fundamentally:** The clause-variable incidence graph of a CSP
instance has dimension O(n) (the maximum degree of the hypergraph),
which grows with the instance size. In the inductive limit over all
instances, the natural cube complex would be infinite-dimensional.
This matches Farley's result: Thompson-like groups need
infinite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complexes.

**Verdict: Route A is BLOCKED.** The finite-dimensional CAT(0) route
does not work for groups acting on the Cantor set.

## 3. Route B: Linear Group Embedding -- UNCERTAIN

### 3.1 The idea

If G_L embeds in GL_n(K) for some n and some field K, then G_L is exact
(Guentner-Higson-Weinberger 2005). Combined with boundary amenability,
this could give bi-exactness.

### 3.2 Assessment

G_L is generated by polynomial-time reversible circuits acting on
{0,1}^n for all n. At each fixed n, the group of reversible circuits
on {0,1}^n is a subgroup of S_{2^n} (the symmetric group on 2^n
elements), which is a finite group and hence trivially embeds in
GL_{2^n}(Z). But the embedding dimension grows exponentially with n.

In the inductive limit n -> infty, G_L is an inductive limit of finite
groups embedded in GL_{2^n}(Z) with embedding dimension going to
infinity. An inductive limit of linear groups of unbounded dimension
need not itself be linear. In fact, Thompson's V is NOT a linear group
(proved by Margulis-Soifer, using the fact that V contains every finite
group as a subgroup, which prevents faithful finite-dimensional
representations over any field).

Since V embeds in G_Bool, and G_L may contain V for some NPC languages,
G_L may also fail to be linear.

**Verdict: Route B is UNCERTAIN and unlikely to close.** The linear
embedding route faces the same obstacle as V: these groups are too
large for finite-dimensional linear representations.

## 4. Route C: Weakened Hypothesis -- THE PRIMARY REPAIR

### 4.1 The key insight

The original argument in Node 1.3.5.5 used:

    Houdayer-Isono (2016): bi-exact G + strongly ergodic action on
    amenable base ==> crossed product is full.

But Houdayer-Isono is NOT the only theorem that produces fullness of
crossed products. A more recent result by Marrakchi (2018) provides
fullness under DIFFERENT (and in our case, easier) hypotheses.

### 4.2 The replacement theorem

**Theorem (Marrakchi, arXiv:1811.08274, 2018).** Let M be a full
factor and let sigma : Gamma --> Aut(M) be an action of a discrete
group Gamma. If Gamma is non-inner amenable, then the crossed product
M rtimes_sigma Gamma is full.

**Remark on scope.** Marrakchi's theorem applies when the BASE is a
full factor. Houdayer-Isono's theorem applies when the base is AMENABLE
(and the group is bi-exact + action strongly ergodic). These are
different settings. Our application needs careful setup to match
Marrakchi's hypotheses.

### 4.3 Can we apply Marrakchi to M_Bool^L?

The naive application fails because the base A|_L is ABELIAN (hence
amenable, NOT a non-trivial full factor). Marrakchi requires M to be a
full factor, not an abelian algebra.

**However, we can restructure the decomposition.** Instead of:

    M_Bool^L = A|_L rtimes G_L     (abelian base, full group)

we can consider a TWO-STEP decomposition. Let H be a normal subgroup
of G_L (to be specified). Then:

    M_Bool^L = (A|_L rtimes H) rtimes (G_L / H)

If we can choose H such that:
(a) A|_L rtimes H is already a full factor, and
(b) G_L / H is non-inner amenable,

then Marrakchi's theorem gives fullness of M_Bool^L.

### 4.4 The correct application: non-inner amenability of G_L directly

There is a more direct application that avoids the two-step
decomposition entirely.

**Key observation.** Marrakchi's paper (arXiv:1811.08274) also proves a
more general result for group measure space constructions. Specifically,
combining his Theorem A with the Jones-Schmidt characterization:

**Theorem (Marrakchi 2018, Corollary of Theorem A + B).** Let Gamma be
a non-inner amenable ICC group acting ergodically on a standard
probability space (X, mu). Then L^infty(X) rtimes Gamma is a full
factor.

This applies directly to our setting with:
- Gamma = G_L (the group generated by L-preserving circuits)
- X = the Cantor set {0,1}^infty with the KMS measure
- L^infty(X) = A|_L (the abelian base)

The hypotheses we need to verify are:
1. G_L is non-inner amenable.
2. G_L is ICC (infinite conjugacy class).
3. The action is ergodic.

**But wait -- we need to be precise about the full theorem statement.**
Marrakchi's fullness result for crossed products over amenable bases
(the group measure space case) actually requires more than just
non-inner amenability when the base is abelian. Let me state the
precise result:

**Theorem (Marrakchi 2018, Theorem B).** Let Gamma curvearrowright
(X, mu) be a nonsingular ergodic essentially free action of a
countable group on a standard probability space. The following are
equivalent:
(i) L^infty(X) rtimes Gamma is full.
(ii) The equivalence relation R_{Gamma curvearrowright X} is strongly
     ergodic.

And by Schmidt (1980) / Jones-Schmidt (1987), strong ergodicity of the
equivalence relation is guaranteed when:
- Gamma is non-amenable, and
- the action is essentially free, and
- the equivalence relation has no amenable normal subrelation that
  "absorbs" approximate invariance.

**The decisive theorem.** For the group measure space case (abelian
base), the correct chain is:

    G_L non-inner amenable + ICC
    ==> L(G_L) is a full II_1 factor (Effros 1975 + Connes 1976)
    ==> L(G_L) does not have property Gamma

Combined with:

    G_L non-amenable + essentially free ergodic action + trivial
    amenable radical
    ==> action is strongly ergodic (Jones-Schmidt 1987)
    ==> L^infty(X) rtimes G_L is full (Marrakchi 2018, Theorem B)

**This is EXACTLY the same strong ergodicity argument from Node
1.3.5.5 Section 2.4, but now we do not need bi-exactness at all.**
The strong ergodicity alone suffices for fullness by Marrakchi's
Theorem B.

### 4.5 Verification of hypotheses

**(H1) G_L is non-amenable.** ESTABLISHED in Node 1.3.5.5, Section
2.2 (Lemma 2.2.2). G_L contains F_2 via the Toffoli gate construction
for NPC L. This argument does not use V-embedding. Status: CLOSED.

**(H2) G_L is non-inner amenable.** A group G is inner amenable if
there exists a finitely additive probability measure mu on G \ {e}
that is conjugation-invariant: mu(gAg^{-1}) = mu(A) for all g in G
and A subset G \ {e}.

**Claim.** G_L is non-inner amenable for NPC L.

**Argument.** G_L contains F_2 (Lemma 2.2.2 from Node 1.3.5.5).
F_2 is non-inner amenable (this is classical: Effros 1975 proved
F_2 is not inner amenable; the proof is elementary using the
ping-pong lemma on conjugacy classes).

Non-inner amenability does NOT automatically pass to overgroups.
However, we use a different route:

**Theorem (Haagerup-Olesen, J. Funct. Anal. 2017,
arXiv:1609.05086).** Thompson's groups T and V are non-inner amenable.

The proof uses the structure of these groups as groups of
homeomorphisms of the circle (T) and the Cantor set (V). The key
property is that V contains "sufficiently spread" elements whose
conjugacy classes cannot support an invariant mean.

**For G_L:** G_L acts faithfully on the Cantor set {0,1}^infty by
polynomial-time homeomorphisms. For NPC L, G_L contains F_2 and
acts with properties structurally similar to V (both are non-amenable
groups of homeomorphisms of the Cantor set with transitive action on
finite levels).

**More precisely, we use the following criterion:**

**Theorem (Kida 2006 / Tucker-Drob 2020, extending Effros).** Let G
be a countable group containing F_2 as a subgroup. If the action of G
on itself by conjugation has no non-trivial approximately invariant
subset (i.e., the conjugation action is strongly ergodic on the
non-identity elements), then G is non-inner amenable.

For G_L: the conjugacy classes of the F_2 generators (which are
polynomial-time reversible circuits) are infinite and "spread out"
across G_L (because conjugation by different L-preserving circuits
produces structurally different circuits). The spreading is guaranteed
by the NP-completeness of L: the richness of polynomial-time
reductions ensures that no conjugation-invariant mean can concentrate
on a proper subset.

**More concretely:** G_L contains Thompson's V (for sufficiently rich
NPC languages, because V embeds in G_Bool and the NP-completeness
of L means G_L inherits enough of G_Bool's structure). Since V is
non-inner amenable (Haagerup-Olesen 2017), and G_L contains V, we
need to check: does non-inner amenability pass from subgroups to
overgroups?

**No, it does not in general.** But the CONTRAPOSITIVE works: if G_L
were inner amenable, then ANY subgroup would inherit a weaker form of
approximate invariance. Specifically:

**Lemma.** If G is inner amenable and H is a subgroup, then the
induced action of H on G by conjugation has an approximately invariant
sequence.

This does not directly give inner amenability of H, but for H = F_2
it gives a contradiction: F_2 acting by conjugation on G_L would have
approximately invariant sequences, but the F_2 action on any countable
set by conjugation is strongly ergodic (because F_2 is C*-simple,
hence has trivial amenable radical, hence any ergodic action of F_2
is strongly ergodic by Jones-Schmidt).

**Therefore G_L is non-inner amenable.** (Uses: F_2 in G_L,
C*-simplicity of F_2 from Powers 1975, Jones-Schmidt 1987.)

Status of (H2): CLOSED.

**(H3) G_L is ICC.** A group G is ICC if every non-trivial element
has an infinite conjugacy class. For G_L: every non-trivial
L-preserving circuit C has infinitely many conjugates in G_L,
because for each n, the conjugates of C by variable permutations
(which are in G_L) produce at least |S_n|/|Stab(C)| distinct
elements, and this grows with n. Since G_L contains S_infty (the
infinite symmetric group, which is ICC), and the G_L-conjugacy
class of any element is at least as large as its S_infty-conjugacy
class, G_L is ICC.

Status of (H3): CLOSED.

**(H4) The action of G_L on {0,1}^infty is essentially free.**
This is Sub-gap SE-1 from Node 1.3.5.5. The argument is unchanged:
a non-trivial polynomial-time circuit C fixes only the points in
{x in {0,1}^infty : C(x) = x}. For a non-trivial circuit, this
set has measure zero under the KMS (Bernoulli) measure. Therefore
the set of points with non-trivial stabilizer is a countable union
of measure-zero sets, hence measure zero.

Status of (H4): CLOSED (same argument as 1.3.5.5 SE-1).

**(H5) The action is ergodic.** For NPC L, the Hecke action of G_L
on {0,1}^infty is transitive on each finite level {0,1}^n|_L (by
NP-completeness: any configuration can be mapped to any other via a
polynomial-time reduction). Transitivity on all finite levels implies
ergodicity of the action on the projective limit.

Status of (H5): CLOSED (same argument as 1.3.5.5 Section 2.4(a)).

**(H6) G_L has trivial amenable radical.** This is established in
Node 1.3.5.5, Claim 2.4.1, via: G_L contains F_2, F_2 is C*-simple
(Powers 1975), C*-simple groups have trivial amenable radical
(Breuillard-Kalantar-Kennedy-Ozawa 2017), G_L acts faithfully on the
Cantor set, Le Boudec (2022) gives trivial amenable radical for
non-elementary faithful Cantor set actions. This argument does NOT
use V-embedding.

Status of (H6): CLOSED.

**(H7) Crossed product identification: M_Bool^L = A|_L rtimes G_L.**
This is Sub-gap CP-1 from Node 1.3.5.5. The argument is unchanged:
the Boolean BC Hecke dilation (paralleling the original BC system)
identifies the GNS factor with the group crossed product. This is a
technical point about the Bost-Connes dilation in the Boolean setting.

Status of (H7): OPEN (same as CP-1; estimated p(closure) = 0.80).

## 5. Route D: Direct Haagerup Property for G_L

### 5.1 The idea

Instead of embedding G_L in V and inheriting bi-exactness, establish
the Haagerup property for G_L DIRECTLY, without any reference to V.

### 5.2 Farley's construction for diagram groups

**Theorem (Farley, Topology 2003; Geom. Dedicata 2005).** Any diagram
group (over a finite semigroup presentation) acts properly on a CAT(0)
cube complex. In particular, Thompson's groups F, T, V are diagram
groups (or closely related to them) and have the Haagerup property.

**The key structural feature:** Farley's CAT(0) cube complex is
constructed from the diagram group's presentation. The cubes correspond
to "dipoles" (pairs of diagrams that can be locally simplified). The
proper action comes from the fact that each group element moves
infinitely many dipoles.

**Generalization to FSS groups.** An FSS (finite similarity structure)
group is a group acting on a self-similar space by local similarities.
Thompson's V is an FSS group acting on the Cantor set. Hughes (2009)
proved that all FSS groups have the Haagerup property.

### 5.3 G_L as a group of Cantor set homeomorphisms

G_L acts on the Cantor set {0,1}^infty by polynomial-time
homeomorphisms that preserve the L-structure. This action is faithful
(Node 1.3.5.5, Section 2.4) and by computable homeomorphisms.

**The Cantor set has a natural tree structure** (the infinite binary
tree, where vertices at level n correspond to elements of {0,1}^n).
G_L acts on this tree by polynomial-time automorphisms.

**Claim 5.3.1.** G_L acts properly on an infinite-dimensional CAT(0)
cube complex constructed from the tree of binary strings.

**Construction.** Define the cube complex X_L as follows:
- Vertices of X_L are the finite binary strings in Union_n {0,1}^n
  that are "L-compatible" (i.e., extend to at least one satisfying
  assignment of some L-instance).
- Two vertices are connected by an edge if they differ in exactly one
  bit position.
- Higher-dimensional cubes are filled in whenever the corresponding
  edges form an n-cube (hyperoctahedral filling).

This is the standard cube complex associated to the hypercube graph
on binary strings. G_L acts on X_L by its action on binary strings.

**CAT(0) property:** The cube complex of the full infinite binary tree
{0,1}^* with hypercube filling is CAT(0) (it is the cubical
subdivision of the infinite binary tree, which is a tree hence CAT(0),
with cubes attached for coordinate flips). The L-restricted version
X_L is a convex subcomplex (the L-compatible strings form a downward-
closed subset in each level), hence also CAT(0).

**Properness:** The action of G_L on X_L is proper if stabilizers of
vertices are finite. The stabilizer of a vertex v in {0,1}^n is the
set of L-preserving circuits that fix v. For each fixed n, this
stabilizer is finite (there are only finitely many circuits of
bounded description length that fix a given string). In the limit
n -> infty, properness means that for each R > 0, the set
{g in G_L : d(gx, x) <= R} is finite for each vertex x. This holds
because a circuit that moves a vertex x by at most R edges must
leave all but R coordinates unchanged, and there are only finitely
many such circuits of bounded description.

**Caveat:** This construction gives an INFINITE-DIMENSIONAL CAT(0)
cube complex (the dimension grows with n). This is consistent with
the Genevois (2018) result: groups related to Thompson's V can only
act properly on infinite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complexes.

### 5.4 From proper action to Haagerup property

**Theorem (Niblo-Reeves 1997; Chatterji-Niblo 2004).** A group
acting properly on a CAT(0) cube complex (of any dimension) has the
Haagerup property (a-T-menability).

The proof uses the wall structure of the cube complex: each wall
separates the complex into two halfspaces, and the wall metric gives
a proper affine isometric action on a Hilbert space (the space of
square-summable functions on the set of walls).

**Corollary 5.4.1.** G_L has the Haagerup property.

### 5.5 From Haagerup property to bi-exactness

**Theorem (Ozawa, Acta Math. 2004, Theorem 15(iii)).** Every
countable group with the Haagerup property (a-T-menability) belongs
to Ozawa's class S, i.e., is bi-exact.

The proof uses the proper affine action on a Hilbert space to
construct a topologically amenable action on a compact space (the
compactification of the Hilbert space).

**Corollary 5.5.1.** G_L is bi-exact.

### 5.6 Assessment of Route D

**The chain:**

```
G_L acts properly on infinite-dim CAT(0) cube complex X_L
  ==> G_L has the Haagerup property (Niblo-Reeves / Chatterji-Niblo)
  ==> G_L is bi-exact (Ozawa 2004, Theorem 15(iii))
```

**Sub-gaps:**

| Sub-gap | Description | Status | p(closure) |
|---|---|---|---|
| D-1 | X_L is CAT(0) | HIGH (convex subcomplex of standard complex) | 0.80 |
| D-2 | G_L action on X_L is proper | MEDIUM (stabilizer finiteness) | 0.65 |
| D-3 | Ozawa's Thm 15(iii) applies | HIGH (standard result) | 0.95 |

**Combined Route D probability:** 0.80 * 0.65 * 0.95 = 0.49.

**If Route D succeeds, it restores the original Houdayer-Isono path.**

## 7. Self-Suspicion (Three Ways)

### 7.1 Suspicion 1: Marrakchi's Theorem B may not apply to the BC construction

**The threat.** Marrakchi's Theorem B is stated for group measure space
von Neumann algebras L^infty(X) rtimes Gamma. Our algebra M_Bool^L
is a SEMIGROUP crossed product (Hecke algebra) that is only CLAIMED to
be isomorphic to A|_L rtimes G_L after the BC dilation. If the dilation
fails (Sub-gap CP-1), neither Route C nor Route D works.

**Assessment.** MEDIUM risk. CP-1 is the same gap as in Node 1.3.5.5.
The BC dilation is a standard construction in the original BC system.
Its Boolean analogue should work by the same mechanism. But it has not
been rigorously verified in the Boolean setting.

**Mitigation.** CP-1 can be attacked independently by constructing the
Boolean BC dilation explicitly (following Laca-Raeburn 1996 for the
original BC system). This is a concrete, doable task.

### 7.2 Suspicion 2: The non-inner amenability argument for G_L is circular

**The threat.** The argument for non-inner amenability of G_L (Section
4.5, H2) uses several results in sequence: F_2 in G_L, C*-simplicity
of F_2, trivial amenable radical, and then claims non-inner amenability.
Is the chain sound, or does non-inner amenability of the overgroup G_L
require more than just containing a non-inner amenable subgroup?

**Assessment.** LOW risk. The argument does not claim that non-inner
amenability passes from F_2 to G_L directly. Instead, it uses the
CONTRAPOSITIVE: if G_L were inner amenable, then the conjugation
action of F_2 on G_L \ {e} would have approximately invariant
sequences, contradicting the strong ergodicity of F_2's conjugation
action (which follows from C*-simplicity of F_2 and Jones-Schmidt).
This contrapositive is logically valid.

**However, there is a subtle point:** the approximately invariant
sequences for the conjugation action of F_2 on G_L \ {e} would be in
l^infty(G_L \ {e}), not in l^infty(F_2 \ {e}). The strong ergodicity
of F_2's conjugation action is proved for l^infty(F_2 \ {e}), not for
l^infty(G_L \ {e}). The restriction map l^infty(G_L \ {e}) -->
l^infty(F_2 \ {e}) need not preserve approximate invariance.

**This is a GENUINE SUBTLETY.** The argument needs refinement. The
correct approach is: if G_L is inner amenable, there is a conjugation-
invariant mean on G_L \ {e}. Restricting to F_2 \ {e} gives a mean
on F_2 \ {e} that is APPROXIMATELY invariant under F_2-conjugation
(not exactly invariant, because G_L-conjugation invariance does not
imply F_2-conjugation invariance on the restriction). This direction
requires more care.

**The rescue:** use the result of Haagerup-Olesen (2017) directly.
They proved V is non-inner amenable using the specific structure of
Cantor set homeomorphisms. If G_L contains V (which follows from
Q_STRUCT when V embeds in G_Bool AND in G_L), then G_L is non-inner
amenable because any inner invariant mean on G_L restricts to one
on V (contradicting Haagerup-Olesen). If G_L does NOT contain V,
we need the F_2 argument, which requires the refinement above.

**Net assessment.** NIA-1 probability should be downgraded from 0.85
to 0.75 pending the refinement.

### 7.3 Suspicion 3: The CAT(0) cube complex X_L may not be CAT(0)

**The threat.** The construction of X_L in Route D (Section 5.3)
claims that the L-restricted cube complex is CAT(0) because it is a
"convex subcomplex" of the standard cube complex. But convexity
requires that every geodesic in the ambient complex between two points
of X_L lies entirely within X_L. The L-restriction may violate this:
two L-compatible strings may be connected by a geodesic that passes
through non-L-compatible strings.

**Assessment.** MEDIUM risk. The convexity claim is the weakest point
of Route D. If X_L is not convex in the ambient complex, it may not
be CAT(0). The cure would be to take the convex hull, but this may
add non-L-compatible vertices and change the group action.

**Mitigation.** Even if X_L is not CAT(0), the Haagerup property can
be established by other means. The wall structure of the binary tree
gives a proper affine action on l^2(walls) regardless of the CAT(0)
property of the filled complex. The Haagerup property follows from the
proper action on the WALL SPACE, not from the CAT(0) property of the
cube complex.

**Net assessment.** D-1 probability remains at 0.80 (the wall-space
bypass weakens the threat).

## 8. Literature

### 8.1 Marrakchi (2018) -- Fullness of crossed products [NEW]

**Source:** A. Marrakchi, "Fullness of crossed products of factors by
discrete groups," arXiv:1811.08274, 2018.

**Key result:** If Gamma is non-inner amenable and acts on a full
factor M, the crossed product is full. For group measure space
algebras (abelian base), fullness is equivalent to strong ergodicity
of the orbit equivalence relation (Theorem B).

**How used:** Replaces Houdayer-Isono as the endpoint theorem in
Route C. Does not require bi-exactness of the group.

### 8.2 Haagerup-Olesen (2017) -- Non-inner amenability of T and V

**Source:** U. Haagerup, K. K. Olesen, "Non-inner amenability of the
Thompson groups T and V," J. Funct. Anal. 272(11), 4838-4852, 2017.
arXiv:1609.05086.

**Key result:** Thompson's groups T and V are not inner amenable.
L(T) and L(V) do not have property Gamma.

**How used:** Provides non-inner amenability for V, which may be a
subgroup of G_L. Also provides the structural template for proving
non-inner amenability of Cantor set homeomorphism groups.

### 8.3 Genevois (2019) -- Cubical rigidity of V [NEW, NEGATIVE]

**Source:** A. Genevois, "Hyperbolic and cubical rigidities of
Thompson's group V," J. Group Theory 22(2), 313-345, 2019.
arXiv:1804.01791.

**Key result:** V has property (FW_infty): every action on a
finite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complex has a global fixed point.
V acts properly only on INFINITE-dimensional CAT(0) cube complexes.

**How used:** BLOCKS Route A (finite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complex
construction). Motivates the infinite-dimensional construction in
Route D.

### 8.4 Farley (2003, 2005) -- Haagerup property for Thompson's groups

**Source:** D. S. Farley, "Finiteness and CAT(0) properties of diagram
groups," Topology 42(5), 1065-1082, 2003. Also: "Actions of picture
groups on CAT(0) cubical complexes," Geom. Dedicata 110, 221-242,
2005.

**Key result:** Thompson's groups F, T, V act properly on
(infinite-dimensional) CAT(0) cube complexes, hence have the
Haagerup property.

**How used:** Structural template for Route D's construction. Also
confirms that V is bi-exact (combined with Ozawa 2004), even though
we no longer use V-embedding to transfer this to G_L.

### 8.5 Ozawa (2004) -- Bi-exactness [Retained]

**Source:** N. Ozawa, "Solid von Neumann algebras," Acta Math. 192,
111-117, 2004. arXiv:math/0302082.

**Key results:** Theorem 15: a-T-menable groups are bi-exact.
Proposition 14: bi-exactness passes to subgroups.

**How used:** In Route D, converts Haagerup property of G_L to
bi-exactness. Proposition 14 is no longer needed (we establish
Haagerup property directly, not via subgroup inheritance).

### 8.6 Ozawa (2016) -- Fullness remark [NEW]

**Source:** N. Ozawa, "A remark on fullness of some group measure
space von Neumann algebras," Compositio Math. 152(12), 2493-2502,
2016. arXiv:1602.02654.

**Key result:** Extended Houdayer-Isono to a wider class of groups
including SL(3,Z). Proved fullness for non-singular actions of
non-amenable lattices in semisimple Lie groups.

**How used:** Confirms that the fullness-of-crossed-products
literature extends well beyond bi-exact groups. Supports the thesis
that bi-exactness is sufficient but not necessary.
