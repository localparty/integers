## 1. Six-Step Method Execution

### Step 1: DIAGNOSE -- Where does the difficulty live?

**Current framing (from OA1).** The OA1 Author sketched two constructions:
(i) tensor amplification + compression (Section 2.4 of node 1.3), and
(ii) Bogoljubov-type on a Fock space realization (Section 2.5 of node 1.3).

Neither was verified. The OA1 Author identified three unresolved
questions: is the compression well-defined? is the result multiplicative?
is it invertible?

**What information is being lost in this framing?** The OA1 construction
was stated for a GENERAL polymorphism on a GENERAL constraint language.
This generality obscures the specific structure of 2-SAT and majority.
The key structural facts being lost are:

(i) 2-SAT has a VERY specific solution space: for a 2-SAT instance Gamma
    on n variables, Sol(Gamma) is a distributive lattice under the
    coordinatewise partial order (Theorem of Feder 1998, implicit in
    Schaefer 1978). The majority operation IS the lattice median.

(ii) The majority polymorphism is IDEMPOTENT (maj(x,x,x) = x), which
     means the diagonal embedding Delta(W) = W otimes W otimes W
     followed by maj-combination gives back W on the diagonal. This
     is the Taylor condition.

(iii) The majority operation is self-dual: maj(1-a, 1-b, 1-c) =
      1 - maj(a,b,c). This additional symmetry constrains the
      construction.

**Diagnosis:** The difficulty was in generality. For the specific case
of 2-SAT + majority, the lattice structure of the solution space
provides the tools needed to verify the construction.

### Step 2: REINTERPRET -- Find a space where the difficulty dissolves

**Reinterpretation:** Instead of working with the abstract factor
M_Bool^{2-SAT} (which is a type III_1 factor whose precise structure
depends on KEY LEMMA 3.4.3), work with the CONCRETE solution-space
operators and verify the algebraic properties at the level of the
solution space.

The key insight: the witness operator W_R for a relation R encodes
the solution set Sol(R) as a projection in the GNS Hilbert space.
The majority polymorphism acts on solutions. The lift alpha_maj acts
on operators by acting on the solutions they encode.

**The space where the difficulty dissolves:** The GNS Hilbert space
H = ell^2(Sol(Gamma), omega) where omega is the KMS_1 weight, and
the operators are FUNCTIONS on solutions. The majority polymorphism
acts on triples of solutions, producing new solutions. The lift to
operators is the "push-forward" of this action on solutions.

### Step 3: UNIFY -- Recognize the template

**Template: Koopman construction.** Given a measure-preserving map
T : (X, mu) -> (X, mu), the Koopman operator K_T : L^2(X, mu) ->
L^2(X, mu) defined by K_T(f) = f circ T is a unitary (if T is
invertible) or an isometry (if T is not invertible).

Our construction is a MULTI-VARIABLE Koopman construction: instead
of a single map T, we have a ternary operation maj : X^3 -> X on
the solution space X = Sol(Gamma). The "Koopman" lift of maj is
a map from operators on X^3 to operators on X.

**Canonical name in our framework:** The polymorphism Koopman lift.
This is the operator-algebraic counterpart of the universal-algebraic
polymorphism operation. The polymorphism acts on TUPLES of solutions;
the Koopman lift acts on OPERATORS over solutions.

**Second template: Conditional expectation.** The map from
(M_Bool^L)^{otimes 3} to M_Bool^L defined by the majority-combination
is a conditional expectation (a positive unital projection of norm 1)
if properly defined. Conditional expectations are multiplicative
(E(abc) = E(a)E(c) when b is in the range) and preserve positivity.
This is the structural guarantee for property (b).

### Step 4: LOCK -- The invariant that protects the result

**The invariant:** The polymorphism condition. For majority and 2-SAT:

**Polymorphism condition (majority for 2-SAT).** For every 2-clause
(x_i OP x_j) in Gamma (where OP is one of {OR, NAND, =>}), and
for every three satisfying assignments a, b, c in Sol(Gamma):

    maj(a,b,c) = (maj(a_1,b_1,c_1), ..., maj(a_n,b_n,c_n))

satisfies every clause of Gamma.

**Proof that majority preserves 2-SAT.** Let (x_i OR x_j) be a clause
(the other types are analogous by duality). Suppose a, b, c satisfy
it. Then:

- At least one of a_i, a_j equals 1 (from a satisfying the clause).
- At least one of b_i, b_j equals 1 (from b satisfying the clause).
- At least one of c_i, c_j equals 1 (from c satisfying the clause).

We need: at least one of maj(a_i,b_i,c_i), maj(a_j,b_j,c_j) equals 1.

Suppose for contradiction both are 0. Then:
- maj(a_i,b_i,c_i) = 0 means at least 2 of {a_i, b_i, c_i} are 0.
- maj(a_j,b_j,c_j) = 0 means at least 2 of {a_j, b_j, c_j} are 0.

Without loss of generality, say a_i = b_i = 0. Then since a satisfies
the clause, a_j = 1, and since b satisfies the clause, b_j = 1. So at
most 1 of {a_j, b_j, c_j} is 0 (at most c_j = 0). Thus
maj(a_j,b_j,c_j) = 1, contradicting our assumption. QED.

This is a direct elementwise verification. It is the STANDARD proof
that majority is a polymorphism of 2-SAT (well-known in the CSP
literature).

**Why it is locked:** The polymorphism condition is a COMBINATORIAL
identity that holds for EVERY triple of satisfying assignments and
EVERY clause. It does not depend on the state, the representation,
or the GNS construction. It is a property of the solution space
itself. No deformation of the operator algebra can break it.

### Step 5: COMPUTE -- The explicit construction of alpha_maj

#### 5.1 Setting up the concrete objects

**The instance.** Let Gamma be a satisfiable 2-SAT instance on n
Boolean variables x_1, ..., x_n. The solution set is:

    Sol(Gamma) = {s in {0,1}^n : s satisfies every clause of Gamma}

**The GNS Hilbert space.** The KMS_1 state of the Boolean BC system
restricted to 2-SAT gives a weight omega on the algebra. The GNS
Hilbert space is:

    H_Gamma = ell^2(Sol(Gamma), omega)

where omega assigns weights to solutions based on the circuit-depth
modular structure. For concreteness, we can take omega to be the
UNIFORM weight on Sol(Gamma) (this is a simplification that does not
affect the algebraic properties we verify; the full KMS_1 weight
adds depth-dependent scaling that cancels in the multiplicativity
check because majority is depth-preserving).

**Notation.** For s in Sol(Gamma), let |s> denote the corresponding
basis vector in H_Gamma. An operator on H_Gamma can be written as:

    A = sum_{s,t in Sol(Gamma)} a_{s,t} |s><t|

The witness operator for a relation R is the projection onto solutions
of R:

    W_R = sum_{s in Sol(R)} |s><s|

**Remark on the factor structure.** The full algebra of operators on
H_Gamma is B(H_Gamma) = M_{|Sol(Gamma)|}(C). The sector M_Bool^{2-SAT}
is a sub-algebra generated by the witness operators {W_R : R in L}
and the circuit isometries {mu_C : C in PCirc^+|_L}. For the purpose
of THIS verification, we work with the witness operators and check
that alpha_maj has the correct algebraic properties on the generators.

#### 5.2 The polymorphism Koopman lift -- explicit formula

**Definition 5.2 (The map alpha_maj on generators).** For each witness
operator W_R in M_Bool^{2-SAT}, define:

    alpha_maj(W_R) = Phi_maj(W_R ox W_R ox W_R)

where:

(i) W_R ox W_R ox W_R is the three-fold tensor product, an operator
    on H_Gamma ox H_Gamma ox H_Gamma = ell^2(Sol(Gamma)^3, omega^3).

(ii) Phi_maj : B(H_Gamma^{ox 3}) -> B(H_Gamma) is the majority
     combination map, defined as follows.

**Definition of Phi_maj.** Define the majority combination map on the
solution space:

    M : Sol(Gamma)^3 -> Sol(Gamma)
    M(a,b,c) = (maj(a_1,b_1,c_1), ..., maj(a_n,b_n,c_n))

This is well-defined by the polymorphism condition (Step 4). Now
define the operator-level map:

    Phi_maj(|a,b,c><a',b',c'|) = |M(a,b,c)><M(a',b',c')|

and extend linearly.

**Explicit formula for alpha_maj(W_R):**

    alpha_maj(W_R)
      = Phi_maj(W_R ox W_R ox W_R)
      = Phi_maj( sum_{a in Sol(R)} sum_{b in Sol(R)} sum_{c in Sol(R)}
          |a,b,c><a,b,c| )
      = sum_{a,b,c in Sol(R)^3} |M(a,b,c)><M(a,b,c)|

Since M(a,b,c) in Sol(R) by the polymorphism condition, each term
|M(a,b,c)><M(a,b,c)| is a rank-1 projection onto a solution.

**Simplification:** The sum over triples (a,b,c) in Sol(R)^3 maps
to Sol(R) via M. For each s in Sol(R), let

    N_R(s) = |{(a,b,c) in Sol(R)^3 : M(a,b,c) = s}|

be the number of triples mapping to s. Then:

    alpha_maj(W_R) = sum_{s in Sol(R)} N_R(s) |s><s|

This is a DIAGONAL operator on H_Gamma with eigenvalue N_R(s) on
the solution s.

**Key observation:** alpha_maj(W_R) is proportional to W_R if and
only if N_R(s) is constant for all s in Sol(R). In general, N_R(s)
varies, so alpha_maj(W_R) is NOT simply W_R but a weighted version.

**Normalization.** To make alpha_maj a unital *-endomorphism, we
normalize:

    alpha_maj^{norm}(W_R) = (1/|Sol(R)|^2) * alpha_maj(W_R)
                           = sum_{s in Sol(R)} (N_R(s)/|Sol(R)|^2) |s><s|

Since sum_{s in Sol(R)} N_R(s) = |Sol(R)|^3 (every triple maps
somewhere), and we want alpha_maj(1) = 1, we should normalize by
|Sol(R)|^2.

**Check: alpha_maj^{norm} of the identity.** The identity operator
in M_Bool^{2-SAT} restricted to the solution space is 1 = sum_{s in Sol(Gamma)} |s><s|. Then:

    alpha_maj^{norm}(1) = sum_{s in Sol(Gamma)} (N_Gamma(s)/|Sol(Gamma)|^2) |s><s|

For 1 (taking R = "all of Sol(Gamma)", so Sol(R) = Sol(Gamma)), N_Gamma(s)
counts triples of solutions whose majority is s. We need sum_s N_Gamma(s)
= |Sol(Gamma)|^3. So the normalized version gives:

    sum_s (N_Gamma(s)/|Sol(Gamma)|^2) = |Sol(Gamma)|^3/|Sol(Gamma)|^2 = |Sol(Gamma)|

This is NOT equal to 1 (unless |Sol(Gamma)| = 1). The normalization
must be different.

**Corrected definition.** We define alpha_maj using a CONDITIONAL
EXPECTATION structure rather than raw pushforward. The correct
construction is:

**Definition 5.2' (Corrected alpha_maj).** Define the isometry:

    V_maj : H_Gamma -> H_Gamma ox H_Gamma ox H_Gamma
    V_maj |s> = (1/|Sol(Gamma)|) sum_{(a,b,c) : M(a,b,c)=s} |a,b,c>

This is well-defined because for each s in Sol(Gamma), the set
{(a,b,c) : M(a,b,c) = s} is non-empty (take a = b = c = s, since
M(s,s,s) = s by idempotence). The normalization factor 1/|Sol(Gamma)|
ensures V_maj is bounded. (The precise normalization to make V_maj
an isometry is 1/sqrt(N_Gamma(s)), but this varies with s. We address
this below.)

**The fiber-dependent normalization.** To make this clean, define for
each s in Sol(Gamma):

    F(s) = {(a,b,c) in Sol(Gamma)^3 : M(a,b,c) = s}

This is the FIBER of M over s. By idempotence, (s,s,s) in F(s),
so F(s) is non-empty for every s in Sol(Gamma). Let N(s) = |F(s)|.

**The correct isometry:**

    V : H_Gamma -> H_Gamma^{ox 3}
    V |s> = (1/sqrt(N(s))) sum_{(a,b,c) in F(s)} |a,b,c>

**Check that V is isometric:**

    <V s | V t> = (1/sqrt(N(s))) (1/sqrt(N(t)))
      sum_{(a,b,c) in F(s)} sum_{(a',b',c') in F(t)} <a,b,c|a',b',c'>

    = (1/sqrt(N(s) N(t))) |F(s) cap F(t)|

For s != t: F(s) cap F(t) = empty (because M(a,b,c) = s and
M(a,b,c) = t with s != t is impossible -- M is a function). So
<Vs|Vt> = 0. Good.

For s = t: <Vs|Vs> = (1/N(s)) |F(s)| = 1. Good.

So V is an isometry. Now define:

**Definition 5.2'' (Final construction of alpha_maj).**

    alpha_maj(A) = V* (A ox A ox A) V

for A in M_Bool^{2-SAT}.

Wait -- this is not right either. The map A -> A ox A ox A is not
linear (it is the diagonal embedding composed with the tensor product,
which is cubic). We need:

**REVISED CONSTRUCTION -- the Koopman endomorphism.** We separate
the construction into two steps:

(1) The majority map M : Sol(Gamma)^3 -> Sol(Gamma) defines a map
    on the UNDERLYING SET of solutions.

(2) The Koopman operator associated to M, acting on L^infty(Sol(Gamma)),
    is:

        alpha_maj(f) = f circ M_diag

    where M_diag(s) = M(s,s,s) = s (by idempotence!). This gives
    the IDENTITY on the diagonal.

This is not useful -- the diagonal action is trivially the identity.
The non-trivial content is in the ACTION ON OFF-DIAGONAL OPERATORS.

#### 5.3 The correct construction (third attempt, final)

The OA1 Author's sketch (Section 2.4 of node 1.3) proposed:

    alpha_f(W_R) = Phi_f(W_R ox ... ox W_R)

The problem is that this is a map on a SINGLE generator W_R,
and extending it to all of M_Bool^{2-SAT} requires verifying
multiplicativity on PRODUCTS of generators.

**The key realization:** We should not define alpha_maj on witness
operators W_R and then try to extend. Instead, we should define
alpha_maj on ALL of M_Bool^{2-SAT} at once, using the
SOLUTION-SPACE STRUCTURE.

**Definition 5.3 (The majority endomorphism of M_Bool^{2-SAT}).** Let
M = M_Bool^{2-SAT} act on H_Gamma = ell^2(Sol(Gamma), omega). The
elements of M (at least the dense sub-algebra generated by witness
operators) can be represented as bounded operators on H_Gamma.

The majority map M : Sol(Gamma)^3 -> Sol(Gamma) satisfies:

(P1) M(s,s,s) = s for all s (idempotence / Taylor condition)
(P2) M(a,b,c) in Sol(Gamma) whenever a,b,c in Sol(Gamma)
     (polymorphism condition)
(P3) M is symmetric: M(a,b,c) = M(sigma(a,b,c)) for all
     permutations sigma in S_3 (majority is symmetric)
(P4) M(a,b,c)_i = maj(a_i, b_i, c_i) for each coordinate i

Define the map alpha_maj on the matrix algebra B(H_Gamma) as follows.
Identify B(H_Gamma) with M_{|Sol|}(C) via the basis {|s> : s in Sol(Gamma)}.
For an operator A = sum_{s,t} a_{s,t} |s><t|, define:

    (alpha_maj(A))_{u,v} = (1/|Sol|^2) sum_{(a,b,c): M(a,b,c)=u}
                                         sum_{(a',b',c'): M(a',b',c')=v}
                                         a_{a,a'} * a_{b,b'} * a_{c,c'}

**THIS IS THE DEFINITION.** It maps an operator A on H_Gamma to a
new operator alpha_maj(A) on H_Gamma. The matrix element (u,v) of
alpha_maj(A) is obtained by summing over all triples of pairs
((a,a'), (b,b'), (c,c')) that map to (u,v) under the majority map,
the PRODUCT of the three matrix elements a_{a,a'}, a_{b,b'}, a_{c,c'}.

**The normalization (1/|Sol|^2)** ensures alpha_maj(I) = I (where I
is the identity). Check:

    (alpha_maj(I))_{u,v} = (1/|Sol|^2) sum_{M(a,b,c)=u, M(a',b',c')=v}
                            delta_{a,a'} delta_{b,b'} delta_{c,c'}
                          = (1/|Sol|^2) sum_{M(a,b,c)=u} sum_{M(a,b,c)=v}
                            [wait, this overcounts]

**Let me be more careful.** When A = I, a_{s,t} = delta_{s,t}. Then:

    (alpha_maj(I))_{u,v}
      = (1/|Sol|^2) sum_{M(a,b,c)=u, M(a',b',c')=v}
          delta_{a,a'} delta_{b,b'} delta_{c,c'}
      = (1/|Sol|^2) sum_{(a,b,c): M(a,b,c)=u AND M(a,b,c)=v} 1
      = (1/|Sol|^2) * N(u) * delta_{u,v}

where N(u) = |F(u)| = |{(a,b,c) : M(a,b,c) = u}|. This is N(u)/|Sol|^2
times delta_{u,v}. For this to equal delta_{u,v}, we need N(u) = |Sol|^2
for all u, which is generally FALSE (the fiber sizes vary).

**Diagnosis:** A single global normalization constant cannot make
alpha_maj unital when the fibers have variable size. We need a
FIBER-DEPENDENT normalization.

**Definition 5.3' (Fiber-normalized majority endomorphism).**

    (alpha_maj(A))_{u,v} = (1/sqrt(N(u) N(v)))
      sum_{M(a,b,c)=u, M(a',b',c')=v} a_{a,a'} a_{b,b'} a_{c,c'}

Check unitality:

    (alpha_maj(I))_{u,v} = (1/sqrt(N(u) N(v))) N(u) delta_{u,v}
                          = sqrt(N(u)/N(v)) delta_{u,v}
                          = delta_{u,v}   (when u = v, N(u)/N(u) = 1)

Good. alpha_maj(I) = I.

**But wait:** The formula involves a PRODUCT a_{a,a'} a_{b,b'} a_{c,c'}
of three matrix elements of A. This means alpha_maj is NOT LINEAR in A.
It is a CUBIC map. This is a fundamental problem -- a *-endomorphism must
be linear.

**Root cause:** The OA1 construction "alpha_f(W) = Phi_f(W ox W ox W)"
is inherently cubic because it takes three copies of the SAME operator W.
To get a LINEAR map, we need to fix TWO of the three copies and let only
one vary, OR use a completely different approach.

#### 5.4 The correct linear construction

**Resolution.** The cubic-map problem arises from trying to make alpha_maj
act on a SINGLE copy of the algebra using three copies of the same
operator. The fix is to use the CONDITIONAL EXPECTATION approach:

**Step 1.** Embed M = M_Bool^{2-SAT} diagonally into M^{ox 3} via:

    Delta(A) = A ox A ox A

This is a *-homomorphism from M into M^{ox 3} (it is multiplicative:
Delta(AB) = AB ox AB ox AB = (A ox A ox A)(B ox B ox B) = Delta(A) Delta(B)).

**Step 2.** Define the compression E_M : M^{ox 3} -> B(H_Gamma) by:

    E_M(X) = V* X V

where V : H_Gamma -> H_Gamma^{ox 3} is the isometry from Section 5.2.

**Step 3.** Define alpha_maj = E_M circ Delta:

    alpha_maj(A) = V* (A ox A ox A) V

**This IS the map.** It is defined on all of B(H_Gamma), it maps into
B(H_Gamma), and we now verify its properties.

**CRITICAL ISSUE with multiplicativity.** The composition E_M circ Delta
involves the diagonal embedding Delta (which IS multiplicative) followed
by the compression E_M (which is NOT multiplicative in general). So:

    alpha_maj(AB) = V* (AB ox AB ox AB) V
    alpha_maj(A) alpha_maj(B) = V* (A ox A ox A) V V* (B ox B ox B) V

These are equal only if V V* acts as the identity on the range of the
tensored operators, i.e., only if V V* commutes with all operators of
the form A ox A ox A. Since V V* is a projection (V is an isometry),
this requires the range of V to be INVARIANT under all A ox A ox A.

**This is generally NOT true.** So alpha_maj as defined in Step 3 is
NOT multiplicative. The diagonal embedding followed by compression
does NOT give a *-homomorphism.

**Diagnosis of the multiplicativity failure.** The issue is structural:
V maps |s> to a superposition of triples in the fiber F(s), and
VV* projects onto the span of these fiber vectors. The operator
A ox A ox A maps fiber vectors to other vectors, but these other
vectors may not lie in the range of V.

#### 5.5 The solution: work on the solution-space level, not the operator level

**Key insight.** The polymorphism acts on SOLUTIONS, not on OPERATORS.
The correct lift is a PULLBACK on the function algebra, not a pushforward
of operators.

Let X = Sol(Gamma). The algebra L^infty(X) (functions on the finite set
of solutions) is a commutative sub-algebra of M_Bool^{2-SAT}. The
witness operators W_R, being diagonal in the solution basis, lie in
L^infty(X).

**The majority pullback on L^infty(X).**

For f in L^infty(X), define:

    (alpha_maj(f))(s) = (1/N(s)) sum_{(a,b,c) in F(s)} f(a) f(b) f(c)

This is again cubic. The same problem.

**The CORRECT linear lift:** Instead of pulling back f through three
copies of itself, we should define alpha_maj as a TRANSFER OPERATOR
(the dual of the pushforward).

**Definition 5.5 (Transfer operator construction).**

The majority map M : X^3 -> X has a pushforward on measures:

    (M_* mu)(s) = mu(F(s)) = sum_{(a,b,c): M(a,b,c)=s} mu(a,b,c)

The dual (pullback on functions) of M_* is:

    (M^* f)(a,b,c) = f(M(a,b,c))

This maps L^infty(X) to L^infty(X^3). To get a map L^infty(X) -> L^infty(X),
we compose with a SECTION of the product:

**The conditional expectation approach.**

Consider the product measure omega^3 on X^3 (where omega is the KMS
weight on X). The marginal of M_*(omega^3) is a measure on X:

    nu(s) = (M_* omega^3)(s) = sum_{(a,b,c): M(a,b,c)=s} omega(a) omega(b) omega(c)

If omega is the uniform measure on X (omega(s) = 1/|X| for all s), then:

    nu(s) = N(s)/|X|^3

The TRANSFER OPERATOR T_M : L^infty(X) -> L^infty(X) is defined by:

    (T_M f)(s) = (1/nu(s)) sum_{(a,b,c): M(a,b,c)=s}
                  f(M(a,b,c)) omega(a) omega(b) omega(c)
               = (1/nu(s)) * f(s) * nu(s)     [since M(a,b,c)=s is the condition]
               = f(s)

This gives the IDENTITY. The transfer operator of the pullback through
M, when averaged against the product measure and conditioned on the
output, is trivially the identity because f(M(a,b,c)) = f(s) is
constant on the fiber.

**This is actually correct and important.** The map T_M = id on
L^infty(X) IS the Taylor condition in disguise: the majority
polymorphism, when viewed as a transfer operator on the function
algebra of solutions, acts as the IDENTITY. This is PATB-DIAGONAL.

**But it means the endomorphism on the diagonal algebra is trivial.**
The non-trivial action of alpha_maj must be on the OFF-DIAGONAL part
of M_Bool^{2-SAT} -- i.e., on operators that are NOT diagonal in the
solution basis.

#### 5.6 The non-trivial construction: action on the full matrix algebra

**The correct construction for the full algebra.**

The algebra M_Bool^{2-SAT} contains both diagonal operators (functions
on solutions) and off-diagonal operators (the circuit isometries mu_C
and their products). The majority endomorphism must act non-trivially
on the OFF-DIAGONAL part.

**Definition 5.6 (alpha_maj on M_Bool^{2-SAT}).**

Define the map M : X^3 -> X as before. Define the "majority kernel"
operator:

    K : H -> H^{ox 3}
    (K psi)(a,b,c) = (1/sqrt(N(M(a,b,c)))) psi(M(a,b,c))

so K maps a function psi on X to a function on X^3 that is constant
on M-fibers (up to normalization). K is the adjoint of the natural
"averaging" operator K* : H^{ox 3} -> H defined by:

    (K* phi)(s) = (1/sqrt(N(s))) sum_{(a,b,c) in F(s)} phi(a,b,c)

**Check: K* K = Id.** For psi in H:

    (K* K psi)(s) = (1/sqrt(N(s))) sum_{(a,b,c) in F(s)}

                   = (1/sqrt(N(s))) sum_{(a,b,c) in F(s)}
                     (1/sqrt(N(s))) psi(s)
                   = (1/N(s)) * N(s) * psi(s) = psi(s). 

So K* K = Id, confirming K is an isometry.

**Now define:**

    alpha_maj(A) = K* (A ox I ox I + I ox A ox I + I ox I ox A) K / 3

for A in B(H). This is a LINEAR map B(H) -> B(H).

**Check unitality.** alpha_maj(I) = K* (I ox I ox I + I ox I ox I + I ox I ox I) K / 3
= K* (3 * I^{ox 3}) K / 3 = K* I^{ox 3} K = K* K = I. Good.

**Check positivity.** If A >= 0, then A ox I ox I >= 0 and similarly
for the other terms, so alpha_maj(A) >= 0. Good.

**Check *-preservation.** alpha_maj(A*) = K* (A* ox I ox I + ...) K / 3
= (K* (A ox I ox I + ...) K / 3)* = alpha_maj(A)*. Good.

**Check multiplicativity?** This is the critical question. Is
alpha_maj(AB) = alpha_maj(A) alpha_maj(B)?

    alpha_maj(AB) = K* (AB ox I + I ox AB + I ox I ox AB) K / 3

    alpha_maj(A) alpha_maj(B) = (K* sum_i (A on slot i) K / 3)
                                 (K* sum_j (B on slot j) K / 3)
                               = (1/9) sum_{i,j} K* (A on i) K K* (B on j) K

Since K K* is a PROJECTION (not the identity on H^{ox 3}), this equals:

    = (1/9) sum_{i,j} K* (A on i) P_{fiber} (B on j) K

where P_{fiber} = K K* is the projection onto the "fiber-constant"
subspace of H^{ox 3}.

For alpha_maj(AB) = alpha_maj(A) alpha_maj(B), we would need:

    K* (AB on slot i) K / 3 = (1/9) sum_j K* (A on i) P_{fiber} (B on j) K

This does NOT hold in general because P_{fiber} does not commute with
operators of the form "B on slot j."

**Conclusion:** The averaged-slot construction is NOT multiplicative.

#### 5.7 The construction that DOES work: the fiber algebra endomorphism

**The root of all the multiplicativity failures:** We have been trying

loses the multiplicativity.

**Definition 5.7 (The fiber algebra construction -- the correct one).**

    H^{ox 3} = H_fiber

(since the fibers partition X^3).

(3) Take the "expectation value" on each fiber: for each s, compute

### Suspicion 1: The construction might be fundamentally nonlinear

**The threat.** Every attempt to construct alpha_maj as a LINEAR map on
the full algebra M_Bool^{2-SAT} ran into the same obstacle: the
polymorphism naturally acts on k-tuples, and compressing to a single
copy introduces nonlinearity (Sections 5.2-5.9). Perhaps the lift
alpha_f simply DOES NOT EXIST as a *-automorphism of the factor. Perhaps
the bridge theorem's claim OA1 is false.

**Assessment.** This is the most serious concern. The eight failed attempts
in Section 5 are not random failures -- they reveal a STRUCTURAL obstacle.
The obstacle is that a polymorphism is an algebraic operation on the
SOLUTION SET (a combinatorial object), and lifting combinatorial operations
to *-automorphisms of type III_1 factors is not a standard construction.

**Counter-argument.** The type III_1 factor M_Bool^{2-SAT} has more
structure than was used in the failed attempts. Specifically:
(i) The modular automorphism group sigma_t provides a one-parameter
    family of automorphisms that are ALREADY linear.
(ii) The crossed-product structure M_Bool^L = L^infty(X) rtimes G_L
    means that automorphisms of G_L automatically induce automorphisms
    of the crossed product.
(iii) If the polymorphism f can be "encoded" as an element of G_L (or
    as a limit of elements of G_L), then alpha_f exists as a standard
    automorphism of the crossed product.

Point (iii) is the most promising. The question is: can the majority
polymorphism be implemented as a CIRCUIT in PCirc^+? If yes, then
mu_{maj} is a circuit isometry in M_Bool, and conjugation by mu_{maj}
is an automorphism.

**But majority is a 3-input, 1-output gate -- it is NOT a permutation
of {0,1}^n.** To implement majority as a reversible circuit, we need
ancilla bits. With ancilla bits, the Toffoli gate (which is universal
for reversible computation) can implement majority. So majority CAN be
implemented as a circuit C_{maj} in PCirc^+, and mu_{C_maj} is a valid
isometry.

**However:** the automorphism Ad(mu_{C_maj}) conjugates by the circuit
isometry for majority. This is an INNER automorphism (if mu_{C_maj} is
a unitary in M_Bool^{2-SAT}}) or an endomorphism (if mu_{C_maj} is a
proper isometry). For the bridge theorem, we need OUTER automorphisms,
not inner ones.

**Risk: HIGH.** The nonlinearity obstacle is real, and the resolution
via circuit isometries gives inner (not outer) automorphisms. The
outerness question is deferred to node 1.3.3.

### Suspicion 2: The PATB-DIAGONAL result might be vacuous

**The threat.** PATB-DIAGONAL says "f(x,...,x) = x means alpha_f fixes
E_diag." We verified this (Section 5.11). But if alpha_f|_{E_diag} = id
for ALL polymorphisms (not just Taylor ones), then the diagonal-fixing
property does not distinguish Taylor from non-Taylor polymorphisms, and
property (d) is vacuous.

**Assessment.** Let us check: for a NON-Taylor polymorphism g (e.g.,
a projection pi_i(x_1,...,x_k) = x_i), does alpha_g also fix E_diag?

For g = pi_1 (projection to first coordinate):
    (alpha_{pi_1}(f))(s) = f(pi_1(s,...,s)) = f(s)

Yes, alpha_{pi_1}|_{E_diag} = id as well, because pi_1(s,...,s) = s.

So EVERY idempotent operation (including projections) fixes E_diag.
The projections are idempotent (pi_i(x,...,x) = x), so they fix the
diagonal just like Taylor operations do. The diagonal-fixing property
does NOT distinguish Taylor from non-Taylor.

**Resolution.** PATB-DIAGONAL does not claim that diagonal-fixing
DISTINGUISHES Taylor from non-Taylor. It claims that the Taylor
condition IS diagonal-fixing. The distinction between Taylor and
non-Taylor comes from the RICHNESS of the clone (exponential vs.
linear growth), not from the diagonal-fixing property of individual
polymorphisms. Every polymorphism in the clone fixes the diagonal.
But Taylor clones have EXPONENTIALLY MANY polymorphisms, all fixing
the diagonal, which collectively force non-fullness. Non-Taylor clones
have only LINEARLY MANY (essentially projections), which do not
generate enough structure for non-fullness.

**Risk: LOW.** The PATB-DIAGONAL result is not vacuous -- it is
UNIVERSAL (all clone elements fix the diagonal). The discrimination
comes from clone SIZE, not from diagonal-fixing per se.

### Suspicion 3: The fiber-size variation might obstruct surjectivity

**The threat.** The computation in Section 5.12 showed that the fiber
sizes N(s) vary: N(010) = 10, N(011) = 22, N(101) = 10, N(111) = 22
for our small instance. This variation means the majority pushforward
is not a measure-preserving map (the uniform measure on Sol^3 pushes
forward to a non-uniform measure on Sol). A non-measure-preserving
map cannot induce a unitary Koopman operator, hence cannot give an
automorphism of the commutative algebra.

**Assessment.** This is correct but may not be fatal. The relevant
algebra is the FULL type III_1 factor, not just the commutative
sub-algebra. Type III factors, by definition, do NOT have a trace
(the weights are not traces). The non-uniformity of the fiber sizes
is COMPATIBLE with the type III structure -- in fact, the RATIO
N(s)/N(t) for different fibers is related to the Radon-Nikodym
derivative, which IS the modular structure.

**Intriguing connection:** The ratio N(011)/N(010) = 22/10 = 2.2.
This ratio might be related to the Connes S-invariant (the spectrum
of the modular operator). For a type III_1 factor, S(M) = R_+,
meaning ALL positive ratios appear as modular eigenvalues. The ratio
2.2 is just one specific value in S(M).

**Risk: MEDIUM.** The fiber-size variation is not an obstruction --
it is a FEATURE of the type III structure. But using it to construct
a genuine automorphism requires the modular-theoretic machinery of
node 1.3.3.

## 3. Status and Honest Assessment

### Status: PARTIAL

**(a) Well-defined:** VERIFIED on the diagonal sub-algebra E_diag
(where alpha_maj = id). OPEN on the full factor M_Bool^{2-SAT}.
The well-definedness on the full factor requires a construction that

**(b) Multiplicative:** VERIFIED on E_diag (trivially, since alpha_maj = id

failed multiplicativity on off-diagonal elements (Sections 5.2-5.9).
The failure is STRUCTURAL: compressing a k-ary operation to a unary
endomorphism inherently conflicts with multiplicativity unless the
modular structure is used.

**(c) Invertible:** NOT VERIFIED. The solution-space pushforward is
non-invertible (because the majority map M : Sol^3 -> Sol is many-to-one).
The fiber sizes vary (Section 5.12 computation), confirming non-injectivity
at the combinatorial level. The full factor automorphism, if it exists,
must use the type III structure to "undo" the many-to-one-ness.

**(d) Fixes E_diag:** VERIFIED. This is a direct consequence of the
Taylor condition (idempotence): f(x,...,x) = x implies alpha_f|_{E_diag} = id.
This matches the PATB-DIAGONAL target. However, the property is
UNIVERSAL for all idempotent operations (including non-Taylor projections),
so it does not by itself distinguish Taylor from non-Taylor.

### What was accomplished

1. Identified the STRUCTURAL OBSTACLE to constructing alpha_f as a
   *-endomorphism: the tension between the k-ary nature of polymorphisms
   and the linearity requirement for endomorphisms (Section 5.10).

2. Verified property (d) (diagonal-fixing) with an explicit computation
   on a small 2-SAT instance (Section 5.12).

3. Verified the polymorphism condition (majority preserves 2-SAT) with
   a direct elementwise proof (Step 4) and a concrete instance check
   (64 triples, all land in Sol).

4. Computed the fiber sizes of the majority map for the concrete instance,
   revealing non-uniform fiber structure (N(s) varies by a factor of 2.2).

5. Identified a promising direction for the full construction: the
   polymorphism may be implementable as a circuit isometry mu_{C_maj}
   via Toffoli gates, giving an automorphism Ad(mu_{C_maj}) of the
   crossed product. But this gives an INNER automorphism (Suspicion 1).

### What remains (feeds into node 1.3.3)

1. **The modular-theoretic construction:** Use the modular automorphism
   group sigma_t and the crossed-product structure to construct alpha_f
   as a genuine *-automorphism of the type III_1 factor. The key idea:
   the polymorphism should modify the Connes cocycle (the 1-cocycle
   from the modular flow) in a way determined by the fiber sizes N(s).

2. **The outerness question:** Even if alpha_f exists as an automorphism,
   it must be OUTER for the bridge theorem. The circuit-isometry
   construction gives inner automorphisms. The outerness must come from
   the CLONE structure (exponentially many polymorphisms cannot all be
   inner -- there are not enough unitaries in the non-injective factor).

3. **The multiplicity argument:** Instead of constructing a SINGLE
   alpha_f, show that the CLONE of polymorphisms collectively generates
   a non-discrete subgroup of Aut(M_Bool^{2-SAT}). This shifts the
   question from "is alpha_f outer?" to "does the clone image in
   Out(M) have accumulation points?" -- which is the non-fullness
   criterion.
