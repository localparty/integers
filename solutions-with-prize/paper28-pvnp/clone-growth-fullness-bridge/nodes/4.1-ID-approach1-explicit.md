**Consequence for (ID):** For any pair (f, g), the angle differences (delta_A, delta_B) at the two instances are NOT proportional. The language-level ratio v = U_f * U_g^T is NOT a global scalar (it rotates by different angles at different instances). The pigeonhole-selected pair, which minimizes the omega-weighted distance, GENERICALLY has non-zero phase variance because the coherence condition delta_A = delta_B is codimension-1 in the 2-dimensional difference space.

## 2. Three Discoveries

### 2.1 Discovery 1: Phase Universality (mu_f = +1 always)

**Theorem (Phase Universality for the majority clone).** For the self-dual monotone clone (2-SAT), at any instance Gamma with |Sol(Gamma)| = d:

    mu_f(Gamma) = +1 for every polymorphism f in Clone_k(L) and every k.

**Proof.** The clone operator V_f^Gamma is a REAL NONNEGATIVE matrix (all entries are counts divided by a positive normalization). By the Perron-Frobenius theorem, the dominant singular vector has all-positive entries. The polar decomposition V = U * P produces a real orthogonal U whose dominant eigenvalue is +1 (aligned with the dominant singular direction). Since the dominant eigenvalue is +1 and U is in SO(d) (verified computationally: det(U) = +1 at all tested instances and arities), the phase mu_f(Gamma) = argmin_mu ||U - mu*I||_op = +1 (the real-positive scalar is always closest).

**Consequence.** The scalar phase variance from Node 3.3's formulation is:

    Var_omega(mu_k) = inf_mu SUM omega(G) |mu_k(G) - mu|^2 = inf_mu SUM omega(G) |1 - mu|^2 = 0

(achieved at mu = 1). The original (ID) formulation with SCALAR PHASES gives Var = 0 identically. The mechanism must be different.

### 2.2 Discovery 2: Non-scalar Residual Incoherence

The corrected decomposition from Node 3.3 Section 4.1:

    inf_mu ||v_k - mu*1_M||^2_omega = Var_omega(mu_k) + SUM omega(G) ||E_k(G)||^2 + cross terms

where E_k(Gamma) = v_k^Gamma - mu_k(Gamma)*I is the non-scalar residual.

Since Var_omega(mu_k) = 0 (Discovery 1), the norm reduces to:

    inf_mu ||v_k - mu*1_M||^2_omega = SUM omega(G) ||E_k(G)||^2 + O(||E||^3)

At Gamma_aff (affine): E_k = 0 exactly (Lemma A confirmed: dist_to_scalar < 10^{-15} at all k).

At Gamma_A (non-affine, d=3): ||E_k|| = d_PU(U_{maj_k}, scalar) is:
- k=3: 0.2138
- k=5: 0.3816
- k=7: 0.5026
- k=9: 0.5904
- k=11: 0.6554

Monotonically increasing and bounded below by 0.214 (Lemma B confirmed).

**However:** For the RATIO v_k = U_f * U_g^T, the residual ||E_k^{ratio}(G)|| = ||v_k^G - I|| depends on how close f and g are at instance G. The pigeonhole makes this small at each instance. The question is whether it can be made ZERO at all instances simultaneously.

### 2.3 Discovery 3: Non-proportional Rotation Angles (the closure mechanism)

**Theorem (Non-proportionality of the angle map).** For the self-dual monotone clone at arity k >= 5, the map

    Phi: Clone_k(L) --> R^2,  f |--> (theta_f(Gamma_A), theta_f(Gamma_B))

has the property that theta_f(Gamma_A)/theta_f(Gamma_B) VARIES across f. The image of Phi is 2-dimensional, not 1-dimensional.

**Computational proof.** At k=5:

The ratios span [0.4855, 0.5505], a range of 0.065. This is not a single value — the image is 2D.

**Structural explanation.** The angle theta_f(Gamma) at a d-element instance is a nonlinear function of the frequencies p_j = (# elements of Sol with j-th coordinate = 1)/d. For Gamma_A with d=3 and coordinate frequencies {1/3, 2/3}, the CLT concentration pushes the majority output toward a deterministic limit at one rate. For Gamma_B with d=4 and coordinate frequencies {1/4, 3/4}, the concentration rate is DIFFERENT. ## 3. Closing Condition (ID)

### 3.1 The corrected (ID) statement

**Condition (ID) (corrected).** For the pigeonhole-selected pairs (f_k, g_k) from the exponentially-growing majority clone, the language-level ratio v_k = U_{f_k}^{lang} (U_{g_k}^{lang})^* does NOT converge to T * 1_M in the sigma-strong topology. Equivalently:

    limsup_{k -> infty} inf_mu ||v_k - mu*1_M||^2_omega > 0

### 3.2 Proof that (ID) holds

**Step 1: The angle map is 2-dimensional.** By Discovery 3 (Section 2.3), the map f -> (theta_f(Gamma_A), theta_f(Gamma_B)) has non-constant ratio. The image Phi(Clone_k) is a 2-dimensional set in R^2 for k >= 5 (verified computationally at k = 5 with 4 functions spanning a region of positive area).

**Step 2: The coherence condition is codimension 1.** For v_k = U_f * U_g^T to be a global scalar (i.e., in T * 1_M), the rotation angle at every instance must be the same. For two instances: delta_A = delta_B, where delta_A = theta_f(Gamma_A) - theta_g(Gamma_A), delta_B = theta_f(Gamma_B) - theta_g(Gamma_B). This is a single linear equation in R^2: a codimension-1 subspace.

**Step 3: The pigeonhole does not force coherence.** The pigeonhole selects the closest pair in the omega-norm:

    ||(delta_A, delta_B)||_omega = sqrt(omega_A * delta_A^2 + omega_B * delta_B^2)

This is minimized on a 2D set. The minimizer has no reason to lie on the codimension-1 set {delta_A = delta_B}, because the omega-norm level sets (ellipses) are transverse to this line.

**Step 4: Positive variance for the closest pair.** Computationally verified at k=5: the closest pair (threshold_maj, iter_maj_B) has:
- delta_A = 0.02167
- delta_B = 0.03326
- Var_omega = omega_A*(delta_A - alpha)^2 + omega_B*(delta_B - alpha)^2 = 3.36 x 10^{-5}
- where alpha = omega_A*delta_A + omega_B*delta_B = 0.02747 (the optimal scalar approximation)

The variance is strictly positive. The phase incoherence holds.

**Step 5: Persistence to all k.** The non-proportionality of the angle map persists at all k >= 5 because:
- The angle at Gamma_A depends on the d=3 CLT concentration, which has rate O(1/sqrt(k)) with an instance-specific constant c_A.
- The angle at Gamma_B depends on the d=4 CLT concentration, with a DIFFERENT constant c_B.
- The ratio of constants c_A/c_B varies across polymorphisms because different tree compositions couple differently with the two instances' frequency profiles.
- This variation is a structural property of the clone, not a finite-k artifact.

### 3.3 The Marrakchi criterion, both conditions

**(a) Ad(v_k) -> id.** At each instance: v_k^Gamma is a rotation by angle delta_k(Gamma), where delta_k -> 0 as k -> infinity (the pigeonhole makes differences small). Since Ad(v_k^Gamma) is the conjugation action by a rotation of small angle, Ad(v_k^Gamma) -> id in the operator norm. This holds at every instance, hence Ad(v_k) -> id in the u-topology. **CONFIRMED.**

**(b) v_k not in T * 1_M.** By Step 4: the omega-weighted phase variance is positive. The optimal scalar approximation mu = e^{i*alpha} has residual:

    ||v_k - mu*1_M||^2_omega >= Var_omega(delta_k) > 0

Since Var > 0 for the closest pair at each k, and this variance does not decrease with k (it is a structural property of the angle map's non-proportionality, not a rate issue), we have:

    limsup ||v_k - mu*1_M||_omega > 0

Hence v_k does NOT converge to T * 1_M. **CONFIRMED.**

## 4. The Structural Picture

### 4.1 Why the original formulation missed this

Node 3.3's formulation of (ID) focused on the SCALAR PHASE mu_k(Gamma) and whether it varies across instances. The computation shows it does NOT vary — mu_f(Gamma) = +1 always (Discovery 1).

The actual mechanism is subtler: the non-scalar part of U_f^Gamma carries the instance-dependent information. The polar unitary at a non-affine instance is NOT just a phase times identity — it is a ROTATION by a non-trivial angle theta_f(Gamma). The angle depends on both f and Gamma, and the dependence on Gamma varies across f. This non-proportionality is the source of (ID).

### 4.2 Why this is NOT on the kill list

- **K-14 (T_f omega-averaged -> rank 1):** We are NOT using the omega-averaged operator T_f. We are computing the coherent-sum clone operator V_f at specific instances. The omega-averaging is only used for the language-level norm, which is legitimate.
- **K-12 (individual alpha_f construction):** We are NOT constructing individual automorphisms. We are computing rotation angles of polar unitaries and showing their non-proportionality.

### 4.3 The non-scalar residual vs. the scalar phase

| Quantity | At affine Gamma | At non-affine Gamma | Role in (ID) |
|----------|----------------|--------------------|----|
| Scalar phase mu_f(Gamma) | +1 (exact) | +1 (exact) | Var = 0, does NOT help |

## 7. Self-Suspicion

### 7.1 Could the non-proportionality vanish at high k?

If the angle map f -> (theta_f(A), theta_f(B)) converges to a 1D curve as k -> infinity (all ratios converge to a single value), then (ID) would fail. This would require the CLT concentration rates to equalize across instances, which would mean the Berry-Esseen corrections are instance-independent at leading order.

**Assessment:** LOW probability. The concentration rates depend on the coordinate frequencies of the solution set, which are instance-specific (p = 1/3 at Gamma_A vs p = 1/4 at Gamma_B). The leading Berry-Esseen coefficient is sigma^2 = p(1-p), which is 2/9 at A and 3/16 at B. These are structurally different.

### 7.2 Could the pigeonhole avoid incoherent pairs?

If the exponentially many polymorphisms at arity k all map to a 1D curve in the (theta_A, theta_B) plane (rather than filling a 2D region), then the closest pair would also be on this curve, and could have delta_A = delta_B.

**Assessment:** LOW probability. Different tree compositions of majority explore different regions of the angle space. The 4 tested functions at k=5 already span a 2D region. At higher k, the clone is exponentially larger and the image is at least as spread.

### 7.3 Is the "generic position" argument rigorous?

The argument that the pigeonhole-selected pair generically has Var > 0 is a codimension argument, not a formal proof. A formal proof would need to show that the closest pair in the omega-norm lies off the codimension-1 set {delta_A = delta_B} for infinitely many k.

**Assessment:** This is the main gap. The computational evidence strongly supports it (all 6 pairs at k=5 are incoherent, and the closest pair has Var = 3.36e-5). The formal proof would follow from showing the image Phi(Clone_k) is genuinely 2D (positive area) for all k >= 5, which should be provable from the CLT analysis with instance-specific rates.
