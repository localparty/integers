**L-4 (Averaged-slot construction):** VERIFIED kill. K*(A on slot i)K/3 fails multiplicativity because P_fiber = KK* does not commute with slot operators. ## 2. The Fundamental Tension

### 2.1 Is it a theorem or an observation?

**WEAKENED.** The Author presents the tension as a pattern extracted from 8 attempts, not as a theorem. There is no formal proof that "no linear multiplicative lift of a k-ary polymorphism to a *-endomorphism of M_Bool^L exists." The 8 attempts are a finite sample. An adversarial analysis should distinguish:

- **For the commutative sub-algebra L^infty(Sol):** The identity result IS a theorem. Any idempotent polymorphism acts as the identity on L^infty(Sol) by f(x,...,x) = x. This is proven in Section 5.11. But this covers only the DIAGONAL part.

- **For the full factor M_Bool^L:** There is no impossibility theorem. The 8 attempts explore natural constructions from the solution-space perspective, but they do not exhaust the construction space. In particular, constructions using the modular flow (which the Author correctly defers to 1.3.3) or the crossed-product structure remain unexplored.

### 2.2 Could there be a third approach?

**YES.** The Author's framing as "same operator in all slots (gives identity) vs. independent copies (gives nonlinear)" is a false dichotomy for the full factor. Three classes of approaches are not attempted:

(A) **Correlated copies via other clone elements:** Fix polymorphisms g,h in the clone. Define a linear map using the single copy a -> f(a, g(s_0), h(s_0)) for fixed reference solutions s_0. This is linear and potentially non-trivial for non-idempotent combinations of different polymorphisms.

(B) **Modular flow conjugation:** The modular flow sigma_t IS a one-parameter family of automorphisms. If the polymorphism can be "encoded" into a perturbation of the modular parameter, this gives a linear automorphism by standard Tomita-Takesaki theory. The Author mentions this possibility but does not attempt it.

(C) **Crossed-product automorphisms from circuit implementations:** The Author notes (Suspicion 1, lines 1157-1177) that majority can be implemented as a Toffoli circuit C_maj, giving an isometry mu_{C_maj} and an inner automorphism Ad(mu_{C_maj}). While inner automorphisms are not useful for the outerness step, the COMPOSITION of an inner automorphism with the modular flow could yield outer automorphisms. This is unexplored.

### 2.3 Does the tension apply to all polymorphisms?

**NO.** Independent analysis shows:

- For MAJORITY, AND, OR (nonlinear over Z/2): the tension holds. All constructions using diagonal copies give the identity, and all using independent copies are nonlinear.

- For XOR/MINORITY (linear over Z/2): the tension BREAKS. The map a -> XOR(a, b, c) = a + b + c (mod 2) is an AFFINE map, hence a group homomorphism. For fixed (b,c), this gives a TRANSLATION on (Z/2)^n, which IS a linear, multiplicative, invertible map on the group algebra. The XOR case DOES produce genuine (inner) automorphisms without encountering the nonlinearity obstruction.

The Author's claim that the tension is universal is false for XOR-SAT. This matters because the bridge theorem must apply to ALL P-time constraint languages, including XOR-SAT. The Author should have noted that XOR avoids the tension (but encounters a DIFFERENT problem: the resulting automorphisms are inner, not outer).

**Verdict on fundamental tension: WEAKENED. It is an observation from 8 attempts on one polymorphism class, not a theorem. The false dichotomy misses at least 3 unexplored construction classes, and the tension does not apply to linear polymorphisms (XOR).**

## 5. The Multiplicity Argument

### 5.1 Logical soundness

**NOT VIABLE as stated.** The argument has four logical gaps:

**Gap A: No automorphisms to work with.** The 8 kills show that individual polymorphisms do NOT produce well-defined *-automorphisms of M_Bool^L. The multiplicity argument requires a map Clone(L) -> Aut(M_Bool^L), but this map has not been constructed. Without it, "the clone collectively generates a non-discrete subgroup of Aut(M)" is vacuous -- there is no map from the clone TO Aut(M).

**Gap B: Non-discrete Aut(M) does NOT imply Inn(M) not closed.** The Author writes (line 1311): "does the clone image in Out(M) have accumulation points? -- which is the non-fullness criterion." But the preceding sentence says "non-discrete subgroup of Aut(M_Bool^L)." These are different statements:

- Non-discrete image in Aut(M): could be entirely within Inn(M), in which case Out(M) might still be discrete. A group can have a dense subgroup while its quotient by a closed normal subgroup is discrete.
- Non-discrete image in Out(M): this IS the non-fullness criterion (HM-FULLNESS).

The Author conflates Aut and Out. The argument needs to show the clone's image in OUT(M) is non-discrete, not just in Aut(M).

**Gap C: No mechanism for "approximately inner but not inner."** The Author asserts (line 1346): "If each polymorphism gives an approximately inner automorphism (a limit of inner automorphisms), and there are exponentially many such limits that are all DIFFERENT, then Inn(M) is not closed." This requires:

1. Each polymorphism gives an approximately inner automorphism (unproven -- there are no automorphisms at all from Gap A).
2. Exponentially many such limits are all different (unproven -- even if the polymorphisms are distinct as functions, their images in Aut(M) could collapse).
3. "Different limits of inner automorphisms" implies Inn(M) not closed (this is correct by definition -- if alpha = lim Ad(u_n) with alpha not in Inn(M), then Inn(M) is not closed).

Step 3 is correct but steps 1 and 2 are unproven.

**Gap D: The argument does not need EACH polymorphism to produce an automorphism -- but it does need SOME mechanism.** The Author's Recommendation 3 (lines 1309-1314) suggests that the clone "collectively" does something. But a collection of non-automorphisms does not collectively become an automorphism. There must be some mechanism by which the clone elements compose or combine to yield automorphisms (or at least well-defined maps on M). No such mechanism is proposed.

### 5.2 Does the multiplicity argument secretly use killed approaches?

**K-9 (BZ biconditional):** The multiplicity argument does NOT use "P -> Taylor." It starts from "Taylor exists" (an assumption) and tries to reach "non-full" (the conclusion of the bridge). This is the FORWARD direction of the bridge theorem. K-9 warns against using the BACKWARD direction ("P -> Taylor"). The multiplicity argument is clean on this kill.

**K-3 (Modular flow produces polymorphism):** The multiplicity argument does NOT claim that the modular flow produces polymorphisms. It claims the reverse: that polymorphisms produce a non-discrete structure in Aut(M). The direction is polymorphism -> automorphism, not modular flow -> polymorphism. Clean on this kill.

### 5.3 Verdict on multiplicity argument

**NOT-VIABLE as a proof strategy in its current form.** The four gaps (A through D) are serious: Gap A is the same gap that the 8 kills identified (no automorphism construction), and the multiplicity argument does not resolve it -- it merely asserts that counting bypasses the need for individual construction, without providing a mechanism for how counting produces automorphisms from non-automorphisms.

**VIABLE as a RESEARCH DIRECTION** if one of the following is provided:

1. A mechanism by which exponentially many solution-space operations produce even ONE non-trivial automorphism (e.g., via Baire category on the space of approximately inner automorphisms).
2. A direct connection between clone size and modular spectral gap (bypassing automorphisms entirely, as the Author's Recommendation 2 suggests on lines 1334-1340).
3. A proof that Inn(M_Bool^L) is not closed by showing the Connes chi(M) invariant is non-trivial for non-full sectors (this would use the information-theoretic channel capacity from Q6-OUTDIM).

**The most promising variant is Recommendation 2 (line 1334-1340): bypass Out(M) entirely via the spectral gap of the modular flow.** This avoids the automorphism construction problem.
