# Node 1.1 (UA1): Exponential Clone Growth for Boolean Taylor Languages

## Direction

Prove |Clone_k(L)| ≥ c·λ^k for Taylor Boolean L.

## Research — Key Results

### Theorem (UA1)

For a Boolean constraint language L on {0,1}, if Pol(L) is Taylor, then

|Clone_k(L)| ≥ (1/2)·2^k for all k ≥ 1.

Constants: c = 1/2, λ = 2. Both are tight (achieved by majority/2-SAT at small k).

### Proof

**Step A.** Taylor on {0,1} ⟹ not essentially unary (BARTO-ETAL-2024) ⟹ tractable CSP (BZ-DICHOTOMY = SCHAEFER-1978) ⟹ at least one of {AND, OR, XOR, majority} ∈ Pol(L) (JEAVONS-1998).

**Step B.** Case analysis:
- **AND ∈ Pol(L):** Clone(AND)_k = {AND_S : ∅ ≠ S ⊆ [k]} = 2^k − 1 ≥ (1/2)·2^k
- **OR ∈ Pol(L):** Dual. Clone(OR)_k = 2^k − 1.
- **XOR ∈ Pol(L):** Clone(XOR)_k = {XOR_S : ∅ ≠ S ⊆ [k]} = 2^k − 1.
- **majority ∈ Pol(L):** Clone = SM (monotone self-dual). |SM_k| = 1, 2, 4, 12, 81, 2646, 1422564,... ≥ 2^{k−1} for all k. For k ≥ 8: Hansel's lemma gives |SM_k| ≥ 2^{C(k,⌊k/2⌋)/k}, super-exponential. QED.

### Computational verification

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AND/OR/XOR | 1 | 3 | 7 | 15 | 31 | 63 | 127 |
| SM (majority) | 1 | 2 | 4 | 12 | 81 | 2646 | 1422564 |
| (1/2)·2^k | 1 | 2 | 4 | 8 | 16 | 32 | 64 |

All entries satisfy bound. ✓

## Self-Suspicion

2. SM_k ≥ 2^{k−1} for k ≥ 8: Hansel's lemma gives super-exponential. Low risk.
3. Conjunction distinctness: elementary truth table verification. Negligible risk.
