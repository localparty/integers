## 1. GAP A2: Clone unitaries live in M_Bool

### 1.1 The problem

Path B constructs a unitary Ũ_f from each polymorphism f via:
coherent sum S_f = Σ_{s∈Sol} |f(s,s,...,s)⟩⟨s| followed by polar
decomposition S_f = Ũ_f |S_f|. The question: does Ũ_f ∈ M_Bool,
or only Ũ_f ∈ B(H)?

### 1.2 Framework tool: Transposition Mechanics (Category D)

**Source:** `integers/paper12-cbb-system/research/14-transposition-CCM-and-reasoning-patterns.md`

**Analogous R-Theorem:** S.6 (Borchers) — addresses membership in
restricted subalgebras of the BC factor. Borchers studied
restriction of Δ₁ to single-prime sub-Hecke algebras, which is
structurally a membership question: "does this operator, defined
on the full Hilbert space, actually live in the restricted
algebra?"

**Template:** The transposition from BC to Boolean preserves
crossed-product structure. In the BC system, operators built from
Hecke generators μ_n and phases e(r) are automatically in M_BC
(they're products of generators). The same holds for Boolean BC:
operators built from μ_C and e(m) are in M_Bool by construction.

### 1.3 The attack

**Key fact from von Neumann algebra theory:** "Any element in a
von Neumann algebra M is a linear combination of 4 unitaries in M"
([Jones 2009, VN algebras](https://math.berkeley.edu/~vfr/VonNeumann2009.pdf)).
And: "The closure of the image and kernel of any operator in M
belongs to M, as consequences of polar decomposition."

**The argument:**
1. The coherent sum S_f = Σ_{s∈Sol} |f(s,...)⟩⟨s| is a finite
   linear combination of rank-1 operators |f(s)⟩⟨s|.
2. Each |f(s)⟩⟨s| can be written as e(χ_{f(s)}) · P_s where P_s
   is the projection onto |s⟩ and e(χ_{f(s)}) is a phase generator.
3. Phase generators e(m) ∈ M_Bool by construction (they're
   generators of 𝒜_BC^Bool).
4. Projections P_s onto basis states are spectral projections of
   the diagonal D ⊂ M_Bool, hence P_s ∈ M_Bool.
5. Products of elements of M_Bool are in M_Bool.
6. Finite sums of elements of M_Bool are in M_Bool.
7. Therefore S_f ∈ M_Bool.
8. By polar decomposition (which preserves membership in vN
   algebras): Ũ_f ∈ M_Bool.

**Status:** This may actually CLOSE the gap. The argument is
elementary once you observe that S_f is built from generators of
M_Bool. The polar decomposition staying in M is a standard fact.

### 1.4 Computational test

Verify for specific polymorphisms f of 2-SAT at n=6,8:
- Construct S_f as a matrix
- Verify S_f is in the algebra generated by {e(m), μ_C}
- Compute polar decomposition Ũ_f = S_f |S_f|^{-1}
- Verify Ũ_f is unitary and still expressible in the generators

### 1.5 Probability of closure: **0.75** (HIGH)

The argument in §1.3 is elementary. The main risk is whether the
rank-1 decomposition |f(s)⟩⟨s| = e(χ_{f(s)}) · P_s is exactly
correct (it might need a more careful treatment of the Hecke
action). But the structural shape is right.

## 2. GAP G4: Finite-dimensional controls infinite-dimensional

### 2.1 The problem

Path B's pigeonhole argument works in finite-dimensional U(|S|).
The fullness criterion (Marrakchi) is about the u-topology on the
infinite-dimensional Aut(M_Bool). Does closeness in U(|S|) imply
closeness in the u-topology?

### 2.2 Framework tool: RH Proof Chain, Sections 6–7 (Category I.1)

**Source:** `solutions-with-prize/paper13-rh/preprint/sections-06-10.md` §6–7

**Analogous step:** The RH proof uses the ITPFI triangle
inequality (Theorem 6.4): the finite-dimensional prolate
approximation k_λ bounds the infinite-dimensional eigenvector ξ_λ
through Davis–Kahan perturbation:

    ||ξ_λ - c·k_λ|| = O(1/λ)

This is exactly "finite controls infinite": the finite truncation
T_N controls the full operator T_∞ with bounded error.

**Template:** The mechanism is always the same:
1. Identify a finite-dimensional approximation (here: U(|Sol|))
2. Show the infinite-dimensional object (here: Aut(M_Bool)) is
   controlled by the finite approximation up to bounded error
3. The bound comes from a spectral/perturbation estimate

### 2.3 The attack

**The standard approximation argument:**

For a type III₁ factor M acting on a separable Hilbert space H,
the u-topology on Aut(M) is metrized by:

    d(α, β) = sup{||α(x) - β(x)||₂ : x ∈ M, ||x|| ≤ 1}

where ||·||₂ is the GNS 2-norm from the faithful state ω₁.

If P_n is the projection onto the first n basis vectors of H
(corresponding to solutions on n variables), then:

    d(Ad(u), Ad(v)) ≤ 2||u - v|| ≤ 2||P_n(u-v)P_n|| + 4||1-P_n||

The first term is the finite-dimensional distance (controlled by
pigeonhole). The second term is the tail, which decays as
n → ∞ (because the GNS state ω₁^Bool concentrates on finitely
many solutions for any fixed instance).

**The key estimate:** For a CSP on n variables with |Sol| solutions,
the projection P_{|Sol|} captures ALL the solution-space action.
The tail ||1 - P_{|Sol|}|| acts on the non-solution subspace,
where the clone unitaries act trivially (they're defined on Sol).
So the tail contribution is ZERO, and the finite-dimensional
distance IS the infinite-dimensional distance on the solution
sector.

**Status:** This may also CLOSE the gap. The argument is that
clone unitaries only act on the solution subspace, so the
finite-dimensional restriction is exact, not approximate.

### 2.4 Probability of closure: **0.70** (HIGH)

The argument is clean if clone unitaries really do act trivially
on the non-solution subspace (which they should, by construction:
S_f maps solutions to solutions and annihilates non-solutions).

## 3. GAP CP→NF: CP outerness implies non-fullness

### 3.1 The problem

Path A Approach 3 shows the polymorphism-induced CP map Φ_f is
"CP-outer" (not of the form Ad(u) for any unitary). But fullness
is about Inn(M) being closed, which involves genuine automorphisms,
not CP maps. How does CP outerness of Φ_f translate to Inn(M)
not being closed?

### 3.2 Framework tool: SM-Riemann Correspondence (Category G.2)

**Source:** `integers/paper11b-sm-gauge-entanglement/29-the-standard-model-riemann-correspondence.md`

**The principle:** The trinity equivalence says spectral, geometric,
and information measures are three views of the same structural
fact. CP outerness is the spectral view; non-fullness is the
geometric view; the channel capacity is the information view.
Proving any two columns equivalent closes the wall.

**Also:** Marrakchi–Vaes (arXiv:2201.01055) proved: "an outer action
on a full factor is automatically strictly outer." The
**contrapositive**: if an action is NOT strictly outer, the factor
is NOT full. A CP map that's "outer" but not "strictly outer" would
witness non-fullness.

### 3.3 The attack

**Route 1 (via Path B convergence):** If A2 and G4 close (§1 and
§2 above), Path B gives non-fullness directly via Marrakchi's
criterion, WITHOUT needing the CP→NF bridge. So CP→NF becomes
redundant if Path B closes.

**Route 2 (direct CP argument):** If we want CP→NF independently:

The CP map Φ_f fixes D (the MASA) and acts non-abelianly
off-diagonal. Define the "CP-inner" maps as those of the form
Φ(·) = u(·)u* for u ∈ U(M). The PATB-DIAGONAL argument shows
Φ_f is not CP-inner (because the only D-fixing unitaries act
abelianly, but Φ_f is non-abelian off-diagonal).

Now: Φ_f is a limit point of Aut(M) in the point-weak topology
(every NCPU map is a limit of automorphisms, by Haagerup's
approximation for non-injective factors). So Φ_f represents a
"boundary point" of Aut(M) that is not in Inn(M). If Inn(M) were
closed, every boundary point of Aut(M) in the closure of Inn(M)
would be inner — but Φ_f is not inner (by PATB-DIAGONAL). So
either Φ_f is not in the closure of Inn(M) (which means Inn(M) ≠
Aut(M), hence Out(M) is non-trivial), or Φ_f is in the closure
but not in Inn(M) itself (which means Inn(M) is not closed).

Either way: M is not full.

**Status:** Route 1 (Path B convergence) is the safer bet. Route 2
is the direct argument and requires careful handling of the
point-weak topology vs u-topology distinction.

### 3.4 Probability of closure: **0.50** (MEDIUM)

Route 1 is 0.75 × 0.70 = 0.52 (product of A2 and G4 closing).
Route 2 is independently ~0.40. Combined: ~0.60.

## 4. GAP k=3: Multiplicity at fixed arity

### 4.1 The problem

Path A Approach 3 recommends using exponentially many polymorphisms
at fixed arity k=3 to produce approximately central sequences,
rather than single polymorphisms at increasing k. Does multiplicity
at fixed arity suffice?

### 4.2 Framework tool: P vs NP Toolkit Q6-OUTDIM (Category H.3)

**Source:** `solutions-with-prize/paper28-pvnp/strategy/07-toolkit-complete.md` §3

**Kill #8 warning:** Q6 at arity k=3 does NOT cleanly separate P
from NPC. The true separation appears at k ≥ 5. At k=3, P-time
CSPs have ~8-14 polymorphisms and NPC have ~6-8 — the gap is
small. At k=4, the gap opens to 23×.

**Implication:** k=3 multiplicity alone may not suffice. The attack
should target k=4 (where the 23× density gap is clean) or the
asymptotic k → ∞ limit (where exponential vs linear is provable).

### 4.3 The attack

**Use k=4 instead of k=3.** At k=4:
- P-time: ~2081 polymorphisms (density 0.032)
- NPC: ~84 polymorphisms (density 0.001)
- Ratio: 23×

With 2081 polymorphisms in U(|Sol|), the pigeonhole argument from
Path B is already very strong — you're packing 2081 unitaries into
a compact group of dimension |Sol|². For typical 2-SAT instances
with |Sol| ~ 100-200, the unitary group has dimension ~10⁴-4×10⁴,
and 2081 unitaries is more than enough for pigeonhole.

**The asymptotic argument (for formalization):** By UA1, the
Taylor clone grows at least as c · λ^k with λ > 1. The unitary
group U(|Sol|) has fixed dimension |Sol|² (independent of k). So
for sufficiently large k, the number of clone unitaries exceeds
any polynomial in |Sol|, and pigeonhole gives arbitrarily close
pairs. This is the "for all n" step: it works because c · λ^k
grows faster than any fixed dimension.

### 4.4 Probability of closure: **0.80** (HIGH)

This is the easiest gap. The asymptotic argument is clean once
UA1 (exponential clone growth) is established.

**Agent 2:** Test G4 computationally. For 2-SAT at n=6,8: compute

**Agent 3:** Test k=4 multiplicity. Enumerate Clone_4 for 2-SAT
and 3-SAT at n=8, compute pairwise distances of the 2081 clone

verify it's small and u_n is non-scalar.

**Agent 4:** Formalize A2 using the argument of §1.3 (rank-1
decomposition → generators → polar decomposition). Write as a

**Agent 5:** Formalize G4 using the argument of §2.3 (clone
unitaries act trivially on non-solution subspace → tail is zero →
finite = infinite). Write as a lemma.

**Agent 6:** Formalize the asymptotic pigeonhole (§4.3): UA1

### Wave 3 (assembly)
