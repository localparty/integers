# CL1 — All non-trivial zeros covered

**Claim:** Proof applies to every non-trivial zero, not just finitely many or density-1.

**Pass criterion:** no height cutoff; all zeros covered.

**Finding:**
- The Hurwitz argument covers all zeros of Ξ in
  {|Im z| < 1/2}, which corresponds (by s = 1/2 + iz) to all
  non-trivial zeros of ζ in the critical strip 0 < Re s < 1.
- Every non-trivial zero of ζ lies in the critical strip, so the
  coverage is complete in principle.
- **But:** the Hurwitz uniform convergence is on compact subsets
  of the strip. Every specific zero is in some compact subset,
  but the argument must cover every compact — i.e., the
  convergence must hold on all compact subsets simultaneously,
  which Paper 13 claims but does not rigorously establish
  (see HZ1, λ vs N issue).

**Status:** PASS in principle; conditional on HZ1.

**Confidence:** 7/10.
