# B3.03: Do Two Primes Suffice?

**The claim:** Two bridge primes with distinct norms suffice for the Gelfond-Schneider contradiction.

**Assessment:** Yes. The argument needs:
1. Two bridge constraints at distinct primes p_1, p_2
2. The transcendence of log(p_1)/log(p_2)
3. The rationality of n_1/n_2

Any two distinct primes give a transcendental log ratio. The proof uses p = 5 (at k=3, proved) and p = 3 (at k=4). As long as the k=4 bridge lemma is valid, two primes suffice.

**Concern:** If only k=3 (p=5) is a formal lemma, the argument needs a second prime. The paper's k=4 bridge (p=3) has a proof written out in Section 3.4, but was classified as "structural" in the adversarial review. The independent review (Concern 2) concludes the proof in Section 3.4 IS complete and should be declared proved.

**Verdict: CORRECT, assuming two bridge lemmas hold.** The Gelfond-Schneider argument itself is unconditional once two integrality constraints are established.
