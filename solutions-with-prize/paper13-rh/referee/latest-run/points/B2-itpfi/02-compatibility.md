# B2.02: Compatibility with Spectral Inclusion

The ITPFI factorization ensures that the cocycle constraint at each bridge depends only on p-local data. This is needed for the Gelfond-Schneider argument: different bridges at different primes give independent constraints.

**Assessment:** The factorization omega_1 = tensor_p omega_1^p is a statement about states, not about spectra. It implies that KMS evaluations factorize across primes. This is used in the cocycle shift derivation (Section 5, Step 3) to justify that the shift at bridge (p,N,k) depends only on the p-local Euler factor.

**Verdict: CORRECT.** The factorization supports the claimed independence of constraints across primes.
