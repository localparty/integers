# D2.01: The 28 Attacks

Section 11 documents 6 cycles of adversarial review with approximately 28 attacks. The attacks are organized by class (circularity, dark states, Euler factorization, higher-order cancellation, Axiom 4 scope, ITPFI verification).

Two paths were killed (Atiyah-Singer and Penrose) with documented reasons. The kills appear honest and well-reasoned.

The surviving attacks and their resolutions are documented in detail. The adversarial protocol (construction, adversarial, synthesis, numerical verification agents) is well-designed.

**Assessment:** The adversarial review is thorough within its own framework. However, it did not identify the gaps found in this referee report (integrality constraint justification, Axiom 1 circularity). This is because the adversarial agents accepted the CBB axiom system as given and looked for internal inconsistencies, rather than questioning the axioms themselves.

**Verdict: Thorough but limited in scope.** The adversarial review found no internal inconsistencies but did not question the foundational axioms.
