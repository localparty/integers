# D2.03: The Empirical Defense

The paper argues: the CBB axioms produce 36 zero-parameter predictions matching experiment, so the axioms are likely correct, so the proof based on them is likely correct.

**Assessment:** This is not a valid mathematical defense. Empirical success of a framework does not constitute proof of its axioms. Many physical theories make correct predictions from axioms that are later understood to be approximations or to have limited domains of validity.

The 36 predictions are impressive as physics, but irrelevant to the mathematical validity of the proof. A proof of RH must stand on mathematical grounds alone.

**Verdict: The empirical defense is scientifically interesting but mathematically irrelevant.**
