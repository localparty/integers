# D1.04: Comparison to Connes

**Why should this approach succeed where Connes' 25-year programme has not?**

Connes' approach via the trace formula requires proving Weil positivity in the operator-algebraic setting. This is known to be equivalent to RH. After 25+ years, this positivity condition remains open.

This paper takes a different route: instead of positivity, it uses the rigidity of discrete cohomological invariants (bridge cocycles). The key claim is that an off-line zero would produce a non-integer shift in a discrete invariant, which is impossible.

**Why Connes might not have found this:**
1. The bridge family is a construction specific to the CBB system (not in the standard BC literature)
2. The cocycle shift mechanism is novel
3. The Gelfond-Schneider application to the cross-bridge constraints is creative

**Why skepticism is warranted:**
1. The bridge family relies on specific number-theoretic coincidences (the four k-values from the crystallographic restriction)
2. The cocycle shift interpretation (Section 5, Step 3-5) conflates KMS evaluation changes with cohomological shifts
3. The integrality constraint (Delta_c in (1/k)Z) is asserted but not rigorously derived from topology
4. Connes, who knows the BC system better than anyone alive, has not endorsed this approach

**Verdict:** The approach is creative and the algebra is correct. The conceptual gaps (identified in B1) are precisely the kind of gaps that distinguish a clever heuristic from a proof.
