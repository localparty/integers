## 6. The perturbation from adding primes

### The key estimate

When adding prime p to the system: |1 - Z_p(t)| ~ 1/p for p >> 1.

| p | |1-Z_p(t=0.1)| | |1-Z_p(t=1)| | 1/p |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| 97 | 9.05e-3 | 1.03e-2 | 1.03e-2 |
| 997 | 9.94e-4 | 1.00e-3 | 1.00e-3 |
| 9973 | 1.00e-4 | 1.00e-4 | 1.00e-4 |

For large p at fixed t: |1-Z_p| -> 1/p (the heat flow has no effect
on large primes because e^{-t(log p)^2} -> 0 for p large).

### Convergence analysis

| Sum | Value (p <= 10000) | Converges? |
|:--|:--|:--|
| sum 1/p | 2.483 (-> inf) | NO (Mertens) |
| sum |1-Z_p(1)| | 2.227 (-> inf) | NO |
| sum |1-Z_p(1)|^2 | 0.263 | YES |
| sum 1/p^2 | 0.452 | YES |
| sum (1/p) e^{-t(log p)^2} | 0.428 (t=1) | YES |

### The Mertens rescue

The naive perturbation sum diverges like log log P. But:

1. **Squared sum converges**: sum_p |1-Z_p|^2 ~ sum 1/p^2 < inf.
   This means the infinite product prod_p Z_p(t) converges for all
   t > 0 (not to zero — it converges to e^{sum log Z_p}).

2. **Heat-regularised sum converges**: sum_p (1/p) e^{-t(log p)^2}
   converges for all t > 0 because e^{-t(log p)^2} decays faster
   than any power of 1/p. At t=1, the tail beyond p=1000 is ~ 10^{-23}.

3. **The infinite product goes to zero** because log Z ~ -log log P.
   This is expected: the GNS-weighted trace is normalised to 1 at t=0
   and must decay because the state weight distributes over infinitely
   many eigenvalues. The PRODUCT CONVERGES in the sense that the partial
   products form a Cauchy sequence, but the limit is 0.

## 10. Honest assessment

### What works

1. **Spectral gap is uniform in P** — the hardest analytic ingredient
   is free here because the smallest prime (2) sets the gap for all P.

2. **Correlations decay exponentially** — both in t (heat flow) and
   in prime distance |log p - log q| (polymer). The decay constants
   are computable and P-independent.

3. **The polymer expansion converges** — K_eff is small (~0.02) and
   decreasing with distance. No divergence.

4. **The Mertens divergence is tamed** — the heat kernel factor
   e^{-t(log p)^2} regularises the sum over primes for any t > 0.

5. **The ITPFI factorisation is exact** — omega_1 = tensor_p omega_1^p
   is a theorem (research/265), giving the product structure.

### What remains

1. **The limiting semigroup**: the GNS-weighted product goes to 0
   as P -> inf. This is expected (infinite-dimensional trace decay).
   The RELEVANT question is whether the limiting operator e^{-tT^2}
   on the full H_1 exists and has discrete spectrum — this requires
   a different norm (not the GNS trace).

2. **Spectral completeness**: the hardest step is showing spec(T_BC)
   = {gamma_n} with NO extras. The heat trace + explicit formula
   can detect extras, but turning this into a proof requires making
   the explicit formula rigorous for this specific test function.

3. **Off-diagonal observables**: the polymer bound was tested for
   number operators N_p, N_q (diagonal). The full cluster expansion
   needs bounds on mu_p e^{-tT^2} mu_q^* (off-diagonal) — these
   are needed for the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction.

### Feasibility rating: 7/10

The highest-rated lead so far. The technology transfer from YM is
genuine — the spectral gap, polymer decay, and uniform contractivity
all carry over with computable constants. The remaining gap (L.5:
continuum/infinite-prime limit) is the prize, but the numerical
evidence is strongly encouraging.
