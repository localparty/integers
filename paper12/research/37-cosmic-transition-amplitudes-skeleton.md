## 1. The Level-Crossing Mechanism (Recap of research/06 §5)

By Phase 2 (research/02) and thread 3a (research/04), the framework's
effective Hamiltonian on the Riemann subspace H_R is

$$
H_{\mathrm{eff}}(\beta_{\mathrm{eff}})
\;=\; H_0 \;+\; V(\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}),
\qquad
H_0 \;=\; T_{\mathrm{BC}}\cdot\frac{\pi^{2}}{2},
\tag{1.1}
$$

with unperturbed eigenvalues E_n^{(0)} = γ_n · π²/2 on the orthonormal
eigenstates {|γ_n⟩}. The matter perturbation V is generated by the
KK reduction of the Standard Model on M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹
(research/05 §5, research/07). Its β_eff dependence is inherited from
the thermal history of the matter sector.

### 1.2 The instantaneous spectrum

Let

$$
E_n(\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}) \;=\; \gamma_n\cdot\frac{\pi^{2}}{2}
\;+\; V_{nn}(\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}) \;+\; O(V^{2}/\Delta E)
\tag{1.2}
$$

denote the perturbed instantaneous eigenvalues of H_eff(β_eff).
A **level-crossing** β_{n→m}^{*} is a value of β_eff at which

$$
E_n(\beta_{n\to m}^{*}) \;=\; E_m(\beta_{n\to m}^{*}).
\tag{1.3}
$$

At such a crossing, the off-diagonal matrix element V_{nm} lifts the
degeneracy, producing an **avoided crossing** with minimum gap

$$
\Delta_{n\to m} \;=\; 2\,|V_{nm}(\beta_{n\to m}^{*})|.
\tag{1.4}
$$

### 1.3 The cosmic trajectory

As the universe cools (β_eff decreases monotonically with cosmic
time), the instantaneous ground state of H_eff(β_eff) sweeps through

$$
|\gamma_5\rangle
\;\xrightarrow{\;\beta_{5\to 2}^*\;}\;

\;\xrightarrow{\;\beta_{2\to 1}^*\;}\;

\tag{1.5}

The **conditions (SR1)–(SR4)** of research/06 §5.3 fix this specific

## 2. The Landau–Zener Formula

### 2.1 The standard two-level LZ Hamiltonian

Linearise H_eff near a crossing β_{n→m}^{*} in the {|γ_n⟩, |γ_m⟩}
two-level subspace. Let τ = t − t_{n→m}^{*} be the cosmic time
relative to the crossing event, and let

$$
\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}
\;:=\;
\left.\frac{d}{dt}\bigl(E_n - E_m\bigr)\right|_{t = t_{n\to m}^{*}}
\tag{2.1}
$$

denote the rate of change of the unperturbed gap at the crossing.
In a frame rotating with the average energy, the linearised
Hamiltonian is

$$
H_{\mathrm{LZ}}(\tau)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2}\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}\;\tau\,\sigma_{z}
\;+\;|V_{nm}|\,\sigma_{x}.
\tag{2.2}
$$

This is the canonical Landau–Zener problem (Landau 1932; Zener 1932;
Zener's solution gives an exact closed form for the asymptotic
transition probability).

### 2.2 The LZ formula

The probability that the universe **stays in the diabatic state**
|γ_n⟩ as it sweeps through the crossing is

$$
P_{\mathrm{stay}}^{n\to m}
\;=\;
\exp\!\left(
-\,\frac{2\pi\,|V_{nm}|^{2}}{\hbar\,|\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}|}
\right).
\tag{2.3}
$$

The probability of **adiabatically transitioning** to the new ground
state |γ_m⟩ is the complement:

$$
\boxed{\;
P_{n\to m}^{\mathrm{adiabatic}}
\;=\; 1 \;-\; \exp\!\left(
-\,\frac{2\pi\,|V_{nm}|^{2}}{\hbar\,|\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}|}
\right).
\;}
\tag{2.4}
$$

This is the central formula of this note. Equation (2.4) is the
explicit Landau–Zener formula for the cosmic transitions; the only
ingredients are |V_{nm}|² and the cooling rate |Ė_n − Ė_m|.

### 2.3 The adiabaticity parameter

Define the **LZ adiabaticity parameter**

$$
\eta_{n\to m}
\;:=\;
\frac{2\pi\,|V_{nm}|^{2}}{\hbar\,|\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}|}.
\tag{2.5}
$$

Then P_{n→m}^{adiabatic} = 1 − e^{−η}. The two regimes are:

- **Adiabatic (η ≫ 1):** P → 1, the universe successfully follows
  the moving ground state. This is the cosmic-timeline prediction.
- **Diabatic (η ≪ 1):** P → η ≪ 1, the universe stays in the old
  state and the transition fails. This would falsify the cosmic
  timeline interpretation.

The framework **predicts** η_{5→2} ≫ 1 and η_{2→1} ≫ 1. The
constraints this places on |V_{nm}|² are made explicit in §6.

## 3. The Cooling Rate

The cooling rate |dΔE/dt| in (2.4) is the rate at which the
unperturbed gap changes as the universe expands. It is set entirely
by **standard FRW cosmology** plus the framework's identification

$$
\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}\;\longleftrightarrow\;\text{cosmic time}
\tag{3.1}
$$

(research/06 §5.4). We compute it in three steps.

### 3.1 The framework β-function and H_BC

The Bost–Connes Hamiltonian H_BC = log N̂ is the multiplicative
analogue of cosmic time. Identity 12 (research/04) gives
H_BC = log N̂, and the framework's β-function dictates how the
matter-induced shift V_{nn}(β_eff) evolves under RG flow:

$$
\frac{d\,V_{nn}}{d\log\mu}
\;=\;
\beta_V\bigl(V_{nn}, \{c_p\}\bigr).
\tag{3.2}
$$

In a one-loop approximation, β_V is linear in V_{nn} with a
coefficient set by the matter content. For the rate calculation we
need only the **leading** β_eff-dependence, and we record the result
schematically:

$$
\frac{d\,V_{nn}}{d\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}}
\;=\;
-\,\frac{1}{\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}}\,\beta_V(V_{nn})
\;=\;
-\,\frac{c_n}{\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}}
\;+\;O(V^{2}),
\tag{3.3}
$$

where c_n is a dimensionless coefficient encoding the matter content.
T1 will deliver c_n = c_n[V_{nm}] as a functional of the same matrix
elements that appear in (2.4). Until then, c_n is a placeholder.

### 3.2 Cosmic time vs β_eff

Standard FRW cosmology with thermal history gives

$$
T \;=\; \frac{1}{\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}\,k_B},
\qquad
T(t) \;\propto\; a(t)^{-1}\quad\text{(radiation era)},
\qquad
T(t) \;\propto\; e^{-H_{\mathrm{inf}}\,t}\quad\text{(inflation)}.
\tag{3.4}
$$

Differentiating,

$$
\frac{d\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}}{dt}
\;=\;
\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}\cdot
\begin{cases}
H_{\mathrm{inf}} & \text{(during inflation)},\\
2H(t) & \text{(radiation era, } H = 1/2t\text{)}.
\end{cases}
\tag{3.5}
$$

Combining (3.3) and (3.5) gives

$$
\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}
\;=\;
\bigl(\dot{V}_{nn} - \dot{V}_{mm}\bigr)
\;=\;
-\,(c_n - c_m)\cdot\frac{1}{\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}}\cdot
\frac{d\beta_{\mathrm{eff}}}{dt}
\;=\;
-\,(c_n - c_m)\cdot\dot{H}_{\mathrm{epoch}},
\tag{3.6}
$$

where H_epoch is H_inflation during the γ_5→γ_2 epoch and H_radiation
during the γ_2→γ_1 epoch.

### 3.3 The cooling rate at each crossing

In summary, the cooling rate at the crossing β_{n→m}^* is

$$
\boxed{\;
|\dot{\Delta E}_{n\to m}|
\;=\;
|c_n - c_m|\cdot H_{\mathrm{epoch}}\cdot
\bigl(\text{order-one geometric factor}\bigr).
\;}
\tag{3.7}
$$

The H_epoch values are **fixed by standard cosmology**, not by the
framework's matrix elements:

- γ_5 → γ_2 (inflation): H_inf ≈ 10^{−5} M_Pl ≈ 10^{14} GeV.
- γ_2 → γ_1 (post-inflation): H_RH at reheating, H_RD throughout
  radiation domination, ranging from ~10^{12} GeV at end of
  inflation down to ~10^{−27} GeV at matter–radiation equality.

The c_n coefficients are framework outputs that T1 will deliver.

## 6. Adiabaticity Conditions

### 6.1 The condition

From (2.5), adiabaticity at crossing n→m requires

$$
\eta_{n\to m}
\;=\;
\frac{2\pi\,|V_{nm}|^{2}}{\hbar\,|c_n - c_m|\cdot H_{\mathrm{epoch}}}
\;\gg\; 1.
\tag{6.1}
$$

Equivalently, the matrix elements must satisfy

$$
|V_{nm}|^{2}
\;\gg\;
\frac{\hbar\,|c_n - c_m|\cdot H_{\mathrm{epoch}}}{2\pi}.
\tag{6.2}
$$

### 6.2 Numerical thresholds (modulo c_n)

Plugging in numbers (and setting |c_n − c_m| = O(1) for the
threshold scale):

| Transition | H_epoch | ℏ·H/(2π) | Required |V_{nm}|² ≫ |
|:-----------|:--------|:---------|:------------------|
| γ_5 → γ_2  | 10^{14} GeV | ~10^{14} GeV | (10^{7} GeV)² |
| γ_2 → γ_1  | 10^{10} GeV (RH) | ~10^{10} GeV | (10^{5} GeV)² |

These are the **scales** that |V_{52}| and |V_{21}| must exceed for
the cosmic transitions to be adiabatic. They are well below the
Planck scale and well above the electroweak scale, so the prediction
is comfortable but not trivially satisfied.

The framework's strong prediction is that **both** transitions
are adiabatic. Failure of either would falsify the cosmic timeline
interpretation. T1 will deliver the actual values of |V_{52}|² and
|V_{21}|² in physical units, completing the test.

### 6.3 Sanity check via Theorem B

For γ_2 → γ_1, Theorem B ties |V_{21}|² to the empirical CC formula
correction coefficient. The corrected empirical value (research/09)
is |V_{12}|²_emp ≈ 0.2425 in dimensionless BC units. Translating to
physical units requires the BC ↔ physical scale identification of
research/04 §6, which sets 1 BC unit ≈ M_Pl² · (some factor).
A back-of-envelope translation gives |V_{21}|²_emp on the order of
the GUT scale squared, comfortably above the (10^{5} GeV)² threshold.

This is a **consistency check, not a numerical prediction**: the
translation factor and the absolute scale of |V_{21}|² remain T1
deliverables.

The 2% match is the empirical signature; the LZ structure is the

### 7.3 Why the e-fold count is rigorous despite the LZ skeleton being open

A subtle but important point. Theorem A is rigorous **regardless of
whether the LZ transitions are adiabatic**. The e-fold count is

$$
N_{n\to m}
\;=\; \log(R_n/R_m)
\;=\; (\gamma_n - \gamma_m)\cdot\pi^{2}/2,
\tag{7.2}
$$

which depends only on the spectrum of R̂. If the LZ transitions
were non-adiabatic, the universe would simply skip the intermediate
state and end up with a different total e-fold count — but if it
**does** make the transition (which we observe empirically through
CMB and structure formation), then the count is exactly 92.78.

The role of the LZ skeleton is to **explain why** the transitions
happen and **predict their rates**, not to validate the count
itself.
