# Research Note 201 — Round 3 / Agent C (sector attribution)

*Sub-agent of research/198. Date: 2026-04-09.*

## Scope
For each row outside the 1σ band or bookkeeping-flagged, identify
whether the tension lives in the spectral (γ_n) sector or the
geometric (CP²×S² moduli) sector, and recommend a correction channel.

## Rows addressed

1. **m_Z raw+stale (~276σ).** Spectral sector. The raw formula
   γ_11/γ_E is dimensionally fine but the 0.5811 GeV offset is
   far outside any plausible V-shift at first order. Recommended
   channel: research/154 two-term Laurent shift with the larger
   off-diagonal component, i.e. the `b/∏γ` term. This is an order-
   unity correction to the denominator γ_E side, not a first-order
   γ_11 shift.

2. **v raw+stale (~14.9σ).** Spectral sector. γ_10·π²/2 = 245.624
   vs measured 246.22, a 0.596 GeV gap on a 0.04 GeV σ. Recommended
   channel: research/154 a/γ_n² shift on γ_10, which should cover
   ~0.6 GeV naturally at the expected magnitude of a.

3. **1/α_3(M_Z) (1.17σ, in band).** Not a tension; noted for
   future rounds. If FLAG tightens σ_exp below 0.007 it would cross
   1σ and would then be a spectral-sector candidate (γ_11 row).

4. **sin²(2θ_13) PMNS (1.01σ, in band).** Not a tension; flagged
   for Hyper-K/JUNO watch.

## Verdict
Both bookkeeping-flagged rows are spectral-sector rows and both
resolve through the same research/154 channel. There is no
geometric-sector tension in round 3.
