## 10. The Carry Cocycle Template

### 10.1 The universal damping factor $(1 - 1/(kN))$

The bridge family exhibits a uniform correction pattern. At each bridge $(p, N)$ with Brauer order $k$ and residue degree $f = \mathrm{ord}_N(p)$, the tree-level observable $\mathcal{O}_0 = kN/f$ (or its functional variant) receives a **carry damping factor**:

$$\kappa_k := 1 - \frac{1}{k \cdot N},$$

**Epistemic status.** The passage from the U(1)-valued cohomological phase in $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(1))$ to a real multiplicative damping of physical amplitudes is an empirical pattern with structural motivation; a rigorous norm/trace derivation --- showing explicitly how a phase $\zeta_k$ in the group algebra reduces a real amplitude by the factor $1/(kN)$ --- is an open target for Paper 25. The results below should be read as structurally motivated identifications whose numerical success ($0.000\%$ at $k = 4$, $0.17\%$ at $k = 3$) supports but does not replace a formal derivation.

The damping arises from two ingredients:

1. **The fraction of carry-active cosets:** $1/k$. Of the $k$ cosets in $(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^*/\langle p \rangle$, the carry cocycle $c_k(\tau^i, \tau^j) = \zeta_k^{\lfloor(i+j)/k\rfloor}$ is non-trivial on exactly one (the "locked" coset where $\lfloor(i+j)/k\rfloor \neq 0$). The remaining $k - 1$ cosets are carry-free.

2. **The per-class Frobenius amplitude:** $1/N$. The Frobenius $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ distributes unit amplitude across the $N$ residue classes of $(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})$, so each class contributes $1/N$ to the total amplitude.

The product $(1/k) \times (1/N) = 1/(kN)$ is the carry correction per coset per class. Subtracting from 1 gives the damping:

$$\kappa_k = \frac{kN - 1}{kN}.$$

The corrected observable is

$$\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{corrected}} = \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{tree}} \times \kappa_k = \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{tree}} \times \frac{kN - 1}{kN}.$$

### 10.5 Why carries produce integers

The carry cocycle is the obstruction to a homomorphism: $c_k(\tau^i, \tau^j) = \zeta_k^{\lfloor(i+j)/k\rfloor}$ is non-trivial precisely when the sum $i + j$ "wraps around" modulo $k$. In the Bost--Connes setting, this wrapping corresponds to the Frobenius element $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ traversing a full $\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$ cycle on the coset space $(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^*/\langle p \rangle$.

The physical content of the carry is that it counts the number of complete Frobenius orbits modulo the Brauer group. When $kN \equiv 1 \pmod{f}$, the total number of Frobenius orbits $(kN - 1)/f$ is an integer, meaning the carry correction aligns the coset and orbit structures into exact commensurability. The observable then reflects this commensurability as an exact rational (or integer) value.

The pattern extends beyond the $k = 4$ and $k = 3$ cases:

$$\frac{kN - 1}{f} \in \mathbb{Z} \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad kN \equiv 1 \pmod{f} \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \text{exact integer landing.}$$
