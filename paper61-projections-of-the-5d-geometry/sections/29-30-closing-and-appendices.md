#### D.3 — The operational rule

**The Invariant Barrier Rule:** A proof attempting to establish a result in $X_i = P_i(M^5)$ can only use mathematical structure that is preserved by $P_i$. If the proof requires invariants that are lost by $P_i$, then:

1. The proof must be done at the $M^5$ level (using the full 5D geometry), OR
2. The proof must be done by composing projections: first projecting to $M^5$ (the "lift"), then projecting to the target space via a different or composed projection operator.

This is the operational content of the phrase *"cross-domain claims require $M^5$-level arguments."*
