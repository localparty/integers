# 5. The G₄ Flux Axion as Inflaton

This section identifies the inflaton with the G₄ flux axion `a_G` —
the phase of the GVW superpotential — resolving the inflaton
identification problem flagged in Paper 6 (§3.1), where the geometric
radion was ruled out by its steep potential (`ε = 32/3 ≫ 1`). The
flux axion enjoys a shift symmetry `a_G → a_G + const` at tree level,
broken by the axion monodromy mechanism to a linear potential
`V ~ μ³ a_G` at large field values. This gives natural slow-roll
inflation with `n_s = 0.965` and `r = 0.03`, matching the predictions
inherited from Paper 6. The inflationary scale μ is set by M₁₁ and
the flux quantum numbers, and the slow-roll trajectory lies along the
flat axion direction while the geometric moduli r₂ and r₃ remain
flux-stabilized at their minimum.
