## 1.M Methodology: The Framework's Reasoning Patterns

This paper is one of seven in a series. Each paper applies the same
small set of reasoning patterns to a different domain of physics.
The patterns are documented in full in `readme.md`; the relevant ones
for this paper are identified below. Understanding which pattern a
derivation uses lets you see *why* the result works, not just *that*
it works — and lets you apply the same move to new problems.

The six patterns of the framework:

| # | Name | Core move |
|---|------|-----------|
| 1 | **Geometric Reinterpretation** | A 4D mystery is a shadow of simpler 5D geometry |
| 2 | **Holonomy Correspondence** | Wilson line VEV around a compact dimension → gauge phase |
| 3 | **Casimir as Scale-Setter** | Quantum vacuum energy on a compact space → a fundamental scale |
| 4 | **Topological Rigidity** | Discrete invariant (winding number, χ, homotopy) → exact quantized result |
| 5 | **Zeta Regularization of KK Towers** | Compact → discrete KK sum → analytic continuation → finite result |
| 6 | **Projection Produces Apparent Pathology** | 4D looks broken because 4D is a partial trace over the geometry |

**Primary patterns in this paper:**

- **Pattern 2** is the organizing principle: the Wilson line of each
  compact dimension determines the gauge phase for that sector.
  S¹ → U(1) (electromagnetism, Paper 1). S² → SU(2) (weak force,
  §6). CP² → SU(3) (strong force, §5). One mechanism, three forces.

- **Pattern 4** gives three generations from χ(CP²) = 3: the Euler
  characteristic is a discrete topological invariant, so the
  generation count is exact and cannot be perturbed.

- **Pattern 3** + **Pattern 5** give Higgs mass naturalness: the
  Hosotani Wilson line mass is generated by the S² Casimir energy
  (Pattern 3), and the KK correction is exactly Z_{S²}(0) = −2/3
  via zeta regularization (Pattern 5). Theorem K.1 forces all
  higher-loop corrections to zero, making the one-loop result
  perturbatively exact.

- **Pattern 5** gives the spectral non-perturbative gap:
  Δ_{5D} ≥ √5/r₃ > 0 from the Lichnerowicz bound on Fubini-Study
  CP² (a consequence of Pattern 4 — the rigid positive curvature
  of CP² cannot be deformed away).

### 1.1 What Papers 1–3 Achieve

Paper 1 established that a single compact extra dimension — the
e-circle `S¹` — provides a geometric account of quantum mechanics,
electromagnetism, gravity, the spin-statistics theorem, the
Aharonov-Bohm effect, perturbative UV finiteness, black hole
entropy, and the CPT theorem. Paper 2 derived the complete
cosmological predictions of the framework, including the dark
matter–baryon ratio, the Hubble and S8 tensions, and the thawing
dilaton dark energy equation of state. Paper 3 resolved the black
hole information paradox and the problem of time.

This is a geometric account of twenty-two phenomena from a single
compact circle, without free parameters beyond `G₄` and the e-circle
circumference `L`.

### 1.2 What Papers 1–3 Do Not Achieve

The Standard Model has three gauge factors:

    G_SM = SU(3)_c × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y

The e-circle accounts for `U(1)`. It does not account for `SU(2)_L`
(the weak force), `SU(3)_c` (the strong force), the three fermion
generations, or the Higgs mechanism.

This is the honest gap. This paper addresses it.

### 1.3 The Thesis

The non-abelian gauge dimensions are not arbitrary additions to the
e-circle. They are the minimal geometric extension required by the
Standard Model gauge group, and they are selected by the
entanglement geometry of the matter content. The e-circle `S¹` is
one factor of a larger internal manifold `CP² × S² × S¹` whose
isometries produce the full SM gauge group — and whose total
dimensionality, 11, is singled out by five independent lines of
reasoning.
