## 1. Introduction: The Honest Gap


### 1.1 What Papers 1–3 Achieve

Paper 1 established that a single compact extra dimension — the
e-circle `S¹` — provides a geometric account of quantum mechanics,
electromagnetism, gravity, the spin-statistics theorem, the
Aharonov-Bohm effect, perturbative UV finiteness, black hole
entropy, and the CPT theorem. Paper 2 derived the complete
cosmological predictions of the framework, including the dark
matter–baryon ratio, the Hubble and S8 tensions, and the thawing
dilaton dark energy equation of state. Paper 3 resolved the black
hole information paradox and the problem of time.

This is a geometric account of twenty-two phenomena from a single
compact circle, without free parameters beyond `G₄` and the e-circle
circumference `L`.

### 1.2 What Papers 1–3 Do Not Achieve

The Standard Model has three gauge factors:

    G_SM = SU(3)_c × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y

The e-circle accounts for `U(1)`. It does not account for `SU(2)_L`
(the weak force), `SU(3)_c` (the strong force), the three fermion
generations, or the Higgs mechanism.

This is the honest gap. This paper addresses it.

### 1.3 The Thesis

The non-abelian gauge dimensions are not arbitrary additions to the
e-circle. They are the minimal geometric extension required by the
Standard Model gauge group, and they are selected by the
entanglement geometry of the matter content. The e-circle `S¹` is
one factor of a larger internal manifold `CP² × S² × S¹` whose
isometries produce the full SM gauge group — and whose total
dimensionality, 11, is singled out by five independent lines of
reasoning.
