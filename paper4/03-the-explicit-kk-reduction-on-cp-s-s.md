## 3. The Explicit KK Reduction on `CP² × S² × S¹`


### 3.1 The 11D Metric Decomposition

The 11-dimensional metric on `M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹` decomposes as:

    ds²₁₁ = g_μν(x) dx^μ dx^ν
           + g_{ab}^{CP²}(y) dy^a dy^b
           + g_{ij}^{S²}(z) dz^i dz^j
           + φ²(x) (dψ + A_μ^{U(1)} dx^μ)²
           + 2 B_μ^I(x) K_I^a(y) dx^μ dy_a
           + 2 C_μ^J(x) L_J^i(z) dx^μ dz_i

where:
- `x^μ` (`μ = 0,1,2,3`) are the 4D spacetime coordinates
- `y^a` (`a = 1,2,3,4`) are coordinates on `CP²`
- `z^i` (`i = 1,2`) are coordinates on `S²`
- `ψ` is the coordinate on `S¹` (the e-circle)
- `K_I^a(y)` are the 8 Killing vectors of `CP²`
- `L_J^i(z)` are the 3 Killing vectors of `S²`
- `A_μ^{U(1)}` is the U(1) gauge field (the e-connection = photon)
- `B_μ^I` (`I = 1,...,8`) are the SU(3) gauge fields (gluons)
- `C_μ^J` (`J = 1,2,3`) are the SU(2) gauge fields (`W^±, Z`)
- `φ(x)` is the e-circle dilaton

### 3.2 The Killing Vectors

**`CP²` — 8 Killing vectors generating `SU(3)`:**

`CP²` is the complex projective plane, the coset space
`SU(3)/(SU(2) × U(1))`. Its isometry group is `SU(3)` (more
precisely, `PSU(3) = SU(3)/Z₃`), with 8 Killing vectors
corresponding to the 8 generators of the `su(3)` Lie algebra —
the Gell-Mann matrices `λ₁, ..., λ₈`. In homogeneous coordinates
`[Z₁ : Z₂ : Z₃]` on `CP²`, the Killing vectors are the real and
imaginary parts of the holomorphic vector fields generated by the
`su(3)` action on `C³`.

**`S²` — 3 Killing vectors generating `SU(2)`:**

The 2-sphere has isometry group `SO(3) ≅ SU(2)/Z₂`. The 3 Killing
vectors correspond to rotations about the three axes — the angular
momentum operators `L_x, L_y, L_z`, or equivalently the Pauli
matrices `σ₁, σ₂, σ₃`.

**`S¹` — 1 Killing vector generating `U(1)`:**

The circle has isometry group `U(1)`, generated by the single
Killing vector `∂/∂ψ`. This is the e-translation that generates
charge conservation — the same `U(1)` symmetry whose Noether charge
is the electric charge (Paper 1, Appendix D).

### 3.3 The Gauge Bosons

The KK reduction produces 12 massless gauge bosons:

| Source | Killing vectors | 4D gauge fields | Force |
|---|---|---|---|
| `CP²` | 8 | `B_μ^1, ..., B_μ^8` | Strong (gluons) |
| `S²` | 3 | `C_μ^1, C_μ^2, C_μ^3` | Weak (`W^+, W^-, Z^0`) |
| `S¹` | 1 | `A_μ` | EM (photon) |
| **Total** | **12** | | **SM gauge bosons** |

The gauge couplings are determined by the volumes of the internal
spaces:

    g₃² = 16πG₁₁ / Vol(CP²)
    g₂² = 16πG₁₁ / Vol(S²)
    g₁² = 16πG₁₁ / Vol(S¹)

where `G₁₁` is the 11D Newton's constant.

### 3.4 The e-Dimension Physics Survives

The `S¹` factor is the e-circle of Papers 1–3. Because `CP² × S² × S¹`
is a product manifold, the `S¹` sector decouples at leading order:

- **Quantum phase** (`φ` = e-coordinate) is unaffected by `CP²` and
  `S²` — the phase lives on the `S¹` factor
- **Entanglement** (e-conservation `Σ φ_i = C`) operates within the
  `S¹` sector
- **Spin-statistics** (winding numbers on `S¹`) is preserved
- **The Born rule** (e-averaging) applies to the `S¹` sector
- **Perturbative finiteness** (Epstein zeta on `S¹` modes) extends to
  the full KK tower on `CP² × S² × S¹`, with the 7D Epstein zeta
  function having its pole at `s = 7/2`, still above `s = 0`

The quantum mechanics of Paper 1 is the `S¹` sector of the full
11D theory. The nuclear forces are the `CP² × S²` sector. Both are
components of a single 11D geometry.
