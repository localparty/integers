## Major Approaches

### 1. Ax's Theorem / Power Series Analog
Ax (1971) proved Schanuel for formal power series and complex function fields. Strong evidence but not arithmetic.

### 2. Baker's Theorem / Linear Forms in Logarithms
Baker (1966-67): transcendence of linear forms in logarithms of algebraic numbers. Schanuel generalizes this. Baker proved; Schanuel conjectured.

### 3. Lindemann–Weierstrass Theorem
Classical: algebraically independent exponentials of algebraically independent algebraic numbers. Special case of Schanuel.

### 4. Gelfond–Schneider / Hilbert 7
Also a special case of Schanuel.

### 5. Model Theory / Zilber's Approach
Zilber constructed "pseudo-exponential fields" satisfying Schanuel axiomatically. Conjectured complex exponentials match; Kirby, Daw, Bays advances.

### 6. Motivic Schanuel (Bertolin, Huber)
Motivic / period-theoretic formulations relating Schanuel to Grothendieck's period conjecture.

### 7. Conditional Consequences (applications under Schanuel)
Many transcendence results proven *conditional* on Schanuel. Yuri Nesterenko's theorems.

### 8. Effective / Algorithmic (Vahanwala, Ouaknine, Worrell)
Algorithmic applications of Schanuel for decidability of elementary classes.

### Our programme's position
One of the ring's weakest vertices (1/10). Our strategy: eigenvalues of the CCM construction (or QG5D spectrum) are algebraically independent — a statement that *is* a version of Schanuel for that class. We do not claim to prove Schanuel in general. Complementary to all transcendence methods.
