## Major Approaches

### 1. Function Field / Geometric Katz–Sarnak
Katz–Sarnak's function-field monograph proved RMT statistics for L-functions of curves over F_q. Provides the template for number-field conjectures.

### 2. Moments Conjectures (Keating–Snaith, CFKRS)
Moments of ζ(1/2 + it) and L(1/2, π) conjecturally match moments of characteristic polynomials of random matrices from appropriate symmetry group (unitary, orthogonal, symplectic).

### 3. Low-Lying Zeros (Iwaniec–Luo–Sarnak)
For families of L-functions near s = 1/2, statistics of lowest zeros match corresponding RMT predictions (for limited test function support).

### 4. Excised Model (Miller, Dueñez, Huynh, Keating, Snaith)
Orthogonal model deviates near the scaling limit for finite conductors; excised model improves fit. Applied to elliptic curves, cusp forms.

### 5. One-Level Densities
Test-function-based statistics: for supported test functions, family density theorems rigorously agree with RMT prediction.

### Our programme's position
Our ring approaches Katz–Sarnak via CCM: the symmetry type of a family is determined by the structure of its adèle-class-space representation, with unitary/orthogonal/symplectic types corresponding to different Haar measures on the spectral realization. This is complementary to the function-field picture (Katz–Sarnak's geometric monodromy) and to the analytic moments programme (CFKRS).
