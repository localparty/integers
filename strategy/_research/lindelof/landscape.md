## Major Approaches

### 1. Large Value Estimates (Guth–Maynard 2024)
The dramatic 2024 result: new large value estimates for Dirichlet polynomials (Annals of Math). Pushes 12th moment bound. Directly improves Lindelöf-progress bounds.

### 2. Moments of ζ (Heath-Brown, Ingham, Conrey–Ghosh, Conrey)
Bound high moments of |ζ(1/2 + it)|. Keating–Snaith conjecture gives predicted values from random matrix theory. Rigorous: Ingham 4th, Heath-Brown 12th (conditionally higher).

### 3. Exponent Pairs / Van der Corput (Huxley, Watt)
Classical method. Huxley's exponent 32/205 (2005) stood as best known for many years.

### 4. Subconvexity Programme (Michel, Venkatesh, Nelson)
Subconvex bounds for L(1/2, π) for various automorphic π. Lindelöf is the best possible (κ=0); progress is a factor of 1/4 - κ for some κ > 0. Many partial results.

### 5. Decoupling / Harmonic Analysis (Bourgain, Guth)
Bourgain's decoupling theorem (with Demeter) gives square-root savings in many contexts. Applied to zeta.

### 6. δ-Method / Circle Method (Munshi)
Ritabrata Munshi's δ-method for GL(3) subconvexity.

### Our programme's position
The ring programme makes Lindelöf a *byproduct* of RH via CCM: once the spectral realization gives zero-free regions consistent with RH, classical arguments bound moments at Lindelöf quality. We do not contribute to the subconvexity programme directly; we use it as parallel evidence and as a sanity check that our spectral bounds are at least as sharp as subconvexity at every height. Where the RH route is conditional, we still get *unconditional* subconvexity by importing the Guth–Maynard bound.
