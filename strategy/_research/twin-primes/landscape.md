## Major Approaches

### 1. Sieve Methods (Brun, Selberg, GPY, Maynard)
Brun's sieve: pairs (p, p+2) have density ≤ c/log²x. GPY (2005): bounded gaps conditional on Elliott–Halberstam. Maynard's multidimensional sieve (2013): unconditional bounded gaps.

### 2. Elliott–Halberstam and Extensions
EH-conjecture on distribution of primes in arithmetic progressions; would yield bounded gaps = 6 (Maynard). Pushed by Motohashi, Pintz, Friedlander, Iwaniec.

### 3. Bounded Gaps Programme (Zhang 2013 → Maynard → Polymath8)
Zhang 2013: bounded gap ≤ 7×10^7. Maynard 2013: ≤ 600. Polymath8: ≤ 246. EH assumption: ≤ 6. Target: ≤ 2.

### 4. Hardy–Littlewood / Circle Method
Conjectural asymptotic for twin prime count; constant 2∏(1 - 1/(p-1)²). Not proved.

### 5. Matomäki–Radziwiłł on Short Intervals
Multiplicative functions in short intervals; parallel to gap methods.

### Our programme's position
Our ring vertex reformulates twin primes as a correlation question on the operator-algebraic spectral picture, extending GPY-style methods. Complementary to Maynard's sieve; does not beat the 246 bound unconditionally but conjecturally reaches the 2 bound under CCM.
