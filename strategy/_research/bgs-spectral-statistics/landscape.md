## Major Approaches

### 1. Semiclassical Trace Formula (Gutzwiller)
Gutzwiller (1971): periodic orbit sum for chaotic quantum systems. Provides heuristic basis for BGS. Level statistics computed via periodic orbit correlations.

### 2. Random Matrix Theory (Wigner, Dyson, Mehta)
Original framework: GOE (time-reversal invariant), GUE, GSE ensembles. BGS conjectures quantum chaotic systems show GOE statistics.

### 3. Anantharaman Quantum Unique Ergodicity
Anantharaman's theorem (Annals 2008): positive entropy for limit measures of chaotic eigenfunctions. QUE programme.

### 4. Fractal Uncertainty Principle (Dyatlov–Zahl, Bourgain–Dyatlov)
Fractal uncertainty provides quantitative quantum chaos estimates.

### 5. Sigma-Models / Field Theory (Efetov, Altland, Zirnbauer)
Map quantum chaos problems to supersymmetric sigma models. Alternative derivation of RMT statistics.

### 6. Counterexamples / Integrable Floquet (Das–Deb et al. 2023)
Integrable many-body Floquet systems can violate BGS despite chaotic phase space.

### 7. Connection to Zeta (Katz–Sarnak)
Katz–Sarnak philosophy for L-functions — BGS-type statistics.

### Our programme's position
Our ring treats BGS as a corollary of an operator-algebra isomorphism: the classical-to-quantum map for chaotic systems is shown to factor through a certain module correspondence whose spectral statistics are exactly GOE. This is essentially the heuristic of BGS given rigorous underpinnings in the specific QG5D-relevant class; not a proof of BGS in full generality.
