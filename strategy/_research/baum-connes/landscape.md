## Major Approaches

### 1. Dirac-dual-Dirac / Kasparov KK-theory
Standard method: construct a K-theory class representing the assembly map, show it is invertible. Kasparov's 1980s framework is foundational. Works for groups with Haagerup property / a-T-menable groups.

### 2. Hyperbolic Groups / Lafforgue Programme
Vincent Lafforgue proved BC for hyperbolic groups (2002 Fields Medal work). Extended Banach algebra analog.

### 3. Counterexamples with Coefficients (Higson–Lafforgue–Skandalis 2002)
Existence of counterexamples to BC *with coefficients* via Gromov's random groups. The coefficient-free version may still hold.

### 4. Coarse BC (Yu, Willett)
For metric spaces rather than groups. Counterexamples for expanders (Higson, Guentner, Yu). Reformulated versions recover many cases.

### 5. Exactness and Amenable Actions (Guentner–Kaminker, Ozawa)
Exact groups / amenable actions imply BC (via Higson–Kasparov). Nonexact groups are the frontier.

### Our programme's position
Our ring vertex targets the *module-theoretic* form of BC in the specific groupoid class that underlies the QG5D operator algebras. For this class the assembly map is the obvious map to prove an isomorphism; our contribution is identifying that class and separating it from the Gromov/expander obstructions. Parallel to Lafforgue's hyperbolic programme; orthogonal to the expander counterexamples.
