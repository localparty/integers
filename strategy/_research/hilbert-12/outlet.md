## 2. Problem statement

Hilbert's original formulation (translation, 1900):

> "The extension of Kronecker's theorem on abelian fields to an arbitrary algebraic realm of rationality. The theorem, that every abelian number field arises from the realm of rational numbers by the composition of fields of roots of unity, is due to Kronecker. This fundamental theorem... gives rise, as Kronecker himself noted, to a further and far-reaching problem: the problem of giving the corresponding theorem for an arbitrary algebraic realm of rationality; that is, of generating abelian extensions of any number field by means of special values of analytic functions, analogous to the exponential function e^(2πi z) for the rational numbers."

Modernized: Find, for each number field K, an explicit class of transcendental functions whose special values generate the maximal abelian extension K^ab of K.

## 3. Prize

**None.** Hilbert's 1900 problem list carries historical prestige but no monetary prize. The Clay Institute's Millennium Problems (2000) are a separate, modern list; Hilbert's 12th is *not* on it.

## 5. Formulation variants

- **Classical (Kronecker-Weber, proven 1886):** ℚ^ab = ⋃ₙ ℚ(ζₙ).
- **Imaginary quadratic (proven, CM theory):** K = ℚ(√{-d}), d > 0 squarefree; K^ab generated by values of j-invariant and Weber functions at CM points.
- **CM abelian varieties (Shimura–Taniyama, 1961):** extends the imaginary quadratic case to CM fields; generates certain abelian extensions via Shimura varieties.
- **Totally real fields (Dasgupta–Kakde, 2023 — p-adic solution):** produce explicit generators of abelian extensions via p-adic L-values / Brumer-Stark units.
- **Stark's conjectures:** predict class-field generators as logarithms of special L-values; a refinement of the Jugendtraum.
- **General case:** OPEN. No known explicit analytic function class for arbitrary K.

## 6. Known partial results + named walls

**Proven cases:**
- ℚ — Kronecker-Weber (Kronecker 1853, Weber 1886, corrected Hilbert 1896).
- Imaginary quadratic — complex multiplication of elliptic curves (Weber, Hasse, Deuring; completed early 20th c.).
- CM fields — Shimura–Taniyama (1961) via abelian varieties with CM.
- **Totally real fields (p-adic):** Dasgupta–Kakde (2023) — proves the Brumer-Stark conjecture up to 2-torsion, yielding p-adic construction of K^ab for totally real K.

**Named walls:**
- *Stark conjecture wall* — the full set of Stark's conjectures remains open; they would unify partial progress.
- *Langlands reframing* — 1973, Langlands proposed Shimura varieties as the analytic objects; but this produces non-abelian extensions, and its status as a "solution to Hilbert 12" is disputed.
- *Archimedean vs p-adic gap* — Dasgupta-Kakde give p-adic generators; archimedean (complex-analytic) construction for totally real fields is still open.
- *Non-abelian generalization* — moving beyond abelian extensions enters the Langlands program.
