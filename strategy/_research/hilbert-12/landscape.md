## Major Approaches

### 1. Stark's Conjectures (Stark, Tate, Gross)
Stark proposed (1970s) that leading terms of Artin L-functions at s=0 should give generators of abelian extensions. Rank-1 Stark conjecture; refined versions by Tate. Gross–Stark (1981) p-adic version.

### 2. Dasgupta–Kakde p-adic Solution (2021)
Proved the integral Gross–Stark conjecture for Brumer–Stark units. This gives a p-adic solution to H12 for totally real number fields — explicit construction of class fields via p-adic analytic formulas. Landmark breakthrough.

### 3. Shimura Reciprocity / Modular Functions (for Imaginary Quadratic)
Kronecker's Jugendtraum / Kronecker–Weber / Shimura: imaginary quadratic case via CM elliptic curves and modular functions (j-invariant). This is the one classical case fully solved (complex multiplication).

### 4. Complex Multiplication for Higher Dimensions (Shimura Varieties)
For CM abelian varieties generate abelian extensions of reflex fields. Partial explicit class field theory.

### 5. Elliptic Stark / Darmon–Pozzi–Rotger
Elliptic-curve-based refinements of Stark conjecture. Stark-Heegner points.

### 6. Equivariant Tamagawa Number Conjecture (Burns, Flach, Greither)
General framework: special values of L-functions encode arithmetic. Case of abelian Artin L-functions relates to Stark.

### Our programme's position
The H12 vertex is one of the less-developed ring vertices (2/10 confidence). Our strategy (KMS states → class fields) is a *physics-motivated* reframing: Bost–Connes' KMS-state classification for number fields is reinterpreted so that the T=0 phase transition corresponds to class field generation. This is complementary to Dasgupta–Kakde's p-adic solution: where they give an explicit formula for Brumer–Stark units, we give a *classification* of which abelian extensions arise. Their solution implies ours for totally real fields; ours conjecturally gives a uniform picture.
