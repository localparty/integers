## Major Approaches

### 1. Smyth 1971 Theorem
Lehmer's conjecture is true for non-reciprocal polynomials. M(f) ≥ M(y³−y−1) ≈ 1.3247 (Smyth's constant) for non-reciprocal.

### 2. Amoroso–David (2006)
Lehmer holds if the Galois group of the splitting field is small (bounded normal subgroup).

### 3. Dynamical / Entropy (Schmidt, Lind)
Mahler measure = entropy of algebraic ℤ^d-action. Dynamical interpretation.

### 4. Arithmetic Dynamics / Parry Functions
A recent preprint (arXiv:1911.10590 Verger-Gaugry) claims a proof using dynamical zeta functions of Parry systems; not accepted.

### 5. Lehmer's Totient Conjecture (related)
Preprint 2025 (202511.0009) — 2-adic closure, computational certificates, GRH-density.

### 6. Computational Search
Small Mahler measures database (Mossinghoff's table): Lehmer's number 1.17628... still the smallest known.
