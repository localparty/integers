## 2. Problem statement

**Hilbert's original 1900 formulation (from the Paris ICM lecture, translated):**

> "Mathematical Treatment of the Axioms of Physics. The investigations on the foundations of geometry suggest the problem: To treat in the same manner, by means of axioms, those physical sciences in which mathematics plays an important part; in the first rank are the theory of probabilities and mechanics."

**Explicit subproblems listed by Hilbert:**
1. "As to the axioms of the theory of probabilities, it seems to me desirable that their logical investigation should be accompanied by a rigorous and satisfactory development of the method of mean values in mathematical physics, and in particular in the kinetic theory of gases."
2. "Boltzmann's work on the principles of mechanics suggests the problem of developing mathematically the limiting processes, there merely indicated, which lead from the atomistic view to the laws of motion of continua."

**Broad modern interpretation:** rigorous axiomatic derivation of (a) probability theory (accomplished by Kolmogorov 1933), (b) kinetic theory / fluid mechanics from Newtonian/Hamiltonian mechanics, (c) quantum mechanics, (d) quantum field theory, (e) general relativity.

## 5. Formulation variants

- **Probability axiomatization:** RESOLVED (Kolmogorov 1933, *Grundbegriffe der Wahrscheinlichkeitsrechnung*; σ-algebra + probability measure).
- **Boltzmann H-theorem / kinetic limit:** derive Boltzmann equation from Hamiltonian N-particle dynamics. Partially proven for short times (Lanford 1975, short-time validity for hard spheres); long-time behavior and Navier-Stokes limit OPEN.
- **Boltzmann → compressible Euler / incompressible Navier-Stokes-Fourier:** Deng-Hani-Ma (2025) claim a full derivation; under review.
- **Quantum mechanics axiomatization:** von Neumann 1932 (Hilbert space formalism) widely accepted; measurement problem remains philosophical / interpretive.
- **Quantum field theory axiomatization:** Wightman axioms (1956), Haag-Kastler algebraic QFT (1964), constructive QFT — all partial. Clay Yang-Mills Millennium problem is a specific piece.
- **General relativity axiomatization:** mathematical relativity (Choquet-Bruhat, Hawking-Ellis); Einstein field equations as Lagrangian, initial-value well-posedness, cosmic censorship — all partially done.
- **Thermodynamics axiomatization:** Carathéodory (1909), Lieb-Yngvason (1999).
- **Ergodic hypothesis → statistical mechanics:** partially, via Birkhoff ergodic theorem; microcanonical ↔ canonical equivalence for specific systems.
- **Elegance of Boltzmann machine learning / statistical mechanics:** contemporary reformulations in machine learning contexts.

## 6. Known partial results + named walls

**Resolved (in various senses):**
- **Probability axiomatization:** Kolmogorov (1933) — complete and universally accepted.
- **Short-time Boltzmann validity (Lanford 1975):** hard-sphere N-particle dynamics converges to Boltzmann equation in the Boltzmann-Grad limit, for time less than 1/3 of mean free time.
- **Linearized Boltzmann → heat / diffusion limits:** various rigorous results (Bardos-Golse-Levermore, DiPerna-Lions, Esposito-Guo et al.).
- **Quantum mechanics von Neumann axioms:** accepted.
- **Yang-Mills existence (Clay Millennium):** Clay $1M prize, OPEN.
- **Navier-Stokes existence (Clay Millennium):** Clay $1M prize, OPEN.
- **2025 claim (Deng-Hani-Ma):** derivation of compressible Euler and incompressible Navier-Stokes-Fourier equations from Newton's laws via Boltzmann. Under review, disputed (Gallavotti 2025 critique).

**Named walls:**
- *Lanford time wall* — Lanford's validity theorem only holds for 1/3 of mean free time; extending is open.
- *Molecular chaos assumption (Stoßzahlansatz) wall* — Boltzmann's assumption of uncorrelated pre-collision velocities breaks down in dense regimes.
- *Boltzmann-Grad limit wall* — the Nd² → const limit forces infinitesimally-small particle radii, which may not represent physical fluids.
- *Turbulence / K41 wall* — deriving Kolmogorov scaling from NS, itself a major open subproblem (see turbulence vertex).
- *QFT rigor wall* — Wightman axioms have been constructed for free fields and some 2D / 3D interacting models (Glimm-Jaffe φ^4_2, φ^4_3), but 4D Yang-Mills is open (Clay problem).
- *Measurement problem wall* — philosophical/interpretive; not a mathematical problem strictly.
- *Cosmological constant / dark energy axiomatization wall* — connecting QFT to GR remains a physical and axiomatic challenge.
