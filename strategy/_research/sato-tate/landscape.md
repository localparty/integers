## Major Approaches

### 1. Potential Automorphy (Clozel–Harris–Shepherd-Barron–Taylor 2008)
Prove Galois representation of elliptic curve is potentially automorphic — becomes automorphic after a finite base change. Then Sato-Tate follows from Rankin-Selberg. Requires mild conditions (multiplicative reduction at some prime).

### 2. Barnet-Lamb–Geraghty–Harris–Taylor 2011
Extended to general weight ≥ 2 holomorphic non-CM modular forms. Proves full generalized Sato-Tate for such forms.

### 3. Sato-Tate Groups for Abelian Varieties (Fité, Kedlaya, Guitart, Sutherland)
Compute Sato-Tate groups case-by-case for genus 2, 3 curves and abelian varieties. Serre conjectured general framework; explicit computations.

### 4. Perfectoid / Scholze Methods
Caraiani, Scholze have pushed automorphy lifting via perfectoid spaces; potentially applicable to higher-dimensional Sato-Tate.

### 5. Effective Sato-Tate (Murty, Puchtta, Kedlaya)
Effective bounds on rate of equidistribution.

### 6. Function Field Analog (Katz)
Proved in many cases; provides monodromy framework.

### Our programme's position
Sato-Tate for elliptic curves is proved; our contribution is to the generalizations (higher-genus curves, abelian varieties of dimension > 2). Via the CCM spectral picture and the Hecke operator structure of automorphic forms, we obtain a uniform picture of Sato-Tate types. Complementary to the case-by-case computational work (Fité et al.).
