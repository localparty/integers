- **Collatz** — Bakuage Co., Ltd. (Japan, July 2021), 120M JPY ≈ US$1.085M. Condition: publication in reputable journal + committee verification.

**Expired or historical monetary prizes:**
- **Goldbach** — Faber & Faber / Bloomsbury USA, $1,000,000 offered 20 March 2000, **expired 15 March 2002** (submission deadline). Publicity tie-in with Apostolos Doxiadis's novel *Uncle Petros and Goldbach's Conjecture*. Unclaimed.

- **Collatz** — Erdős ($500, informal), Conway (reported $1,000), Althöfer list (miscellaneous small prizes).

- **Hilbert 12, Hilbert 6** — on Hilbert's 1900 list; historical prestige but no monetary prize.

**No prize:**

## Inconclusive / NEEDS-VERIFICATION items

The following items had research that is definitive but worth noting for the second-pass PAC brief writer:

1. **Goldbach Faber prize** — clearly documented as $1M (not €1M as user mentioned). The prize was USD, not euros. Expired 2002. See search-log.md.
2. **Collatz Erdős $500 / Conway $1000** — Erdős $500 confirmed by Wikipedia. Conway's $1000 is widely cited but I could not locate a definitive primary source (reported by MathPrize.net and Althöfer's page); treat as informal but widely believed.
3. **Collatz Bakuage 120M JPY** — confirmed active (PRNewswire, MathPrize.net, multiple news outlets, July 2021).
4. **Hilbert 6 (Deng-Hani-Ma 2025)** — arXiv:2503.01800, March 2025 breakthrough claim, under scrutiny (Gallavotti 2025 critique on arXiv:2504.06297). Status: under review.
5. **Mochizuki IUT status** — still not accepted outside Mochizuki's Kyoto school as of 2026; Scholze-Stix gap unresolved.
6. **Katz-Sarnak — Wikipedia page** — no dedicated Wikipedia page exists; the authoritative outlet is the 1999 AMS monograph and the 1999 *Bulletin AMS* survey article.
7. **Lindelöf — numerical wall** — Bourgain 2017 (*JAMS*) holds as of 2026: μ(1/2) ≤ 13/84 ≈ 0.1548. No further improvement located in this search.
