## Major Approaches

### 1. Hilbert–Pólya / Spectral (Connes–Consani–Marcolli, Berry–Keating, LeClair)
Conjecture: zeros = eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator. Connes' adèle-class-space programme, beginning 1996, is the deepest incarnation: an explicit trace formula on the noncommutative space of adèle classes would imply RH. Berry–Keating proposed a heuristic Hamiltonian H = xp. Recent work: Yakaboylu (2024) Hamiltonian approach; LeClair–Mussardo scattering model where eigenvalues match zeros. Bender–Brody–Müller Hamiltonian proposal. Sierra's cosmological analog. Sarnak's perspective: any successful Hilbert–Pólya programme must embed GUE statistics. Our programme uses CCM specifically.

### 2. Selberg-style Critical-Line Theorems
Selberg (1942): positive proportion of zeros on critical line. Levinson (1974): >1/3. Conrey (1989): >40%. Bui–Conrey–Young more recently pushing above 41%. These are *critical-line* proofs and do not bound zeros off-line.

### 3. Weil Explicit Formula / Positivity Criteria
Weil's refined criterion: RH iff a certain distribution is positive. Li's criterion (1997): RH iff Li coefficients λ_n ≥ 0. Bombieri has pushed this approach. Connes' programme reformulates Weil's criterion as trace positivity on adèle-class-space.

### 4. Random Matrix Theory / Montgomery–Dyson–Odlyzko–Katz–Sarnak
Montgomery's pair correlation; Dyson's identification with GUE eigenvalues. Odlyzko's numerical verification at height 10^22. Keating–Snaith moments conjecture: moments of ζ on critical line ~ corresponding random-matrix moments. This gives only statistical/heuristic support; does not by itself prove RH.

### 5. Sieve / Bombieri–Friedlander–Iwaniec Methods
Sieve-theoretic approaches to zero-density and primes in arithmetic progressions. Bombieri–Vinogradov theorem is roughly "RH on average for Dirichlet L-functions". These give RH-quality results in statistical senses.

### 6. Noncommutative Geometry / Tropical / Characteristic-1 (Connes–Consani)
Arithmetic site, tropical geometry, scaling site. The most audacious programme: replace number fields by characteristic-1 analogs where the Weil-style proof (for curves over F_q) can be repeated.

### 7. Li–Keiper / Positive Moment Sequences
Bridging analytic criteria with RH; Jensen polynomial criteria (Griffin–Ono–Rolen–Zagier 2019) prove RH for finitely many cases at a time but approach a full criterion.

### 8. Computational / Verification (Platt, Odlyzko, Gourdon)
Platt has verified GRH for primitive characters up to height ~3.8×10^13, >29 billion L-functions. Gourdon (2004) verified zeta to ~10^13. No counterexamples found. Necessary condition only.

### Our programme's position
The programme's RH route is a *specialisation* of the CCM adèle-class-space programme: we inherit Connes' trace formula as an external dependency (Tier 4) and supply a 6-layer deduction connecting the spectral picture to the operator-algebraic structure already built for Yang-Mills. All critical-line proofs (Selberg → Conrey) are parallel evidence: if our route succeeds it subsumes them; if it fails they remain. We anticipate reviewers from each of the above schools and address their critiques explicitly in the PROOF-CHAIN.
