Each row names the link, the chain it belongs to, the nature of the wall, and what would close it. Tiers are ordered by how catastrophically the programme would shift if the link closed (Tier 0) vs how naturally the programme is equipped to approach it (Tier 4).

## The five weakest points globally

If I had to name five — the ones that would shift the programme most if closed:

1. **H4** (YM L18) — external, Clay-gate, Routes A-D exhausted
2. **CCM 2025 peer review** (RH L1, cascades to GRH+BGS+Goldbach) — external
3. **PvNP L5 backward** — seven routes failed, REP↔OA is the priority watch
4. **Hodge L3-L4** — blocks Hodge Clay result; motivic BC extension needed
5. **Cramér L4 Route B** (ITPFI direct return-time distribution) — PROGRAMME-INTERNAL, within reach

The fifth one is important — **Cramér L4 Route B is the highest-leverage programme-internal wall that we can actually attack**. The other four are either external (1, 2) or have been exhaustively tried (3) or depend on the cold zone (4). Route B is the first South-Trough-thickening opportunity where the framework's own tools (ITPFI tensor product + Araki-Woods λ_p = 1/p) should give a direct computation.
