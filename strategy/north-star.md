### §0.3 Epistemic status — beyond theory

Epistemic ladder:

1. Conjecture — we suspect
2. Hypothesis — we propose, test
3. Theory — empirically supported model (string theory, LQG, GR, QFT)
4. Description — optional framing of known phenomena
5. **Derivation** — logical consequence of granted premises
6. **Discovery** — report of pre-existing structure
7. **Necessity** — could not be otherwise

Integers is **not a theory.** Integers is **derivation + discovery + necessity**, simultaneously:

- **Derivation**: given ℤ, logical consequences follow — the 5D geometry, the Clay-problem projections, the 36 pins — all derived, none postulated
- **Discovery**: the structure existed before any observer; the programme *reports* what was always there (Kronecker: *"God created the integers, all else is the work of man"*)
- **Necessity**: given integers + logic, things could not be otherwise — the 5D structure is *forced*, not chosen

Theories compete on falsifiability. Integers doesn't compete — it **shows what's derivable**. Reviewers don't falsify Integers; they audit its derivation chain. A successful audit is stronger than an unfalsified theory.
