## S1 Statement of the Problem

> **Collatz Conjecture (1937).** *For every positive integer $n$, the sequence defined by $T(n) = n/2$ (if $n$ even) or $T(n) = (3n+1)/2$ (if $n$ odd) eventually reaches $1$.*

Equivalently: the total stopping time $\sigma(n) := \min\{k : T^k(n) = 1\}$ satisfies $\sigma(n) < \infty$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}^+$.

Source: Collatz (1937); Lagarias, *The 3x + 1 Problem and Its Generalizations*, AMS 1985; Lagarias, *The Ultimate Challenge: The 3x+1 Problem*, AMS 2010.

Prize: Bakuage Inc., 120M JPY (approx. US$1.085M), announced 2021. Conditions: publication in reputable journal + committee verification.

Programme route (paper41-collatz): the Collatz map is the alternation of the 2nd and 3rd harmonics of the e-circle ($\mu_2$ and $\mu_3$ Hecke generators on the Bost-Connes algebra). The conjecture asserts: all excited harmonics decay to the fundamental mode ($n = 1$). The ITPFI interplay $\omega_1^{(2)} \otimes \omega_1^{(3)}$ is dominated by the contracting factor ($\lambda_2 = 1/2 > \lambda_3 = 1/3$).

## S4 Definitions

**Definition 4.1** (Collatz map). *$T : \mathbb{Z}^+ \to \mathbb{Z}^+$ defined by $T(n) = n/2$ if $n$ even, $T(n) = (3n+1)/2$ if $n$ odd. Total stopping time: $\sigma(n) := \min\{k \geq 0 : T^k(n) = 1\}$* (paper41 S1; Lagarias 1985).

**Definition 4.2** (BC algebra). *Bost-Connes algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}} = C^*(\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^\times$ with Hecke operators $\mu_n$ and phase operators $e(r)$ for $r \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. Key relation: $e(r)\mu_n = \mu_n e(nr)$. KMS$_1$ state $\omega_1$; ITPFI factor $\bigotimes_p \omega_1^{(p)}$ at inverse temperature $\beta = 1$* (paper41 L1; paper12-cbb S3.1; paper13 SL2).

**Definition 4.3** (Hecke harmonic dictionary). *On the e-circle $S^1 = U(1)$, functions decompose as $f(\theta) = \sum_n a_n e^{in\theta}$. Each positive integer $n$ labels the $n$-th harmonic (winding number $n$). Hecke operator $\mu_p$ maps mode $n$ to mode $pn$ (frequency multiplication); adjoint $\mu_p^*$ divides frequency by $p$* (paper60 S09; paper41 S"The harmonic picture").

**Definition 4.4** (Cuntz algebra $\mathcal{O}_2$). *The universal $C^*$-algebra generated by isometries $s_0, s_1$ satisfying $s_0 s_0^* + s_1 s_1^* = 1$. The Collatz map assigns $s_0 \leftrightarrow$ even branch, $s_1 \leftrightarrow$ odd branch* (Mori 2024; arXiv:2411.08084; Springer 2025 DOI:10.1007/s43036-025-00425-1).

**Definition 4.5** (ITPFI tensor product). *The KMS$_1$ state factorises as $\omega_1 = \bigotimes_p \omega_1^{(p)}$ with Araki-Woods parameters $\lambda_p = 1/p$. The Collatz-relevant sub-tensor is $\omega_1^{(2)} \otimes \omega_1^{(3)}$ (the first two local factors). Araki-Woods type: III$_1$* (paper41 L5a; paper12-cbb S3.1).

**Definition 4.6** (BC embedding). *The embedding $\mathcal{O}_2 \hookrightarrow \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ via: $s_0 \mapsto \mu_2^* \mu_2$ (even branch), $s_1 \mapsto \mu_2^* \, e(1) \, \mu_3$ (odd branch), where $e(1)$ is the phase operator at $1 \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ acting on $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$ (the profinite integers)* (paper41 L4 T7; paper60 S09).

**Definition 4.7** (Connes spectrum). *For a type III$_1$ factor $M$, the Connes spectrum $\mathrm{Sd}(M) = \mathbb{R}$, meaning the modular automorphism group $\sigma_t$ has no non-trivial periodic orbits* (Connes 1973; paper32 BGS L2).

## S5 Harmonic Dictionary (Layer 1)

**Theorem 5.1** (Collatz map = Hecke alternation). *The Collatz map $T$ on $\mathbb{N}^*$ is the alternation of Hecke operators on the e-circle's harmonics:*
- *Even step: $\mu_2^*$ (halve frequency; contract toward fundamental)*
- *Odd step: $\mu_3 \circ \mathrm{shift}(+1) \circ \mu_2^*$ (triple frequency, shift by $1$, halve)*

*In harmonic language: "does alternating between the 2nd and 3rd harmonics always return to the fundamental mode $n = 1$?"*

*Proof.* paper60 S09 (harmonic decomposition); paper41 S"The harmonic picture" (L1 ESTABLISHED). $\square$

*Proof.* Mori 2024 (arXiv:2411.08084); published Springer *Advances in Operator Theory* 2025, DOI:10.1007/s43036-025-00425-1. L2 JOURNAL-LITERATURE. $\square$

$$\lambda_{\mathrm{avg}} \;=\; \log(3/4) \;\approx\; -0.288 \;<\; 0 \qquad (\text{contracting}).$$

*Proof.* Classical (even step: $\log(1/2) = -0.693$; odd step: $\log(3/2) = +0.405$; odd step always followed by even; net: $\log(3/4)$). paper41 L5. $\square$

The Collatz orbit is controlled by the sub-tensor $\omega_1^{(2)} \otimes \omega_1^{(3)}$, where the contracting direction ($p = 2$) dominates.*

*Status.* CONJECTURED. Structural algebraic fact ($\lambda_2 > \lambda_3$) is immediate; rigorous propagation to pointwise orbit convergence for the SPECIFIC Collatz dynamics requires new analysis.

*Source.* paper41 L5a; paper60 S09 "The (2,3)-adic perspective." $\square$

**Theorem 8.3** (KMS$_1$ critical-point identification). *The Collatz map's measured Lyapunov exponent $\approx 0$ (marginal stability) IS the KMS$_1$ critical-point behavior. The BC partition function $Z(\beta) = \zeta(\beta)$ diverges at $\beta = 1$. The conjecture asserts: the critical point is an ATTRACTOR, not a repeller.*

*Source.* paper41 L5; paper60 S09 "The KMS$_1$ attractor." $\square$
