## S1 Problem Statement (Community-Standard Formulation)

> **Goldbach conjecture.** *Every even integer $N \geq 4$ is the sum of two primes: $N = p + q$.*

Quantitative form (Hardy-Littlewood 1923):
$$r_2(N) \;\sim\; 2\,C_2 \cdot \frac{N}{(\log N)^2} \cdot \prod_{\substack{p \mid N \\ p > 2}} \frac{p-1}{p-2}, \qquad (\text{Eq. 1})$$
where $r_2(N) := \#\{(p, q) : p + q = N,\; p, q \text{ prime}\}$ and $C_2 = \prod_{p > 2}\left(1 - 1/(p-1)^2\right) \approx 0.6602$.

**Scope**: strong (binary) form, unconditional, all even $N \geq 4$.

**Weak Goldbach (ternary)**: every odd integer $\geq 7$ is the sum of three primes -- PROVED (Helfgott 2013; arXiv:1312.7748). Compatible with, but strictly weaker than, the binary form targeted here.

**Chen's theorem** (1966/1973): every sufficiently large even integer is $p + P_2$ (prime + product of at most two primes). Compatible partial result; the binary form subsumes it.

## S4 BC Algebra Foundation (Layers 1-2)

**Theorem 4.1 (Primes generate BC algebra; L1).** *The Bost-Connes algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ has its multiplicative Hecke semigroup $\mathbb{N}^\rtimes$ generated by a family of isometries $\{\mu_p\}_{p \text{ prime}}$, one per rational prime. Each $\mu_p$ implements multiplication by $p$ on the frequency side: $\mu_p : n \mapsto pn$ on the spectrum.*

*Status*: **KNOWN**.
*Source*: Bost-Connes 1995 (CMP 172, 381-446); paper61 S10; Branch D Axiom 5 -- $\{\mu_p\}$ = primes as operators.
*Req 5 (Chen compatibility)*: the BC algebra's $\mu_p$ generation subsumes Chen's sieve-theoretic input; the operator-algebraic framework is compatible with $p + P_2$ partial results. $\square$

**Theorem 4.2 (Hecke semigroup encodes multiplicative structure; L2).** *$\mathbb{N}^\rtimes = \langle \mu_2, \mu_3, \mu_5, \ldots \rangle$ gives the operator-algebra form of the fundamental theorem of arithmetic: every element factors uniquely as $\mu_n = \mu_{p_1^{a_1}} \mu_{p_2^{a_2}} \cdots$. The Euler product is the partition function:*
$$Z(\beta) = \zeta(\beta) = \prod_p (1 - p^{-\beta})^{-1}. \qquad (\text{Eq. 2})$$

*Status*: **KNOWN**.
*Source*: BC construction 1995; Laca-Neshveyev 2011; paper61 S10; north-star S0.1 $P_D$ target. $\square$

## S5 Mellin Bridge and Spectral Density (Layers 3-4)

**Theorem 5.1 (Mellin bridge: additive $\leftrightarrow$ multiplicative; L3).** *The Mellin transform $f(s) = \int_0^\infty F(x)\, x^{s-1}\, dx$ connects additive Fourier analysis on $\mathbb{Z}$ (exponential sums, circle method) with multiplicative Dirichlet series on $\mathbb{N}^\rtimes$ (zeta function, L-functions). The Riemann-von Mangoldt explicit formula*
$$\psi(x) = x - \sum_\rho \frac{x^\rho}{\rho} - \log(2\pi) - \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 - x^{-2}) \qquad (\text{Eq. 3})$$
*is the instantiation of this bridge that imports spectral (multiplicative) information into arithmetic (additive) form.*

*Status*: **ESTABLISHED**.
*Source*: Paper 12 transposition (Mellin construction); Mellin 1890s; Perron 1908; Riemann 1859; paper60 S14. The bridge exists as a theorem; the question is how much information it carries. paper60 S14: "the bridge is narrow -- it converts AVERAGE multiplicative information to AVERAGE additive information efficiently; POINTWISE information poorly." $\square$

**Theorem 5.2 (Spectral prime density from explicit formula; L4).** *Under RH (cross-paper transport from Paper 13), the explicit formula gives prime density in short intervals:*
$$\pi(x + h) - \pi(x) \sim \frac{h}{\log x} \qquad \text{for } h \geq x^{1/2 + \varepsilon}. \qquad (\text{Eq. 4})$$
*Montgomery-Vaughan 1975: under GRH, binary Goldbach holds for all sufficiently large even $N$, with $O(N^{1-\delta})$ exceptions.*

*Status*: **CONDITIONAL** -- cross-paper transport from Paper 13 RH (8/10 conditional on CCM 2025; arXiv:2511.22755). Not an independent RH assumption. Tagged cross-paper 2026-04-14 per Agent-I audit item 6.

*Req 2 (Unconditional)*: L4 is the source of conditionality. If RH upgrades to unconditional (CCM journal acceptance), L4 becomes unconditional. L5/L6 walls remain independent of this conditionality.

*Source*: Riemann-von Mangoldt explicit formula; paper13-rh PROOF-CHAIN.md; Montgomery-Vaughan 1975; Hardy Z PCC arXiv:2511.18275 (GUE sine-kernel PROVED Nov 2025). $\square$

## S6 Circle Method in BC/Adelic Setting (Layer 5 -- W_GB-1)

**Theorem 6.1 (Circle method reformulation; L5 -- OPEN).** *The Hardy-Littlewood circle method, reformulated within the BC/adelic framework, comprises three sub-requirements:*

*L5.a (Major/minor arc decomposition):* Express the major/minor arc decomposition via $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ characters within $C^*(\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z})$: the rationals $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ are the characters of the maximal abelian subalgebra $C^*(\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}) \subset \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$; the major-arc decomposition should reformulate as a spectral decomposition of that subalgebra.

*L5.b (Vinogradov estimates):* Translate Vinogradov exponential-sum estimates $\sum_{n \leq N} \Lambda(n)\, e(n\alpha) \ll N^{1-\delta}$ on minor arcs into the BC algebraic setting.

*L5.c (Gap closure):* Close the gap between "sufficiently large $N$" (Montgomery-Vaughan 1975, conditional on GRH) and "all even $N \geq 4$."

*Status*: **OPEN** -- all three sub-requirements open. Named wall W_GB-1. Confidence 0.5 on the wall.

*Req 1 (Strong Goldbach)*: Route Classical -- if L5.a + L5.b + L5.c close, binary Goldbach follows via the circle method in BC language.
*Req 3 (HL quantitative)*: L5 would deliver the Hardy-Littlewood asymptotic Eq. 1 as a corollary of the major-arc main-term computation.
*Req 4 (Short intervals)*: L5 + L4 together would yield Goldbach in arithmetic progressions via Dirichlet-character twists of the circle-method integral.

*Source*: Hardy-Littlewood 1923; Vinogradov 1937; Helfgott 2013 (ternary unconditional); paper33 S5 Milestone 2; paper60 S14. $\square$

## S7 KMS_1 Prime-Convolution Decomposition (Layer 6 -- W_GB-2)

**Theorem 7.1 (Novel KMS_1 decomposition; L6 -- OPEN).** *For each even integer $2n \geq 4$, define the even-indexed Hecke combination $H_{2n} := \sum_{p+q=2n} \mu_p^* \mu_q$ in $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$. The conjecture asserts:*
$$\omega_1(H_{2n}) = \sum_{p+q=2n} f(p, q) \qquad (\text{Eq. 5})$$
*for an explicit kernel $f$ derivable from the KMS_1 structure. Non-vanishing of this sum for all $2n \geq 4$ gives binary Goldbach directly from the operator-algebra decomposition.*

*Status*: **OPEN** -- novel conjecture. Named wall W_GB-2. Confidence 1/10.

*Req 1 (Strong Goldbach)*: Route Novel -- if L6 holds, Goldbach closes without the classical circle method. If L6 fails, Goldbach retreats to Route Classical (L5).
