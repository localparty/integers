  Calculations that would make the paper airtight:

  1. The hydrogen atom in 5D. The paper opens saying QM "predicts hydrogen to twelve decimal places." We
  should show E_n = −13.6/n² eV falls out of the 5D Schrödinger equation with Coulomb potential.
  Straightforward and expected by reviewers.
  2. Black hole entropy from the e-circle. If the theory is finite, it should predict the
  Bekenstein-Hawking entropy S = A/(4l_P²). The KK modes on the e-circle near a horizon give a natural
  state-counting. This would be the paper's single most impressive result — deriving black hole
  thermodynamics from the e-dimension.
  3. Gravitational wave signatures. The massive KK graviton modes have additional polarizations beyond
  the two of standard GR. What does LIGO/LISA see (or not see) from the e-dimension? At m₁ ~ 10 meV, the
  KK modes are far above GW detector frequencies — but stating this explicitly with the numbers closes a
  question reviewers will ask.
  4. Running of coupling constants. Above the KK scale E_KK ~ ℏc/R ~ 10 meV, the theory is 5D. The gauge
  couplings run differently in 5D than 4D (power-law vs logarithmic). Does this modify unification? Even
  a negative result (the KK scale is too low to affect unification) is worth stating.
  5. FRW cosmology in 5D. The Friedmann equations modified by the e-circle. Does the scalar field
  (dilaton) affect inflation, the CMB, Big Bang nucleosynthesis? The scalar mass of ~10 meV makes it
  dynamical at late times — is it a dark energy candidate beyond just Casimir?
  6. The CPT theorem in 5D. The spin-statistics derivation (Appendix B) is one half of the
  CPT-spin-statistics connection. The other half — CPT invariance — should follow from the 5D Lorentz
  structure. This closes the loop with Streater-Wightman.

  Visualizations that would complete the suite:

  7. KK black hole — The e-circle shrinking at the horizon. Shows how the 5D geometry differs from
  Schwarzschild.
  8. Casimir energy on the e-circle — Bosonic modes (periodic) vs fermionic modes (anti-periodic),
  showing how the SM field content produces positive vacuum energy.
  9. Perturbative finiteness — The KK mode sum converging under zeta regularization. Show the partial
  sums approaching the zeta-regularized value, making the mechanism visual.
  10. Coupling constant running — α, α_weak, α_strong as functions of energy, showing the KK scale where
  5D behavior kicks in.
