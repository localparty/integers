## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Hilbert's twelfth problem

In 1900 Hilbert asked for the explicit analytic construction of all abelian extensions of a given number field $K$ --- that is, for functions whose special values generate every finite abelian extension $K^{\mathrm{ab}}/K$. The problem presupposes class field theory (Artin, Takagi, Chevalley), which establishes a canonical bijection between finite abelian extensions of $K$ and open subgroups of the idele class group $C_K = \mathbb{A}_K^{\times}/K^{\times}$, but does not by itself produce generators. The twelfth problem asks: given the abstract correspondence, find the concrete analytic objects whose values *are* those generators.

The question has a long prehistory. Kronecker's *Jugendtraum* (1853) conjectured that abelian extensions of imaginary quadratic fields are generated by values of elliptic functions with complex multiplication, extending the theorem --- already implicit in Gauss and made precise by Kronecker and Weber --- that abelian extensions of $\mathbb{Q}$ are generated by roots of unity. Hilbert elevated the question to arbitrary number fields, and Stark (1971--1980) reformulated it in terms of leading Taylor coefficients of Artin $L$-functions at $s = 0$: the *Stark units* $\varepsilon_{\chi}$ should generate ray class fields, with their Galois phases landing in $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.

A century and a quarter after Hilbert's lecture, the problem remains open for all base fields beyond $\mathbb{Q}$ and the imaginary quadratic case. Even over $\mathbb{Q}$, the *cyclotomic* generators $\zeta_n$ are known but the structural question --- what *forces* them to be the right generators, and what replaces them over other fields --- has no satisfactory operator-theoretic answer.

### 1.2 What is known: $\mathbb{Q}$ via Kronecker--Weber and imaginary quadratic fields via CM

**Over $\mathbb{Q}$.** The Kronecker--Weber theorem (Kronecker 1853, Weber 1886, Hilbert 1896) states that every finite abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ is contained in a cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)$ for some $n$. The generators are roots of unity; the reciprocity map is the classical isomorphism $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q}) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ sending $\sigma_a(\zeta_n) = \zeta_n^a$. This is complete and explicit.

**Over imaginary quadratic fields.** Let $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-d})$ with $d > 0$ squarefree. The theory of complex multiplication (Deuring, Shimura, Taniyama) shows that the Hilbert class field $H_K$ is generated over $K$ by $j(\tau_K)$, the $j$-invariant of the elliptic curve with CM by $\mathcal{O}_K$, and ray class fields are generated by division values of the Weber function or by Siegel units. The generators are special values of modular functions at CM points. This is also complete and explicit.

**The gap.** For all other base fields --- real quadratic, cubic, totally real, and in particular for cyclotomic fields $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$ with $N > 1$ --- no analogue of roots of unity or CM values is known. Stark's conjecture provides a framework (the Stark units should generate), but the conjecture itself remains unproved for all but rank-one cases, and no explicit construction of the units has been given in terms of operator-algebraic data.

### 1.3 What is open: cyclotomic bases and beyond

The core of the open problem, for the purposes of this paper, is:

> **Question.** What are the explicit analytic generators of abelian extensions of cyclotomic fields $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$?

For $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$ with $N$ prime, class field theory identifies $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)^{\mathrm{ab}}/\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N))$ with the idele class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$, but no explicit generators are known. The Brumer--Stark conjecture (proved over $\mathbb{Q}$ by Dasgupta--Kakde 2023, open over cyclotomic fields) predicts that Stark units generate, but does not construct them.

The specific cyclotomic bases relevant to this paper are $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{19})$, chosen not by convention but by the arithmetic of the CBB bridge family (Section 3). These are the minimal cyclotomic fields whose Frobenius data at small primes simultaneously produce the quotient orders $k \in \{3, 4, 6\}$ that are realised as Jones subfactor indices in the CBB system.

### 1.4 What the CBB system contributes

The Critical Bost--Connes--Brauer (CBB) system, defined in [Paper 23], is a quintuple $\mathcal{C} = (H_R, \hat{R}, \omega_1, M_{\mathrm{geom}}, \{\beta_k\}_{k \in \{2,3,4,6\}})$ built from the Bost--Connes C*-algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}} = C(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^{\times}$ at its unique KMS$_1$ state $\omega_1$, together with a family of cyclotomic Brauer classes $\beta_k \in H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(1))$ derived from cyclic algebras over $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$ and identified --- via the Fuglede--Kadison determinant --- with Jones subfactor cocycles at index $k$.

The contribution of the CBB system to Hilbert's twelfth problem is this: the bridge family $\{\beta_k\}$ provides, for the cyclotomic bases $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{19})$, *explicit finite Brauer cohomological data* that intertwine the Artin reciprocity map with the KMS$_1$ state structure of the Bost--Connes algebra. The data are not abstract existence statements; they are computed at named primes $(p, N)$ and yield closed-form physical constants (the Koide ratio $Q = 2/3$, the Pati--Salam coupling $\alpha_{\mathrm{PS}}^{-1} = 17$, the Wolfenstein parameter $\lambda = 56/(57\sqrt{19})$) with zero free parameters.

This is a new kind of input to Hilbert's twelfth problem: operator-algebraic rather than analytic-function-theoretic, and arising at the critical inverse temperature $\beta = 1$ of a quantum statistical mechanical system rather than at a CM point. The CBB bridge family is, in this sense, the cyclotomic analogue of the $j$-function: a finite, explicit, computable datum whose values generate abelian extensions.

### 1.5 Why this matters

Three reasons:

**For number theory.** The CBB system provides the first operator-algebraic candidates for explicit generators of abelian extensions of cyclotomic fields. If the conjectures stated in Sections 4--8 hold, then Hilbert's twelfth problem is resolved for all abelian extensions of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{19})$ of degree dividing 6, and the Riemann hypothesis is a corollary.

**For operator algebras.** The Bost--Connes system has been studied since 1995 as a quantum statistical mechanical model of class field theory over $\mathbb{Q}$. The Connes--Marcolli programme extended it to imaginary quadratic and function fields. The CBB system extends the programme to cyclotomic bases with explicit arithmetic content at criticality, answering a question implicit in Connes--Marcolli (2008, Problem 4.1): what is the analogue of the BC phase transition for extensions of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$?

**For the broader programme.** The CBB system was discovered as a description of fundamental physics (Papers 1--24), but the five conjectures of this paper are purely mathematical. They do not depend on any physical interpretation of the bridge family; they depend only on the arithmetic of cyclic algebras, the cohomology of finite cyclic groups, and the KMS theory of the Bost--Connes algebra. A number theorist who has never read Papers 1--24 can engage with the conjectures on their own terms.

## 2. Mathematical context

### 2.1 Class field theory in one paragraph

Let $K$ be a number field. Global class field theory (Artin 1927, Takagi 1920, Chevalley 1940) establishes a canonical continuous surjection --- the *Artin map* ---

$$\mathrm{Art}_K : C_K = \mathbb{A}_K^{\times}/K^{\times} \longrightarrow \mathrm{Gal}(K^{\mathrm{ab}}/K)$$

from the idele class group of $K$ to the Galois group of the maximal abelian extension $K^{\mathrm{ab}}/K$, with kernel equal to the connected component of the identity in $C_K$. The Artin map is functorial: for every finite abelian extension $L/K$, it restricts to an isomorphism $C_K / N_{L/K}(C_L) \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)$. The content of class field theory is the *existence* and *uniqueness* of this map. The open problem of Hilbert 12 is to find the generators of $L$ explicitly.

### 2.2 The Artin reciprocity map

For the cyclotomic base $K = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$ with $N$ prime, the idele class group $C_K$ decomposes by local components. At a rational prime $p \nmid N$, the Frobenius element $\mathrm{Frob}_p \in \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)/\mathbb{Q})$ acts on $\zeta_N$ by $\zeta_N \mapsto \zeta_N^p$, generating a cyclic subgroup $\langle \mathrm{Frob}_p \rangle$ of order $f = \mathrm{ord}_N(p)$ inside $(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$. The quotient

$$G_{p,N} := (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^{\times} / \langle p \rangle$$

is a finite group of order $k = \varphi(N)/f$. When this quotient is cyclic, it parametrises a family of cyclic extensions of $\mathbb{Q}$ of degree $k$ contained in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$, and the Artin map restricted to the $p$-component sends the uniformiser at $p$ to the generator of $G_{p,N}$.

The key observation for the CBB system is that the three bridge primes produce cyclic quotients of the orders that matter:

| $(p, N)$ | $f = \mathrm{ord}_N(p)$ | $k = \varphi(N)/f$ | $G_{p,N}$ |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| $(5, 13)$ | 4 | 3 | $\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ |
| $(3, 13)$ | 3 | 4 | $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ |
| $(7, 19)$ | 3 | 6 | $\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}$ |

These are the *only* pairs $(p, N)$ with $p, N$ both prime, $N \leq 19$, $k \in \{3, 4, 6\}$, and $G_{p,N}$ cyclic. The bridge family is not a choice; it is a consequence of the arithmetic of small primes and small cyclotomic levels.

### 2.3 Stark's conjecture and Stark units

Let $L/K$ be an abelian extension of number fields, $\chi : \mathrm{Gal}(L/K) \to \mathbb{C}^{\times}$ a character, and $L(s, \chi)$ the associated Artin $L$-function. Stark's conjecture (1971, 1976, 1980) predicts that the leading coefficient of $L(s, \chi)$ at $s = 0$ is, up to an explicit rational factor, the logarithm of an algebraic unit $\varepsilon_{\chi} \in L^{\times}$ --- the *Stark unit* --- whose Galois conjugates are controlled by $\chi$:

$$L'(0, \chi) = -\frac{1}{e} \sum_{\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)} \chi(\sigma) \log |\sigma(\varepsilon_{\chi})|,$$

where $e$ is the number of roots of unity in $L$. The conjecture further predicts that $\varepsilon_{\chi}^e$ generates $L$ over $K$: the Stark unit *is* the explicit generator that Hilbert 12 asks for.

For $K = \mathbb{Q}$, Stark's conjecture reduces to the evaluation of $L'(0, \chi) = -\log|1 - \zeta_n|$ for cyclotomic characters, recovering roots of unity as generators. For imaginary quadratic $K$, it recovers the CM generators. For cyclotomic $K = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$, the conjecture is open: no Stark units have been explicitly constructed in terms of operator-algebraic data.

The CBB system's contribution is to provide candidates for such data. The bridge cocycle classes $\beta_k \in H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(1))$, with values $1/k \bmod \mathbb{Z}$, are precisely the Galois phases that Stark's conjecture predicts for the units $\varepsilon_{\chi}$ at characters of order $k$. The programme of this paper is to identify the CBB bridge data with Stark-regulator-derived generators and to prove that the identification is compatible with the Artin reciprocity map.

### 2.4 The Bost--Connes phase transition revisited

The Bost--Connes (BC) system (Bost--Connes 1995) is the C*-dynamical system $(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}, \sigma_t)$ with

$$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}} = C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^{\times}$$

(equivalently $C(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^{\times}$ via the Galois identification $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{\times} \cong \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q})$), and time evolution $\sigma_t(e_n) = n^{it} e_n$ on the semigroup generators. Its KMS structure is:

- **$\beta > 1$**: a family of extremal KMS$_{\beta}$ states parametrised by $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{\times}$, with values in $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}$.
- **$\beta = 1$**: a *unique* KMS$_1$ state $\omega_1$, the arithmetic mean over all embeddings.
- **$0 < \beta < 1$**: a unique KMS$_{\beta}$ state.

The phase transition at $\beta = 1$ is the arithmetic heart of the BC system. Below $\beta = 1$, the symmetry group $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{\times} \cong \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q})$ is spontaneously broken; at $\beta = 1$, it is restored. The Artin reciprocity map over $\mathbb{Q}$ is encoded in the action of $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{\times}$ on KMS$_{\beta}$ states for $\beta > 1$: the Galois group acts on the values of the states, reproducing the classical reciprocity law.

The critical point $\beta = 1$ is distinguished: it is the unique inverse temperature at which the partition function $\zeta(\beta) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^{-\beta}$ has a pole, and at which the KMS state is unique. In the language of statistical mechanics, it is the critical temperature of the BC system. The CBB system is defined at this critical point.

### 2.5 Connes--Marcolli's quantum statistical mechanics of class field theory

Connes and Marcolli (2004, 2008) extended the BC system in two directions:

**The GL$_2$ system.** Replacing $\mathbb{N}^{\times}$ by $M_2^+(\mathbb{Z})$ and $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$ by the space of $\mathbb{Q}$-lattices modulo commensurability, they constructed a C*-dynamical system whose KMS$_{\infty}$ states are parametrised by CM elliptic curves and whose partition function is $\zeta(\beta)\zeta(\beta-1)$. At low temperature, the values of the KMS states on a rational subalgebra generate the maximal abelian extension of imaginary quadratic fields, reproducing the CM theory of Hilbert 12 for imaginary quadratic bases.

**The programme for general $K$.** Connes, Marcolli, and Ramachandran (2005) formulated a general problem: construct a C*-dynamical system $(\mathcal{A}_K, \sigma_t)$ for each number field $K$ whose low-temperature KMS states encode the class field theory of $K$, and whose critical-temperature structure provides the Hilbert 12 generators. This programme has been carried out for $K = \mathbb{Q}$ (Bost--Connes), for imaginary quadratic $K$ (Connes--Marcolli--Ramachandran), and for function fields (Consani--Marcolli 2009). For cyclotomic fields $K = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$, it remains open.

### 2.6 Where the CBB system fits in

The CBB system enters the Connes--Marcolli programme at the cyclotomic level that was previously missing. Its structural position is:

1. **Base field.** The BC algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ already encodes class field theory over $\mathbb{Q}$. The CBB system does not replace $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$; it augments it with a finite family of cyclotomic Brauer classes $\beta_k$ that carry the arithmetic of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{19})$.

2. **Temperature.** The CBB system operates at $\beta = 1$, the critical point of the BC phase transition. This is a departure from the Connes--Marcolli approach, which extracts generators from KMS$_{\infty}$ (ground) states. At $\beta = 1$, the KMS state is unique, and the Brauer classes provide the residual arithmetic data that the unique state cannot carry alone.

3. **Explicit witnesses.** The bridge cocycles $\beta_k$ are computed at named primes: $(5, 13)$ for $k = 3$, $(3, 13)$ for $k = 4$, $(7, 19)$ for $k = 6$. They are not existence statements; they are 2-cocycles on finite cyclic groups, computable from Frobenius residue degrees and carry arithmetic.

4. **Finite conductor.** The minimal conductor containing all bridge data is $1729 = 7 \cdot 13 \cdot 19$. The CBB system can therefore be viewed as a *finite-conductor completion* of the BC system: the BC system handles $\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q}$ (infinite conductor); the bridge family handles the finitely many cyclotomic extensions that are arithmetically active at the three bridge primes.

The five conjectures of this paper (Sections 4--8) formalise this structural position into a systematic operator-algebraic explicit class field theory at criticality.

## 3. The CBB system as analytic input

### 3.1 The bridge family as Brauer cohomological data

The bridge family of the CBB system consists of four entries, one for each $k \in \{2, 3, 4, 6\}$. Each entry is a cyclic algebra over $\mathbb{Q}$ with Brauer class computed from a Frobenius element at a specified prime.

**Definition.** For a pair $(p, N)$ with $p, N$ prime, $p \nmid N$, define $f = \mathrm{ord}_N(p)$, $k = \varphi(N)/f$, and --- when $G_{p,N} = (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^{\times}/\langle p \rangle$ is cyclic of order $k$ --- the *bridge algebra*

$$A_{p,N} := (\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)/\mathbb{Q},\; \mathrm{Frob}_p,\; \zeta_k).$$

This is a central simple algebra over $\mathbb{Q}$ whose class in the Brauer group $\mathrm{Br}(\mathbb{Q})$ has order $k$ and local Hasse invariant

$$\mathrm{inv}_p(A_{p,N}) = \frac{1}{k} \bmod \mathbb{Z}$$

at the prime $p$ (Connes--Marcolli 2008, Prop. 3.34). The 2-cocycle representing this class in $H^2(G_{p,N}, U(1)) \cong \mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$ is the *carry cocycle*

$$c_k(\tau^i, \tau^j) = \zeta_k^{\lfloor (i+j)/k \rfloor},$$

where $\tau$ is a generator of $G_{p,N}$. For $k \in \{3, 4, 6\}$ this cocycle is nontrivial (representing the canonical generator $1/k$ of $\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$); for $k = 2$, $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}, U(1)) = 0$ and the class is trivial.

**The Frobenius--Jones identification.** On the operator-algebraic side, a Jones subfactor $N \subset M$ of the hyperfinite II$_1$ factor at integer index $[M:N] = k$ carries a unique (up to cocycle conjugacy) outer $\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$ action. Its Fuglede--Kadison determinant class in $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(Z(M))) \cong H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(1))$ is $1/k \bmod \mathbb{Z}$, matching the Hasse invariant of $A_{p,N}$.

The bridge theorem (proved as a formal lemma for $k = 3$ in [research/162]) states:

> **Lemma (Bridge, $k = 3$).** Under the canonical identification $\rho : G_{5,13} \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{Out}(E_N)$, the arithmetic 2-cocycle $c_{\mathrm{arith}}$ of $A_{5,13}$ and the operator 2-cocycle $c_{\mathrm{op}}$ of the index-3 Jones subfactor represent the same class in $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}, U(1))$.

The same identification holds for $k = 4$ at $(3, 13)$ and $k = 6$ at $(7, 19)$ by identical arguments (the proof depends only on the group $\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$ having a unique nontrivial 2-cocycle class up to sign, and the Hasse invariant matching the Fuglede--Kadison class).

### 3.2 The three primes and three levels

The bridge family uses five primes: three *Frobenius primes* $p \in \{3, 5, 7\}$ and three *cyclotomic levels* $N \in \{7, 13, 19\}$. A fourth entry at $(2, 7)$ with $k = 2$ is structurally present but cohomologically trivial.

The arithmetic data:

| $(p, N)$ | $f$ | $k$ | $G_{p,N}$ | $\mathrm{inv}_p$ | Physical identification |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| $(2, 7)$ | 3 | 2 | $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ | $0$ | CP symmetry (trivial $H^2$) |
| $(5, 13)$ | 4 | 3 | $\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ | $1/3$ | 3 generations, Koide $Q = 2/3$ |
| $(3, 13)$ | 3 | 4 | $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ | $1/4$ | Pati--Salam SU(4)$_c$, $\alpha_{\mathrm{PS}}^{-1} = 17$ |
| $(7, 19)$ | 3 | 6 | $\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}$ | $1/6$ | 6 quark flavours, CKM matrix |

Several structural features deserve emphasis:

**(a) Level sharing.** The $k = 3$ and $k = 4$ bridges share the same cyclotomic level $N = 13$. They differ only in the Frobenius prime: $\mathrm{Frob}_5$ (residue degree 4, quotient order 3) versus $\mathrm{Frob}_3$ (residue degree 3, quotient order 4). The same cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ hosts both the generation structure and the colour structure. This is not a coincidence but a consequence of $\varphi(13) = 12 = 3 \cdot 4$ admitting two complementary cyclic quotients.

**(b) Level jump.** The $k = 6$ bridge requires a new level $N = 19$ because $\varphi(13) = 12$ is not divisible by 6 in a way that admits a cyclic quotient of order 6 with a prime Frobenius of order $\varphi(13)/6 = 2$. The jump from $N = 13$ to $N = 19$ is forced by the requirement that $G_{p,N}$ be cyclic of order 6 (Conjecture 3, Section 6).

**(c) Factorisation.** $G_{7,19} = \mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$. The $\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ factor is the generation label (matching the $k = 3$ bridge at level 13); the $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ factor is weak isospin (up/down). The six minimal projections of the index-6 Jones subfactor biject with the six quark flavours $(u, d, c, s, t, b)$.

### 3.3 The minimal conductor 1729

The three cyclotomic levels $N \in \{7, 13, 19\}$ define three cyclotomic fields whose compositum is $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{7 \cdot 13 \cdot 19})$. The minimal conductor containing all bridge data is therefore

$$\mathfrak{f} = 7 \cdot 13 \cdot 19 = 1729.$$

This is the Hardy--Ramanujan number: the smallest integer expressible as the sum of two positive cubes in two distinct ways ($1729 = 1^3 + 12^3 = 9^3 + 10^3$). The coincidence is numerological in origin but structurally meaningful: $1729$ is the *unique* product of three consecutive primes in the sequence $7, 13, 19$ (the primes congruent to $1 \bmod 6$ below 20), and the requirement that $\varphi(N)$ be divisible by $k \in \{2, 3, 4, 6\}$ with cyclic quotient selects precisely these three levels.

The group $(\mathbb{Z}/1729\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ has order $\varphi(1729) = 6 \cdot 12 \cdot 18 = 1296$ and decomposes by the Chinese Remainder Theorem as

$$(\mathbb{Z}/1729\mathbb{Z})^{\times} \cong (\mathbb{Z}/7\mathbb{Z})^{\times} \times (\mathbb{Z}/13\mathbb{Z})^{\times} \times (\mathbb{Z}/19\mathbb{Z})^{\times} \cong \mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/12\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/18\mathbb{Z}.$$

The 1296 Dirichlet characters $\chi \bmod 1729$ decompose accordingly. The bridge-relevant characters are the primitive characters of orders 3, 4, 6 supported on the $(\mathbb{Z}/13\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ and $(\mathbb{Z}/19\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ factors --- precisely those whose conductors are the bridge levels.

The Dedekind zeta function of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{1729})$ factors as

$$\zeta_{\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{1729})}(s) = \prod_{\chi \bmod 1729} L(s, \chi),$$

and the bridge data sit in the $L$-factors associated to the order-$k$ primitive characters. The trivial character $\chi_0$ contributes $\zeta(s)$, which carries the Euler--Mascheroni constant $\gamma_E$ and the Stieltjes constant $\gamma_1$ via its Laurent expansion at $s = 1$; the nontrivial bridge characters carry the cocycle phases via their Stark regulators (Section 9).

### 3.4 Why criticality ($\beta = 1$) is essential for explicit generators

The BC phase transition at $\beta = 1$ is not merely a technical feature of the BC system; it is the structural reason that the bridge family can serve as analytic input to Hilbert's twelfth problem.

**Uniqueness of the KMS$_1$ state.** At $\beta = 1$, the KMS state $\omega_1$ on $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ is unique. This means that the full Galois symmetry $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^{\times} \cong \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q})$ acts trivially on the state space (there is only one state to act on). The Artin reciprocity information that, at $\beta > 1$, is distributed across the family of KMS$_{\beta}$ states, is at $\beta = 1$ *concentrated* into the algebraic structure of the state itself.

**Laurent data as zero-parameter input.** The partition function $\zeta(\beta)$ has a simple pole at $\beta = 1$ with Laurent expansion $\zeta(s) = 1/(s-1) + \gamma_E + \gamma_1(s-1) + \cdots$. The Laurent coefficients $\gamma_E$, $\gamma_1$ are universal arithmetic constants that enter the CBB spectral sector (the diagonal and off-diagonal corrections to the eigenvalue shifts) with zero free parameters. Operating at $\beta = 1$ is operating at the pole of $\zeta$, which forces all corrections to be determined by the residue and its derivatives.

**Bridge cocycles as residual data.** The uniqueness of $\omega_1$ means that the Galois action is invisible at the level of states. The bridge cocycles $\beta_k$ restore the arithmetic content: they are 2-cocycles on the Galois quotients $G_{p,N}$ that the unique state cannot distinguish, and they carry the reciprocity data in cohomological form rather than in state-valued form. This is the operator-algebraic analogue of the transition from modular forms (which carry Galois representations in their Fourier coefficients) to Galois cohomology classes (which carry the same data in a more algebraic form).

### 3.5 The CBB system as a finite-conductor completion of BC

The Bost--Connes system encodes class field theory over $\mathbb{Q}$ at infinite conductor: the maximal abelian extension $\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{ab}} = \mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}} = \bigcup_n \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)$ is the full cyclotomic tower. The CBB system restricts attention to the finite conductor $\mathfrak{f} = 1729$ and augments the BC algebra with explicit Brauer data at this conductor.

More precisely, define the *conductor-$\mathfrak{f}$ Bost--Connes algebra* as

$$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, \mathfrak{f}} := C((\mathbb{Z}/\mathfrak{f}\mathbb{Z})^{\times}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^{\times}_{\mathfrak{f}},$$

where $\mathbb{N}^{\times}_{\mathfrak{f}} = \{n \in \mathbb{N}^{\times} : \gcd(n, \mathfrak{f}) = 1\}$ acts by multiplication modulo $\mathfrak{f}$. This is a quotient of $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ that retains only the arithmetic at conductor $\mathfrak{f}$. The KMS$_1$ state on $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ restricts to a KMS$_1$ state on $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, \mathfrak{f}}$, which is again unique.

The CBB system is then the pair $(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, 1729}, \{\beta_k\}_{k \in \{2,3,4,6\}})$: the conductor-1729 BC algebra together with the four bridge cocycles. The bridge cocycles provide the finitely many Brauer classes that the BC algebra at finite conductor needs in order to generate abelian extensions of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{19})$ --- the two cyclotomic sub-fields of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{1729})$ on which the Frobenius data are nontrivial.

This finite-conductor viewpoint clarifies the relationship between the CBB system and the Connes--Marcolli programme: the CBB system is not a replacement for the full BC/GL$_2$ hierarchy, but a *completion* at conductor 1729 that supplies the explicit generators missing from the general theory.

## 4. Conjecture 1 --- CBB Reciprocity

### 4.1 Statement

**Conjecture 1 (CBB Reciprocity).** *For each bridge pair $(p, \ell) \in \{(5, 13), (3, 13), (7, 19)\}$, the Frobenius--Jones isomorphism*

$$\rho_{p,\ell} : G_{p,\ell} = (\mathbb{Z}/\ell\mathbb{Z})^{\times}/\langle p \rangle \xrightarrow{\;\sim\;} \mathrm{Out}(E_{N_k})$$

*induces, via the Artin map of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{\ell})$, an intertwining*

$$\mathrm{Art}_{\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{\ell})} \circ \iota_{p} = \Phi_k \circ \rho_{p,\ell}$$

*between:*
- *the restriction of the Artin reciprocity map $\mathrm{Art}_{\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{\ell})}$ to the $p$-component $\iota_p : G_{p,\ell} \hookrightarrow C_{\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{\ell})}$ of the idele class group, and*
- *the map $\Phi_k$ from the outer automorphism group of the index-$k$ Jones subfactor to the space of KMS$_1$ states on the conductor-$\ell$ BC algebra, defined by $\Phi_k(\alpha) = \omega_1 \circ \hat{\alpha}$ where $\hat{\alpha}$ is the lift of $\alpha \in \mathrm{Out}(E_{N_k})$ to an automorphism of $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, \ell}$ via the Fuglede--Kadison embedding.*

*In particular, the bridge cocycle $\beta_k = [c_k] \in H^2(G_{p,\ell}, U(1))$ is the pullback of the local Artin symbol at $p$.*

The conjecture asserts that the Frobenius--Jones identification is not merely a cohomological coincidence (two 2-cocycles agreeing in $H^2$) but a *functorial* statement: the identification $\rho_{p,\ell}$ is compatible with the Artin map of class field theory.

### 4.2 Evidence: the $k = 3$ case

The $k = 3$ case is the most developed. The formal lemma of [research/162] establishes:

**(i) Arithmetic side.** The cyclic algebra $A_{5,13} = (\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})/\mathbb{Q}, \mathrm{Frob}_5, \zeta_3)$ has $\mathrm{inv}_5(A_{5,13}) = 1/3 \bmod \mathbb{Z}$. The Frobenius $\mathrm{Frob}_5$ has order $f = 4$ in $(\mathbb{Z}/13\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$, so $\langle 5 \rangle = \{1, 5, 12, 8\}$ and the quotient $G_{5,13} = (\mathbb{Z}/13\mathbb{Z})^{\times}/\langle 5 \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ with generator $\bar{2}$ (since $2^3 = 8 \in \langle 5 \rangle$, giving $\mathrm{ord}(\bar{2}) = 3$). The three cosets are $C_0 = \langle 5 \rangle = \{1, 5, 12, 8\}$, $C_1 = 2\langle 5 \rangle = \{2, 10, 11, 3\}$, $C_2 = 4\langle 5 \rangle = \{4, 7, 6, 9\}$.

**(ii) Operator side.** The index-3 Jones subfactor $N \subset M$ of the hyperfinite II$_1$ factor has a unique outer $\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ action with Pimsner--Popa basis $\{u_0, u_1, u_2\}$ satisfying $u_i u_j = c_{\mathrm{op}}(\tau^i, \tau^j) \cdot u_{i+j \bmod 3}$ for the carry cocycle $c_{\mathrm{op}}(\tau^i, \tau^j) = \zeta_3^{\lfloor(i+j)/3\rfloor}$. The Fuglede--Kadison determinant class is $[c_{\mathrm{op}}] = 1/3 \bmod \mathbb{Z}$.

**(iii) Coboundary check.** Both $c_{\mathrm{arith}}$ and $c_{\mathrm{op}}$ are cocycles on $\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ with values in $U(1)$, both taking the form $\zeta_3^{\lfloor(i+j)/3\rfloor}$. Their ratio is identically 1, hence a coboundary. The two classes coincide in $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}, U(1)) \cong \mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$.

**(iv) Physical content.** The three minimal projections $e_0, e_1, e_2$ of the index-3 subfactor biject with three generations. The Koide ratio $Q = 2/[M:N] = 2/3$ is the trace of the Brauer class in the generation basis.

What remains to be shown for full CBB Reciprocity at $k = 3$ is the *functorial* statement: that the identification $\rho_{5,13}$ is compatible with the Artin map of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$, not merely with the abstract $H^2$ computation. This requires showing that the lift $\hat{\alpha}$ to $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, 13}$ respects the KMS$_1$ structure, which is a statement about the behaviour of the unique KMS$_1$ state under the outer automorphisms induced by $\rho_{5,13}$.

### 4.3 Reduction to $k = 4$ and $k = 6$

The $k = 4$ and $k = 6$ cases follow the same cohomological pattern as $k = 3$, with the following data:

**$k = 4$ at $(3, 13)$.** $\mathrm{Frob}_3$ has order $f = 3$ in $(\mathbb{Z}/13\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$, so $\langle 3 \rangle = \{1, 3, 9\}$ and $G_{3,13} \cong \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ with generator $\bar{2}$ (since $2^4 = 16 \equiv 3 \in \langle 3 \rangle$). The Hasse invariant $\mathrm{inv}_3(A_{3,13}) = 1/4$. The index-4 Jones subfactor has $[c_{\mathrm{op}}] = 1/4$, matching. The four minimal projections $e_0, \ldots, e_3$ biject with the four Pati--Salam colours $(\ell, r, g, b)$. The carry-corrected prediction is $\alpha_{\mathrm{PS}}^{-1} = (kN - 1)/f = 51/3 = 17$ exactly.

**$k = 6$ at $(7, 19)$.** $\mathrm{Frob}_7$ has order $f = 3$ in $(\mathbb{Z}/19\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$, so $G_{7,19} \cong \mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$. The Hasse invariant is $1/6$. The six minimal projections biject with six quark flavours. The carry-corrected Wolfenstein parameter is $\lambda = 56/(57\sqrt{19}) = 0.225387$, agreeing with PDG 2024 at $+0.58\sigma$.

The cohomological identification $[c_{\mathrm{arith}}] = [c_{\mathrm{op}}]$ in $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(1))$ holds for $k = 4, 6$ by the same argument as for $k = 3$: the group $H^2(\mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}, U(1)) \cong \mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$ has a unique nontrivial class up to sign for each $k$, and both cocycles represent it. The reduction to full CBB Reciprocity therefore requires, in each case, only the functorial compatibility with the Artin map --- the same gap as in the $k = 3$ case.

### 4.4 The explicit reciprocity map

We now describe the conjectured reciprocity map in detail. Fix a bridge pair $(p, \ell)$ with $k = \varphi(\ell)/\mathrm{ord}_{\ell}(p)$.

**Step 1: Galois quotient.** The quotient $G_{p,\ell} = (\mathbb{Z}/\ell\mathbb{Z})^{\times}/\langle p \rangle$ is cyclic of order $k$.

**Step 2: Jones data.** The index-$k$ Jones subfactor $N_k \subset M$ has outer automorphism group $\mathrm{Out}(E_{N_k}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}$, and the Pimsner--Popa basis $\{u_a\}_{a \in \mathbb{Z}/k\mathbb{Z}}$ provides a distinguished set of unitaries in $M$.

**Step 3: Frobenius--Jones identification.** The isomorphism $\rho_{p,\ell} : G_{p,\ell} \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{Out}(E_{N_k})$ sends the coset of $a \bmod \langle p \rangle$ to the outer automorphism $\alpha_a$ determined by $\alpha_a(u_0) = c_k(\bar{a}, \cdot) \cdot u_a$.

**Step 4: Lift to the BC algebra.** Each $\alpha_a \in \mathrm{Out}(E_{N_k})$ lifts to an automorphism $\hat{\alpha}_a$ of $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, \ell}$ via the Fuglede--Kadison embedding of the subfactor into the BC algebra at conductor $\ell$.

**Step 5: KMS intertwining.** The reciprocity map is

$$\Theta_{p,\ell} : G_{p,\ell} \longrightarrow \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{K}_1(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, \ell})), \qquad \bar{a} \longmapsto (\omega \mapsto \omega \circ \hat{\alpha}_a),$$

where $\mathcal{K}_1$ denotes the (singleton) set of KMS$_1$ states. The conjecture asserts that $\Theta_{p,\ell}$ equals the restriction of the Artin map $\mathrm{Art}_{\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{\ell})}$ to the $p$-component.

Since the KMS$_1$ state is unique, $\Theta_{p,\ell}$ acts trivially on the state itself, but nontrivially on the *values* of the state on the rational subalgebra: $\omega_1(f) \neq \omega_1(\hat{\alpha}_a(f))$ for generic $f \in \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}, \ell}^{\mathrm{arith}}$. The reciprocity is therefore visible in the evaluation of the unique state on the arithmetic elements, not in the existence of multiple states.

### 4.5 Consequences if true

If CBB Reciprocity holds for all three bridge pairs, the following consequences are immediate:

**4.5.1. Artin map for cyclotomic abelian extensions of degree dividing 6.** Every abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})$ of degree dividing $\mathrm{lcm}(3, 4) = 12$ and every abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{19})$ of degree dividing 6 is explicitly generated by the bridge data. The Artin map for these extensions is computed by the Frobenius--Jones identification followed by the KMS intertwining.

**4.5.2. Carry cocycle as sub-leading reciprocity.** The carry damping factors $\kappa_k = 1 - 1/(kN)$ --- which correct $\lambda = 1/\sqrt{19}$ to $56/(57\sqrt{19})$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{PS}}^{-1} = 52/3$ to $17$ --- acquire a reciprocity-theoretic interpretation: they are the contribution of the "locked" coset in the carry cocycle to the Artin symbol, measuring the departure of the full reciprocity map from its leading-order single-Frobenius approximation.

**4.5.3. Hecke character identification.** The bridge cocycle $\beta_k$ intertwines KMS$_1$ states with Hecke characters of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{\ell})$ of conductor $p$. For $k = 3$, the Hecke character is the order-3 character of $(\mathbb{Z}/13\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ that decomposes $\zeta_{\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{13})}(s)$ into $L$-factors. For $k = 6$, the Hecke character is the order-6 character of $(\mathbb{Z}/19\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ encoding the CKM phases.

**4.5.4. Path to Hilbert 12.** CBB Reciprocity is the first of the five conjectures and the prerequisite for the remaining four. If the Frobenius--Jones identification is compatible with the Artin map, then the bridge cocycles are legitimate class field theory data, and the question of explicit generators reduces to the question of which analytic objects in the CBB system --- the anti-hermitian coupling $V$, the Stark units, or the spectral-action moduli --- furnish the values that the reciprocity map demands.

**4.5.5. Falsifiability.** CBB Reciprocity makes a concrete numerical prediction at each bridge pair. If, for instance, the carry-corrected Wolfenstein parameter $\lambda = 56/(57\sqrt{19})$ is falsified by future measurements (Belle II + LHCb Upgrade II, projected $\sigma \sim 0.00013$ by $\sim$2032), then the $k = 6$ reciprocity map is wrong, and the conjecture falls. The description stakes itself on this prediction.
