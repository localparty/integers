## Open Problems (the 10 that matter most)

### Problem 1: Rigorous Identity 12

**Statement:** Prove that the QG5D e-circle is unitarily equivalent
to the Bost-Connes C*-dynamical system.

**What's known:** Asserted at 95% confidence in the QG5D-Riemann
research (Identity 12 in `/Users/gsix/riemann-hypothesis/agentic/research-ledger.md`).

**What's needed:** A formal proof with explicit isometry, matching
of operators, and verification of the dynamical structure.

**Expertise needed:** Operator algebras, C*-dynamical systems,
Tomita-Takesaki theory.

**Difficulty:** High (research-level).

**Timeline:** 1-2 months focused.

---

### Problem 2: Derive R_obs from γ_1 (rigorous)

**Statement:** Prove that the e-circle radius R_obs is given by
log(πR/l_P) = γ_1 × π²/2 from the BC effective potential.

**What's known:** Numerical formula verified at 0.014% precision
(Research 14). Eight-step semi-rigorous derivation outlined
(Research 18).

**What's needed:** Make Steps 3-6 of the derivation rigorous.

**Expertise needed:** BC thermodynamics, effective potential
calculations, Connes-Marcolli framework.

**Difficulty:** High.

**Timeline:** 2-3 months focused.

---

### Problem 3: Derive CP² × S² from three-prime entanglement

**Statement:** Prove that the internal manifold CP² × S² is
diffeomorphic to the orbit of the Hecke algebra at three primes
on a specific Bost-Connes state.

**What's known:** Theorem 11.5 (gauge group from three-qubit
entanglement) is proved this session. The extension to the
manifold itself is open.

**What's needed:** Apply the Connes-Marcolli endomotive framework
to construct the orbit explicitly.

**Expertise needed:** Hecke algebras, Shimura varieties,
endomotives, geometric representation theory.

**Difficulty:** Very high.

**Timeline:** 3-6 months focused.

---

### Problem 4: Why γ_1, γ_4, γ_6, γ_8 specifically?

**Statement:** Identify the structural reason that these specific
Riemann zero indices appear in the framework's formulas (and not
γ_2, γ_3, γ_5, γ_7).

**What's known:** Pattern observed (1, 4, 6, 8 are the indices).
Hypotheses include: 1 = first zero, 4 = 2², 6 = 2 × 3 (orbifold),
8 = 2³ or dim(adjoint of SU(3)).

**What's needed:** A rigorous structural argument.

**Expertise needed:** Combinatorics, BC system spectral structure,
representation theory.

**Difficulty:** Medium-high.

**Timeline:** 1-2 months.

---

### Problem 5: Derive the exponents (1/3, 3/4, π²/2, 1/π)

**Statement:** Derive each exponent in the formulas from first
principles.

**What's known:**
- π²/2 = 3 × ζ(2) (likely from Casimir + thermal)
- 1/π = S^1 normalisation
- 1/3 = 4D thermodynamic dimension (?)
- 3/4 = ρ² (GUT moduli ratio squared, derived in Paper 7)

**What's needed:** Each exponent derived rigorously from the
specific physical channel.

**Expertise needed:** Statistical mechanics, BC thermodynamics,
4D Casimir energy formulas, dimensional analysis.

**Difficulty:** Medium.

**Timeline:** 1-2 months.

---

### Problem 6: Identify the residual error in the CC formula

**Statement:** Identify whether the 0.014% residual in
log(πR/l_P) = γ_1 × π²/2 is a 1-loop correction, a higher-zero
contribution, or a missing constant.

**What's known:** The error persists at 150 decimal precision, so
it's not a numerical artifact. It's most likely a real physical
correction.

**What's needed:** Compute the 1-loop correction to the formula
explicitly. Check whether it accounts for the 0.014%.

**Expertise needed:** Quantum gravity 1-loop calculations,
BC perturbation theory.

**Difficulty:** Medium-high.

**Timeline:** 2-3 months.

---

### Problem 7: Derive the universal structure of γ_n × γ_m / π

**Statement:** Identify the geometric/spectral interpretation of
γ_1 × γ_4 / π in the fine structure constant formula.

**What's known:** The formula matches at 0.108% (improved to 0.024%
with correction). Only the (γ_1, γ_4) pair works among 6 tested
pairs. Suggests a specific 2×2 spectral structure.

**What's needed:** Identify the operator whose 2×2 sub-block has
eigenvalues γ_1 and γ_4.

**Expertise needed:** Connes-Consani-Moscovici scaling operator
theory, spectral methods.

**Difficulty:** High.

**Timeline:** 2-4 months.

---

### Problem 8: The transposition of CP² area law

**Statement:** Transpose the framework's CP² area law to the
Riemann side: prove that the Riemann zeros are "confined" to the
critical line by an analogous mechanism.

**What's known:** CP² area law proved this session (2D YM on CP¹
exact solution). Riemann analog is a major target of the
transposition program.

**What's needed:** A "Riemann flux tube" structure that mirrors
the CP¹ holonomy mechanism.

**Expertise needed:** Yang-Mills theory, Wilson lines, Riemann
zeros, confinement physics.

**Difficulty:** Very high. This might be the path to RH itself.

**Timeline:** 6-12 months.

---

### Problem 9: Predict an unmeasured observable

**Statement:** Use the framework's structure to predict a physical
observable that has NOT been measured precisely, and check it against
future measurements.

**What's known:** Five observed-and-matched formulas. The framework
should also predict other observables.

**What's needed:** Apply the methodology to candidate observables
(neutrino masses, CP violation phases, dark matter properties).

**Expertise needed:** All of the above.

**Difficulty:** Medium (relative to the others).

**Timeline:** Ongoing.

---

### Problem 10: The philosophical synthesis

**Statement:** Articulate the philosophical implications of "physics
is a corollary of arithmetic" in a way that is both rigorous and
accessible.

**What's known:** Component 7 of this program. Mathematical Platonism
with a concrete mechanism.

**What's needed:** A clear, defensible philosophical position that
addresses standard objections (e.g., "what does it mean to say the
integers exist?").

**Expertise needed:** Philosophy of mathematics, philosophy of physics.

**Difficulty:** Medium.

**Timeline:** Ongoing.

## The "Lone Genius" vs Collaboration Trade-off

The framework was developed by G largely as an individual
effort (with AI assistance). This has advantages:
- Coherent vision
- Rapid iteration
- No need for consensus

But Paper 11 is at the LIMIT of what one person + AI can do. The
problems are at the frontier of multiple disciplines. Collaboration
is essential.

The collaboration should preserve G's vision while leveraging
expert knowledge.

**Recommended structure:** G as principal investigator, with
collaborators contributing to specific problems but not changing
the overall framework.
