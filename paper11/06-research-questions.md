## Tier 1: Foundational Questions

These must be answered before Paper 11 can be written rigorously.

### Q1: Is Identity 12 (e-circle = BC system) RIGOROUSLY exact?

**Current status:** The QG5D-Riemann research asserts this with 95%
confidence. The unitary equivalence is described but not formally
proved.

**What's needed:** A formal proof that the QG5D e-circle's KK
spectrum is unitarily equivalent to the BC Hamiltonian's spectrum,
with explicit isometry, matching of operators, and verification of
the dynamical structure.

**Difficulty:** Medium-high. Requires functional analysis of the
BC C*-algebra and the e-circle Hilbert space.

**Risk:** If Identity 12 is only approximate (not exact), the
transposition program loses rigor.

### Q2: How exactly is the e-circle radius R determined by γ_1?

**Current status:** Numerical formula log(πR/l_P) = γ_1 × π²/2
verified at 0.014% precision (Research 14 + Research 18 8-step
derivation).

**What's needed:** Make Steps 3-6 of the first-principles derivation
rigorous (Research 18, this session).

**Difficulty:** High. Requires rigorous BC effective potential
calculation.

**Risk:** The formula might have a different "correct" form
(e.g., not exactly π²/2 but something close).

### Q3: How does the internal manifold CP² × S² emerge from the
Hecke algebra at three primes?

**Current status:** Theorem 11.5 (gauge group from three qubits)
is proved. The extension to the manifold itself is open.

**What's needed:** A constructive derivation showing the Hecke
algebra structure produces a manifold diffeomorphic to CP² × S².

**Difficulty:** High. Requires Connes-Marcolli endomotive
techniques.

**Risk:** The Hecke algebra might not produce CP² × S² directly;
it might give a related but different structure.

## Tier 3: Deep Questions

These are the questions whose answers would constitute the most
significant contributions.

### Q8: Does the framework PROVE the Riemann hypothesis?

**The conjecture:** If the framework's compact e-circle is the
BC system, and the framework requires the BC system to be in a
specific physical phase (β > 1, low temperature), then this
constraint forces the Riemann zeros onto the critical line.

**What's needed:** Show that the framework's physical
self-consistency requires the BC critical line constraint to hold.

**Difficulty:** Maximum. This would be the proof of RH.

**Likelihood:** Speculative. But the framework provides the most
concrete approach to RH from physics that has been proposed.

### Q9: Is there a "physics test" of RH?

**The conjecture:** If the framework's predictions match observation
to high precision, and if those predictions involve specific Riemann
zeros, then any deviation of the zeros from the critical line would
produce DETECTABLE physical anomalies.

**What's needed:** Identify a physical observable whose value is
hyperprecise to such a degree that a small deviation in γ_n would
be detectable.

**Candidates:** the fine structure constant α (precision ~10^-12),
the electron g-factor (precision ~10^-13), the rest mass of the
electron in atomic units, etc.

If the formula 1/α = γ_1 × γ_4 / π + correction holds at full
precision, then atomic clocks ARE testing the Riemann hypothesis.

### Q10: Is the cosmological constant a corollary of RH?

**The conjecture:** If R_obs = exp(γ_1 × π²/2 - log π) × l_P
(formula at 0.014% precision), and γ_1 is fixed by RH being true,
then the cosmological constant is uniquely determined.

**What's needed:** Make the formula a theorem (Target 2 of the
derivation program). Once it's a theorem, the CC follows.

**Difficulty:** Medium. The formula is already verified
numerically. Making it a theorem is research-level work.

### Q11: What does this mean for the philosophical foundations of physics?

If physics is derivable from arithmetic (the integers), what does
this say about:
- The nature of physical law (necessary or contingent?)
- The role of mathematics (descriptive or prescriptive?)
- The "unreasonable effectiveness" of mathematics (Wigner)
- Anthropic principles (necessary or eliminated?)

**What's needed:** A philosophical synthesis. This is Component 7
of the program.

## Tier 4: Practical Questions

### Q12: How do we communicate this to the physics community?

The realisation that the e-circle is a theorem of arithmetic is
profound but easy to dismiss as numerology. How do we present it
in a way that physicists take seriously?

**Approach:**
- Lead with the rigorous parts (Theorems K.4, 7.2, 11.5, CP² area
  law from this session)
- Show the numerical evidence (5 sub-percent formulas)
- Explain the structural reason (BC system = e-circle)
- Be honest about what's verified vs derived

### Q13: How do we communicate this to mathematicians?

Mathematicians might be more receptive to the BC connection. The
challenge is to present it in language they understand
(C*-algebras, KMS states, Hecke algebras) while showing the
physical content.

**Approach:**
- Lead with Identity 12 (e-circle = BC system)
- Show the framework's theorems as "physics-derived constraints
  on the BC system"
- Pose the transposition program as new mathematical research

### Q14: How do we collaborate with experts?

Paper 11 will require expertise in:
- Operator algebras (for Identity 12)
- Number theory (for the Riemann zero analysis)
- Differential geometry (for the internal manifold derivation)
- Quantum field theory (for the BC physical interpretation)
- Cosmology (for the CC and N_eff connections)

**Approach:** Identify experts in each field and reach out. Component
10 will detail this.
