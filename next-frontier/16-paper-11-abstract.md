# Paper 11: Abstract

## Title

**Why SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1): The Standard Model Gauge Group from
Three-Qubit Entanglement on the e-Circle**

## Abstract

We prove that the gauge group of the Standard Model,
[SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1)] / Z₆, emerges as a consequence of three-qubit
entanglement on the compact e-circle established in Paper 1. Three
fermion generations (whose number is fixed by χ(CP²) = 3) each carry
a two-state system on the e-circle, forming a three-qubit Hilbert space
(C²)^⊗³. The Noether conservation law for e-translation invariance
imposes a global constraint Σφᵢ = Q_e on the three coordinates.

Five theorems chain this entanglement structure to the Standard Model.
First, we compute explicitly the tangent space to the orbit of the
GHZ state under SU(2)³ and show it carries the A₂ root system of
su(3), with Cartan matrix ((2,-1),(-1,2)) verified numerically.
Second, we prove that the symmetry group of the e-conservation
constraint is the 2-torus T² with the product-one constraint a₁+a₂+a₃ = 0,
which coincides exactly with the continuous stabiliser of the GHZ state
under SU(2)³. By the SLOCC orbit classification (Dür-Vidal-Cirac 2000),
the GHZ orbit is the unique open orbit with this stabiliser, so
e-conservation selects the GHZ class.

Third, applying the Kirillov orbit method, we identify the GHZ orbit
with the flag manifold Fl(1,2;3) = SU(3)/T². The non-product structure
of the stabiliser T² (which is the Cartan torus of SU(3), not of SU(2)³)
forces a gauge group enhancement: the three SU(2) factors merge into
SU(3) on the orbit. The strong force is what happens when three weak
forces are entangled through e-conservation.

Fourth, we decompose the three-qubit space (C²)^⊗³ under the resulting
SU(3) × U(1)_e action, obtaining

    (C²)^⊗³ = 1₀ ⊕ 3̄₁ ⊕ 3₂ ⊕ 1₃

with hypercharge Y = n/3 (the GUT normalisation). The colour triplet
{|011⟩, |101⟩, |110⟩} represents the three patterns of distributing two
e-coordinate excitations among three generations: red, green, blue are
the three ways to choose which generation contributes the excitation.
**Colour charge IS the entanglement pattern.** Colour singlets — states
where all generations are equally entangled — correspond to mesons and
baryons, which propagate freely.

The full Standard Model gauge group, including the Z₆ quotient, follows
from the discrete (Z₂)² stabiliser of the GHZ state and the Z₃ quotient
of the A₂ root and weight lattices. We verify that all SM fermion
representations satisfy the Z₆ constraint and that exotic representations
(such as 8 of SU(3) with weak doublet) are forbidden.

We complete the holonomy correspondence that runs through the entire
series: S¹ → U(1) (Coulomb, Aharonov-Bohm phase), S² → SU(2) (Higgs,
Wilson line VEV), CP² → SU(3) (Confining, CP¹ holonomy from 2D YM
exact solution). The CP² confinement mechanism is established via the
exact solvability of 2D Yang-Mills on the CP¹ ⊂ CP² generator of
H₂(CP², Z), giving σ = g²C₂(fund)/2 = 2g²/3 for SU(3).

The Standard Model gauge group is not an input, not a choice from a
landscape, not a parameter to be fitted. It is the unique gauge group
whose representation theory matches the entanglement geometry of three
qubits on a compact circle. Three is the unique number of generations
giving exactly the SM gauge group: fewer give too few forces, more give
too many. Combined with χ(CP²) = 3 from Paper 4 (which forces three
generations topologically), the circle closes: geometry determines the
generation count, and the generation count determines the gauge group.

What this paper establishes, conditional only on the framework of
Papers 1-4, is that the question "why these symmetries?" has a
geometric answer. The Standard Model is not arbitrary. It is the
unique gauge structure compatible with three-qubit entanglement on
the e-circle of Paper 1.
