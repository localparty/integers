## 7.2 Unique Predictions of the CP² Framework

**Prediction 1: Glueball spectrum**

The mass spectrum of glueballs (bound states of gluons with no quarks)
is determined by the eigenvalues of the SU(3) Laplacian on CP². The
lightest glueball (`J^PC = 0^{++}`) has mass:

    m_G ~ 2√σ_geom ~ 2 × 437 MeV ≈ 874 MeV

After mixing with the `f_0(980)` scalar meson, the physical mass
shifts to ~1.5 GeV — consistent with lattice QCD predictions and
the `f_0(1500)` candidate.

The framework predicts a SPECIFIC glueball spectrum from the CP²
harmonic analysis. The SU(3) harmonics on CP² give a tower of
glueball states with masses:

    m_{G,n} ~ n^{1/2} × m_{G,1}

where `n` is the Casimir of the SU(3) representation. This is a
distinctive prediction for future measurements at BESIII, LHCb,
or the EIC.

**Prediction 2: The confining string in lattice QCD**

The confining string in the geometric picture has a specific internal
structure: it is the projection of a CP² gauge field configuration
with non-trivial Chern class. The string worldsheet theory is the
sigma model on CP², not just a free Nambu-Goto string.

This CP² string has a specific set of worldsheet excitations
corresponding to the CP² isometries. The Luscher term correction
to the linear potential:

    V(R) = σR − π/(12R) + ... (Nambu-Goto)

is modified to:

    V(R) = σR − c_{CP²}/(12R) + ...

where `c_{CP²}` is the central charge of the CP² worldsheet theory.
For the CP² sigma model: `c = 2` (corresponding to 2 = dim(CP²)/2
real internal degrees of freedom). This predicts:

    V(R) = σR − π/(6R) + ...

The factor of 2 enhancement of the Luscher term is a specific,
testable prediction for lattice QCD measurements of the static
quark potential.

**Prediction 3: No free quarks at ANY energy**

The CP² topological argument for confinement (§2.2) does not depend
on the coupling constant — it depends on the topology of the internal
manifold. The non-contractible 2-cycle in CP² always has non-trivial
Chern class, regardless of the energy scale.

This means confinement in the framework is ABSOLUTE — no quark can
ever escape the hadron, even at ultra-high energies, unless the CP²
geometry itself changes (which would require a phase transition at
`M_3 ~ 10¹⁵ GeV`). Asymptotic freedom is not the opposite of
confinement in this picture — both are consequences of the SU(3)
holonomy on CP² at different energy scales.

**Prediction 4: The θ_QCD = 0 prediction (no axion)**

Confirmed from Paper 4 §7.6. The CP² geometry selects θ_QCD = 0

This is now the MOST DISTINCTIVE prediction of the series: if any
