## Abstract

We show that the CP² internal manifold provides a geometric mechanism
consistent with color confinement: chromoelectric flux is topologically
constrained by the non-trivial 2-cycle structure of CP²
(H₂(CP², ℤ) = ℤ), and this constraint produces a string-tension formula
that reproduces the experimental value √σ ≈ 440 MeV at leading order.
This paper does not prove the Yang-Mills area law — a task that remains
an open mathematical problem — but it proposes a concrete geometric
origin for confinement and derives several of its quantitative
consequences. The proposal completes the holonomy correspondence that
runs through the entire framework:

| Compact space | Gauge group | Holonomy effect | Relevant homology | Scale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `S¹` (e-circle) | `U(1)` | Aharonov-Bohm phase | H₁(S¹,ℤ)=ℤ (1-cycle) | meV |
| `S²` (weak) | `SU(2)` | Higgs mechanism | H₂(S²,ℤ)=ℤ (2-cycle) | 100 GeV |
| `CP²` (strong) | `SU(3)` | Color confinement | H₂(CP²,ℤ)=ℤ (2-cycle) | 200 MeV |

In each sector, the physics is determined by the vacuum expectation value
of the Wilson holonomy operator evaluated on the representative cycle of
the non-trivial homology group of the compact internal space. For the
S¹ sector this is a genuine Wilson loop (1-cycle); for the S² and CP²
sectors the relevant objects are holonomies evaluated on 2-cycles. This
is a structural unification — three compact spaces, three distinct
topologies, three phases of gauge theory — as detailed in §8.

We make four contributions. First, we identify color flux tubes as the
4D projections of gauge configurations constrained by the non-trivial
CP¹ ⊂ CP² 2-cycle. The flux tube tension is derived from the CP²
geometry:

    σ = (3/8π²) × g₃²(M_3) / r₃²

The string tension formula requires one observational input — the strong
coupling α_s(M_Z) = 0.118, which fixes the CP² compactification scale
r₃ via the KK gauge coupling relation g₃² = 16πG₁₁/Vol(CP²) (Paper 4).
The theoretical uncertainty in the coefficient c ∈ [1/(3π²), 1/(2π²)]
propagates to ~25% uncertainty in √σ (since √σ ~ √c). The geometric
prediction is √σ ∈ [410, 510] MeV at leading order; the experimental
value √σ = 440 MeV is consistent with this range.

Second, we show that the Z₃ orbifold warp factor produces an exponential
hierarchy of Yukawa couplings: y_i ∝ exp(c_i × kπR), where c_i are
dimensionless bulk mass parameters. The functional form — exponential
hierarchy from warp factor — is derived from geometry. The specific
values of the bulk mass splittings (Δc^u ≈ 0.78–1.00 for up-type
quarks, Δc^d ≈ 0.8 for down-type quarks) are extracted from the
observed mass ratios, not predicted from first principles. With these
two fit parameters (in place of the SM's six independent Yukawa
couplings), the framework reproduces all six quark masses within 20–40%.
The advance is the compression of the parameterization: two geometric
parameters replace six unrelated SM Yukawa couplings, with the
exponential structure mandated by the warp factor geometry.

Third, we derive the baryon-to-photon ratio from the CP² Chern-Simons
structure and Z₃ Yukawa phases. The CP violation driving baryogenesis
has two geometric sources that play distinct roles: the CP² Chern-Simons
topology, which provides the non-perturbative B-violation background
(Sakharov condition 2), and the Z₃ Yukawa phases (leptonic
`δ_CP = −90°`), which provide the quantitative CP asymmetry ε_CP in
the leptogenesis formula. The result:

    η_B ~ J_CKM × (sphaleron rate) / H|_{T_c}

as an order-of-magnitude estimate, with all inputs from geometry. The
leading-order calculation overshoots the observed `η_B = 6 × 10⁻¹⁰`
by ~10³ before washout corrections; resonant leptogenesis from the Z₃
near-degeneracy (Appendix D) gives η_B ∈ (1–3) × 10⁻¹⁰, consistent
with observation within an O(1) systematic uncertainty from the
Z₃-breaking parameter α. The bulk leptogenesis result of Paper 2, §E
provides the precision prediction.

Fourth, we show that the proton mass is a derived quantity:

    m_p ≈ 3Λ_QCD × f(α_s)

where `Λ_QCD ≈ 190 MeV` is computed from the CP² geometry via the QCD
β-function using α_s(M_Z) as input, and `f(α_s)` encodes the MIT bag
model correction. The derivation chain is:

    CP² radius r₃ [+ α_s(M_Z)] → Λ_QCD → √σ → m_p

The proton mass — the fundamental scale of nuclear physics — emerges from
the geometry of `CP²` run through 13 orders of magnitude by the QCD beta
function, using α_s(M_Z) as the single observational input.

The unifying insight: the Aharonov-Bohm effect, the Higgs mechanism,
and color confinement are three manifestations of one geometric principle.
The holonomy of a gauge connection on the representative cycle of each

the corresponding gauge theory — Coulomb (S¹/U(1)), Higgs (S²/SU(2)),
or confining (CP²/SU(3)).
