# Forum of Mathematics, Pi — Submission Guidelines

**Source:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/forum-of-mathematics-pi/information/author-instructions
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## General
- Cambridge University Press journal.
- **Fully open-access.** "All published papers are free online to readers in perpetuity."
- Sister journal to Forum of Mathematics, Sigma.
- Forum of Mathematics Pi publishes top-quality research in any area of mathematics.

## Article types
- Research Article.

## Submission process
- Via Cambridge's partner submission system at cup.msp.org.
- Authors create separate account (distinct from Cambridge Core).
- Review the "Preparing your materials" page before submission.

## Review process
- Peer-reviewed (single-blind typical).
- Edited by an international editorial board.

## Open-access model
- Article Processing Charges (APCs) may apply.
- APC waivers / discounts available through Cambridge's open-access agreements.
- Without funding: waiver options listed at open-access instructions page.

## Clay Qualifying Outlet status
**YES (subject to CMI verification).** Forum of Mathematics Pi meets all §6e criteria:
- Named editorial board.
- Peer review process.
- Cambridge University Press publisher with worldwide repute.
- MathSciNet-indexed.

## Implications for QG5D programme
- Open-access is attractive for cascade priority (anyone can read immediately).
- Suitable for papers with broad mathematical interest (not strictly specialized).
- Competing target alongside Annals / Inventiones / JAMS.
