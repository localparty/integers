# Annals of Mathematics — Submission Guidelines

**Source:** https://annals.math.princeton.edu/submission (WebFetch successful 2026-04-14)
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## Submission process
- Manuscripts submitted via email to **annals@math.princeton.edu** as PDF files or arXiv numbers.
- Languages accepted: English, French, German.

## Required components
- Abstract: ~200 words max, self-contained, no bibliography references.
- For non-English submissions: abstracts in original language AND English.
- Complete bibliography: article titles, page ranges, cited references.
- BibTeX format preferred.

## LaTeX / formatting
- Authors encouraged to use LaTeX with the `aomart` class file (CTAN).
- Not required for initial submission; accepted papers must use the journal style.
- Avoid forced line breaks or page breaks.

## Figures
- Publication-quality vector graphics (EPS or PDF).
- Original source files provided after acceptance.

## Key policies

### AI/LLM policy (CRITICAL)
> The journal does not consider papers generated using AI products. Authors must declare any AI usage as supplemental information.

**Implication for QG5D:** This is the hardest venue-AI policy the programme faces. Significant use of Claude Opus 4.6 must be declared. Papers that are HEAVILY AI-generated may be rejected outright by Annals. The strategy must be:
- Human authorship (G Six) with AI as tool (must be transparent about use).
- The mathematical content must be verifiable human-mathematics (not AI-hallucinated).
- Consider Annals for papers with strongest human-authored mathematical substance; other venues (Inventiones, JAMS) for others.

### Article Processing Charges
- **No APCs.** Publication is free.

### Copyright
- Authors may post accepted versions on personal webpages and institutional repositories.
- CC-BY licensing allowed only in open-access environments (like arXiv).
