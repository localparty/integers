# arXiv Endorsement Policy

**Source:** https://info.arxiv.org/help/endorsement.html
**2025 policy update:** https://blog.arxiv.org/2025/12/10/updated-endorsement-policy-for-arxiv-mathematics/
**2026 policy update:** https://blog.arxiv.org/2026/01/21/attention-authors-updated-endorsement-policy
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## Who needs endorsement
> arXiv requires that users be endorsed before submitting their first paper to arXiv or a new category.

## How endorsements work
- Endorsements happen PER CATEGORY. Must have at least one positive endorsement per endorsement domain.
- Endorsers must have authored sufficient papers within the endorsement domain in the past 3-5 years.
- Most high-level subject areas (e.g., hep-th, cond-mat, q-bio) are endorsement domains; physics uses individual subject classes as domains.

## Endorser responsibilities
> The endorsement process is not peer review. Endorsers should verify the paper is appropriate for its subject area but need not thoroughly review it for correctness.

## 2025/2026 policy changes
- **arXiv Mathematics:** "Updated endorsement policy" (Dec 2025) — due to unsustainable increase in non-scientific submissions from automatically endorsed users, institutional email addresses alone are NO LONGER sufficient.
- **Automatic endorsement based solely on institutional email is DISCONTINUED.**
- Math-ph (Mathematical Physics) is a sub-section of Physics and will follow endorsement rules for Physics.
- Jan 2026 update: broader endorsement-policy change referenced.

## Strategy for QG5D first submission
1. Identify an established author in math.NT or math.MP who would endorse (a past collaborator, a friendly reader of Zenodo deposits, a conference contact).
2. Request endorsement via the arXiv endorsement system.
3. Once endorsed, first submission proceeds normally.

## Fallback
- If no endorser found initially: post to Zenodo first for DOI + priority timestamp, then approach potential endorsers with the stable DOI reference.
- Programme strategy (see `/strategy/ym/00-millenium-strategy.md §9`) prescribes: Zenodo → GitHub → arXiv (via endorser referral) → journal.
