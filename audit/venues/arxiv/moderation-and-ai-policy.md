# arXiv Moderation + AI Policy

**Source:** https://info.arxiv.org/help/moderation/index.html
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## Accepted material
Research articles. Must be "of interest, relevance, and value to the disciplines we serve" and demonstrate scholarly merit.

## Rejected material
- Non-research: course projects, research proposals, news, political causes.
- Inappropriate tone or scholarly form.
- Lacking originality, novelty, significance.
- Falsified or plagiarized content.
- Copyright infringement.
- Obscenity, violence, sexualized content.

## Duplicated content
Multiple similar submissions from one author trigger consolidation requests.

## Submission rate limit
> Authors are asked not to exceed three papers per day for back catalog submissions.

## AI policy (verbatim-summary from moderation page)
> Authors must report in their work any significant use of sophisticated tools, including text-to-text generative AI. Authors remain individually responsible for all its contents, irrespective of how the contents were generated. AI tools should not be listed as authors.

**Implication for QG5D:** Claude Opus 4.6 is a sophisticated AI tool. Significant use must be reported (e.g., an acknowledgment paragraph naming the model). Claude MUST NOT be listed as a co-author on arXiv.

## Review articles / position papers (Nov 2025 policy)
> In November 2025, arXiv announced it would no longer accept computer science review articles and position papers that had not been vetted by an academic journal or conference.

This applies to **cs.** category. For math/physics categories, the explicit rule is less strict but the spirit is similar: original research is preferred.

## Appeals process
- Appeals are possible.
- Decisions upon appeal are FINAL with NO feedback.
