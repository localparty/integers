# Yang-Mills — Jaffe-Witten Requirements (itemized)

**Source:** `00-problem-statement.md` (verbatim §4 of Jaffe-Witten 2000/2006)
**Clay §5d discipline:** every requirement must be ADDRESSED. Silence is a §5d violation.

## The 7 formal requirements

| # | Requirement | Verbatim phrase | J-W source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Any compact simple gauge group G** | "for any compact simple gauge group G" | §4 theorem |
| 2 | **On R^4** | "a non-trivial quantum Yang-Mills theory exists on R^4" | §4 theorem |
| 3 | **Mass gap Δ > 0** with m = sup{Δ} finite | "has a mass gap Δ > 0"; "we require m < ∞" | §4 theorem, §4 prose |
| 4 | **Wightman or OS axioms (full)** | "Existence includes establishing axiomatic properties at least as strong as those cited in [Streater-Wightman 1964, Osterwalder-Schrader 1973/75]" | §4 theorem |
| 5 | **Short-distance AF match** | "Correlation functions of the quantum field operators should agree at short distances with the predictions of asymptotic freedom and perturbative renormalization theory" | §4 prose |
| 6 | **Stress tensor + OPE** with AF-prescribed local singularities | "the existence of a stress tensor and an operator product expansion, having prescribed local singularities predicted by asymptotic freedom" | §4 prose |
| 7 | **Non-triviality** | "non-trivial quantum Yang-Mills theory" | §4 theorem |

## Implicit structural requirements (from §3-§4 prose)

- Hilbert space H carrying a representation of the Poincaré group
- Hamiltonian H, momentum P̃ self-adjoint generators
- Unique-up-to-phase Poincaré-invariant vacuum Ω with HΩ = 0
- Positive energy: spectrum of H supported in [0, ∞)
- Gauge-invariant local quantum field operators in correspondence with gauge-invariant polynomials in F and covariant derivatives (e.g., Tr F_{ij} F_{kl})
- Locality (light-cone-separated fields commute / anti-commute)

## Clay-named hard sub-problems (§6)

- **Mass gap uniform in volume** — "no present ideas point the direction"
- **Infinite-volume limit T^4 → R^4** — "nor do present methods suggest how to obtain"

These two are called out as the deepest obstructions. A solution must either prove them or disclose an attempted bypass (§5d).

## §5(b)/(c) special note

YM is NOT a §5(b) problem (no "either direction" clause). A counterexample falls under §5(c): full prize if it "effectively resolves the Problem"; otherwise a small non-Prize award may be granted.
