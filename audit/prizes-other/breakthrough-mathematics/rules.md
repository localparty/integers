# Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics — Rules

**Source:** https://breakthroughprize.org/Rules/3
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## Prize amount
$3 million. One prize per year. Exceptional cases may split among joint contributors.

## Eligibility (verbatim-summary)
- Recognizes "a single person's contribution to the field of mathematics, with special attention to recent developments (in the last 10 years, although earlier contributions may also be considered)."
- **No age restrictions.**
- Individuals may receive the prize more than once.

## Nominations
- **Self-nominations NOT allowed.**
- Anyone except the candidate may nominate.
- A valid nomination must include:
  - Basic biographical information on nominee and nominator
  - At least one third-party letter of recommendation
  - Up to 10 citations from a specified academic publishing database
- Submission via online form at breakthroughprize.org/Nominations during the open nomination window.

## Related prizes (nomination opens same window)
- **New Horizons in Mathematics Prize:** up to three $100,000 awards for early-career researchers who have not held a PhD for more than 10 years at the start of the nomination calendar year.
- **Maryam Mirzakhani New Frontiers Prize:** up to three $50,000 awards for early-career women mathematicians who have completed their PhDs within the previous two years.

## Selection
- Past recipients of the Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics are invited to serve on the Selection Committee.
