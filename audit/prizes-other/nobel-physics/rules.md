# Nobel Prize in Physics — Rules

**Source:** https://www.nobelprize.org/nomination/physics/
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## Nomination
- Nomination is BY INVITATION ONLY.
- Invitations sent to thousands of specially selected university professors and other scholars globally.
- Members of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences and Nobel Prize laureates may also propose candidates.
- **Self-nominations are NOT allowed.** Cannot "spontaneously submit a proposal without receiving an invitation."

## Timeline
- September: Nomination forms sent out.
- 31 January of following year: nominations must reach the Nobel Committee.
- October: announcement.

## Eligibility
- For discoveries/inventions that have conferred the greatest benefit on humankind.
- Up to three laureates per prize.
- Must be ALIVE at time of announcement.
- Posthumous award only if laureate was alive at announcement but died before ceremony.

## Confidentiality
- Names of nominees and other information not revealed until 50 years later.
