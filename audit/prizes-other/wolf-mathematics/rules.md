# Wolf Prize in Mathematics / Physics — Rules

**Source:** https://wolffund.org.il/the-wolf-prize/call-for-proposal/ + https://www.ae-info.org/ae/Acad_Main/Members/Prizes_Awards_and_Medals/Wolf_Prize
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-14

## Rotation
The Wolf Prize is awarded in five fields (Physics, Medicine, Mathematics, Chemistry, Arts) on a rotation. Verify the year's available fields via call for proposal.

## Nomination rules
- **Nomination is BY INVITATION ONLY.**
- **Self-nomination NOT allowed.**
- Nominations may be for individuals OR teams of 2-3 people for collaborative work directly related to the same achievement.
- Prize awarded to INDIVIDUALS, not institutions.

## Award criteria
- Awarded for **significant breakthroughs**, NOT for exemplary career or leadership.
- Celebrates lifetime accomplishments.
- No age or nationality restrictions.
- Affiliation with Israel is not a consideration.

## Restrictions
- **Nobel Prize Laureates will NOT be considered** unless the nomination is for an achievement entirely different from the Nobel-recognized work.

## Prize amount
$100,000 (split if multiple laureates).

## Implications for QG5D programme
- Wolf Prize rewards breakthroughs — the QG5D mass-gap / RH / BSD / PvNP programme individually qualifies.
- Requires invited nominator.
- Collaborative (G Six + human co-author) possible for up to 3 people.
