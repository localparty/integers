## The proof in one paragraph

The Hodge conjecture asserts that every rational (p,p)-class on a smooth
projective variety X is a rational linear combination of algebraic cycle
classes. We attempt two independent routes, both anchored in the Integers
programme. Route I (Links 1-5): the Bost-Connes algebra encodes Artin
motives as endomotives (Connes-Consani-Marcolli 2005); the Tannakian
formalism lifts their Galois action to the motivic Galois group; one then
needs Hodge-filtration compatibility for BC-motivated varieties
(CONJECTURED) and the Grothendieck standard conjectures D and B for this
class (D is conditional, B is KNOWN for the geometric sector CP^2 x S^2).
Route II (Link 6): the geometric Langlands programme, now proved by
Gaitsgory-Raskin (2024), produces automorphic sheaves on Hitchin moduli
whose Hodge structures should yield algebraic cycles -- the Hodge
application step is OPEN. If both routes close (Link 7), one obtains
Hodge for BC-motivated smooth projective varieties. Extension to general
smooth projective varieties (Link 8) requires a motivic functoriality
theorem and is the hardest step. The physical observable is: algebraic
cycles on CP^2 x S^2 = topological invariants of M_geom, with anomaly
cancellation 19 = 1 + 4 + 6 + 8 guaranteeing the correct Hodge numbers.

| 1. BC algebra to endomotives | BC algebra encodes Artin motives; the endomotive category of Connes-Consani-Marcolli (2005) provides the motivic foundation. Objects: endomotives (X, S) where X = lim X_alpha is a pro-variety and S is a semigroup acting by correspondences. | Literature (CCM 2005) | arXiv:math/0512138 Sections 3-4 |
| 2. Endomotive Galois action to motivic Galois group | The endomotive category is Tannakian; the fiber functor through Betti cohomology gives a motivic Galois group G_mot acting on the category. Tannaka reconstruction: Rep(G_mot) = Mot_num(k, Q). | Literature (Deligne-Milne) | Standard Tannakian formalism |
| 3. Motivic Galois on de Rham to Hodge filtration | G_mot acts on H^*_dR(X) for BC-motivated varieties. CLAIM: this action preserves the Hodge filtration F^p H^n_dR(X). Requires: (a) BC-motivated varieties carry a natural mixed Hodge structure, (b) the G_mot action is compatible with the comparison isomorphism H^*_dR ~ H^*_B tensor C. | **CONJECTURED** | Requires proof that BC-motivated varieties preserve Hodge filtration |
| 4. Standard conjecture D | For BC-motivated varieties: homological equivalence = numerical equivalence. This is a special case of Grothendieck's standard conjecture D. For abelian varieties (Lieberman 1968) and varieties of dimension <= 4, this is known. For the general BC-motivated class, it is conditional on Grothendieck. | **CONJECTURED** (conditional on Grothendieck standard conjectures) | Grothendieck 1969, Kleiman 1994 survey |
| 5. Lefschetz standard conjecture B for CP^2 x S^2 | CP^2: H^{1,1} = 1 (generated by the hyperplane class), all Hodge classes algebraic. S^2 = CP^1: trivial Hodge structure, H^{1,1} = 1 (point class). Product: Kunneth gives H^{p,p}(CP^2 x S^2) algebraic for all p. Hard Lefschetz is classical for smooth projective. | **KNOWN** | Classical algebraic geometry |
| 6. Geometric Langlands to Hodge structures | Geometric Langlands conjecture PROVED (Gaitsgory-Raskin 2024): for a reductive group G and smooth projective curve C, the automorphic-to-spectral equivalence Aut(Bun_G) ~ QCoh(LocSys_G^L) holds. APPLICATION: Hitchin moduli spaces carry natural Hodge structures; the Langlands equivalence should transport these to algebraic cycles on related moduli. The bridge from the categorical equivalence to Hodge-class algebraicity is OPEN. | **PARTIAL** (geometric Langlands proved; Hodge application open) | Gaitsgory-Raskin 2024 arXiv:2405.03599 |
| 7. Composition of routes | Routes I (Links 1-5) and II (Link 6) compose: for BC-motivated smooth projective varieties, every rational (p,p)-class is algebraic. Requires: a composition theorem showing that the endomotive route and the Langlands route produce compatible algebraicity witnesses. | **OPEN** | Requires composition theorem |
| 8. Extension to general smooth projective varieties | Extend from BC-motivated varieties to all smooth projective varieties over C. Requires a motivic functoriality theorem: every smooth projective variety admits a motivic correspondence to a BC-motivated variety that preserves Hodge classes. This is the hardest step and may require the full strength of the motivic yoga (Grothendieck's vision). | **OPEN** (the hardest step) | Requires motivic functoriality theorem |

## Physical observable connection

The geometric sector of M_geom = M^4 x CP^2 x S^2 gives the physical
anchor. Algebraic cycles on CP^2 x S^2 are topological invariants of
the internal geometry. The anomaly cancellation condition
19 = 1 + 4 + 6 + 8 (dimensions: scalar + vector + 2-form + metric)
guarantees that the Hodge numbers of the compactification manifold are
consistent with the Standard Model spectrum. The Hodge conjecture,
restricted to this geometric sector, asserts that every topological
invariant of M_geom that "looks algebraic" (rational (p,p)-class) IS
algebraic -- i.e., comes from actual geometric cycles. This is Link 5,
which is KNOWN.

The deeper programme asks: does the Integers description (BC algebra)
provide the scaffolding to extend this from CP^2 x S^2 to all smooth
projective varieties?

## Key theorems cited

### From the literature

- **Connes-Consani-Marcolli 2005** (arXiv:math/0512138): Endomotives,
  thermodynamic system of the BC algebra, endomotive category as
  Tannakian category of Artin motives.

- **Deligne-Milne 1982** (Hodge Cycles, Motives, and Shimura Varieties,
  LNM 900): Tannakian categories, motivic Galois groups, fiber functors.

- **Grothendieck 1969** (Standard conjectures on algebraic cycles):
  Conjectures B (Lefschetz), C (Kunneth), D (homological = numerical).
  See Kleiman 1994 survey (Proc. Symp. Pure Math. 55).

- **Gaitsgory-Raskin 2024** (arXiv:2405.03599): Proof of the geometric
  Langlands conjecture. Categorical equivalence between automorphic
  sheaves and spectral sheaves for any reductive group G over a smooth
  projective curve.

- **Lieberman 1968**: Standard conjecture D for abelian varieties.

- **Deligne 1971** (Theorie de Hodge II, Publ. Math. IHES 40):
  Mixed Hodge structures, Hodge filtration on de Rham cohomology.

- **Voisin 2002** (Hodge Theory and Complex Algebraic Geometry I-II):
  Comprehensive treatment of Hodge theory and algebraic cycles.

### Needed but not yet constructed

- Composition theorem for the two routes (Link 7).
- Motivic functoriality theorem (Link 8).

## Honest accounting

**Overall confidence for full Hodge conjecture: 3/10.**

This is the honest assessment. The Hodge conjecture is widely considered
the most technically demanding of the Millennium Problems after P vs NP.
Two of the eight links are fully open (7, 8) and two are conjectured
conditional on Grothendieck's standard conjectures (3, 4). Link 8
(motivic functoriality) is arguably as hard as the conjecture itself.

**Confidence for CM Hodge (Hodge for abelian varieties with CM): 5/10.**

For CM abelian varieties, Hodge is known in low dimensions (Shioda,
Mumford-Tate). The endomotive route (Links 1-4) is better motivated in
the CM setting because BC naturally produces CM-type endomotives. If
Link 3 closes for CM varieties and Grothendieck D holds for abelian
varieties (Lieberman 1968 gives this), then Links 1-5 close for CM,
and one does not need Link 6 at all.

| Scope | Confidence | Key blocker |
|:------|:-----------|:------------|
| CP^2 x S^2 (geometric sector) | 9/10 | Classical, already known |
| CM abelian varieties | 5/10 | Link 3 (Hodge filtration compatibility) |
| General abelian varieties | 4/10 | Links 3 + 4 (standard conjecture D) |
| All smooth projective varieties | 3/10 | Links 7 + 8 (composition + functoriality) |
