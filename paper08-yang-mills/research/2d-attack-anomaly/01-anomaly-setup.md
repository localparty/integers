# 01. Symmetries of the Z₃-Twisted CP² Sigma Model

## I. The Model

### I.1 Definition

The 2D $\mathbb{CP}^2$ sigma model on $\mathbb{R} \times S^1_L$
with $\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted boundary conditions.

**Fields.** $z = (z_1, z_2, z_3)$ with $|z|^2 = 1$, modulo
$z \sim e^{i\alpha} z$ (the $U(1)$ gauge redundancy). The target
space is $\mathbb{CP}^2 = S^5/U(1)$.

**Action.**
$$S = \frac{1}{g^2} \int_{\mathbb{R} \times S^1_L}
  |D_\mu z|^2 \, d^2x$$

where $D_\mu z = \partial_\mu z - (\bar{z} \cdot \partial_\mu z) z$
is the covariant derivative with respect to the composite $U(1)$
gauge field $A_\mu = -i \bar{z} \cdot \partial_\mu z$.

### I.2 The twisted boundary conditions

The $\mathbb{Z}_3$-twist acts on $z$ as:
$$z(x^0, x^1 + L) = \Omega \, z(x^0, x^1)$$

where $\Omega = \text{diag}(1, \omega, \omega^2)$ with
$\omega = e^{2\pi i/3}$, and $x^1$ is the compact direction.

**Why $\Omega$ is in $SU(3)$.** $\det(\Omega) = 1 \cdot \omega
\cdot \omega^2 = \omega^3 = 1$. The twist lies in the center
$\mathbb{Z}_3 \subset SU(3)$, the global symmetry group that
acts as $z \mapsto U z$ for $U \in SU(3)$.

**Physical meaning.** The twist breaks $SU(3)_{\text{global}}$
to its maximal torus $U(1)^2$ plus permutation symmetry. It
distributes the holonomy of the $SU(3)$ symmetry equally around
the three center elements. This is the analogue of the
$\mathbb{Z}_N$-symmetric compactification in gauge theory
(Unsal 2008).

## II. The Global Symmetries

### II.1 Center symmetry: $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$

**Definition.** The center symmetry is the cyclic permutation of
the twist sectors. It acts as:
$$\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}: \quad z_j(x^0, x^1) \mapsto
  z_{j+1 \bmod 3}(x^0, x^1)$$

simultaneously with $x^1 \mapsto x^1$ (it permutes the fields,
not the coordinates).

More precisely: define the translation operator $T_{L/3}$ that
shifts $x^1 \mapsto x^1 + L/3$. Acting on a field obeying the
$\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted boundary condition, $T_{L/3}$ maps:
$$T_{L/3}: z_j \mapsto \omega^{j-1} z_j$$

This is equivalent to multiplication by the center element
$\Omega$. The $\mathbb{Z}_3$ center symmetry is generated by
$T_{L/3}$.

**Order parameter.** The Polyakov loop (holonomy around $S^1$):
$$P = \frac{1}{3} \text{Tr}\left[\mathcal{P} \exp\left(
  i \int_0^L A_1 \, dx^1 \right)\right]$$

Under $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$: $P \mapsto \omega P$.

- $\langle P \rangle = 0$: $\mathbb{Z}_3$ **preserved** (confined phase)
- $\langle P \rangle \neq 0$: $\mathbb{Z}_3$ **broken** (deconfined phase)

**STATUS: This definition is standard. [ESTABLISHED,
Unsal 2008, Dunne-Unsal 2012]**

### II.2 Discrete chiral symmetry: $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$

The $\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}$ model has an anomalous $U(1)_A$ axial
symmetry at the classical level. Instantons break it:
$$U(1)_A \xrightarrow{\text{instanton}} \mathbb{Z}_{N}$$

For $N = 3$: the surviving discrete symmetry is $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$.

**Action on fields.** The discrete chiral symmetry acts on the
topological theta angle:
$$\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}: \quad \theta \mapsto \theta + \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

In terms of the dual photon $\sigma$ (the $U(1)$ gauge field
dualized in 2D), this is a shift:
$$\sigma \mapsto \sigma + \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

**Origin.** The instanton amplitude in the $\mathbb{CP}^2$ model
carries a phase $e^{i\theta}$. The instanton vertex breaks
$U(1)_A$ but preserves the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ subgroup that shifts
$\theta$ by multiples of $2\pi/3$ (since the instanton has
topological charge 1, and the action is $2\pi$-periodic in
$\theta$; the residual symmetry is $\mathbb{Z}_N$ because the
minimal fractional instanton in the twisted model has charge $1/N$).

More explicitly: in the semiclassical regime (small $L$), the
effective potential for the dual photon $\sigma$ is:
$$V(\sigma) \sim -e^{-S_f} \cos\left(\frac{\sigma}{N}\right)$$

The $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ symmetry is $\sigma \mapsto \sigma
+ 2\pi/3$, which shifts $\sigma/N = \sigma/3$ by $2\pi/9$.
This is a symmetry of the full theory (all instanton sectors)
but not of each individual term.

**STATUS: The identification of this discrete chiral symmetry
requires care. For CP^1 (N=2), the discrete chiral symmetry is
Z_2, and it is clearly identified in Dunne-Unsal 2012. For N=3,
the Z_3 chiral symmetry is the standard anomaly-reduced subgroup
of U(1)_A. [ESTABLISHED]**

### II.3 Charge conjugation: $\mathcal{C}$

$$\mathcal{C}: \quad z_j \mapsto \bar{z}_j, \quad
  A_\mu \mapsto -A_\mu$$

This maps the $\mathbb{CP}^2$ model to itself (the Fubini-Study
metric is invariant under complex conjugation). It sends
$\theta \mapsto -\theta$ and $\sigma \mapsto -\sigma$.

Combined with the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ twist: $\mathcal{C}$ commutes
with the center symmetry $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and acts as
inversion on $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$.

### II.4 Parity: $\mathcal{P}$

$$\mathcal{P}: \quad x^1 \mapsto -x^1, \quad
  A_1 \mapsto -A_1$$

Parity reverses the $S^1$ direction. It maps $\theta \mapsto
-\theta$ and $\sigma \mapsto -\sigma$.

## III. The Symmetry Algebra and Its Extensions

### III.1 The full symmetry group

At $\theta = 0$, the discrete symmetry group of the
$\mathbb{Z}_3$-twisted $\mathbb{CP}^2$ model is:
$$G = \mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)} \times \mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}
  \rtimes \mathcal{C}$$

with $\mathcal{C}$ acting as inversion on $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$
(and trivially on $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$).

### III.2 The non-trivial extension

**CLAIM [ESTABLISHED].** The symmetries $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and
$\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ do not commute at the level of the Hilbert
space. Their commutation relation is:
$$\hat{C} \hat{\chi} = \omega \, \hat{\chi} \hat{C}$$

where $\hat{C}$ generates $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$, $\hat{\chi}$
generates $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$, and $\omega = e^{2\pi i/3}$.

This means $G$ is not $\mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_3$ but
rather the Heisenberg group $H_3 = \mathbb{Z}_3 \rtimes
\mathbb{Z}_3$ of order 9 (the non-abelian group with presentation
$\langle a, b \mid a^3 = b^3 = 1, \, ab = \omega \, ba \rangle$).

**Origin of the non-commutativity.** In the effective quantum
mechanics on the dual photon $\sigma$:
- $\hat{C}$ shifts the conjugate momentum (the electric flux):
  $e \mapsto e + 1$
- $\hat{\chi}$ shifts $\sigma$: $\sigma \mapsto \sigma + 2\pi/3$

These are canonically conjugate operations. Their commutator
produces the phase $\omega$, which is the 't Hooft anomaly.

**STATUS: This non-commutativity is the HEART of the anomaly
argument. It is well-established in the literature (Gaiotto,
Kapustin, Komargodski, Seiberg 2017; Tanizaki, Kikuchi 2017).
The key reference for the CP^{N-1} context is Tanizaki-Sulejmanpasic
2018.**

## IV. Summary of Symmetries

| Symmetry | Generator | Action | Order |
|----------|-----------|--------|-------|
| $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ (center) | $\hat{C}$ | Cyclic permutation of twist sectors / $T_{L/3}$ | 3 |
| $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$ (chiral) | $\hat{\chi}$ | $\theta \to \theta + 2\pi/3$ / $\sigma \to \sigma + 2\pi/3$ | 3 |
| $\mathcal{C}$ (charge conj.) | $\hat{\mathcal{C}}$ | $z \to \bar{z}$, $\theta \to -\theta$ | 2 |

**The crucial algebraic relation:**
$$\hat{C} \hat{\chi} = e^{2\pi i/3} \, \hat{\chi} \hat{C}$$

This non-commutativity is the mixed 't Hooft anomaly between
$\mathbb{Z}_3^{(C)}$ and $\mathbb{Z}_3^{(\chi)}$. It is the
starting point for the anomaly matching argument developed in
the next file.
