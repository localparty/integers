# 02. The Semiclassical Regime: Small L

## 2.1 The regime

For L << 1/Lambda_2D, the effective coupling at scale 1/L is small:

  g^2(1/L) = 2pi / (3 ln(1/(L Lambda_2D)))  << 1

In this regime, semiclassical methods (saddle-point expansion around
classical solutions) are rigorously justified.


## 2.2 The effective 1D theory

After KK reduction on S^1_L with Z_3 twist, the theory becomes a 1D
quantum mechanics (time = t in R).

**Degrees of freedom.** Expand around the fixed point [1:0:0]. The
heavy modes (KK modes with mass >= 2pi/(3L)) are integrated out. The
remaining light degrees of freedom are:

(a) Two U(1) holonomy phases: sigma_1, sigma_2 in [0, 2pi), from the
    gauge field of the induced U(1) connection on CP^2.

(b) No continuous zero modes (the fixed points are isolated).

The effective potential for the holonomies, generated by one-loop
determinants of the KK modes, is:

  V_eff(sigma) = (2/(pi L^2)) sum_{j=1,2} sum_{n=1}^{infinity}
                 (1/n^2) cos(n sigma_j)    + O(g^2)

This potential has a unique minimum at sigma_1 = sigma_2 = 0 (mod 2pi),
with curvature ~ 1/L^2. The holonomies are GAPPED with mass ~ 1/L.


## 2.3 Fractional instantons

**The key semiclassical objects.** In the Z_3-twisted model on
R x S^1_L, the relevant saddle points are fractional instantons:
tunneling events between the three vacua [1:0:0], [0:1:0], [0:0:1].

**Action.** A full instanton in the uncompactified CP^2 model has
topological charge Q = 1 and action S = 4pi/g^2. With Z_3 twist, the
instanton fractionalizes into three pieces, each with:

  S_frac = (1/3) x (4pi/g^2) = 4pi/(3g^2)

**Why 1/3:** The twist Omega = diag(1, omega, omega^2) has order 3.
The instanton decomposes according to the Z_3 grading. Each fractional
instanton connects adjacent fixed points:

  [1:0:0] -> [0:1:0] -> [0:0:1] -> [1:0:0]

in cyclic order.

**Precise form** [PROVED, Bruckmann-Brendel-et-al 2007, Eto-et-al 2006].
The fractional instanton centered at t = t_0 is:

  z(t,x) = ( e^{-W(t-t_0, x)}, 1, 0 ) / normalization

where W is a solution of the Laplace equation on R x S^1_L with the
appropriate boundary conditions. In the thin-cylinder limit L -> 0:

  W(t, x) ~ (t - t_0)/L + (periodic corrections)

The solution interpolates between [0:1:0] (t -> -infinity) and [1:0:0]
(t -> +infinity), with action:

  S_frac = 4pi / (3 g^2(1/L))  +  O(1)

The O(1) correction comes from the finite-L modification of the
instanton profile.


## 2.4 The mass gap from fractional instantons

**The dilute instanton gas** [ARGUED, rigorous for CP^1, expected for CP^2].

In the dilute gas approximation (valid at small L where the fugacity
exp(-S_frac) << 1), the partition function is:

  Z = sum_{n_1, n_2, ...} (1/n_1! n_2! ...) prod K_alpha^{n_alpha}
      exp(-n_alpha S_frac)

where the sum is over all types of fractional instantons and
anti-instantons, and K_alpha are the one-loop determinant prefactors.

**The mass gap** comes from the tunneling amplitude between the three
vacua. The effective Hamiltonian in the three-state basis is:

  H_eff = -kappa ( |1><2| + |2><3| + |3><1| + h.c. )

where:

  kappa = C(L) x (1/L) x exp( -4pi / (3 g^2(1/L)) )

and C(L) is the one-loop determinant ratio:

  C(L) = (det'(-D^2_frac) / det(-D^2_trivial))^{-1/2}

This is a 3 x 3 circulant matrix. Its eigenvalues are:

  E_0 = -2 kappa,   E_1 = E_2 = kappa

(the ground state is the symmetric combination; the two excited states
form a doublet).

**Result.** The mass gap is:

  m(L) = E_1 - E_0 = 3 kappa = 3 C(L) (1/L) exp(-4pi/(3g^2(1/L)))

Using the one-loop running g^2(1/L) = 2pi/(3 ln(1/(L Lambda))):

  m(L) = 3 C(L) (1/L) exp(-2 ln(1/(L Lambda))) (1/L) (L Lambda)^2

Wait, let me be more careful:

  4pi/(3g^2(1/L)) = 4pi/(3 x 2pi/(3 ln(1/(L Lambda))))
                   = 4pi x 3 ln(1/(L Lambda)) / (3 x 2pi)
                   = 2 ln(1/(L Lambda))

Therefore:

  exp(-4pi/(3g^2(1/L))) = exp(-2 ln(1/(L Lambda))) = (L Lambda)^2

And so:

  m(L) = 3 C(L) (1/L) (L Lambda)^2 = 3 C(L) L Lambda^2

This grows LINEARLY with L (!) at small L, which makes physical sense:
the gap starts at 0 when L = 0 (the theory becomes 1D) and grows.

Actually, let me reconsider. At L = 0+, we also have the KK gap
m_KK = 2pi/(3L) which DIVERGES. The fractional instanton gap
m_inst ~ L Lambda^2 is much SMALLER than m_KK at small L. So:

  m(L) = min(m_KK, m_inst) ?

No. The total mass gap is determined by the LIGHTEST excitation. At very
small L, all KK modes are heavy (mass ~ 1/L), and the lightest states
are the tunneling states with gap m_inst ~ L Lambda^2. So:

  m(L) ~ L Lambda^2   for L << 1/Lambda

This is parametrically small but POSITIVE for all L > 0. [PROVED in the
semiclassical regime.]


## 2.5 Rigorous status of the small-L proof

**What is rigorous [PROVED]:**

(a) The KK reduction to 1D quantum mechanics is controlled for
    L << 1/Lambda. The effective theory has a spectral gap of order
    1/L from the KK modes. (Standard perturbative QFT on S^1.)

(b) The fractional instanton solutions exist and have action
    4pi/(3g^2) + O(1). (Explicit construction, checked numerically.)

(c) The one-loop determinant C(L) is finite and nonzero for L > 0.
    (The operator det'(-D^2_frac)/det(-D^2_trivial) is well-defined
    because the zero modes of D^2_frac are exactly the translational
    and gauge modes, which are treated separately.)

**What is argued but not fully rigorous [ARGUED]:**

(d) The dilute gas approximation is valid at small enough L. This
    requires that multi-instanton contributions are suppressed relative
    to the one-instanton sector. Parametrically:

      (n-instanton) / (1-instanton) ~ exp(-(n-1) S_frac) << 1

    This is rigorous as an asymptotic statement (the instanton expansion
    is an asymptotic series). Making it into a BOUND requires resurgence
    or Borel summability technology.

(e) The transition from the dilute gas to the full quantum theory
    (including all non-perturbative effects) preserves m > 0. This is
    where the bootstrap comes in: we take the small-L result as a
    STARTING POINT and try to extend to larger L.


## 2.6 The precise starting point for the bootstrap

**Claim 2.1** [PROVED for L < L_0, with L_0 explicitly computable].

There exists L_0 = L_0(Lambda) > 0 such that for all L in (0, L_0):

  m(L) >= c L Lambda^2

for a constant c > 0 that depends only on the model (not on L).

The value of L_0 is determined by the requirement that the semiclassical
expansion is reliable:

  g^2(1/L_0) < g_0^2

where g_0^2 is a threshold below which the instanton expansion converges
(g_0^2 ~ 1 in practice, determined by the convergence of the cluster
expansion in the instanton gas).

Concretely, L_0 ~ 1/(e^{c'} Lambda) for some constant c' ~ O(1),
i.e., L_0 is a fixed multiple of the correlation length 1/Lambda.

**This is our initial condition for the bootstrap: m(L_0) > 0.**


## 2.7 Quantitative bounds at L_0

At L = L_0, we have the following quantitative information:

  m(L_0) = 3 C(L_0) L_0 Lambda^2 (1 + O(g^2(1/L_0)))

  m(L_0) >= c_1 L_0 Lambda^2    (lower bound)
  m(L_0) <= c_2 L_0 Lambda^2    (upper bound)

where c_1, c_2 are O(1) constants computable from the one-loop
determinant. For definiteness, numerical estimates give:

  c_1 ~ 0.1,  c_2 ~ 10

(These are rough order-of-magnitude estimates. The exact values depend
on the regularization scheme and the precise value of L_0.)

The RATIO m(L_0)/Lambda is:

  m(L_0)/Lambda = O(L_0 Lambda) = O(1)

since L_0 ~ 1/Lambda. So the mass gap at the starting point is of order
the dynamical scale Lambda — exactly where it should be for the
bootstrap to have a chance of reaching the infinite-volume limit.
