## Part F, Point 2: Reflection Positivity — The Full Chain

**Weight:** MEDIUM
**Verdict:** CLOSABLE GAP

**Finding:**

(a) Lattice reflection positivity for the Wilson action is the Osterwalder-Seiler theorem (1978), which uses the checkerboard decomposition specific to nearest-neighbor actions. The KK-enhanced theory adds internal connections at each site and bond couplings $V_\ell$. The question is whether the full action $S_{4D} + S_{int}$ has the checkerboard structure. The Wilson plaquette part $S_{4D}$ satisfies RP by Osterwalder-Seiler. The internal part $S_{int}$ decomposes into on-site terms $S_{YM}^{int}(A_x)$ (which are positive and local, preserving RP) and bond couplings $V_\ell$ (nearest-neighbor interactions). The bond couplings have the form $V_\ell(U_\ell, A_x, A_{x+\hat{\mu}}) = (1/a^2) \int |A_{x+\hat{\mu}} - U_\ell^{-1} A_x U_\ell|^2 dvol$, which is a positive-definite quadratic form. The full action is a sum of nearest-neighbor terms, each positive semi-definite. The checkerboard decomposition extends to this case because: (i) on each time-slice, the on-site and spatial-bond terms are contained within the slice; (ii) the temporal-bond terms couple adjacent time-slices with a positive-definite kernel. This is exactly the structure required for Osterwalder-Seiler. **However, the preprint relies primarily on the Wilson action RP (which is for the standard theory) and handles the KK theory separately in Appendix D.** The argument in Appendix D uses a product manifold lemma (Lemma D.1) and is physically motivated but less rigorous for the interacting (non-product) case. The preprint acknowledges (Appendix D, Section D.6) that cross-terms "require the tools of constructive field theory" for rigorous treatment. **This is a closable gap**: the RP for the full KK action can be proved by extending the Osterwalder-Seiler checkerboard argument to include the bond couplings, since they are nearest-neighbor and positive-definite. Estimate: 1 page.

(b) RP through the RG: the proof does NOT require RP at intermediate scales. The RG transforms the effective action, which may not be RP at intermediate steps (Balaban's block-spin can generate non-local terms that spoil the checkerboard structure). But the proof only uses RP at two endpoints: (i) the lattice theory (where RP is proved by Osterwalder-Seiler), and (ii) the continuum limit (where RP is preserved by weak limits). The effective action at intermediate scales is not required to be RP — it is used only for the form factor bounds, which depend on the transfer matrix spectrum and analyticity, not on RP. SOUND.

(c) RP in the continuum limit via lower semicontinuity: the argument requires $\theta f \cdot f$ to be a continuous bounded function of the field configuration for Schwartz-class $f$. On the lattice, gauge fields take values in the compact group SU(N), so all observables are bounded. In the continuum limit, the Schwinger functions are distributional limits, and the pairing $\langle \theta f, f \rangle_\mu$ involves integration of smeared Schwinger functions, which are continuous bounded functions of the distribution-valued field. The lower semicontinuity of non-negative quadratic forms under weak convergence of measures is standard (cf. Glimm-Jaffe, Chapter 18). SOUND.

**Impact on the claimed result:** The RP for the KK-enhanced lattice theory is closable (extend Osterwalder-Seiler to include the positive-definite bond couplings). Intermediate RP is not required. Continuum RP is preserved by weak limits. Overall: closable gap, 1 page.
