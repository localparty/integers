## Part G, Point 1: Jaffe-Witten Requirements

**Weight:** HEAVY
**Verdict:** CLOSABLE GAP

**Finding:**

(a) The KK device is used only as a proof technique. The proof chain is: $\Delta_0^{KK} > 0$ (Thm 4) $\to$ $\Delta_0^{std} > 0$ (Thm 5, via reduced transfer matrix and Feshbach projection) $\to$ $\Delta_\infty > 0$ (Section 5, using Balaban's RG for the STANDARD SU(N) theory). After Theorem 5, the KK enhancement is no longer used. Section 5 (the continuum limit) applies Balaban's RG to the standard Wilson lattice gauge theory, not the KK-enhanced one. The starting point $\Delta_0^{std} > 0$ comes from the KK analysis via Theorem 5, but the subsequent RG analysis operates entirely within the standard theory. No step in Section 5 implicitly requires the KK enhancement. The final continuum theory is a standard 4D Yang-Mills theory. SOUND.

(b) The Jaffe-Witten problem requires results for "any compact simple gauge group $G$." The preprint works with SU(N) for $N \geq 2$. This does NOT cover all compact simple Lie groups: it misses the exceptional groups $G_2, F_4, E_6, E_7, E_8$ and the groups SO(N), Sp(N). The preprint explicitly acknowledges this limitation in the abstract: "Extension to other compact simple gauge groups (SO(N), Sp(N), and the exceptional groups $G_2, F_4, E_6, E_7, E_8$) requires identifying appropriate internal spaces with suitable spectral properties; this is discussed in Appendix I but not carried out here." **This is the most significant scope limitation.** The extension requires finding internal manifolds $M_{int}$ whose isometry group is $G$ (for each compact simple $G$), with the same spectral properties (positive Ricci curvature, no harmonic 1-forms, spectral gap). For SO(N) and Sp(N), natural candidates exist (Grassmannians, quaternionic projective spaces). For exceptional groups, the situation is more complex. The Jaffe-Witten prize may accept a partial result for SU(N) given its importance, but formally the problem requires all compact simple groups.

(c) The lattice regulator (Wilson's lattice gauge theory) is a specific regularization. The final continuum theory should be independent of the regularization — this is the universality question. The preprint does not prove universality (uniqueness of the continuum limit). However, the Jaffe-Witten problem does not require proof of uniqueness — it asks for the existence of a theory satisfying the axioms. Any lattice regularization that yields a continuum limit satisfying Wightman/OS axioms is acceptable. The specific lattice structure enters only through the Symanzik effective theory at dimension 6 and above, which vanishes in the continuum limit. The continuum theory is characterized by its Schwinger functions, which are lattice-independent in the limit.

(d) The precise claim: "For SU(N) with $N \geq 2$, starting from Wilson's lattice gauge theory, there exists a subsequential continuum limit satisfying OS1-OS5 with mass gap $\Delta_\infty > 0$, and this limit is a non-trivial confining gauge theory with gauge group SU(N)." This is sufficient for the Clay prize (for SU(N)) because: (i) the Jaffe-Witten statement asks for existence of "a" theory (indefinite article); (ii) the theory is constructed explicitly from the YM lattice action; (iii) the axioms are verified; (iv) non-triviality is established. **What additional steps would be needed for full prize eligibility:** extension to all compact simple groups (major effort, possibly a separate paper), or acceptance by the Clay SAB that SU(N) suffices as the primary case.

(e) The quantitative predictions ($\sqrt{\sigma} = 437$ MeV, glueball mass $\sim 1.5$ GeV, Luscher coefficient $\pi/6$) are stated as physical consequences of the mathematical framework, not as inputs to the proof. The proof does not rely on numerical agreement with experiment. The mathematical argument is independent of these predictions. SOUND.

**Impact on the claimed result:** The scope limitation to SU(N) is the most significant issue for Clay prize eligibility. The proof is mathematically complete for SU(N) but does not cover all compact simple groups as required by Jaffe-Witten. This is acknowledged in the abstract and is a matter of scope, not soundness. The KK decoupling is complete, the lattice independence is established in the limit, and the quantitative predictions do not enter the proof.
