## Part D, Point 2: Stability of Boltzmann Deviation Order

**Weight:** HEAVY
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

This is the single most important technical innovation in the paper. My interrogation of each sub-point:

(a) Definition D.2 defines dev(O) >= p in terms of a factorization of the spectral weight $W_\alpha^{(m)}(\mathbf{n}) \cdot e^{-E(\mathbf{n})}$ into at least $p$ deviation factors $(e^{E_m - E_{n_j}} - 1)$ with bounded residual. This definition IS equivalent to the intuitive notion "the connected matrix element is suppressed by $\hat{\Delta}^p$" -- this equivalence is exactly what the spectral lemma (Section 5.5.3) proves. The definition is STRONGER than requiring the absence of low-excitation channels (the old "excitation number" notion), because dev >= p allows channels with $\#(\mathbf{n}) < p$ to be present, provided the weight vanishes to order >= p at the diagonal. This distinction is critical: for $Tr(D_0 F)^2$, the vacuum intermediate channel ($n_1 = 0$) is present but carries weight factor $(e^{E_m - E_0} - 1)^2$, which provides two vanishing powers. The definition correctly captures this.

(b) The linear combination lemma is stated and proved in Section 5.5.3. The proof is elementary: for $O = \sum_i c_i O_i$ with $\sum |c_i| \|O_i\| < \infty$ and dev($O_i$) >= p for all $i$, the spectral representation of $O$ is indexed by pairs $(i, \alpha_i)$ with weight $c_i \cdot W_{\alpha_i,i}^{(m)}(\mathbf{n})$. Each term inherits the factorization from $O_i$, with residual bounded by $|c_i| \cdot |\tilde{W}_{\alpha_i,i}^{(m)}|$. The total residual bound is $\sum_i |c_i| \sum_{\alpha_i} C_{\alpha_i,i} \leq M < \infty$. For operators with different temporal extents $R_i$, shorter operators are zero-padded with identity time slices -- inserting $\hat{T} \cdot I \cdot \hat{T}$ at additional slots. This preserves the deviation structure because the identity operator $I$ contributes no new non-diagonal weight: for the $I$ insertion, $\langle n_j|I|n_{j+1}\rangle = \delta_{n_j, n_{j+1}}$, so no new channels are opened. The deviation order of the zero-padded operator is at least that of the original. This is handled correctly by the definition of dev in terms of the minimal representation.

(c) The role of (B1) analyticity is precisely to ensure that $\delta E_k^{d=6}$ is a GENUINE linear combination with convergent coefficients, not a formal asymptotic series. The analyticity radius $\rho$ determines the domain of convergence. Outside this domain (near the boundary of $\Omega_s$), the expansion may not converge -- but this is the large-field region, whose contribution is separately controlled by Balaban's large-field estimates $O(e^{-c/g_k^{2\epsilon}})$. The spectral lemma is applied only within $\Omega_s$, where the analyticity ensures the expansion converges. The matrix element $\langle 1|\delta E_k^{d=6}|1\rangle_c$ involves expectation values with respect to the Boltzmann measure, which concentrates on typical configurations. In the gapped phase, typical configurations have $|F_{\mu\nu}| \sim g_k$ (small), well within the analyticity domain $|F_{\mu\nu}| < g_k^{1-\epsilon}$. The probability of configurations near the boundary of $\Omega_s$ is exponentially suppressed by the Boltzmann weight. So yes, typical configurations contributing to the matrix element lie within the analyticity domain.

(d) The transition from perturbative to non-perturbative is the key insight. The r05 referee initially flagged this as a GENUINE GAP, then revised to CLOSABLE after careful re-examination. The key realization: the classification is EXHAUSTIVE -- every C-even dimension-6 gauge-invariant operator has dev >= 2 by structure, not by perturbative identification. The non-perturbative operator $\delta E_k^{d=6}$ is (by (B1)) a convergent sum of operators from this classification. The deviation order of the sum is at least 2 by the linear combination lemma. The uniqueness of $S_{YM}$ as the sole dimension-4 operator ensures the projection to dimension 6 is exact (no dimension-4 contamination). The projection is well-defined non-perturbatively because (B1) guarantees convergent Taylor expansion, so "dimension-6 part" is the coefficient of $a^2$ in the expansion, which is unambiguous. Dimension-8 and higher contribute $O(a^4)$ corrections with dev >= 4.

(e) The diagonal vanishing $(e^{E_1-E_1}-1)^2 = 0$ IS structural, not kinematic. It arises from the squared-derivative structure of all C-even dimension-6 operators. The deviation order is a property of the operator CLASS, not of a specific matrix element. For the non-perturbative operator, the spectral weights are inferred from the classification via the linear combination lemma. The concern about cancellations: can dev >= 2 for each term but dev < 2 for the sum? No -- the linear combination lemma directly addresses this. The weight of the sum at each channel is the sum of the individual weights. If each individual weight admits $p$ deviation factors, so does the sum (with bounded total residual). Cancellations between terms can only INCREASE the vanishing order (reduce the residual), not decrease it.

**Impact on the claimed result:** None. The stability of deviation order argument is the genuine innovation of the paper and it is sound. The logic is: (B1) -> convergent expansion -> each term classified -> each class has dev >= 2 -> linear combination lemma -> total dev >= 2. No step is circular or assumes the conclusion.
