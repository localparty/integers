## Part E, Point 1: Inductive Stability

**Weight:** MEDIUM
**Verdict:** SOUND

**Finding:**

(a) The factor 1/4 comes from the kinematic identity $\hat{\Delta}_k^2 = \hat{\Delta}_{k+1}^2/4$, arising because doubling the lattice spacing doubles the dimensionless gap: $\hat{\Delta}_{k+1} = 2\hat{\Delta}_k(1 + O(g_k^4))$. The old form factor $C_k g_k^4 \hat{\Delta}_k^2$, expressed in step-$(k+1)$ variables, becomes $C_k g_k^4 \hat{\Delta}_{k+1}^2/4 \cdot (1 + O(g_k^2))$. The change of lattice does introduce corrections from expressing the old state $|1\rangle_k$ on the new lattice $\Lambda_{k+1}$, but these are controlled by the $O(g_k^2 C_k)$ term in the recursion. Specifically: the coarsened operator $E_k^\downarrow$ on $\Lambda_{k+1}$ inherits the derivative structure of $E_k$ with coefficient $|c_{d_O}^\downarrow| \leq |c_{d_O}^{(k)}|(1+O(g_k^2))$. The additional corrections from the lattice change are subleading relative to the $1/4$ contraction.

(b) The wave function correction uses Kato perturbation theory: $\|\delta 1\| \leq C g_k^4/\hat{\Delta}_k$. This requires $\|E_k\| \leq C g_k^4$ to be small compared to $\hat{\Delta}_k$ (the spectral gap of the unperturbed transfer matrix). On the AF trajectory, $g_k^4 \sim 1/k^2$ while $\hat{\Delta}_k$ remains O(1) in lattice units (the dimensionless gap is bounded below by $C_\infty > 0$). So the ratio $\|E_k\|/\hat{\Delta}_k \sim g_k^4 \to 0$ as $k \to \infty$. For the finitely many initial steps where $g_k^4 = O(1)$, Kato perturbation theory still applies as long as $\|E_k\| < \hat{\Delta}_k$, which is satisfied because $\hat{\Delta}_0 \sim O(1)$ (the lattice mass gap is of order the QCD scale in lattice units) and $\|E_k\| \leq C g_k^4$ with $C$ a moderate constant. The cross terms from $|\delta 1\rangle$ contribute $O(g_k^8/\hat{\Delta}_k)$, which is subleading.

(c) The Gronwall bound gives $\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(1+\alpha g_j^2) \leq k^{\alpha/(b_0 \ln 2)}$, a power law. The exponent $\gamma = \alpha/(b_0 \ln 2)$ depends on $N$ through $b_0 = 11N/(48\pi^2)$ and $\alpha$ (which depends on N through the adjoint Casimir). For large N, $b_0 \sim N$ while $\alpha \sim N^2$ (from the adjoint Casimir), giving $\gamma \sim N$. The convergence of $\sum C_k g_k^4 \hat{\Delta}_k^2$ is guaranteed by $4^{-k}$ for ALL $\gamma$: $\sum k^{\gamma-2} 4^{-k} < \infty$ is a convergent series for any $\gamma$ (the geometric factor $4^{-k}$ dominates any polynomial $k^\gamma$). The constants become N-dependent (the total sum is $O(1)$ for each fixed N), which is acceptable: the Clay prize requires the proof to work for each fixed N, not uniformly in N.

**Impact on the claimed result:** None. The inductive stability is robust: the 1/4 contraction dominates the O(1) addition from new operators, and the doubly exponential convergence $4^{-k}$ overwhelms any polynomial growth from accumulated corrections.
