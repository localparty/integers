## Part D, Point 1: Exhaustiveness of the Dimension-6 Classification

**Weight:** MEDIUM
**Verdict:** SOUND

---

**Finding:**

**(a) The Lüscher-Weisz basis and lattice operators.** The classification of dimension-6, gauge-invariant, $\mathcal{C}$-even operators appears in Appendix J (Theorem J.1) and Section 5.3. Theorem J.1 states: "Every local, gauge-invariant, $\mathcal{C}$-even, parity-even operator of engineering dimension 6 on the 4D hypercubic lattice is a linear combination of $\mathrm{Tr}(D_\rho F_{\mu\nu})^2$ and its equations-of-motion (EOM) variants."

The claim that no additional lattice-only operators arise at dimension 6 rests on:
1. Products of plaquettes at the same vertex have dimension $4n$ ($n \geq 2$), never 6.
2. A dimension-6 operator requires exactly two covariant lattice finite differences acting on $\mathrm{Tr}(F^2)$ (or its Lorentz variants). Any such operator, upon taking the continuum limit, reduces to a linear combination of $\mathrm{Tr}(D_\rho F_{\mu\nu})^2$ and EOM terms.
3. The cubic operators $\mathcal{O}_2 = \mathrm{Tr}(F^3)$ and $\mathcal{O}_3 = d^{abc}F^3$ are $\mathcal{C}$-odd and have zero coefficient in the $\mathcal{C}$-invariant effective action.

This coincides with the Lüscher-Weisz (1985) operator basis for the Symanzik effective theory at $O(a^2)$. The standard Symanzik argument (Symanzik 1983) is that operators redundant by equations of motion do not appear as independent operators in the on-shell $S$-matrix, but can appear in the off-shell effective action. The preprint addresses this correctly: EOM-redundant operators are included in the classification and shown to have $\mathrm{dev} \geq 2$ by the EOM reduction (Section 5.6, III.3). The argument is stated and proved for the specific Wilson action used. SOUND.

**(b) The second two-derivative operator.** The operator $\mathcal{O}_{\text{EOM}} = \mathrm{Tr}(D_\mu F^{\mu\rho} D_\nu F^\nu{}_\rho)$ was flagged by the r05 referee. Section 5.6, III.3 addresses it explicitly and completely:

- By the Yang-Mills EOM ($D_\mu F^{\mu\nu} = 0$ on-shell) and the Bianchi identity, $\mathcal{O}_{\text{EOM}} = \mathrm{Tr}(D_\rho F_{\mu\nu})^2 + \text{commutator terms}$.
- The commutator terms have the form $\mathrm{Tr}(F_{\mu\nu}[F^{\mu\rho}, F^\nu{}_\rho])$: three field-strength factors, zero derivatives. These are $\mathcal{C}$-odd (odd power of $F$ under $F \to -F^T$) and have coefficient zero in the $\mathcal{C}$-invariant effective action.
- An explicit spectral verification is provided: $\mathcal{O}_{\text{EOM}} = \sum_\rho (D_\mu F^{\mu\rho})^2$, and the factor $D_\mu F^{\mu\rho}$ acts as $(\text{covariant divergence of } F)$ which annihilates the vacuum state by Gauss's law. More precisely, the squared deviation structure $\sum_n (e^{E_m - E_n} - 1)^2 |...|^2$ gives $\mathrm{dev}(\mathcal{O}_{\text{EOM}}) \geq 2$.

The r05 gap is closed. SOUND.

**(c) Lattice artifacts at dimension 6 and operator mixing.** The concern: Wilson action $O(a^2)$ artifacts could mix dimension-6 operators into the dimension-4 part through operator mixing under block-spin integration.

The preprint's response (PROOF-CHAIN.md [CONFIRMED] item "Dimension-6 projection is exact"): "The unique local, gauge-invariant, $\mathcal{C}$-even, parity-even operator of engineering dimension 4 is $S_{\text{YM}}$: operators $s_P^2$ have dimension 8; $\mathrm{Tr}(F\tilde{F})$ is parity-odd and absent; redundant operators are not generated by the block-spin integration. Balaban defines $g_{k+1}$ as the coefficient of $S_{\text{YM}}$ (CMP 109, Sec. 2), so the subtraction is exact."

The argument that $s_P^2$ has dimension 8 (not 6) is correct: the Wilson plaquette $s_P = 1 - \frac{1}{N}\mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}\,U_P \approx \frac{1}{2N} a^4 \mathrm{Tr}(F_{\mu\nu}^2)$ has dimension 4. The product $s_P^2 \approx \frac{1}{4N^2} a^8 (\mathrm{Tr}(F^2))^2$ has dimension 8. No dimension-6 artifact from two-plaquette products exists. Single-plaquette products with extra finite differences give dimension-6 operators that are already in the classification. SOUND.

**Impact on the claimed result:** No gap. The dimension-6 classification is exhaustive and correctly handles the EOM-related second operator. This classification is the backbone of the stability of deviation order argument (Part D2), and its soundness is essential for $\Delta_\infty > 0$.
