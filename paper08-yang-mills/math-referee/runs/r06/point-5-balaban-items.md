## (a) The uniqueness of $S_\mathrm{YM}$ as the sole dimension-4 operator

**Claim under attack:** On the lattice, operators like $\sum_P (\mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}\,U_P)^2$ contain lower-dimensional contributions that could contaminate the dimension-4 sector.

**Finding: SOUND.**

The concern is that $(\mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}\,U_P)^2$ contains a dimension-0 constant term $N^2$ and a dimension-4 term $-N a^4 \mathrm{Tr}\,F^2$. But the dimension-4 term is proportional to $\mathrm{Tr}\,F^2$, which is $S_\mathrm{YM}$ up to a constant. It is NOT an independent dimension-4 operator.

The uniqueness argument is correct:
- $s_P^2 = [\mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}(\mathbf{1} - U_P)]^2$ has leading term $O(a^8)$ in the field-strength expansion, i.e., engineering dimension 8. Its dimension-4 contribution is proportional to $\sum_P s_P = S_\mathrm{YM}$, already absorbed by coupling renormalization.
- $\mathrm{Tr}(F\tilde{F})$ is parity-odd (the lattice action and Haar measure are parity-invariant, so this operator cannot appear).
- Redundant operators (vanishing by equations of motion) are not generated by the block-spin integration.

The dimension-4 operator space is one-dimensional (spanned by $S_\mathrm{YM}$). Balaban's coupling renormalization extracts this unique coefficient, and the remainder is genuinely dimension $> 4$. This is proved by the uniqueness of $S_\mathrm{YM}$, which is a lattice-level statement (not a continuum approximation).

The question about "continuum dimension vs. lattice dimension" is addressed: Balaban defines dimension by the engineering scaling of the operator (counting powers of the lattice spacing $a$), which is well-defined on the lattice. The dimension-6 projection is exact when the effective action is analytic (B1), because the Taylor expansion in $a^2$ converges.

## (b) The caveat about page numbers

**Claim under attack:** A proof citing equation numbers from secondary sources without checking against the primary source is not "unconditional."

**Finding: CLOSABLE GAP.**

The verification report states:

> "Specific page/equation numbers are from the extraction in Appendix H and have not been checked against the published journal pages."

The PROOF-CHAIN.md states:

> "The proof chain is complete and unconditional, subject to the caveat that the specific Balaban equation/page references have not been independently checked."

The word "unconditional" is used in the mathematical sense: no remaining conjectures or open steps. The argument stands or falls on the CONTENT of Balaban's results, not on their page numbers. The content -- convergent polymer expansion with exponentially decaying activities, propagator analyticity, kernel analyticity -- is well-established in the constructive QFT literature and confirmed by independent sources (Dimock 2011, Brydges 1986).

However, for a paper claiming to solve a Millennium Problem, the epistemic distinction between "confirmed from extraction documents" and "confirmed from published journal text" should be stated more prominently. The proof is complete conditional on the correctness of the extraction -- and the extraction is likely correct, but this has not been independently verified.

**Closing the gap requires:** A referee with access to the published CMP volumes 95--119 checking the specific propositions and theorem numbers cited. This is a verification task, not new mathematics. The argument does not depend on the precise page numbers; it depends on the specific mathematical content being present in Balaban's work.

## (c) The CMP 95 Proposition 3.2 reference

**Claim under attack:** Does a Proposition 3.2 with the claimed content (propagator analyticity) exist in Balaban CMP 95?

**Finding: CLOSABLE GAP -- I cannot independently verify this.**

Balaban's CMP 95 paper is "Propagators and renormalization transformations for lattice gauge theories. I" (*Commun. Math. Phys.* **95**, 17--40, 1984). The claimed content of Proposition 3.2 is:

> The propagator $G_k(V) = (-D^2[V] + m_W^2)^{-1}$ is analytic in $V$ in the small-field region, with $\|G_k\| \leq C/m_W^2$ and exponential decay $|G_k(x,y;V)| \leq C e^{-m_W|x-y|}$.

This content is exactly what one would expect from a paper on propagator construction for lattice gauge fields with gauge fixing. The result is standard: the covariant Laplacian $-D^2[V]$ depends polynomially on $V_\ell$, the mass term $m_W^2 > 0$ ensures invertibility, and the exponential decay follows from the mass gap in the free propagator. Dimock (2011, Theorem 3.1) establishes the analogous result in the scalar setting.

The content is almost certainly correct. Whether the specific numbering "Proposition 3.2" matches the published text is a bibliographic detail that I cannot verify from the preprint files alone.

**Impact: The argument does not depend on the proposition number.** If the result is at Proposition 3.1 or Proposition 3.3 instead, the mathematical argument is unchanged. A referee with journal access should confirm.
