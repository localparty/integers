  What Happened                                                                      
                                                                                  
  I tried to compute f_c(0) from the cluster expansion. The cluster expansion gives  
  f_c(0) ≤ C × M_O — a constant, independent of aΔ. The (aΔ)^{d_O} scaling that the  
  dimensional analysis predicted does NOT appear from the cluster expansion. The
  lattice operator norm is "dimension-blind."                                        
                                                                                  
  This means the double suppression argument from Section 45 has a real gap: the     
  (aΛ)² factor was assumed from dimensional analysis but cannot be derived from the
  lattice alone.                                                                     
                                                                                  
  The Fundamental Insight                                                            
  
  The form factor bound IS the continuum limit. Proving |f_c(0)| ≤ C(aΔ)^{d_O} is    
  equivalent to proving that the lattice operator approaches its continuum form in
  the one-particle sector. This is not a step toward the continuum limit — it IS the 
  continuum limit.                                                                

  But It's a SPECIFIC, NARROW Version

  Instead of proving the full continuum limit (all correlation functions, all OS     
  axioms), we only need the form factor of irrelevant operators in the one-particle
  state. This is a much more specific object.                                        
                                                                                  
  The Path Forward

  The form factor bound IS proved in perturbation theory (to all orders in g²). Each 
  perturbative order gives f_c ~ g^{d_O-2} × (aΔ)^{d_O-4}. The instanton corrections
  are exponentially small (e^{-8π²/g²}).                                             
                                                                                    
  The remaining step: promote the perturbative bound to a non-perturbative one. In   
  Balaban's framework, this means extending his multi-scale estimates from the
  effective action (which he controls) to three-point matrix elements (which he      
  doesn't, but the same technology applies).
