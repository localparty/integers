## CMP 119 verbatim quotes

Source: T. Balaban, "Convergent Renormalization Expansions for Lattice Gauge Theories,"
*Commun. Math. Phys.* **119**, 243–285 (1988). PDF read directly:
`journals/balaban-CMP119-1988.pdf`.

### Unnumbered Theorem (Introduction, p. 245)

> **Theorem.** *If $\varrho_k$ satisfies the assumptions described in detail in Sect. 2,
> then $T\varrho_k$ satisfies also the corresponding assumptions.*

This is the inductive-step propagation theorem. It is the statement accurately cited by
Appendix H §3 Step (a): "Theorem (unnumbered, p. 245)." The citation is correct for
this specific claim (that the RG step $T$ preserves the inductive domain). It is *not*
the UV stability bound; it is the propagation mechanism that, iterated, yields stability.

### Theorem 1 (§2, p. 262)

> **Theorem 1.** *There exist constants, introduced in the above description, such that if
> the sequence of coupling constants $\{g_k\}$, determined by the recursive renormalization
> group (Callan-Symanzik) equations (0.18), (0.20) [I], satisfies the inequalities (1.0.33),
> then the sequence of densities $\{\varrho_k\}$, generated by successive applications of the
> renormalization group operations $\mathbf{RT}$ to the initial density
> $\varrho_0 = \exp[-(1/g_0^2)A - E]$, satisfies all the inductive assumptions. The
> constants satisfy numerous restrictions introduced in the proof.*

This is the main convergence theorem of CMP 119. It is a **fully numbered** theorem in §2
and is the primary reference for the inductive convergence claim in Link 2. Appendix H
should cite this as **CMP 119, Theorem 1 (§2, p. 262)**, not as "main unnumbered theorem."

### Corollary 3 "Ultraviolet Stability" (§2, p. 264)

> **Corollary 3** (Ultraviolet Stability). *Under the assumptions of Theorem 1 there exist
> constants $E_-, E_+$ independent of $\eta$ and $T$, but depending on $g_k$, such that*
>
> $$\chi_k(T_\eta)\exp\!\left[-\frac{1}{g_k^2}A(U_k(V_k)) - E_-|T_\eta|\right]
>   \;\leq\; \varrho_k(V_k) \;\leq\; e^{E_+|T_\eta|}.$$

This is the **Ultraviolet Stability** result in its precise form. The preprint's §5.1.2
theorem box — stating that the effective action remains bounded and the RG iteration does
not leave the domain of analyticity — is the content of **Corollary 3**, proved on the
foundation of Theorem 1. This is the correct citation for §5.1.2.

### Theorem 2 (§2, p. 263, for completeness)

> **Theorem 2.** *Under the assumptions of Theorem 1 there exists a constant $E_1$
> independent of $j, k, \Omega, \{\Omega_j\}, \{\Lambda_j\}$, $T$...* [Uniform bounds
> on effective actions — inequality (2.43).]

Theorem 2 provides the uniform bounds underlying Corollary 3. Not directly needed for
the Link 2 citation fix but noted for completeness.

**§5.1.0 KK-to-standard reduction.**

*Before invoking Balaban's UV stability theorem, we verify that the effective 4D action
input to his construction satisfies his inductive hypotheses.*

At each fixed outer bare-refinement scale $K$, the input to Balaban's inner block-spin
RG ($k = 0$, inner index) is the effective 4D action

$$S_\mathrm{eff}[U] \;=\; S_\mathrm{4D}[U] \;+\; \delta S_\mathrm{KK}[U],$$

where $S_\mathrm{4D}[U] = \beta_0 \sum_P s_P$ is the standard Wilson plaquette action and
$\delta S_\mathrm{KK}[U]$ is the KK correction arising from integrating out the fiber
connections $A_x \in \mathcal{A}_0(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1})$. By the cluster expansion of
Section 4 (Theorem 3), $\delta S_\mathrm{KK}$ decomposes as a sum of connected cluster
activities

$$\delta S_\mathrm{KK}[U] \;=\; \sum_{X \subset \Lambda_0(K)} \tilde{K}_0(X, U),$$

satisfying $|\tilde{K}_0(X, U)| \leq e^{-\tilde{\kappa}|X|}$ with
$\tilde{\kappa} \asymp m_1 a_0/6 > 0$. Here $m_1$ is the first KK excitation mass and
$a_0 = a^* \cdot 2^{-K}$ is the bare spacing. The exponent $1/6$ is the Kotecky--Preiss
rate from Theorem 3's convergence condition
$2\beta + \alpha < \frac{m_1 a_0}{6} - \ln(c_d K C_0^{1/6})$.
At bare scale $a^* \gg r_3$:

$$\|\delta S_\mathrm{KK}\|_{L^\infty(\text{per bond})} \;\leq\; C_\mathrm{KK}\,e^{-m_1 a^*/6} \;\ll\; g_0^4.$$

**Verification of Balaban's inductive hypotheses (CMP 109, §3; CMP 119, Theorem 1, §2,
p. 262) for $S_\mathrm{eff}$.**

(H1) *Polymer form.* $S_\mathrm{eff} = g_0^{-2} S_\mathrm{YM} + \sum_X \tilde{K}_0(X,U)$
with activities decaying at rate $\tilde{\kappa} \asymp m_1 a_0/6 > 0$. The combined
polymer rate $\kappa := \min(\kappa_\mathrm{Balaban}, \tilde{\kappa}) > 0$ is positive and
$k$-independent.

(H2) *Gauge invariance.* $\delta S_\mathrm{KK}$ is gauge-invariant (it is the output of
a gauge-invariant path integral over the fiber sector at fixed $U$).

(H3) *Analyticity.* $S_\mathrm{4D}$ is polynomial in $U_\ell$. Each $\tilde{K}_0(X, U)$
is analytic in $U_\ell$ in the small-field region $\|U_\ell - \mathbf{1}\| < r_0$, with
radius $r_0 > 0$ independent of $k$ (same analyticity argument as Appendix H §3, Step (b),
applied to the fiber cluster expansion).

(H4) *Remainder bound.* $\|\delta S_\mathrm{KK}\|_{L^\infty} \leq C_\mathrm{KK} e^{-m_1 a^*/6}
< g_0^4$, within Balaban's open-condition small-field domain. The choice $a^* \gg r_3$
makes the left side non-perturbatively small; $e^{-m_1 a^*/6} \ll g_0^4$ since
$m_1 a^* \sim a^*/r_3 \gg 1$.

(H5) *Curvature structure.* $\delta S_\mathrm{KK}[U]$ does not contribute to the plaquette
curvature $|F_{\mu\nu}[U]|$ (no additional plaquette variables; the $A_x$ have been
integrated out). Balaban's large-field/small-field boundary $|F_{\mu\nu}| \geq g_0^{1-\varepsilon}$
is set by the Wilson curvature alone and is unchanged (CMP 119, §2; confirmed by
`sections/K-balaban-general-groups.md` §K.4).

*Conclusion: $S_\mathrm{eff}$ lies in Balaban's domain. UV stability (Corollary 3,
§2, p. 264, CMP 119) applies without modification.*

## Self-suspicion

**S1 — Is the exponent correction ($1/2 \to 1/6$) actually safe for (H4)?**

Replacing $1/2$ by $1/6$ weakens the decay estimate (slower suppression). The question
is whether $e^{-m_1 a^*/6} \ll g_0^4$ still holds. Answer: yes, decisively. At
$a^* \gg r_3$, the product $m_1 a^* \sim a^*/r_3$ is enormous — Wave 3 Critic estimates
$m_1 a^* \sim 10^{15}$ for physical scales. Therefore $e^{-m_1 a^*/6} \sim e^{-10^{15}/6}$,
which is still non-perturbatively zero compared to any fixed $g_0^4$. The factor $1/6$
versus $1/2$ is irrelevant at these scales. (H4) holds with the corrected exponent. The
suspicion is resolved; the correction is conservative (it does not weaken the chain).

**S2 — Does the Corollary 3 citation assignment to §5.1.2 match the content?**

Corollary 3 states: $\chi_k(T_\eta) \exp[-(1/g_k^2) A(U_k(V_k)) - E_- |T_\eta|] \leq \varrho_k(V_k) \leq e^{E_+ |T_\eta|}$.
The §5.1.2 theorem box claims that the effective action is bounded and the RG iteration
does not leave the domain of analyticity for $g_0$ small. This maps onto Corollary 3:
the upper bound $e^{E_+ |T_\eta|}$ gives the energy density bound; the lower bound with
$\chi_k$ gives the confinement to the small-field (analytic) domain. The content match
is correct. However, a referee might observe that the analyticity-domain statement is
more precisely Theorem 1 (which asserts all inductive assumptions, including analyticity,
are propagated), with Corollary 3 giving the quantitative bounds. The correct citation
for §5.1.2 is both: **Theorem 1 + Corollary 3**. The patch cites both throughout.
