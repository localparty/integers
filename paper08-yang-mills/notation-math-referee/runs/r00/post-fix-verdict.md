## Overall Assessment

**Is the mass gap $\Delta_\infty > 0$ proved?**
Yes, with explicit conditionality.

**What is the conditionality?**
The continuum mass gap $\Delta_\infty > 0$ is conditional on the **K-uniform spectral lemma constant**: the bound $C_{\rm new}(K) \leq C_*$ uniformly in the bare-scale index $K$. This requires showing that the spectral lemma constant $C_p$ (proved $k$-independent within each bare theory) is also $K$-independent, i.e., that the two-particle threshold gap $E_2 - E_1 \geq \hat\Delta/2$ and the Combes--Thomas locality estimate hold with constants that do not degrade as $a_0(K) \to 0$. The lattice mass gap $\Delta_0 > 0$ at any fixed coarse lattice spacing is unconditional.

**Is the proof honest about its scope?**
Yes. The preprint clearly labels the K-uniform spectral lemma as "Tier 1B, pending" in $\S$5.4.7 and PROOF-CHAIN.md $\S$IV.5. The four Clay structural ingredients beyond the mass gap are stated as explicit open conjectures (L.1--L.4) with detailed accounting of what is rigorous and what is not. The abstract's summary table says "Proved" for $\Delta_\infty > 0$ without qualification, which slightly overstates the conditional status, but PROOF-CHAIN.md $\S$IV.5 and the body of $\S$5.4 are honest.

**The single most important remaining issue:**
The K-uniform bound $C_{\rm new}(K) \leq C_*$: proving that the spectral lemma constant does not degrade as the bare lattice spacing $a_0(K) \to 0$ in the outer refinement sequence.
