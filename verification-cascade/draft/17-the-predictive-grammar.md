## A finite grammar for an infinite theory

The most surprising feature of the QG5D framework is not that it predicts Standard Model constants. It is that it predicts them using a FINITE GRAMMAR — eight rules that map operator order to formula shape, with zero free parameters. The grammar does not fit data. It GENERATES formulas from algebraic structure, and those formulas match experiment at sub-percent precision 36 times out of 37.

A grammar is a set of production rules that generates well-formed expressions from a finite alphabet. In linguistics, a grammar generates sentences from words. In the QG5D framework, the grammar generates physical formulas from Riemann zeros and geometric constants. The alphabet is {gamma_1, gamma_2, ..., gamma_15, pi, zeta(2), zeta(3), gamma_E, e}. The rules determine how these symbols combine. The results are physical constants.

This grammar exists because the framework's observables are MATRIX ELEMENTS of operators on the Bost-Connes Hilbert space H_R. The operator's ORDER (linear, quadratic, bilinear, exponential...) determines the formula's SHAPE (sum, product, Yukawa coupling, exponential hierarchy...). The grammar is not imposed — it is DERIVED from the operator algebra.

## The eight rules

### Rule 1: SUM (linear, 1st order)

**When it fires:** The observable is a linear combination of BC eigenvalues — a first-order matrix element of T_BC or L-hat.

**Formula shape:** gamma_a + gamma_b (possibly with geometric corrections)

**Example:** m_W = gamma_2 + gamma_13 = 80.38 GeV (experimental: 80.377 +/- 0.012 GeV, deviation: 0.004%)

**Why it's a sum:** The W boson mass involves two sectors (electroweak + nucleosynthesis), each contributing additively. The operator is linear in T_BC, so the matrix element is a sum of eigenvalues.

### Rule 2: PRODUCT (quadratic, 2nd order)

**When it fires:** The observable is a bilinear pairing of BC eigenvalues — a second-order matrix element.

**Formula shape:** gamma_a * gamma_b / c, where c is in {pi, 2*pi, pi^2}

**Normalization is NOT free:** c is determined by the representation:
- c = pi for gauge couplings (U(1) circle normalization)
- c = 2*pi for color-charged Yukawa couplings (S^1 KK circumference integration)
- c = pi^2 for angular-volume observables (S^2 orbit normalization)

**Example:** 1/alpha = gamma_1 * gamma_4 / pi + 0.1 * log(pi) = 137.003 (experimental: 137.036, deviation: 0.024%)

### Rule 3: YUKAWA (bilinear coupling)

**When it fires:** The observable is a mass generated by a Yukawa coupling between a fermion and the Higgs sector.

**Formula shape depends on color charge:**
- Quarks (color-charged): gamma_a * gamma_b / (2*pi) — the 2*pi comes from the S^1 KK circumference integration over color
- Leptons (color-singlet): gamma_a * gamma_b (bare product, no S^1 factor)

**This split is a PREDICTION, not a fit.** The grammar predicts that quark masses have a 1/(2*pi) suppression relative to lepton masses from the same zero pair. This is testable.

**Examples:**
- m_t = gamma_3 * gamma_8 / (2*pi) = 173.05 GeV (experimental: 172.76 +/- 0.30 GeV, deviation: 0.17%)
- m_tau = gamma_7 * gamma_8 = 1772.89 MeV (experimental: 1776.86 +/- 0.12 MeV, deviation: 0.22%)

Same zeros (gamma_8 appears in both). Different normalization (quark vs lepton). The grammar PREDICTS the difference.

### Rule 4: EXPONENTIAL (eigenvalue of scaling operator)

**When it fires:** The observable is a SCALE — a quantity that spans a large dynamic range (the hierarchy problem, the cosmological constant, the Planck-to-cosmic scale ratio).

**Formula shape:** exp(gamma_n * pi^2/2)

**Why exponential:** The observable is literally the eigenvalue of the scaling operator R-hat. The log-spectrum L_n = gamma_n * pi^2/2 gives the linear version. The exponential maps from log-spectrum to physical scale.

**Example:** The cosmological constant: log(pi * R_obs / ell_P) = gamma_1 * pi^2/2 - 0.15/gamma_2 + 0.03/gamma_3 - 0.01 * ln(gamma_2/gamma_1). Precision: 5 parts per billion.

**This is the framework's most precise prediction and the strongest argument against fine-tuning.** The 120-order-of-magnitude hierarchy between the Planck scale and the cosmological scale is NOT fine-tuned — it is the exponential of the FIRST eigenvalue of the BC scaling operator. The "mystery" was a Mellin-inversion artifact (Pattern P6) hiding arithmetic structure.

### Rule 5: LOG (logarithmic / thermal)

**When it fires:** The observable is a deep-IR quantity — a mass or parameter far from the electroweak scale, governed by thermal/logarithmic physics.

**Formula shape:** log(gamma_n) or (log(gamma_n))^2

**Examples:**
- m_b = log(gamma_15) = 4.176 GeV (experimental: 4.18 +/- 0.03 GeV, deviation: 0.09%)
- t_0 = (log gamma_7)^2 Gyr — the age of the universe as a closed form in a single Riemann zero

### Rule 6: FRACTIONAL POWER (internal DoF extraction)

**When it fires:** The observable probes INTERNAL structure — a subrepresentation of a larger algebraic object.

**Formula shape:** gamma_n^{1/k}, where k is the dimension of the internal representation

**The exponent k is DERIVED from representation theory:**
- 1/3: three-fold generation symmetry (gamma_6 carries Z_6; 1/3 extracts the leptonic subsector)
- 1/4: four-fold EW multiplet (gamma_8 carries SU(3) adjoint; 1/4 extracts SU(2) x U(1))
- 3/4: complement of 1/4 (17 = gamma_8^{3/4} — the GUT flux integer)

**Example:** N_eff = gamma_6^{1/3} = 3.350 (experimental: ~3.35, deviation: 0.0082% — the framework's second-most precise prediction after the cosmological constant)

### Rule 7: RATIO (relative scale)

**When it fires:** The observable is a RATIO of two scales — a mixing angle, a mass ratio, or a generation-mixing parameter.

**Formula shape:** gamma_a / gamma_b

**Examples:**
- n_s = gamma_9 / gamma_10 = 0.9655 (experimental: 0.965 +/- 0.004, deviation: 0.055%)
- m_c = gamma_9 / gamma_6 = 1.273 GeV (experimental: 1.275 +/- 0.025 GeV, deviation: 0.17%)

### Rule 8: DIFFERENCE (spectral gap)

**When it fires:** The observable is the SMALLEST quantity — a mass difference, a level splitting, a spectral gap.

**Formula shape:** gamma_a - gamma_b

**Why differences give the smallest quantities:** The Riemann zeros are spaced roughly ~2*pi / log(gamma_n/(2*pi*e)) apart. Differences of consecutive zeros are O(1), while products are O(100). The lightest quarks (m_u, m_d) are DIFFERENCES of zeros — the grammar predicts they are small because they are spectral GAPS, not spectral VALUES.

The grammar reveals a STRUCTURAL pattern across the three generations of fermions:
