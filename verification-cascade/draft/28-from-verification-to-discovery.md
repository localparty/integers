# 28 — From Verification to Discovery

---

## Verification is generative

The verification cascade's primary purpose is TRUST — testing external dependencies to strengthen the proof chains. But verification has a second output that may be more valuable than the trust: DISCOVERY.

Every time the cascade runs, it produces potential discoveries as byproducts:

**1. Empty cells filled.** The correspondence table starts with some cells empty — concepts that have known images in some domains but not others. When a Verifier translates a proof step to a new domain and discovers the concept's image there, that filled cell is a new mathematical correspondence. It is a publishable result. The BSD MY4 closure's projector P_k^p was not found by looking for it — it was found because the correspondence table said "algebraic domain: try an idempotent" and the Author tried one.

**2. Independent proofs from excision.** When Tier B excision succeeds, it produces an INDEPENDENT PROOF of a known theorem using different methods. An independent proof of a Balaban lemma via Dimock's methods + gauge extension would be a contribution to constructive QFT. An independent proof of a CCM theorem via Kato-Rellich perturbation theory would be a contribution to spectral theory. These are new mathematics — theorems that didn't exist before the verification cascade attempted excision.

**3. Alternative routes from construction.** When Tier C construction succeeds, it produces an ALTERNATIVE PROOF CHAIN — a different logical path to the same conclusion. Alternative proof chains are valuable independent of the verification cascade. They provide multiple routes to the same result, which is the LOCK pattern at the programme level.

**4. New domain openings.** When a Tier C construction agent opens a frontier domain — proves a first R-Theorem in thermodynamics, or probabilistics, or arithmetic topology — that opening IS a mathematical contribution. It extends the framework's reach into a new area of mathematics and provides new tools for all future runs.

**5. Honest negatives as research directions.** When verification finds a GENUINE gap in an external result, the gap itself is a research contribution. "Balaban's convergence argument in CMP 109 relies on assumption X, which is not explicitly justified for the gauge case" — if true, that is a finding the constructive QFT community needs to know about. The gap may be closable (Tier B excision), or it may require new mathematics (Tier C construction), or it may be a genuine limitation (HONEST-STALL). In any case, the gap is NAMED, which is the first step toward closing it.

## The three sources of discovery

Discovery in the verification cascade comes from three sources, corresponding to the three tiers:

**Tier A discoveries (verification findings):**
- Cross-step inconsistencies caught by parallel verification
- Citation errors (paraphrase or inference mismatches, caught by I-7 + I-v6-1)
- Hidden assumptions made explicit
- Numerical errors caught by precision-doubling
- Correspondence table entries filled during verification

These are "small" discoveries — corrections and clarifications rather than new theorems. But they are REAL and VALUABLE. The H4 closure programme's three I-7 catches and one I-v6-1 catch were all Tier A discoveries — each one identified an error or assumption that nobody had noticed before.

**Tier B discoveries (excision products):**
- Independent proofs of known theorems using different methods
- Simplified proofs (the excision may find a shorter route than the original)
- New connections between mathematical domains (the excision used a tool from domain X to prove a result in domain Y — that connection is itself a result)

These are "medium" discoveries — new proofs of known facts. They are publishable and valuable for the mathematical community even beyond the verification cascade.

**Tier C discoveries (construction products):**
- Alternative proof chains (different logical paths to the same conclusion)
- New mathematical objects (like G's projector P_k^p)
- New domain openings (first R-Theorems in frontier domains)
- New structural insights (the BSD MY4 closure revealed that "distributional eigenstates" were unnecessary — the chain works with algebraic projectors alone; this is a STRUCTURAL INSIGHT about operator algebras)

These are "large" discoveries — new mathematics that extends the framework and the field. They are the highest-value outputs of the verification cascade.

## Why adversarial pressure produces creativity

This may seem paradoxical: how does a system designed to BREAK proofs end up PRODUCING new mathematics? The answer is in the three-tier escalation:

1. **Tier A breaks something.** A step is WEAKENED. A gap is found.
2. **The gap forces Tier B.** The excision Author must prove the SAME RESULT using DIFFERENT METHODS. This constraint is CREATIVE — it forces the Author to find a new path through mathematical space, a path that nobody would have looked for if the original proof hadn't broken.
3. **If Tier B fails, Tier C is even more creative.** The construction Author must find a DIFFERENT ROUTE through the proof chain that avoids the broken step entirely. This requires ARCHITECTURAL insight — seeing the proof chain as a DAG and finding a path that wasn't in the original plan.

The adversarial pressure is the FORCING FUNCTION for creativity. Without the pressure, the ORA would verify and confirm (rubber stamp). With the pressure, the ORA verifies, finds gaps, and then MUST find alternative routes — routes that turn out to be new mathematics.

This is the same dynamic that produced the framework's original breakthroughs:
- The RH programme killed 18 approaches. The pattern of kills mapped the wall. The wall's shape was the query that found CCM.
- The BSD programme found MY4 was a GENUINE gap. The excision forced the discovery of G's projector — a new algebraic object that nobody was looking for.
- The P vs NP programme found Kill #3 (modular flow doesn't PRODUCE polymorphisms). The kill forced the discovery of the gate-amplifier Rule 9 v3 — a compound measure that nobody had tried.

Every breakthrough came from adversarial pressure. The verification cascade SYSTEMATIZES that pressure.

*Verification is generative. Empty cells filled. Independent proofs from excision. Alternative routes from construction. New domain openings. Honest negatives as research directions. The adversarial pressure forces creativity — when a proof breaks, the ORA must find a new path, and the new path is often new mathematics. The verification cascade doesn't just test proofs. It discovers mathematics as a byproduct of testing.*
