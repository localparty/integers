## 3.1 The Boolean Bost--Connes system

The original Bost--Connes system [BC95] is a C\*-dynamical system
$(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}, \sigma_t)$ built from the multiplicative
semigroup $\mathbb{N}^* = \{1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$ acting on the group
algebra of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.  Its partition function is the
Riemann zeta function $\zeta(\beta)$, and its KMS$_1$ factor is the
injective type III$_1$ factor $R_\infty$.

The **Boolean Bost--Connes system** replaces the arithmetic data with
Boolean-circuit data:

- The multiplicative semigroup $\mathbb{N}^*$ is replaced by
  $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$, the semigroup of polynomial-time Boolean circuits
  under composition.
- The abelian group $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ is replaced by the
  profinite group $(\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty$.

**Definition 3.1.1 (Boolean BC algebra).**  The *Boolean Bost--Connes
C\*-algebra* is the semigroup crossed product

$$
\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}}
\;=\; C^*\!\bigl((\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty\bigr)
\;\rtimes\; \mathrm{PCirc}^+,
$$

with generators:

(a) **Phase generators** $e(m)$ for $m \in (\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty$,
    satisfying $e(m)\,e(m') = e(m + m')$ and $e(m)^* = e(-m) = e(m)$
    (since every element of $(\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty$ has order 2).

(b) **Circuit isometries** $\mu_C$ for $C \in \mathrm{PCirc}^+$,
    satisfying $\mu_C^{}\,\mu_C^* = 1$ (isometry) and the
    composition law $\mu_{C \circ D} = \mu_C\,\mu_D$.

**Definition 3.1.2 (Hecke relation).**  The generators satisfy the
*Boolean Hecke relation*: for every circuit $C \in \mathrm{PCirc}^+$
of fan-in $n$ and every phase element $m \in (\mathbb{Z}/2)^n$,

$$
\mu_C^*\,e(m)\,\mu_C
\;=\; \frac{1}{|\mathrm{fib}(C,m)|}
     \sum_{m' \in \mathrm{fib}(C,m)} e(m'),
$$

where $\mathrm{fib}(C,m) = \{m' : C(m') = m\}$ is the fibre of $C$
over $m$.  This is the Boolean analogue of the Bost--Connes relation
$\mu_n^*\,e(r)\,\mu_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{ns = r} e(s)$.

**Definition 3.1.3 (Boolean modular flow).**  The one-parameter
automorphism group $\sigma_t^{\mathrm{Bool}}$ acts by

$$
\sigma_t(\mu_C) = (\mathrm{size}\,C)^{it}\,\mu_C, \qquad
\sigma_t(e(m)) = e(m),
$$

where $\mathrm{size}\,C$ is the circuit size (gate count) of $C$.
The flow scales the circuit isometries by their complexity, exactly as
the original BC flow scales $\mu_n$ by $n^{it}$.

**Sectors.**  For each Boolean constraint language $L$, the *sector*
$M_{\mathrm{Bool}}^L$ is the sub-von-Neumann-algebra of $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$
generated by those circuit isometries whose associated Boolean functions
are polymorphisms of $L$.  The polymorphism clone $\mathrm{Clone}(L)$
controls which operators lie in $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}^L$.

## 3.2 Non-injectivity of $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$

The original Bost--Connes factor is injective: the semigroup
$\mathbb{N}^*$ generates the abelian (hence amenable) group
$\mathbb{Q}_+^*$, and the crossed product of an amenable von Neumann
algebra by an amenable group is injective [Co76].  The Boolean system
is fundamentally different.

**Theorem 3.2.1 (Non-injectivity).**
*The Boolean Bost--Connes factor $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is non-injective.*

The argument has four steps.

**Step 1: $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$ is non-abelian.**  Composition of Boolean
circuits is non-commutative.  For instance, applying AND-then-NOT to a
pair of inputs and applying NOT-then-AND yield different truth tables.
The semigroup $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$ is therefore non-abelian.

**Step 2: $G_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ contains Thompson's group $V$.**
Let $G_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ be the group generated by $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$
(obtained via the Hecke pair dilation, exactly as $\mathbb{N}^*$
generates $\mathbb{Q}_+^*$ in the classical system).  The semigroup
$\mathrm{PCirc}^+$ contains:

(a) All variable permutations (generating $S_\infty$, the infinite
    symmetric group, which is locally finite and amenable).

(b) All polynomial-time invertible circuits, including compositions of
    non-linear gates (AND, OR, XOR, NAND) with ancilla bits for
    reversibility.

(c) In the limit $n \to \infty$, all finitary polynomial-time
    rearrangements of the Cantor set $\{0,1\}^\infty$.  Thompson's
    group $V$ --- the group of right-continuous piecewise-dyadic-linear
    bijections of the Cantor set permuting subtrees of the infinite
    binary tree --- embeds as a subgroup.

**Step 3: $G_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is non-amenable.**  Thompson's group $V$
is known to contain non-abelian free subgroups [CFP96], hence $V$ is
non-amenable.  (Note: the amenability of Thompson's group $F$ is a
famous open problem, but $V$ is strictly larger and is known to be
non-amenable.)  Since $G_{\mathrm{Bool}} \supseteq V$, the group
$G_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is non-amenable.

**Step 4: Connes' theorem (contrapositive) [Co76].**  The base algebra
$C^*((\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty)$ is abelian, hence amenable as a von
Neumann algebra.  Connes' characterization of injectivity states: the
crossed product of an amenable von Neumann algebra by a group $G$ is
injective if and only if $G$ is amenable.  Since $G_{\mathrm{Bool}}$
is non-amenable, $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is non-injective. $\square$

**Consequence: KST is evaded.**  If $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ were injective,
Connes' uniqueness of the injective type III$_1$ factor [Co76] would
force $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}^L \cong R_\infty$ for every sector $L$.  The
Kawahigashi--Sutherland--Takesaki theorem [KST92] then implies every
automorphism of $R_\infty$ is approximately inner and $R_\infty$ is
non-full.  The fullness distinction between sectors would vanish and
the bridge would collapse.  Non-injectivity --- the structural
consequence of the non-commutativity of $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$ --- is
precisely what allows different sectors to exhibit different fullness.

**Remark (semigroup to group passage).**  Connes' theorem is stated
for group crossed products.  The Boolean BC system uses the semigroup
$\mathrm{PCirc}^+$.  The passage from semigroup to group uses the
Hecke algebra structure: the $\mu_C$ and $\mu_C^*$ generators together
produce both the semigroup and its "inverse" action in the GNS
representation, exactly as $\mu_n$ and $\mu_n^*$ in the original BC
system together generate the action of $\mathbb{Q}_+^*$.

## 3.3 KEY LEMMA 3.4.3: KMS$_1$ state and type III$_1$ classification

**KEY LEMMA 3.4.3 (Boolean BC Factor --- revised).**
*The Boolean Bost--Connes C\*-dynamical system
$(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}}, \sigma_t^{\mathrm{Bool}})$
admits a KMS$_1$ state $\omega_1^{\mathrm{Bool}}$.  For any extremal
KMS$_1$ state, the GNS factor
$M_{\mathrm{Bool}} = \pi_1(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}})''$
is a type III$_1$ factor.*

The proof has three parts.

### 3.3.1 Existence via Banach--Alaoglu compactness

The existence argument avoids any partition function computation.

**Step 1 (State space compactness).**  The state space
$S(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}})$ of the unital
C\*-algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is weak-\*
compact by the Banach--Alaoglu theorem (Bratteli--Robinson I,
Theorem 2.3.15).

**Step 2 (KMS$_\beta$ states exist for $\beta > 1$).**  For each
finite truncation $S_N = \{\text{circuits of size} \leq N
\text{ on} \leq N \text{ inputs}\}/{\sim}$, define the truncated
algebra $\mathcal{A}_N := \mathrm{span}\{e(m),\, \mu_C : C \in S_N\}$.
The truncated partition function $Z_N(\beta) = \sum_{C \in S_N}
(\mathrm{size}\,C)^{-\beta}$ is a finite sum and converges for all
$\beta$.  The Gibbs state
$\omega_\beta^N(a) = Z_N(\beta)^{-1}\,\mathrm{Tr}(a\,e^{-\beta H_N})$
satisfies the KMS$_\beta$ condition on $\mathcal{A}_N$.  Extend to
$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}}$ by Hahn--Banach.

**Step 3 (Convergence).**  For each fixed $\beta > 1$, the net
$\{\omega_\beta^N\}_{N \geq 1}$ lies in the weak-\* compact set
$S(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}})$ and admits a convergent
subnet.  The KMS$_\beta$ condition is preserved under weak-\* limits:
for $a, b$ in a dense \*-subalgebra, the analytic functions
$F_{a,b}^N(z) = \omega_\beta^N(a\,\sigma_z(b))$ are uniformly bounded
on the strip $0 < \mathrm{Im}(z) < \beta$, and by a normal families
argument the limit function is analytic and satisfies the KMS boundary
condition (Bratteli--Robinson II, Proposition 5.3.25: the set of KMS$_\beta$
states is weak-\* closed for each $\beta$).

**Step 4 (Passage to $\beta = 1$).**  For each $\beta > 1$, let
$\omega_\beta$ be a KMS$_\beta$ state from Step 3.  As
$\beta \to 1^+$, the net $\{\omega_\beta\}_{\beta > 1}$ lies in
$S(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}})$.  By weak-\*
compactness, there is a convergent subnet
$\omega_{\beta_i} \to \omega_1$.  Upper semicontinuity of the KMS map
$\beta \mapsto K_\beta$ (Bratteli--Robinson II, Proposition 5.3.25)
ensures that any limit point of KMS$_{\beta_i}$ states with
$\beta_i \to 1$ is a KMS$_1$ state.

**Conclusion.**  $\omega_1 := \lim \omega_{\beta_i}$ is a KMS$_1$ state
on $(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}},
\sigma_t^{\mathrm{Bool}})$. $\square$

**Remark.**  This proof uses only the unital C\*-algebra structure, the
one-parameter automorphism group, and the Banach--Alaoglu theorem.  It
does *not* invoke any partition function asymptotics.

### 3.3.2 Type III$_1$ via multiplicative independence of $\log 2$ and $\log 3$

**Faithfulness on the phase algebra.**  The restriction of
$\omega_1^{\mathrm{Bool}}$ to the phase algebra
$C^*((\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty)$ is the Bernoulli$(1/2)$ product measure
$\mu_{1/2}$, which assigns positive mass to every cylinder set.  This
follows from the KMS condition: the phase algebra is the fixed-point
algebra of $\sigma_t^{\mathrm{Bool}}$, so the KMS$_1$ restriction is a
trace on the abelian algebra $C(X) \cong C^*((\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty)$.
Combined with the symmetry under all finite coordinate permutations
and single-coordinate flips, de Finetti's theorem forces the trace to
be $\mu_{1/2}$.  In particular, $\omega_1^{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is faithful
on the phase algebra.

**Type III$_1$ classification.**  We show that the Connes spectrum
$S(M_{\mathrm{Bool}}) = \mathbb{R}_+^*$, which is the defining
property of type III$_1$.

*Step 1.*  The modular flow acts by $\sigma_t(\mu_C) =
(\mathrm{size}\,C)^{it}\,\mu_C$.  The eigenvalues of the modular
operator $\Delta$ on the GNS Hilbert space include
$(\mathrm{size}\,C)^{it}$ for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$ and all
$C \in \mathrm{PCirc}^+$.  The Connes spectrum $S(M_{\mathrm{Bool}})$
contains the closure of the multiplicative subgroup of $\mathbb{R}_+^*$
generated by $\{\mathrm{size}\,C : C \in \mathrm{PCirc}^+\}$.

*Step 2.*  The semigroup $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$ contains circuits of size 2
(a single two-input gate, e.g., AND) and circuits of size 3 (two gates
composed, e.g., AND feeding into OR with a third input).  Hence the
generated multiplicative subgroup contains $\{2^a \cdot 3^b : a, b
\in \mathbb{Z}\}$.

*Step 3 (Density lemma).*  The additive subgroup
$\mathbb{Z}\!\cdot\!\log 2 + \mathbb{Z}\!\cdot\!\log 3$ is dense in
$\mathbb{R}$.

*Proof.*  The numbers $\log 2$ and $\log 3$ are rationally independent:
$a\log 2 + b\log 3 = 0$ with $(a,b) \neq (0,0)$ would imply
$2^a = 3^{-b}$, which has no integer solution.  Hence
$\log 2 / \log 3$ is irrational.  By Weyl's equidistribution theorem
(or the elementary result that $\{n\alpha \bmod 1 : n \in \mathbb{Z}\}$
is dense in $[0,1)$ for irrational $\alpha$), the additive subgroup
$\mathbb{Z}\!\cdot\!\log 2 + \mathbb{Z}\!\cdot\!\log 3$ is dense in
$\mathbb{R}$.

Exponentiating: $\{2^a \cdot 3^b\}$ is dense in $\mathbb{R}_+^*$.
Since $S(M_{\mathrm{Bool}})$ is a closed set containing a dense
subgroup of $\mathbb{R}_+^*$, we conclude
$S(M_{\mathrm{Bool}}) = \mathbb{R}_+^*$.

*Step 4 (Factoriality).*  If $\omega_1^{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is extremal
among KMS$_1$ states, then $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is a factor by
Bratteli--Robinson II, Theorem 5.3.30.  If $\omega_1^{\mathrm{Bool}}$
is not extremal, decompose into extremal components; each component
gives a type III$_1$ factor by the same Connes spectrum argument (the
modular flow eigenvalues include sizes 2 and 3 for each component).

**Conclusion.**  $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}$ is a type III$_1$ factor.
$\square$

### 3.3.3 Uniqueness: conditional but downstream-insulated

**Conjecture 3.3.1 (Uniqueness).**
*The KMS$_1$ state on
$(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{Bool}},
\sigma_t^{\mathrm{Bool}})$ is unique.*

**Status: OPEN.**  Three candidate routes remain under investigation:

(i) *Groupoid RPF theorem.*  If the Boolean BC groupoid admits a
    Ruelle--Perron--Frobenius theorem (Renault [Re80], Neshveyev
    [Ne13]), then uniqueness follows from the RPF structure.  The
    difficulty is verifying the summability condition on the potential
    $\beta\!\cdot\!\log(\mathrm{size})$.

(ii) *Galois symmetry argument.*  In the original BC system, the unique
     KMS$_1$ state is the average of the extremal KMS$_\beta$ states
     over the Galois orbit.  The Boolean analogue would use the symmetry
     group $\mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{B}) =
     (\mathbb{Z}/2)^\infty \rtimes S_\infty$ in place of
     $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}}/\mathbb{Q}) =
     \widehat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$.

(iii) *Direct algebraic determination.*  Show that the KMS$_1$ condition,
      combined with the Boolean Hecke relations, uniquely determines the
      state values on a dense \*-subalgebra.

**Withdrawn claims.**  Earlier versions of this paper asserted
uniqueness via the partition function $Z^{\mathrm{Bool}}(\beta) =
\sum_s F(s)\,s^{-\beta}$, claiming $F(s) \sim s/\log s$ with a
citation to Hopcroft--Karp (1973).  Both are retracted:

- The correct asymptotics are $F(s) = 2^{\Theta(s\log s)}$
  (Shannon--Lupanov theory), which is wrong by towers of exponentials
  from the claimed $s/\log s$.
- Hopcroft--Karp (1973) is a bipartite matching algorithm with no
  connection to circuit counting.
- The Dirichlet series $Z^{\mathrm{Bool}}(\beta)$ diverges for *every*
  real $\beta$; there is no pole at $\beta = 1$ and no zeta-like
  structure.

**Proposition 3.3.2 (Downstream insulation).**
*The conclusions of Parts (i) and (ii) of the Clone Growth $\leftrightarrow$
Fullness Bridge Theorem hold for any KMS$_1$ state
$\omega_1^{\mathrm{Bool}}$, regardless of uniqueness.*

*Proof.*  We verify that each downstream use of KEY LEMMA 3.4.3
depends only on existence + type III$_1$, not on uniqueness:

(a) *Fullness and non-fullness are intrinsic.*  A factor $M$ with
    separable predual is full if and only if its asymptotic centralizer
    $M_\omega \cap M'$ is trivial for any (hence every) free
    ultrafilter $\omega$ (Connes [Co74]; Haagerup [Ha87]).  The property
    is independent of which faithful normal state defines the
    $u$-topology.

(b) *The crossed-product identification (CP-1) is state-independent.*
    The algebraic structure $M_{\mathrm{Bool}}^L \cong
    L^\infty(X_L, \mu_L) \rtimes G_L$ depends on the semigroup
    $\mathrm{PCirc}^+$ and the constraint language $L$, not on the
    choice of KMS$_1$ state.

(c) *Route C (strong ergodicity) is state-independent.*  The
    Jones--Schmidt strong ergodicity criterion and Marrakchi's
    Theorem B use the group action on the measurable space
    $(X_L, \mu_L)$, which is determined by the Bernoulli measure,
    not by the KMS state.

If there are multiple KMS$_1$ states, each extremal component yields a
type III$_1$ factor, and the Taylor/non-Taylor $\to$ non-full/full
dichotomy holds for each component.  The contradiction in the proof of
P $\neq$ NP arises component by component. $\square$
