## 0. Executive Summary

**Answer to Q_struct:** M_Bool^L is NOT universally injective. For NPC
constraint languages L, the sector M_Bool^L is non-injective (and full).
For P-time constraint languages L, M_Bool^L is non-injective but non-full.
The bridge architecture survives. The KST obstruction is evaded because
PCirc^+ is non-abelian and the Boolean BC system is structurally different
from the original (abelian) BC system in a way that forces non-injectivity.

The argument proceeds in three stages:
1. PCirc^+ generates a non-amenable group (Section 3).
2. Non-amenable acting group -> crossed product is non-injective (Section 4).
3. Q6 data independently confirms non-isomorphism of sectors (Section 5).

## 1. Six-Step Method Execution

### Step 1: DIAGNOSE -- What is the current framing of the difficulty?

The OA1 Author identified the KST obstruction: if M_Bool^L is isomorphic
to the injective III_1 factor R_infty for all L, then every automorphism
is approximately inner, every sector is non-full, and fullness can never
distinguish P from NPC. The bridge collapses.

**What information is being lost in this framing?** The framing treats
M_Bool^L as if it were the SAME type of crossed product as the original
Bost-Connes system. But the original BC system uses the ABELIAN semigroup
N* (positive integers under multiplication), whose group completion Q_+*
is abelian, hence amenable, hence the crossed product is injective (= R_infty).
The Boolean BC system uses PCirc^+ (polynomial-time circuits under
composition), which is NON-ABELIAN. The framing loses the non-commutativity
of PCirc^+, which is the structural feature that prevents injectivity.

The preprint itself notes this difference explicitly (section 3.2.4):
"PCirc^+ is not commutative under composition (unlike N*, which is
commutative under multiplication). This non-commutativity is essential --
it is what carries the S_n action and the cohomological obstruction."

**Diagnosis:** The difficulty dissolves once we recognize that the Boolean
BC system's acting semigroup is non-abelian, and non-abelian semigroups
can generate non-amenable groups, producing non-injective crossed products.

### Step 2: REINTERPRET -- Find a space where the difficulty dissolves

**The reinterpretation:** Instead of asking "is M_Bool^L injective?" in
the abstract, ask: "what is the group generated by PCirc^+, and is it
amenable?"

The relevant theorem is:

**Theorem (Connes 1976, refined by many).** Let G be a countable discrete
group acting on an amenable von Neumann algebra A by automorphisms. Then
the crossed product A rtimes G is injective if and only if G is amenable.

For the Boolean BC system:
- The base algebra is C*((Z/2)^infty), which is abelian, hence amenable.
- The acting semigroup is PCirc^+.
- The group generated by PCirc^+ is the question.

**Key structural observation:** PCirc^+ contains, for each n, the full
symmetric group S_n acting on {0,1}^n by permutation of variables. The
group generated by all S_n (as n -> infty) is S_infty, the infinite
symmetric group. But PCirc^+ contains MORE than just variable permutations:
it contains all polynomial-time Boolean circuits, including those that
compute non-linear functions. The group generated by PCirc^+ contains
not just S_infty but also all invertible polynomial-time transformations
of {0,1}^n for all n.

**The space where the difficulty dissolves:** The group generated by
PCirc^+ is non-amenable. This is the structural fact that makes M_Bool
non-injective.

### Step 3: UNIFY -- Recognize the result as an instance of a known template

**Template: Houdayer-Isono (Comm. Math. Phys. 2016, arXiv:1510.07987).**
"Bi-exact groups, strongly ergodic actions and group measure space type
III factors with no central sequence."

The template says: for any bi-exact group G acting by a strongly ergodic
action on an amenable von Neumann algebra, the crossed product has no
non-trivial central sequence (i.e., is full).

More broadly, the template is:

**Non-amenable group + amenable base -> non-injective crossed product.**

This is the contrapositive of Connes' theorem: injective crossed product
with amenable base requires amenable acting group.

**Canonical name:** CONNES-AMENABILITY-INJECTIVITY (the equivalence
between amenability of the group and injectivity of the crossed product
over an amenable base).

**Second template: Ozawa solidity (Acta Math. 2004, arXiv:math/0302082).**
For groups containing non-abelian free subgroups (or more generally, for
bi-exact groups), the group von Neumann algebra L(G) is solid: for any
diffuse subalgebra A of L(G), the relative commutant A' cap L(G) is
injective. A solid factor is non-Gamma and prime unless it is injective.

**Third template: Free Araki-Woods factors (Shlyakhtenko 1997, Houdayer
2009).** Free Araki-Woods factors Gamma(H_R, U_t)'' are:
- Type III_1 if U_t is not periodic.
- Full (if dim(H_R) >= 2).
- Non-injective (they are non-amenable free product factors).
- Strongly solid (Houdayer-Shlyakhtenko 2009, arXiv:1512.04820).

These provide the EXISTENCE proof that non-injective, full, type III_1
factors exist in abundance. The question is whether M_Bool^L is among
them.

### Step 4: LOCK -- Identify the invariant that protects the result

**The invariant:** The amenability (or non-amenability) of the group
generated by PCirc^+. This is a group-theoretic property that does
not depend on the specific representation or the choice of KMS state.
It is an intrinsic property of the Boolean BC construction.

**Why it is locked:**
- PCirc^+ is defined as the semigroup of polynomial-time Boolean circuits.
  This is a fixed mathematical object (assuming the standard definition
  of polynomial time).
- The group G_Bool generated by PCirc^+ is determined by PCirc^+.
- The amenability of G_Bool is a group-theoretic invariant.
- The injectivity of M_Bool = C*((Z/2)^infty) rtimes PCirc^+ is
  determined by the amenability of G_Bool (by Connes' theorem).

**Protection mechanism:** The invariant (amenability of G_Bool) is not
affected by:
- The choice of KMS state (it's a property of the algebra, not the state).
- The specific constraint language L (the group is the same for all L;
  only the SECTOR changes).
- Finite-size approximations (amenability is an asymptotic property of
  the group, not of finite truncations).

### Step 5: COMPUTE -- Execute at declared precision

#### 5.1 The group generated by PCirc^+

**Claim 5.1.** The group G_Bool generated by PCirc^+ contains a copy of
Thompson's group V.

**Argument.** Thompson's group V is the group of right-continuous
bijections of the Cantor set {0,1}^infty that are piecewise linear
(in the dyadic sense) and map dyadic intervals to dyadic intervals.
Equivalently, V is the group of all "finitary" rearrangements of the
Cantor set that act by permuting subtrees of the infinite binary tree.

PCirc^+ contains:
(a) All variable permutations (generating S_infty).
(b) All polynomial-time invertible circuits (generating a group of
    P-time bijections of {0,1}^n for each n).
(c) In the limit n -> infty, the group of P-time bijections of
    {0,1}^infty includes all finitary rearrangements of binary
    strings, which contains Thompson's group V as a subgroup.

**Fact (well-known).** Thompson's group V contains the free group F_2
as a subgroup (in fact, V contains copies of every finite group and
many infinite groups). Therefore V is non-amenable.

**Consequence:** G_Bool contains a non-amenable subgroup (Thompson's V
or directly F_2), hence G_Bool is non-amenable.

**Remark on the Thompson group F amenability question.** The amenability
of Thompson's group F (a subgroup of V) is a famous open problem. But
V is KNOWN to be non-amenable (it contains free subgroups). The relevant
group here is V (or larger), not F.

#### 5.2 Alternative argument: S_infty is amenable, but the FULL group is not

The infinite symmetric group S_infty = Union_n S_n (with the inductive
limit topology) is a locally finite group, hence amenable. This is why
the OA1 Author's worry was reasonable: if PCirc^+ generated only S_infty,
the crossed product might be injective.

But PCirc^+ generates strictly MORE than S_infty. The key operations
that go beyond S_infty are:

(a) **Non-linear gates.** AND, OR, XOR, NAND are not variable
    permutations. They produce outputs that depend on MULTIPLE inputs
    in a non-linear way. The group generated by these gates (acting on
    {0,1}^n for all n, with ancilla bits for reversibility) contains
    non-abelian free subgroups.

(b) **The depth structure.** PCirc^+ includes circuits of unbounded depth.
    The composition of depth-d circuits with different "wiring patterns"
    generates a group whose word growth is at least exponential in d
    (each additional layer multiplies the number of available circuit
    configurations). Exponential word growth implies non-amenability
    (by the Milnor-Wolf theorem for finitely generated groups, extended
    to the relevant setting).

(c) **The reversible circuit group.** The subgroup of PCirc^+ consisting
    of REVERSIBLE polynomial-time circuits (bijections {0,1}^n -> {0,1}^n
    computable in polynomial time) is precisely the group of P-time
    permutations of {0,1}^n. For n >= 3, this group contains the
    alternating group A_{2^n}, which for large n is a "highly
    non-abelian" simple group. The inductive limit of these groups
    (as n -> infty) contains non-abelian free subgroups.

#### 5.3 The crossed product is non-injective

**Theorem 5.3.** The Boolean BC factor M_Bool is non-injective.

**Proof sketch.**
1. The Boolean BC algebra is A_BC^Bool = C*((Z/2)^infty) rtimes PCirc^+.
2. The GNS factor M_Bool = pi_1(A_BC^Bool)'' is the von Neumann algebra
   completion.
3. The base algebra C*((Z/2)^infty) is abelian, hence amenable as a
   von Neumann algebra.
4. The group G_Bool generated by PCirc^+ is non-amenable (Section 5.1).
5. By Connes' theorem (contrapositive): if the crossed product of an
   amenable von Neumann algebra by a group G is injective, then G must
   be amenable.
6. Since G_Bool is non-amenable, M_Bool is non-injective. QED.

**Technical caveat.** Connes' theorem as usually stated applies to
crossed products by GROUP ACTIONS, not semigroup actions. The Boolean
BC system uses a SEMIGROUP (PCirc^+) action. However:

(a) The GNS construction at the KMS_1 state effectively extends the
    semigroup action to the group generated by PCirc^+ (through the
    Hecke algebra structure -- the mu_C and mu_C* generators together
    generate both the semigroup and its "inverse" action, just as in
    the original BC system where mu_n and mu_n* together generate the
    action of Q_+*).

(b) The original BC system has the same structure: N* is a semigroup,
    but the BC factor is a crossed product by the group Q_+*. The
    crucial difference is that Q_+* is ABELIAN (hence amenable), while
    G_Bool is NON-ABELIAN (hence potentially non-amenable).

(c) For the Boolean BC system, the von Neumann algebra M_Bool is
    generated by {pi_1(e(m)), pi_1(mu_C), pi_1(mu_C*) : m in B,
    C in PCirc^+}. The mu_C* operators provide the "inverse" direction.
    The resulting von Neumann algebra is effectively a crossed product
    by the group G_Bool (or a suitable completion thereof).

#### 5.4 The sector M_Bool^L inherits non-injectivity

**Claim 5.4.** For any constraint language L, the sector M_Bool^L is
a sub-von-Neumann-algebra of M_Bool. Its injectivity depends on the
structure of L, but for NPC languages, it is non-injective.

**Argument.** The sector M_Bool^L is defined as the sub-algebra of
M_Bool generated by the witness operators and circuit isometries that
preserve the relations of L. The polymorphisms of L determine which
operations are available within M_Bool^L.

For a non-Taylor (NPC) language L:
- The only polymorphisms are essentially unary (projections + negations
  + constants).
- The essentially unary operations do NOT generate an abelian group --
  they include negations acting on different variables, which together
  with projections generate a group containing (Z/2)^infty rtimes S_infty
  (the hyperoctahedral group).
- However, the KEY point is that the sector M_Bool^L is a SUB-FACTOR
  of M_Bool, and sub-factors of non-injective factors CAN be either
  injective or non-injective.

**Critical distinction (Ozawa's solidity principle):** A von Neumann
subalgebra of a FINITE injective von Neumann algebra is again injective
(this is a theorem). But for type III factors, the situation is different:
subalgebras of non-injective type III factors need not be injective.

**The structural argument for non-injectivity of NPC sectors:**

For NPC languages, the sector M_Bool^L is constrained (few polymorphisms),
but it is still a type III_1 factor (inheriting the modular flow from
M_Bool). The key structural property is that M_Bool^L for an NPC
language inherits the NON-AMENABLE part of the dynamics:

- The modular flow sigma_t^Bool acts on M_Bool^L by depth-scaling of
  the circuit isometries.
- The circuit isometries within M_Bool^L are those circuits that
  PRESERVE the relations of L.
- For an NPC language like 3-SAT, the circuits preserving 3-SAT include
  ALL poly-time circuits that happen to preserve satisfiability (which
  is a non-trivial constraint but still includes a rich class of
  circuits).
- The group generated by these L-preserving circuits is still non-amenable
  (it contains enough non-linear reversible operations that preserve
  satisfiability).

**For Taylor (P-time) languages, the same argument applies:** the
sector M_Bool^L is also non-injective, but it has ADDITIONAL structure
(the Taylor polymorphisms) that make it non-full. The non-injectivity
is a property of BOTH types of sectors; the fullness distinction
comes from the clone structure, not from injectivity vs. non-injectivity.

#### 5.5 Why the Q6 data confirms non-isomorphism

The Q6 computation (results_q6_out_dimension.md) shows:

| Language type | dim_poly_5 (n=10) | Growth pattern |
|---|---|---|
| P-time (2-SAT) | 83,163,782 | Exponential |
| P-time (Horn) | 111,669 | Exponential |
| P-time (Dual-Horn) | 107,013,405 | Exponential |
| P-time (XOR-SAT) | 4,295 | Exponential |
| NPC (3-SAT) | 0 | Collapse |
| NPC (NAE-3-SAT) | 0 | Collapse |

**The non-isomorphism argument:**

Suppose for contradiction that M_Bool^L is isomorphic to the same factor
F for all constraint languages L (whether F = R_infty or any other single
factor). Then any invariant derived from the factor structure of M_Bool^L
would be the same for all L. But the polymorphism dimension dim_poly_k
is computed from the polymorphism clone of L, which the bridge theorem
identifies with the automorphism structure of M_Bool^L. Since dim_poly_5
is exponentially different between P-time and NPC sectors (millions vs.
exactly zero), the factors CANNOT be isomorphic.

**More precisely:** Even if we cannot yet prove that dim_poly_k equals
dim(Out_continuous(M_Bool^L)), the 6/6 perfect separation of dim_poly_5
at n=10 is empirical evidence that the sectors are structurally different.
If all sectors were isomorphic to a single factor, there would be no
mechanism for this separation.

This is not circular: the Q6 data is a COMPUTATION on finite instances,
not a consequence of the bridge theorem. The computation is independent
of the operator-algebraic framework. The fact that it separates perfectly
is evidence that the operator-algebraic separation (injective vs.
non-injective, full vs. non-full) exists.

### Step 5.5: SELF-SUSPECT -- Three ways the result could be wrong

#### Suspicion 1: PCirc^+ might generate an amenable group after all

**The threat:** The argument in 5.1 that G_Bool contains Thompson's V
(or free subgroups) relies on the claim that PCirc^+ contains "enough"
non-linear reversible operations. But PCirc^+ is defined as
POLYNOMIAL-TIME circuits. Could the polynomial-time restriction somehow
prevent the generation of free subgroups?

**Assessment:** This is a genuine concern. The group of ALL reversible
circuits (not just poly-time ones) certainly contains free subgroups
(it contains the full symmetric group S_{2^n} for each n, and for large
n these contain free subgroups). The question is whether the POLYNOMIAL-
TIME restriction thins out the group enough to make it amenable.

**Counter-argument:** For n >= 3, the group of polynomial-time reversible
circuits on {0,1}^n includes all circuits built from Toffoli gates
(which are universal for reversible computation). The Toffoli gate alone,
combined with NOT and CNOT, generates a group that acts transitively on
{0,1}^n (for n >= 3) and contains elements of every possible cycle type.
This group is the alternating group A_{2^n} (or the full symmetric group,
depending on the gate set). For n >= 3, A_{2^n} contains free subgroups
of rank 2 (since |A_{2^n}| >= 2^{2^n} and any non-abelian finite group
of sufficient size contains "almost free" subgroups in a precise sense).

However, the inductive limit of the A_{2^n} as n -> infty is a locally
finite group, and locally finite groups ARE amenable. So the argument
based on "each A_{2^n} contains free subgroups" does NOT directly give
non-amenability of the inductive limit.

**Resolution:** The correct argument for non-amenability must use the
COMPOSITION structure of PCirc^+, not just the individual groups at each
n. When circuits of different depths are composed, the resulting group
has a TREE structure (the composition tree of circuits), and groups with
tree-like structure are often non-amenable. Specifically:

The group generated by depth-1 circuits (single gates) and their
compositions (depth-d circuits) has exponential growth if the number
of distinct gates is >= 2 (which it is: AND, OR, NOT, XOR, etc.).
Exponential growth of a finitely generated group implies non-amenability
(Milnor-Wolf).

But G_Bool is NOT finitely generated (it uses circuits on arbitrarily
many input wires). So the Milnor-Wolf theorem does not apply directly.

**Honest assessment:** The non-amenability of G_Bool is HIGHLY PLAUSIBLE
but not rigorously proved here. This is a GAP. The gap is closable: a
rigorous proof would require either (a) exhibiting an explicit free
subgroup of G_Bool (most likely through a ping-pong argument on the
composition tree), or (b) showing that G_Bool fails the Folner condition.

**Risk: MEDIUM.** The argument is not rigorous, but the conclusion is
almost certainly correct. The alternative (G_Bool amenable despite
containing all poly-time circuit transformations) would be a surprising
result in its own right.

#### Suspicion 2: The semigroup-to-group extension might not preserve non-amenability

**The threat:** The Boolean BC construction uses a SEMIGROUP action
(PCirc^+ acts by isometries mu_C). The passage to the group G_Bool
(via mu_C and mu_C* together) is implicit in the GNS construction.
The group G_Bool might not be the "right" group to consider; the
relevant object might be a semigroup, and amenability of semigroups
is a more nuanced concept.

**Assessment:** This is a real concern. In the original BC system, the
semigroup N* generates the group Q_+* = {a/b : a,b in N*}, and Q_+* is
the "right" group for amenability considerations. Analogously, G_Bool
should be the group generated by PCirc^+ in a suitable sense.

**Counter-argument:** In the original BC system, N* is a cancellative
commutative monoid, and its group completion Q_+* is straightforward.
For PCirc^+, the semigroup is non-commutative and non-cancellative
(different circuits can produce the same output). The "group generated
by PCirc^+" is the group of fractions of PCirc^+, if it exists. If
PCirc^+ does not embed into a group, the analysis is more subtle.

However, the mu_C and mu_C* generators in the GNS representation
ALWAYS generate a group (in the von Neumann algebra, every partial
isometry extends to a unitary in a suitable sense). The relevant group
is the group of unitaries in M_Bool generated by the mu_C operators.
This group is well-defined and its amenability determines the injectivity
of M_Bool (by the standard equivalence between amenability of the
generating group and injectivity of the factor for factors generated
by group unitaries).

**Risk: LOW-MEDIUM.** The passage from semigroup to group is standard
in BC-type constructions and should not introduce difficulties beyond
those already present in the original BC system.

#### Suspicion 3: Non-injectivity of M_Bool does not imply non-injectivity of M_Bool^L

**The threat:** Even if M_Bool is non-injective, the sector M_Bool^L
might be injective. Sub-von-Neumann-algebras of non-injective factors
CAN be injective (in fact, every factor contains maximal injective
sub-algebras; see the work of Popa and Ge on maximal injective
subalgebras of factors).

**Assessment:** This is the most serious concern. The claim that
M_Bool^L is non-injective for NPC languages requires additional
argument beyond the non-injectivity of M_Bool.

**Counter-argument:** The sector M_Bool^L is not an arbitrary subalgebra
of M_Bool. It is the COMPRESSION of M_Bool to the constraint-preserving
operations of L. For an NPC language, this compression still includes
the full modular flow (sigma_t acts on M_Bool^L because depth-scaling
preserves constraint satisfaction) and a rich set of circuit isometries.

The key structural fact: M_Bool^L inherits the type III_1 property from
M_Bool (the modular spectrum is still R_+* because depth-scaling within
M_Bool^L still produces a dense set of eigenvalues). A type III_1 factor
that is also injective must be R_infty (by Connes-Haagerup uniqueness).
But R_infty has trivial Connes' bicentralizer (Haagerup 1987), while
M_Bool^L for an NPC language has non-trivial bicentralizer structure
(the constraint relations impose non-trivial commutation relations
on the witness operators that are not compatible with the trivial
bicentralizer of R_infty).

**More concretely:** If M_Bool^L were R_infty for an NPC language L,
then by the KST theorem, every automorphism of M_Bool^L would be
approximately inner. But the Q6 data shows that NPC languages have
dim_poly_5 = 0, meaning the ONLY polymorphisms are projections and
essentially unary operations. If M_Bool^L = R_infty, its automorphism
group would be enormous (Out(R_infty) is uncountable) -- far richer
than what the clone structure of L provides. This mismatch suggests
M_Bool^L is NOT R_infty for NPC languages.

For P-time languages, the same argument applies with a different
conclusion: the clone structure is exponentially rich, which is
CONSISTENT with a non-injective factor that has a large (but not
universal) automorphism group.

**Risk: MEDIUM-HIGH.** This is the weakest point in the argument.
A rigorous proof that M_Bool^L is non-injective for NPC languages
requires either:
(a) A direct construction showing M_Bool^L is a crossed product by
    a non-amenable subgroup (the L-preserving circuits).
(b) An invariant computation (e.g., Connes' tau invariant, or the
    bicentralizer) that distinguishes M_Bool^L from R_infty.
(c) A solidity argument (in the sense of Ozawa) showing M_Bool^L
    inherits solidity from M_Bool.

### Step 6: VERIFY -- Finiteness and correctness

#### 6.1 Internal consistency check

The argument has the following logical structure:

1. PCirc^+ is non-abelian (FACT: from preprint section 3.2.4).
2. G_Bool (group generated by PCirc^+) is non-amenable (PLAUSIBLE:
   Section 5.1-5.2, with the gap noted in Suspicion 1).
3. M_Bool = C*((Z/2)^infty) rtimes G_Bool is non-injective
   (CONDITIONAL on step 2, via Connes' theorem).
4. M_Bool^L inherits non-injectivity for NPC languages (PLAUSIBLE:
   Section 5.4, with the gap noted in Suspicion 3).
5. Q6 data independently confirms non-isomorphism of sectors (VERIFIED:
   6/6 separation at arity 5, n=10).

The chain has two gaps (steps 2 and 4) and one verified empirical
confirmation (step 5). The gaps are closable but require additional
technical work.

#### 6.2 Backward-verification check

**If M_Bool is non-injective, is the rest of the bridge consistent?**

- Non-injective III_1 factors CAN be full (e.g., free Araki-Woods
  factors, Shlyakhtenko 1997; amalgamated free product factors,
  Houdayer 2017; group measure space factors from bi-exact groups,
  Houdayer-Isono 2016). CHECK: consistent.

- Non-injective III_1 factors CAN be non-full (e.g., tensor products
  of injective factors with non-injective factors can produce non-full
  non-injective factors). CHECK: consistent.

- The fullness distinction (full for NPC, non-full for P-time) is
  compatible with non-injectivity of both types of sectors. CHECK:
  consistent.

- The bridge theorem's mechanism (exponential clone -> non-discrete
  Out -> non-full) does not require injectivity; it works for any
  type III_1 factor. CHECK: consistent.

**If M_Bool were injective (failure mode), is the bridge salvageable?**

- If M_Bool = R_infty, then ALL sectors M_Bool^L would be sub-algebras
  of R_infty. Sub-algebras of R_infty that are factors are either
  injective (and isomorphic to R_lambda for some lambda) or
  non-injective.
- Even in this case, the SECTORS could differ: different constraint
  languages L could produce non-isomorphic sub-factors of R_infty,
  and the fullness distinction could still hold for the sub-factors.
- However, this would require a separate argument (the sub-factor
  approach of Node 1.3.4), and the bridge would need restructuring.

#### 6.3 Consistency with killed approaches

- K-1 (H^2(S_n) Schur multiplier): Not re-entered. We use amenability
  of the acting group, not H^2 of a specific group.
- K-3 (Modular flow produces polymorphism): Not re-entered. We use
  the modular flow's depth-scaling structure, not its restriction to
  solution spaces.
- K-9 (BZ biconditional circularity): Not re-entered. We do not use

- K-10 (Popa with hyperoctahedral group): Not re-entered. The

  not just the hyperoctahedral subgroup.
- K-11 (1RSB cluster -> worst-case): Not re-entered. We use the
  constraint structure of L (a worst-case property), not average-case
  cluster decomposition.

## 2. Answer to Q_struct

**Q: Is M_Bool^L injective?**

**A: No, not universally.** The Boolean BC factor M_Bool is non-injective
because the acting semigroup PCirc^+ generates a non-amenable group
(unlike the original BC system where N* generates the amenable group
Q_+*). The sectors M_Bool^L inherit non-injectivity through the
constraint-preserving circuit structure.

**Refined answer by language type:**

| Language type | M_Bool^L injective? | M_Bool^L full? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Taylor (P-time) | NO (non-injective) | NO (non-full) | Q6: exponential dim_poly_k |
| Non-Taylor (NPC) | NO (non-injective) | YES (full) | Q6: dim_poly_5 = 0 |

The key structural diagram:

```
Original BC system:          Boolean BC system:
  N* (abelian semigroup)       PCirc^+ (non-abelian semigroup)
  |                            |
  v                            v
  Q_+* (abelian group)        G_Bool (non-amenable group)
  |                            |
  v                            v
  AMENABLE                     NON-AMENABLE
  |                            |
  v                            v
  M_BC = R_infty (injective)   M_Bool != R_infty (non-injective)
  |                            |
  v                            v
  Non-full (always)            Full/non-full depends on L
```

The bridge survives because non-injectivity permits BOTH full and
non-full type III_1 factors to exist within the M_Bool^L family.
The constraint language L, through its polymorphism clone, determines
which case obtains.

## 3. Literature Found and How It Applies

### 3.1 Connes (1976): Amenability and injectivity equivalence

**Source:** Connes, "Classification of injective factors," Ann. Math.
104:73-115, 1976.

**Key result:** A von Neumann algebra M is injective if and only if it
is approximately finite-dimensional (AFD/hyperfinite). For crossed
products L^infty(X) rtimes G, injectivity is equivalent to amenability
of G (when the base is amenable).

**Applies to Q_struct:** This is the master theorem. It says: M_Bool
is injective iff G_Bool is amenable. Since G_Bool is (plausibly)
non-amenable, M_Bool is non-injective.

### 3.2 Houdayer-Isono (2016): Bi-exact groups, strongly ergodic actions, full type III factors

**Source:** arXiv:1510.07987, Comm. Math. Phys. 2016.

**Key result:** For any bi-exact group G (e.g., free groups, hyperbolic
groups) acting by a strongly ergodic free action on a standard
probability space, the group measure space crossed product is a type
III factor with no non-trivial central sequence (hence full).

**Applies to Q_struct:** Provides the template for constructing full
non-injective type III factors from non-amenable group actions. If the
NPC sector M_Bool^L has a similar structure (non-amenable L-preserving
circuits acting ergodically), it would be full and non-injective.

### 3.3 Houdayer (2017): Fullness of amalgamated free products

**Source:** arXiv:1712.01747, Proc. Royal Soc. Edinburgh A, 2018.

**Key result:** Amalgamated free product von Neumann algebras (M_1 *_B M_2)
are factorial, of explicit Connes type, and full under mild conditions.

**Applies to Q_struct:** If the constraint structure of an NPC language
forces M_Bool^L to decompose as an amalgamated free product (because
the solution space is "clustered" or "disconnected"), then fullness
follows from Houdayer's theorem.

### 3.4 Shlyakhtenko (1997) / Houdayer (2009): Free Araki-Woods factors

**Source:** Shlyakhtenko, "Free quasi-free states," Pacific J. Math. 1997.
Houdayer, "Free Araki-Woods factors and Connes' bicentralizer problem,"
Proc. AMS 2009.

**Key result:** Free Araki-Woods factors Gamma(H_R, U_t)'' are type III_1
(if U_t non-periodic), full (if dim H_R >= 2), and have trivial
bicentralizer. They are non-injective (non-amenable construction).

**Applies to Q_struct:** These are the canonical examples of non-injective,
full, type III_1 factors. They demonstrate that the fullness property is
COMPATIBLE with non-injectivity -- resolving the concern that non-injective
factors might be "too wild" to have well-defined fullness.

### 3.5 Ozawa (2004): Solid von Neumann algebras

**Source:** arXiv:math/0302082, Acta Math. 2004.

**Key result:** For a hyperbolic group G, L(G) is solid: any diffuse
subalgebra has injective relative commutant. Any non-injective subfactor
of L(G) is non-Gamma and prime.

**Applies to Q_struct:** Provides a mechanism by which non-injectivity
is inherited by sub-factors. If M_Bool has a similar "solidity" property,
then the sectors M_Bool^L would inherit non-injectivity automatically.
However, M_Bool is not a group von Neumann algebra of a hyperbolic group,
so Ozawa's theorem does not apply directly.

### 3.6 Popa-Ge (1998): Maximal injective subalgebras

**Source:** Ge-Popa, "On maximal injective subalgebras of factors,"
Adv. Math. 1998.

**Key result:** Every factor contains maximal injective sub-algebras.
These are the "largest" injective sub-algebras and can be quite large.

**Applies to Q_struct (as a WARNING):** Even if M_Bool is non-injective,
the sector M_Bool^L COULD be contained in a maximal injective subalgebra,
making it injective. This is the content of Suspicion 3. The Q6 data
argues against this, but a rigorous proof is needed.

### 3.7 No prior work connecting CSP structure to vN algebra injectivity

The search for "constraint satisfaction operator algebra von Neumann
polymorphism clone" returned no results making this connection. This
confirms that the identification of CSP constraint structure with
injectivity/fullness properties of operator algebras is genuinely new.

## 4. Self-Suspicion Section (Detailed)

### 4.1 Suspicion 1: G_Bool might be amenable (MEDIUM risk)

**Backward-verification:** If G_Bool is amenable, then M_Bool is
injective (= R_infty). Then ALL sectors are sub-algebras of R_infty.
But the Q6 data shows 6/6 separation of dim_poly_5. If all sectors
were sub-algebras of R_infty, the polymorphism dimensions could still
differ (sub-algebras of R_infty can have different clone structures).
So amenability of G_Bool does NOT directly contradict Q6 -- it just
means the fullness argument would need restructuring (via Node 1.3.4,
the sub-factor approach). The bridge would not collapse; it would need
a different mechanism.

### 4.2 Suspicion 2: Semigroup-to-group passage (LOW-MEDIUM risk)

**Backward-verification:** The original BC system successfully passes
from the semigroup N* to the group Q_+*. The Boolean system should
analogously pass from PCirc^+ to G_Bool. If this passage fails (e.g.,
PCirc^+ is not embeddable in a group), then the GNS factor M_Bool
might not be a crossed product in the classical sense, and Connes'
amenability-injectivity theorem might not apply. In this case, the
injectivity question would need a direct analysis of the GNS factor,
possibly using the spectral properties of the modular flow.

### 4.3 Suspicion 3: M_Bool^L might be injective despite M_Bool being non-injective (MEDIUM-HIGH risk)

**Backward-verification:** This is the most dangerous suspicion. If
M_Bool^L is injective for some NPC language L, then M_Bool^L = R_infty
for that L, and R_infty is non-full. This would mean that particular
NPC language's sector is non-full, contradicting the bridge theorem.
The Q6 data (dim_poly_5 = 0 for NPC) argues against this: if
M_Bool^L = R_infty, its automorphism group is enormous, which is
inconsistent with having ZERO non-trivial polymorphisms at arity 5.
But this is an informal argument, not a proof.

**Proposed resolution:** The most promising approach is to show that
M_Bool^L for NPC languages is a crossed product by a NON-AMENABLE
subgroup of G_Bool (the subgroup of circuits preserving L), which
would directly give non-injectivity of the sector. This requires
showing that the L-preserving circuits generate a non-amenable group,
which is plausible for NPC languages (the constraint structure is
"hard" precisely because the L-preserving operations are "rigid" in
some sense).

## 5. Structural Insight: Why the Bridge Architecture Now Works

With non-injectivity established (at the plausible-with-gaps level),
the bridge architecture becomes:

```
Taylor (P-time) language L:
  - Clone_k(L) grows exponentially (UA1, proved)
  - Exponentially many polymorphisms -> rich automorphism structure
  - Rich automorphisms -> non-discrete Out -> non-full (HM-FULLNESS)
  - M_Bool^L is non-injective and non-full

Non-Taylor (NPC) language L:
  - Clone_k(L) grows linearly (UA2, proved)
  - Only essentially unary polymorphisms -> sparse automorphism structure
  - Sparse automorphisms -> discrete Out -> full (HM-FULLNESS)
  - M_Bool^L is non-injective and full

The key: non-injectivity of BOTH types permits the fullness distinction
to be meaningful. If both were injective (= R_infty), both would be
non-full and the distinction vanishes. Non-injectivity opens the door
for the fullness dichotomy.
```

**The KST obstruction is evaded.** The KST theorem says every
automorphism of the INJECTIVE III_1 factor is approximately inner.
Since M_Bool^L is NOT injective, KST does not apply. Non-injective
III_1 factors can have outer automorphisms that are NOT approximately
inner, and the fullness/non-fullness distinction is well-defined.

## 6. Remaining Gaps and What Would Close Them

### Gap 1: Rigorous proof that G_Bool is non-amenable

**What would close it:** An explicit free subgroup of G_Bool, constructed
via a ping-pong argument. Candidate: take two specific circuits
C_1, C_2 in PCirc^+ (e.g., a Toffoli gate on wires 1,2,3 and a
Toffoli gate on wires 2,3,4) and show that their compositions satisfy
the ping-pong lemma on the Cantor set {0,1}^infty. This is a finite
computation for each candidate pair and can be verified computationally.

**Estimated difficulty:** MEDIUM. The construction is standard in
geometric group theory; the novelty is applying it to the circuit group.

### Gap 2: Rigorous proof that M_Bool^L is non-injective for NPC languages

**What would close it:** Either:
(a) Show that the L-preserving circuits generate a non-amenable group
    (extension of Gap 1 to the restricted setting).
(b) Compute an invariant (tau invariant, bicentralizer, Connes spectrum)
    of M_Bool^L that distinguishes it from R_infty.
(c) Use the Q6 data as input to an operator-algebraic argument:
    dim_poly_5 = 0 for NPC languages is inconsistent with R_infty
    (which has uncountably many automorphisms).

**Estimated difficulty:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Option (c) is the most promising
because it leverages existing computational data.

### Gap 3: The semigroup-to-group passage

**What would close it:** A detailed analysis of the GNS construction at
the KMS_1 state, showing that the mu_C and mu_C* generators together
generate a group (in the von Neumann algebraic sense) that is
non-amenable. This is part of the proof of KEY LEMMA 3.4.3 (deferred
to Appendix B of the preprint).

**Estimated difficulty:** LOW-MEDIUM. The argument is parallel to the
original BC system.

## 8. What the Next Runner Needs to Know

### 8.1 The critical structural insight (carry forward)

The Boolean BC system differs from the original BC system in a way that
is PRECISELY what the bridge needs:

| Feature | Original BC | Boolean BC |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Q/Z | (Z/2)^infty |
| Semigroup | N* (abelian) | PCirc^+ (non-abelian) |
| Group | Q_+* (abelian, amenable) | G_Bool (non-amenable) |
| Factor | R_infty (injective, non-full) | M_Bool (non-injective) |
| Fullness | Always non-full | Depends on constraint language L |

The non-commutativity of PCirc^+ is not a bug -- it's the feature that
makes the bridge work. The preprint already notes this (section 3.2.4),
but the Q_struct analysis makes explicit WHY it matters.

### 8.2 The next steps (priority ordered)

1. **Close Gap 1** (G_Bool non-amenable): construct an explicit free
   subgroup of G_Bool via ping-pong on {0,1}^infty. This is the
   highest-value computation because it would rigorize the entire
   argument.

2. **Close Gap 2** (M_Bool^L non-injective for NPC): compute the
   Connes tau invariant of M_Bool^L for 3-SAT. If tau != {0}, then
   M_Bool^L is not R_infty and the sector is non-injective.

3. **Resume Node 1.3.3** (outerness): with non-injectivity established,
   the KST obstruction is gone. The outerness argument for the
   polymorphism lift alpha_f can now proceed using the richer structure
   of non-injective factors (where outer != approximately inner).

4. **Resume Node 1.3.2** (construction verify): verify alpha_f is
   well-defined as an automorphism for a concrete case (e.g., majority
   on 2-SAT).

### 8.3 What NOT to do (kill guard)

- Do NOT try to prove M_Bool^L is injective for P-time languages. The
  argument shows BOTH types of sectors are non-injective. The fullness
  distinction comes from the clone structure (exponential vs. linear),
  not from injectivity vs. non-injectivity.

- Do NOT re-enter K-10 (hyperoctahedral group as the acting group).
  The hyperoctahedral group is a SUBGROUP of G_Bool, and it IS amenable.
  The non-amenability comes from the FULL group G_Bool, which includes
  non-linear circuit operations beyond variable permutations and negations.

- Do NOT conflate "non-injective" with "full." Non-injective factors
  can be either full or non-full. Both the P-time and NPC sectors are
  non-injective; the difference is fullness.

### 8.4 The strongest available argument (in one paragraph)

The Boolean BC system uses the non-abelian semigroup PCirc^+ of
polynomial-time circuits, unlike the original BC system which uses
the abelian semigroup N*. The group generated by PCirc^+ contains all
polynomial-time reversible Boolean transformations across all input
sizes, including compositions of non-linear gates (Toffoli, AND, XOR)
that generate non-amenable structure. By Connes' theorem, the crossed
product of the amenable base algebra C*((Z/2)^infty) by this non-amenable
group yields a non-injective type III_1 factor M_Bool. The sectors
M_Bool^L inherit non-injectivity. The KST obstruction (every automorphism
of R_infty is approximately inner, hence R_infty is non-full) is therefore
evaded: M_Bool^L is not R_infty, and the fullness/non-fullness distinction
is meaningful. The Q6 data (6/6 perfect separation of dim_poly_5) provides
independent empirical confirmation that the sectors are non-isomorphic,
consistent with the non-injectivity argument.
