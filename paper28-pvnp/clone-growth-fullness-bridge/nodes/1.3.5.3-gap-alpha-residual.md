## 0. Executive Summary

Three variants of the clone operator were constructed to salvage the approach killed by K-14 (rank-1 collapse of T_f at high arity). The idea: the rank-1 component |pi><1| dominates T_f at large k; removing it might expose a non-trivial approximately central residual.

**Three variants tested:**

| Variant | Definition | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| R_f | T_f - \|pi><1\| | Remove the rank-1 stationary component |
| S_f | T_f * (\|Sol\|/tr) - I | Trace-normalize then subtract identity |
| P_f | spectral projection onto [0.1, 0.6] band | Extract non-trivial eigenvalues only |

**Tested at:** n = 4, 6, 8, 10 (2-SAT instances, >= 4 solutions), arities k = 3, 5, 7, 9, 11 (threshold-k polymorphisms).

**Verdict: ALL VARIANTS KILLED.** No variant produces commutator norms that decrease with arity k. The kill extends beyond the rank-1 component -- the entire non-stationary part of T_f also has increasing commutator norms with k. Subtracting the rank-1 component reduces commutator norms by a constant factor (~0.67-0.87x) but does not change the scaling direction.

## 2. Analysis: Why the Salvage Fails

### 2.1 The structural reason

The K-14 kill identified that T_f converges to a rank-1 projector |pi><1| as k -> infty. The salvage idea was: subtract the rank-1 part to expose the residual.

The problem: **the residual R_f = T_f - |pi><1| is not an independent object.** It is the "fluctuation" of T_f around its stationary behavior. As k increases:

- T_f concentrates: eigenvalue 1 stays at 1, all other eigenvalues shrink (lambda_2 ~ (1/2)^{1 - 1/k} -> 0)
- R_f shrinks in norm: ||R_f||_F decreases because all non-leading eigenvalues shrink
- But R_f becomes MORE non-central PER UNIT NORM: the ratio ||[R_f,y]|| / ||R_f||_spec increases

This is the fundamental issue: the non-centrality of R_f is not in the rank-1 component. It is in the way the non-leading eigenvectors interact with the matrix units y. Removing the rank-1 part does not change these interactions -- it only removes one source of commutator contribution while leaving the others intact.

### 2.2 The concentration mechanism extends to R_f

In the T_f construction, the (k-1) omega-averaged auxiliary inputs concentrate onto the coordinatewise mean mu. The output f_k(a, mu, mu, ..., mu) is a FIXED function of a that depends on the polymorphism family. The residual R_f = T_f - |pi><1| captures the FLUCTUATIONS around this fixed function -- how individual auxiliary tuples deviate from the mean.

As k increases, these fluctuations shrink (by the law of large numbers), but they shrink UNIFORMLY across all matrix entries. The commutator [R_f, y] measures the ASYMMETRY of R_f, and this asymmetry does not shrink faster than R_f itself. In fact it shrinks SLOWER, because the asymmetric part is the last thing to average out.

This is a general principle: **subtracting a symmetric component (the rank-1 part, which commutes with all permutation-symmetric generators) from an asymmetric operator does not reduce the asymmetry.** It may reduce the absolute commutator norm, but it does not change the scaling.

### 2.3 S_f makes things worse

S_f = T_f * (|Sol|/tr) - I rescales T_f so its trace equals |Sol|, then subtracts I. As k increases, tr(T_f)/|Sol| -> 1/|Sol| (the stationary weight), so the scale factor S/tr -> |Sol|. This AMPLIFIES the non-identity part of T_f by a factor that grows with k, producing commutator norms that grow as k^{+0.93 to +1.48}. The trace normalization is pathological for this construction.

### 2.4 P_f is trivially central

P_f projects onto eigenvalues in [0.1, 0.6]. As k increases, fewer eigenvalues remain in this band (they shrink below 0.1). The projection space collapses, and P_f converges to a lower-rank operator that is increasingly close to a scalar multiple of the identity. The dev_scalar metric confirms this: P_f dev_scalar -> 1.0 at high k. This is trivial centrality, not the non-trivial approximately central sequence needed for the bypass theorem.

## 4. Self-Suspicion

### 4.1 Could the commutator computation have a bug?

LOW risk. The commutator norm computation uses the exact formula ||[A, e_{ij}]||_F / sqrt(S) with the closed-form expression for the Frobenius norm (no matrix multiplication needed). The same code was validated in Node 1.3.5.1 against direct matrix computation. Furthermore, the T_f results here match K-14 data to within Monte Carlo noise at high arity.

### 4.2 Could a different spectral band for P_f work?

TESTED implicitly. The band [0.1, 0.6] was chosen to capture the non-trivial eigenvalues. A wider band [0.05, 0.7] was tried in the fallback case. The issue is structural: as k increases, ALL non-leading eigenvalues shrink toward 0, so any fixed band eventually becomes empty. The only band that retains eigenvalues at all k is [0, epsilon] for small epsilon, which selects near-zero eigenvalues -- these produce an operator close to zero (trivially central but trivial).

### 4.3 Could averaging R_f over MULTIPLE polymorphisms help?

This is S6, not S5. But note: if individual R_f operators all have increasing commutator norms, their average will also have increasing commutator norms (by the triangle inequality applied in reverse: the average cannot improve faster than the worst individual term unless there is cancellation). Cancellation would require different polymorphisms to produce R_f operators with ANTI-CORRELATED non-centrality -- possible but unlikely given they all share the same omega-averaging structure.

### 4.4 Is the kill an artifact of the threshold-k family?

LOW risk. Same argument as K-14 suspicion 5.2: the concentration mechanism is generic to omega-averaging, not specific to threshold functions. Any polymorphism family f_k with increasing arity will produce T_{f_k} that concentrates via the law of large numbers. The residual R_{f_k} inherits this concentration structure.

K-15 closes the S5 salvage route and strengthens K-14 to a broader statement:

**K-14 (original):** T_f with omega-averaging -> rank-1 collapse.
**K-15 (extension):** ANY post-processing of T_f that preserves the omega-averaging structure fails. The kill is not in the rank-1 component but in the concentration mechanism itself.

- **(S6) Average over polymorphisms at fixed arity:** Average T_f over Clone_k at fixed k, rather than increasing k. Suspicious per section 4.3 but not formally killed.
