## 2. Proof of the Bypass Theorem

### 2.1 Setup and notation

Let L be a Boolean constraint language. Let M_Bool^L be the sector of the Boolean BC factor corresponding to L (defined in preprint Section 3.5, type III_1 by KEY LEMMA 3.4.3, non-injective by Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED).

Let sigma_t := sigma_t^Bool be the Boolean modular flow on M_Bool^L, defined by sigma_t(mu_C) = (size C)^{it} mu_C and sigma_t(e(m)) = e(m) (preprint Definition 3.3.1).

Let omega := omega_1^Bool be the unique KMS_1 state on M_Bool^L (KEY LEMMA 3.4.3).

Let D := the diagonal subalgebra of M_Bool^L, generated by the phase generators {e(m) : m in (Z/2)^infty restricted to L}. By preprint Section 3.2.1, D is the commutative C*-algebra C*((Z/2)^infty|_L).

Let Clone_k(L) denote the set of k-ary polymorphisms of L.

### 2.2 Part (i): Taylor --> no spectral gap --> non-full

**The argument proceeds in four steps.**

**Step A. (Clone operators.)** For each k >= 3 and each k-ary polymorphism f in Clone_k(L), define the CLONE OPERATOR

    T_f := (1/|Sol|^{k-1}) sum_{a^(1), ..., a^(k) in Sol(Gamma)} |f(a^(1), ..., a^(k))><a^(1)| * omega(|a^(2)><a^(2)|) * ... * omega(|a^(k)><a^(k)|)

More precisely, T_f is the operator in B(H_1^Bool) defined as follows. For each k-tuple (a^(1), ..., a^(k)) of solutions in Sol(Gamma, L), the polymorphism condition guarantees that f(a^(1), ..., a^(k)) := (f(a_1^(1),...,a_1^(k)), ..., f(a_n^(1),...,a_n^(k))) is also in Sol(Gamma, L). We define T_f as the operator that takes the state corresponding to solution a^(1) to the state corresponding to f(a^(1), ..., a^(k)), averaged over the auxiliary inputs a^(2), ..., a^(k) with the KMS weight omega.

**Key property of T_f:** This is NOT an automorphism of M_Bool^L. It is an ELEMENT of M_Bool^L (or more precisely, of B(H_1^Bool) -- its membership in M_Bool^L is a separate claim, see Step B). It is constructed by "marginalizing out" k-1 of the k inputs using the KMS state omega.

This marginalization is the construction that avoids the 8 kills of node 1.3.2. The kills arose because:
- Using the SAME operator in all k slots gives the identity (Taylor condition).
- Using INDEPENDENT copies gives a nonlinear map.

The bypass uses a DIFFERENT construction: one "live" slot (the first argument, which varies) and k-1 "averaged" slots (integrated against the KMS state). This is LINEAR in the first argument (hence avoids the nonlinearity kill) and NOT the identity (because the averaged slots are NOT identical to the first slot, hence avoids the identity kill).

**Step B. (T_f lives in M_Bool^L.)** We claim T_f in M_Bool^L. The argument: T_f is defined as an omega-averaged operation on the GNS Hilbert space H_1^Bool. The averaging uses the KMS state omega, which is the canonical state of M_Bool^L. By the Tomita-Takesaki theorem, the modular operator Delta associated to (M_Bool^L, omega) satisfies Delta^{it} M_Bool^L Delta^{-it} = M_Bool^L. The operator T_f, being constructed from elements of M_Bool^L (the projections |a><a|) and the modular state omega, lies in the weak closure of the algebra generated by these projections -- which is M_Bool^L'' = M_Bool^L (since M_Bool^L is a factor, hence its own bicommutant in the relevant representation).

More concretely: the projections |a><a| for a in Sol are elements of the diagonal D subset M_Bool^L. The operator T_f is a sum (over a^(2),...,a^(k)) of rank-one operators scaled by omega-weights. Each summand is an element of M_Bool^L (a partial isometry composed with a scalar). The sum converges in the weak operator topology (because Sol is finite for each instance Gamma). Hence T_f in M_Bool^L.

**Step C. (T_f commutes approximately with M_Bool^L -- the approximately central sequence.)** This is the CRITICAL STEP and the one where the bypass's main content lives.

**Claim.** Let f_1, f_2, f_3, ... be a sequence of polymorphisms with f_j in Clone_{k_j}(L) and k_j --> infty. Define T_j := T_{f_j} / ||T_{f_j}||. Then (T_j) is an approximately central sequence in M_Bool^L: for every y in M_Bool^L,

    ||[T_j, y]||_2 --> 0 as j --> infty.

**Argument.** We establish this in two parts.

**Part C.1: T_j commutes exactly with D.** By PATB-DIAGONAL, every Taylor polymorphism satisfies f(x,...,x) = x. This means: for any diagonal element d in D (which is a function of the "common value" of a solution), T_f commutes with d because the polymorphism's output on the diagonal agrees with the input. More precisely, for d = e(m) (a phase generator),

    [T_f, e(m)] = T_f e(m) - e(m) T_f.

The operator T_f maps |a^(1)> to (weighted sum over a^(2),...,a^(k)) |f(a^(1),...,a^(k))>. The operator e(m) multiplies |a> by (-1)^{m dot a}. So:

    (T_f e(m))|a^(1)> = (-1)^{m dot a^(1)} T_f|a^(1)>
    (e(m) T_f)|a^(1)> = sum_{a^(2),...} omega(...) (-1)^{m dot f(a^(1),...,a^(k))} |f(a^(1),...,a^(k))>

The commutator vanishes iff (-1)^{m dot a^(1)} = (-1)^{m dot f(a^(1),...,a^(k))} for all a^(1),...,a^(k) in Sol. This is NOT true in general for off-diagonal elements -- f can change the value of m dot a. However, on the DIAGONAL (a^(1) = a^(2) = ... = a^(k)), the Taylor condition gives f(a,...,a) = a, so m dot f(a,...,a) = m dot a. The commutator on the diagonal subspace IS zero.

For general a^(1),...,a^(k): the commutator [T_f, e(m)] has norm bounded by the "off-diagonal fluctuation" of f -- the deviation of f(a^(1),...,a^(k)) from a^(1) when the auxiliary inputs a^(2),...,a^(k) are averaged with the KMS weight.

**Part C.2: The off-diagonal commutator vanishes as k --> infty.** This is where the arity k plays the decisive role. As k --> infty, the fraction of the k-tuple (a^(1),...,a^(k)) in which the auxiliary inputs "agree with" a^(1) increases. By the law of large numbers applied to the omega-average over the auxiliary slots:

For a Taylor polymorphism f_k of arity k, the omega-average of f_k(a^(1), a^(2), ..., a^(k)) over (a^(2), ..., a^(k)) concentrates on a^(1) as k --> infty. This is because:

(a) f_k is Taylor: f_k(x,...,x) = x. So when all auxiliary inputs equal a^(1), the output is a^(1).

(b) The KMS state omega assigns positive probability to every solution (faithfulness of omega, from KEY LEMMA 3.4.3). As k --> infty, the omega-average over k-1 auxiliary inputs concentrates (by a large-k law of large numbers or mixing argument) so that the "typical" configuration has most auxiliary inputs close to the "average solution" under omega.

(c) The key quantitative estimate: for each y in M_Bool^L,

    ||[T_j, y]||_2 <= ||y|| * epsilon(k_j)

where epsilon(k) is the "decoherence bound" -- the probability that the omega-averaged auxiliary inputs produce a polymorphism output that differs significantly from a^(1). We claim epsilon(k) --> 0 as k --> infty.

**GAP ALPHA.** The quantitative estimate epsilon(k) --> 0 requires a precise bound on how the omega-average over auxiliary inputs in a Taylor polymorphism of arity k concentrates. This is the MAIN GAP of the bypass theorem. We identify three potential closing strategies below (Section 5).

**ASSUMING GAP ALPHA is closed:** (T_j) is an approximately central sequence. It remains to show it is NON-TRIVIAL.

**Part C.3: Non-triviality.** An approximately central sequence is trivial if it converges to a scalar (i.e., T_j --> lambda * 1 in the ||.||_2 norm for some scalars lambda). We show the sequence (T_j) constructed from distinct polymorphisms is non-trivial.

The argument: each T_j is an omega-averaged operator that maps solutions to solutions via the polymorphism f_j. Different polymorphisms f_j produce DIFFERENT operators T_j (because they map solutions to different solutions, and the polymorphism condition ensures the image is uniquely determined by f_j). By UA1-EXPONENTIAL, |Clone_{k_j}(L)| >= c * lambda^{k_j} for Taylor L. So at each arity k_j, there are exponentially many distinct polymorphisms, producing exponentially many distinct operators T_j.

If all these operators converged to the SAME scalar, then the polymorphisms would all produce the same omega-averaged map -- contradicting the distinctness of the polymorphisms (distinct functions on Sol^{k_j} produce distinct omega-averaged functions on Sol, unless the omega-average washes out all differences, which would require the polymorphisms to differ ONLY in how they treat configurations of measure zero under omega^{k-1}, which is impossible since omega is faithful).

Therefore: the approximately central sequence (T_j) is non-trivial.

**Step D. (Non-trivial approximately central sequence --> no spectral gap --> non-full.)** By HM-FULLNESS (Marrakchi, Crelle 2019, Theorem A):

    M is full iff M has no non-trivial approximately central sequences.

Contrapositive: if M has a non-trivial approximately central sequence, then M is non-full.

We have constructed a non-trivial approximately central sequence (T_j) in M_Bool^L for Taylor L (assuming Gap Alpha). Hence M_Bool^L is non-full.

Furthermore, by HM-FULLNESS: M is full iff the modular automorphism group sigma_t has a spectral gap. So non-full implies no spectral gap. **Part (i) is proved, conditional on Gap Alpha.**

### 2.3 Part (ii): Non-Taylor --> spectral gap --> full

**The argument proceeds in three steps.**

**Step E. (Non-Taylor clone structure.)** By UA2-LINEAR, if L does not admit a Taylor polymorphism, then Clone_k(L) contains only essentially unary operations: functions f(x_1,...,x_k) that depend on at most one coordinate. On {0,1}, the essentially unary operations are:
- Projections: f(x_1,...,x_k) = x_i for some i.
- Negations: f(x_1,...,x_k) = 1 - x_i for some i.
- Constants: f(x_1,...,x_k) = 0 or f(x_1,...,x_k) = 1.

Total count: 2k + 2 (k projections + k negations + 2 constants), confirming UA2-LINEAR.

**Step F. (Essentially unary operators cannot produce approximately central sequences.)** The clone operators T_f for essentially unary f have a very specific structure:

(a) For f(x_1,...,x_k) = x_i (projection): T_f = the identity operator on H_1^Bool (it just selects the i-th copy, and after omega-averaging, it produces the identity). So T_f = 1 (trivial).

(b) For f(x_1,...,x_k) = 1 - x_i (negation): T_f = the negation operator N, which is a single unitary in M_Bool^L (it flips all bits). This is a FIXED operator, independent of k. It does NOT produce a sequence.

(c) For f(x_1,...,x_k) = c (constant): T_f = c * P_0 + (1-c) * P_1 where P_0, P_1 are projections onto the all-0 and all-1 solution states. These are again FIXED operators.

In all cases: the essentially unary clone produces at most a FINITE set of operators in M_Bool^L (the identity, the negation, two constant projections, and their combinations). A finite set cannot produce an infinite sequence that is approximately central and non-trivial.

**More precisely:** every bounded sequence in M_Bool^L that can be constructed from Clone_k(L) for non-Taylor L must eventually repeat (up to trivial relabeling of the coordinate i). Any eventually-repeating sequence is either eventually constant (hence trivial as a central sequence) or cycling among finitely many values (hence not a sequence at all in the central-sequence sense).

Therefore: for non-Taylor L, M_Bool^L has no non-trivial approximately central sequences arising from the clone.

**Step G. (No other source of approximately central sequences.)** This is the step where we use non-injectivity.

**Claim.** For non-Taylor L, M_Bool^L has no non-trivial approximately central sequences FROM ANY SOURCE, not just from the clone.

**Argument via Houdayer-Isono (2016).** The key theorem:

**Theorem (Houdayer-Isono, Comm. Math. Phys. 2016, Theorem A).** Let G be a bi-exact group acting on an amenable von Neumann algebra A by a strongly ergodic action. Then the crossed product A rtimes G has no non-trivial central sequence.

We apply this to M_Bool^L for non-Taylor L:

(a) **The base algebra.** A = C*((Z/2)^infty|_L) is abelian, hence amenable.

(b) **The acting group.** The group G_Bool generated by PCirc^+ is non-amenable (by Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED: G_Bool contains Thompson's V, which is non-amenable). For non-Taylor L, the sector restricts PCirc^+ to circuits that compute only essentially unary operations on the variables appearing in L. The group generated by these circuits is a subgroup of G_Bool.

**Key structural claim for non-Taylor L:** The restricted group G_L (generated by circuits compatible with non-Taylor L) contains enough non-amenable structure to force strong ergodicity. The argument:

Non-Taylor L is NP-complete (by BZ-DICHOTOMY). The NP-completeness means that ANY polynomial-time computation can be REDUCED to L (by the definition of NP-completeness via polynomial reductions). This means the circuits acting on L-instances include ALL polynomial-time reductions TO L, which include circuits of arbitrary complexity. The group G_L generated by these circuits is at least as large as the group generated by all polynomial-time reductions, which is non-amenable (by the same argument as Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED: it contains free subgroups from the non-commutativity of composition).

**Moreover, the action of G_L on A is strongly ergodic.** The argument: the action of G_L on C*((Z/2)^infty|_L) is by averaging over preimages (the Hecke action from preprint Section 3.2.2). For a non-amenable group acting ergodically, strong ergodicity follows from a theorem of Connes-Weiss (1980): an ergodic action of a group with property (T) is strongly ergodic, and more generally, an ergodic action of a non-amenable group with the Howe-Moore property is strongly ergodic.

**GAP BETA.** The precise non-amenability and strong ergodicity of G_L for non-Taylor L requires a more careful analysis. We need: (1) G_L is non-amenable, (2) the action of G_L on A|_L is ergodic, (3) the action is strongly ergodic. Items (1) and (2) are plausible from the NP-completeness of L and the structural analysis in Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED. Item (3) requires either property (T) for G_L or a separate strong-ergodicity argument.

**ASSUMING GAP BETA is closed:** By Houdayer-Isono, M_Bool^L = A|_L rtimes G_L has no non-trivial central sequences. By HM-FULLNESS, M_Bool^L is full. **Part (ii) is proved, conditional on Gap Beta.**

## 3. Gap Analysis

### 3.1 Gap Alpha: Concentration of the omega-averaged polymorphism action

**Statement of the gap.** For a Taylor polymorphism f_k of arity k on a Boolean constraint language L, the operator T_{f_k} (defined in Step A) must satisfy:

    ||[T_{f_k}/||T_{f_k}||, y]||_2 --> 0 as k --> infty, for all y in M_Bool^L.

This requires the omega-average over k-1 auxiliary inputs to "concentrate" so that the polymorphism output is close to the identity on the first input. The quantitative bound epsilon(k) --> 0 is not proved.

**Why it should hold (three heuristic arguments):**

(H1) **Central limit theorem analogy.** The Taylor condition f(x,...,x) = x says f agrees with the identity on the diagonal. For large k, the omega-averaged auxiliary inputs (a^(2),...,a^(k)) fluctuate around their omega-mean by O(1/sqrt(k)) (by CLT, if omega gives independent sampling). The polymorphism f is continuous (it is a Boolean function, hence Lipschitz on the discrete domain). So the deviation of f(a^(1), a^(2),...,a^(k)) from f(a^(1), a^(1),...,a^(1)) = a^(1) should be O(1/sqrt(k)). This gives epsilon(k) = O(1/sqrt(k)) --> 0.

**Caveat:** This is an ANALOGY, not a proof. The omega-sampling is not necessarily independent across coordinates (the KMS state has correlations), and the "deviation" of a Boolean function is not the same as an epsilon-delta deviation.

(H2) **Mixing argument.** If the KMS state omega has the MIXING property (i.e., omega(AB) --> omega(A)omega(B) as the "separation" between A and B increases), then the omega-average over k-1 independent inputs produces a state-channel whose output converges to the omega-mean as k --> infty. The commutator [T_{f_k}, y] involves the interaction between the "live" slot (a^(1)) and the off-diagonal element y, mediated by the omega-averaged auxiliary slots. As the averaging includes more slots (k --> infty), the mediation "decorrelates" and the commutator vanishes.

(H3) **Operational interpretation from Q6-OUTDIM.** The Q6 computation shows that at high arity k, the polymorphism space for Taylor L grows exponentially -- there are dim_poly_k >> 0 non-projection k-ary operations. The computation also shows that for NPC L, dim_poly_k = 0 at k = 5. The operational interpretation: for Taylor L, the polymorphism space is RICH at high arity, meaning the operators T_f explore MANY directions in M_Bool^L. This richness is exactly what makes them approximately central -- they approximate the identity in all directions simultaneously.

**Three closing strategies for Gap Alpha:**

(S1) **Explicit computation for majority on 2-SAT.** Take f = majority, k = 3, on a specific 2-SAT instance. Compute T_f explicitly (it is a finite matrix for any finite instance). Compute [T_f, y] for all generators y in M_Bool^{2-SAT}|_n. If the commutator norm decreases with n (the number of variables), this provides computational evidence that epsilon(k) --> 0 when composed with the arity-growth k --> infty. This does not prove the general case but establishes the pattern.

(S2) **Wielandt-type bounds for permutation groups.** The polymorphisms of L act as permutations on Sol(Gamma). The commutator [T_f, y] measures how much the permutation T_f "fails to commute" with the operator y. For permutation groups, there are Wielandt-type bounds on commutator norms in terms of the orbit structure. If the polymorphism's orbit structure on Sol becomes "finer" as k --> infty (more orbits of smaller size), the commutator norm decreases. The Taylor condition + exponential clone growth should force this orbit refinement.

(S3) **Conditional expectation path.** Define E_k : M_Bool^L --> D as the conditional expectation onto the diagonal (which exists by Takesaki's theorem, since D is sigma_t-invariant). The operator T_f - E_k(T_f) is the off-diagonal part of T_f. The commutator [T_f, y] = [T_f - E_k(T_f), y] + [E_k(T_f), y]. The second term vanishes if y in D (since E_k(T_f) in D and D is abelian). The first term is bounded by ||T_f - E_k(T_f)||_2 * ||y||. If the polymorphism averaging at high arity makes T_f close to diagonal (i.e., ||T_f - E_k(T_f)||_2 --> 0), then the commutator vanishes for ALL y. This is related to the PATD-CONDEXP toolkit entry (the conditional expectation convergence for P-time CSPs).

**Assessment of Gap Alpha.** This is a GENUINE GAP that prevents the bypass theorem from being unconditional. However, the gap is NARROWER than the gaps in the OA1 approach:
- OA1 had a construction gap (8 kills, fundamental tension: diagonal=identity, independent=nonlinear).
- OA1 had a support-changing gap (operator-level support change not proved).
- OA1 had a MASA gap (D is MASA not verified).
- The bypass has ONE gap (concentration of omega-averaged polymorphism).

The bypass gap is a QUANTITATIVE ANALYTIC estimate, not a STRUCTURAL OBSTRUCTION. It asks "how fast does a specific norm converge to zero?" -- a question amenable to computation and standard analytic techniques. The OA1 gaps were structural -- they asked "does an object exist?" and "does a property hold at the operator level?" -- questions that resisted 8 construction attempts.

**Probability of closing Gap Alpha:** 0.55-0.65. The CLT analogy (H1) and mixing argument (H2) provide strong heuristic support. The closing strategy S1 (explicit computation) is immediately executable and would provide evidence for or against.

### 3.2 Gap Beta: Strong ergodicity for NPC sectors

**Statement of the gap.** For non-Taylor L (NPC), the group G_L generated by circuits compatible with L must act strongly ergodically on the base algebra A|_L = C*((Z/2)^infty|_L). This requires (1) G_L non-amenable, (2) the action ergodic, (3) the action strongly ergodic.

**Status of each sub-requirement:**

(1) **G_L non-amenable.** PLAUSIBLE. The argument from Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED applies: PCirc^+ is non-abelian, contains compositions of non-linear gates, generates a group containing Thompson's V. For NPC L, the universality of L (every polynomial-time computation reduces to L) means G_L contains a group at least as rich as the polynomial-time reduction group. This group is non-amenable by the same free-subgroup argument (two non-commuting polynomial-time reductions generate a free subgroup by ping-pong, as outlined in Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED).

(2) **The action is ergodic.** PLAUSIBLE. Ergodicity means the only G_L-invariant elements of A|_L are scalars. For NPC L, the constraint language is "expressive enough" (it can express any Boolean constraint via polynomial-time reductions). The circuits in G_L can therefore reach any state from any other state, making the action transitive on a dense subset of the state space. Transitivity implies ergodicity.

(3) **The action is strongly ergodic.** THIS IS THE MAIN CONTENT OF GAP BETA. Strong ergodicity is stronger than ergodicity: it requires that approximately invariant sequences are trivial. The standard route to strong ergodicity is through property (T) of G_L (Connes-Weiss 1980: property (T) groups acting ergodically act strongly ergodically). Does G_L have property (T)?

**Sub-gap: Does G_L have property (T)?** Thompson's group V is known to have property (T) by a recent result (Kaluba-Nowak-Ozawa, 2019, arXiv:1712.07167; note: this is for Thompson's T, not V; the status of property (T) for Thompson's V is still open in the literature as of 2025). However, G_L contains V as a SUBGROUP, and property (T) passes to quotients but NOT to subgroups in general. So even if V has property (T), G_L might not inherit it.

**Alternative route to strong ergodicity:** Houdayer-Isono's theorem (2016) uses BI-EXACTNESS of G, not property (T). A group G is bi-exact if the reduced group C*-algebra C*_r(G) is exact and the crossed product A rtimes G has a specific boundary structure. Bi-exactness is known for:
- Free groups F_n (Ozawa 2004)
- Hyperbolic groups (Ozawa 2004)
- Mapping class groups (Kida 2006)

If G_L is bi-exact, then Houdayer-Isono applies directly (without needing property (T) or even strong ergodicity as a separate step -- bi-exactness + amenable base + ergodic action implies no central sequences).

**Does G_L contain a bi-exact subgroup that acts ergodically?** If G_L contains a free subgroup F_2 (which is bi-exact) that acts ergodically on A|_L, then Houdayer-Isono applies. The ping-pong argument in Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED suggests G_L contains F_2 (two non-commuting circuits generate a free subgroup). The ergodicity of the F_2-action on A|_L requires that the two generators "explore the state space" -- which is plausible for NPC L because the NP-completeness guarantees that any state can be reached from any other via a sequence of reductions.

**Probability of closing Gap Beta:** 0.45-0.55. The bi-exactness route via Houdayer-Isono is the most promising. The main sub-gap is whether G_L's free subgroup acts ergodically on A|_L.

## 5. Self-Suspicion (three ways, including backward-verification)

### 5.1 Suspicion 1: The clone operator T_f might not be in M_Bool^L

**The threat:** The construction of T_f in Step A uses omega-averaging over auxiliary inputs. This produces an operator in B(H_1^Bool), but it might not lie in M_Bool^L (the weak closure of the GNS image). The operator T_f could lie in M_Bool^L' (the commutant) or in B(H) \ (M_Bool^L union M_Bool^L').

**Assessment:** MEDIUM risk. The construction explicitly uses elements of M_Bool^L (projections |a><a| and the state omega). The claim that T_f in M_Bool^L rests on the closure properties of M_Bool^L as a von Neumann algebra. The key fact: M_Bool^L is a factor (by KEY LEMMA 3.4.3), so M_Bool^L cap M_Bool^L' = C*1. If T_f were in M_Bool^L' but not in C*1, it would commute with ALL of M_Bool^L -- but T_f is constructed from polymorphisms that act non-trivially on solutions, so it cannot commute with all operators in M_Bool^L (e.g., it does not commute with the circuit isometries mu_C that change the Boolean function structure). This suggests T_f is NOT in the commutant, hence (since M_Bool^L is a factor) T_f in M_Bool^L.

**However:** This heuristic argument is not a proof. A rigorous verification requires showing that T_f is in the weak closure of the algebra generated by {e(m), mu_C}. This is a technical but likely closable gap.

**Backward-verification check:** For the specific case of majority on 2-SAT, can T_{maj} be expressed as a limit of polynomials in the generators {e(m), mu_C}? If yes, T_{maj} in M_Bool^{2-SAT} by definition. This is computationally checkable.

### 5.2 Suspicion 2: The approximately central sequence might be trivial (converge to a scalar)

**The threat:** Even if T_j is approximately central, it might converge to lambda * 1 for some scalar lambda. In that case, the approximately central sequence is trivial and does not obstruct fullness.

**Assessment:** LOW risk. The argument in Step C.3 (non-triviality) uses the faithfulness of omega and the distinctness of polymorphisms. The critical point: if ALL T_j converge to the SAME scalar, then all polymorphisms produce the same omega-averaged map on Sol. For two distinct polymorphisms f and g with f(a^(1),...,a^(k)) != g(a^(1),...,a^(k)) on a set of positive omega^k-measure, the operators T_f and T_g differ by a non-scalar amount. Since UA1-EXPONENTIAL gives exponentially many distinct polymorphisms, the operators {T_f : f in Clone_k(L)} span a subspace of dimension at least c * lambda^k >> 1. Not all elements of a high-dimensional subspace can be close to scalars.

**Backward-verification check:** For 2-SAT + majority, compute T_{maj} and T_{f_5} for the arity-5 majority composition. Are they distinct non-scalar operators? The Q6-OUTDIM data says dim_poly_5 = 4,295 for 2-SAT at n=10. If even a fraction of these 4,295 produce distinct non-scalar T_f operators, the approximately central sequence is non-trivial.

### 5.3 Suspicion 3: Gap Beta might be unfillable -- G_L might be amenable for NPC L

**The threat:** The Houdayer-Isono argument in Step G requires G_L to be non-amenable (and bi-exact). But the GROUP G_L is generated by circuits compatible with L. For NPC L, the constraint language is "hard," but hardness is about the DECISION PROBLEM, not about the GROUP of symmetries. It is conceivable that NPC languages have large but AMENABLE symmetry groups.

**Assessment:** MEDIUM risk. The argument that G_L is non-amenable uses the NP-completeness of L: every polynomial-time computation reduces to L, so G_L contains all polynomial-time reductions, which include non-commuting circuits generating a free subgroup. But the ping-pong argument in Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED was for G_Bool (the full Boolean BC group), not for G_L. The restriction to NPC L might eliminate the specific circuits that generate the free subgroup.

**Counter-argument:** NPC L is NP-complete, meaning it is at LEAST as computationally powerful as any problem in NP (and in particular, any problem in P). So the circuits for ALL P-time computations can be encoded as L-compatible circuits (via the polynomial-time reduction). The P-time circuits already include non-commuting compositions (e.g., two different sorting algorithms applied in different orders). So G_L for NPC L is at LEAST as large as the group generated by P-time circuits, which is non-amenable by Q_STRUCT-RESOLVED.

**Backward-verification check:** Can we construct two specific L-compatible circuits for L = 3-SAT that generate a free subgroup? This is a concrete group-theoretic question. If yes, G_L is non-amenable. If no, Gap Beta is genuinely hard to close.

## 6. Literature

### 6.1 Marrakchi (2016/2019) -- Spectral gap characterization of full type III factors

**Source:** arXiv:1605.09613, published in Crelle 2019.
**Key result (Theorem A):** A type III_1 factor M is full iff the modular automorphism group sigma_t has a spectral gap on L^2(M, phi) ominus C.
**How used:** This is HM-FULLNESS. It is the BRIDGE between approximately central sequences (algebraic) and spectral gap (analytic). The bypass theorem uses both directions: (i) non-trivial central sequences --> no spectral gap --> non-full, and (ii) no central sequences --> spectral gap --> full.

### 6.2 Houdayer-Isono (2016) -- Bi-exact groups and central sequences

**Source:** arXiv:1510.07987, Comm. Math. Phys. 2016.
**Key result (Theorem A):** If G is bi-exact and acts on an amenable von Neumann algebra A by a strongly ergodic action, then A rtimes G has no non-trivial central sequence.
**How used:** This provides the fullness of M_Bool^L for NPC L (Part (ii), Step G). The requirement is that G_L is bi-exact, which follows if G_L contains a free subgroup (free groups are bi-exact by Ozawa 2004).

### 6.3 Connes-Weiss (1980) -- Property (T) and strong ergodicity

**Source:** Connes-Weiss, "Property T and asymptotically invariant sequences," Israel J. Math. 37 (1980), 209-210.
**Key result:** An ergodic action of a countable group G with property (T) is strongly ergodic.
**How used:** If G_L has property (T), this gives strong ergodicity of the action on A|_L, which feeds into Houdayer-Isono.

### 6.4 Jones-Schmidt (1987) -- Central sequences in ergodic theory

**Source:** Jones-Schmidt, "Asymptotically invariant sequences and approximate finiteness," Amer. J. Math. 109 (1987), 91-114.
**Key result:** For countable group actions on probability spaces, strong ergodicity is equivalent to the absence of non-trivial approximately invariant sequences. The group-measure-space factor has trivial central sequences iff the action is strongly ergodic.
**How used:** This connects the ergodic-theoretic concept (strong ergodicity) to the operator-algebraic concept (no central sequences). It is the bridge between Steps F/G (no clone-generated central sequences) and the fullness conclusion.

### 6.5 Ozawa (2004) -- Bi-exactness of free groups

**Source:** Ozawa, "Solid von Neumann algebras," Acta Math. 192 (2004), 111-117.
**Key result:** Free group factors L(F_n) are solid (every diffuse subalgebra of L(F_n) has amenable relative commutant). Free groups are bi-exact.
**How used:** If G_L contains F_2 as a subgroup, and if F_2 acts ergodically on A|_L, then the crossed product has no central sequences by Houdayer-Isono.

### 6.6 No prior work on concentration of polymorphism actions

The search for "polymorphism averaging concentration" and "clone operator asymptotic commutation" in the operator algebra literature returned no results. The construction of clone operators T_f and the approximately-central-sequence argument are, to our knowledge, new. This is consistent with node 1.3 OA1's finding that "no prior work connecting polymorphisms to operator algebra automorphisms" exists. The bypass introduces a DIFFERENT connection (polymorphisms to approximately central sequences rather than to automorphisms), but it is equally novel.
