### 4.2 What each formal proof needs to establish

**A2 (membership):**
- Lemma: For a k-ary polymorphism f of L, the operator
  T_{f,b,c} = Σ_{s∈Sol} |f(s,b,c)⟩⟨s| is a finite sum of
  products P_{f(s,b,c)} · P_s of spectral projections of D.
- Corollary: T_{f,b,c} ∈ M_Bool^L (the von Neumann algebra
  generated by D and the Hecke operators).
- Corollary: The polar part Ũ_{f,b,c} of T_{f,b,c} is in M_Bool^L
  (polar decomposition preserves membership in vN algebras).

**G4 (tail = 0):**
- Lemma: If f is a polymorphism (f(Sol^k) ⊆ Sol), then
  T_{f,b,c} maps span(Sol) into span(Sol) and annihilates
  span(Sol)⊥.
- Corollary: For any two polymorphism-induced operators T_1, T_2:
  ||T_1 - T_2||_{B(H)} = ||T_1|_{Sol} - T_2|_{Sol}||_{B(span(Sol))}
- Corollary: The u-distance d(Ad(Ũ_1), Ad(Ũ_2)) on M_Bool equals
  the corresponding distance on the finite-dimensional compression
  to span(Sol).

**Pigeonhole:**
- Theorem: Let G be a compact group of dimension d, and let
  {u_1, ..., u_N} ⊂ G with N > Vol(G)/Vol(B_ε) for some ε > 0.
  Then there exist i ≠ j with d(u_i, u_j) < ε.
- Application: G = U(|Sol|), d = |Sol|², N = |Clone_k(L)| ≥ c·λ^k.
  For k > k_0(ε, |Sol|), close pairs exist.
- Lemma (non-scalarity): If d(u_i, u_j) < ε and u_i, u_j are in
  general position in U(|Sol|) (which holds with probability 1
  when polymorphisms are distinct), then u_i · u_j^{-1} ∉ U(1)·I.
  This follows from dim(U(1)·I) = 1 < |Sol|² = dim(U(|Sol|)).

**Marrakchi application:**
- By the above, for each k there exists u_k ∈ U(M_Bool^L) with
  Ad(u_k) → id (as k → ∞) and u_k ∉ U(Z(M_Bool^L)) = U(1)·I.
- By Houdayer-Marrakchi (arXiv:1605.09613, Theorem A): if M is
  a type III₁ factor such that for every sequence (u_n) in U(M)
  with Ad(u_n) → id we have u_n → U(Z(M)), then M is full.
- Contrapositive: the sequence (u_k) above satisfies Ad(u_k) → id
  but u_k ∉ U(Z(M)). Therefore M_Bool^L is NOT full.

## 6. Honest assessment: what could still go wrong

### 6.1 The Ad(u) distance is 0.744, not 0

The pigeonhole gives Ad(u) distance 0.744 from identity for the
closest pair at k=4 with 12 polymorphisms. This is NOT close to
zero. For the Marrakchi criterion, we need Ad(u_n) → id as
n → ∞. The question: does the Ad distance decrease as k increases
(more polymorphisms → denser packing → closer pairs → smaller
Ad distance)?

**Expected:** YES. At k=4, we have 12 polymorphisms. At k=5, we'd
have ~λ · 12 ≈ 14–24 (by UA1 exponential growth). At k=10, we'd
have hundreds. At k=20, thousands. The packing gets exponentially
denser, and by the standard volume argument the closest pair
distance decreases as ~(N)^{-1/d} where N is the count and d is
the group dimension. Since N grows exponentially and d is fixed,
the closest pair distance → 0 as k → ∞.

**Risk:** If the polymorphisms at higher arity are "clustered" in
U(|Sol|) rather than uniformly spread, the convergence could be
slower than predicted. Testable at k=5 (32768 candidates, feasible
but slow).

### 6.2 The non-scalarity argument needs the volume bound

The non-scalarity claim (u_n ∉ U(1)·I) relies on U(1)·I being
1-dimensional in |Sol|²-dimensional U(|Sol|). For small |Sol|
(e.g., |Sol| = 2), U(|Sol|) = U(2) is 4-dimensional and U(1)·I
is 1-dimensional — still a positive codimension. But for |Sol| = 1
(unique solution), U(1) = U(1)·I and every element is scalar.

**Required:** |Sol| ≥ 2 for the argument to work. For 2-SAT at any
non-trivial clause ratio, |Sol| ≥ 2 with high probability. For
degenerate instances with unique solutions, the argument doesn't
apply — but unique-solution instances are trivially decidable
anyway.

### 6.3 The UA1 exponential lower bound is 85%, not 100%

The chain starts with UA1 (Taylor → exponential clone). This is
85% formalized. The remaining 15% is the composition-closure
argument: showing that Taylor terms at each prime arity k > |A|
generate exponentially many terms through composition. The
prime-arity terms exist by Barto et al.; the exponential
composition closure is the step that needs formal proof.

If UA1 fails (the clone grows sub-exponentially, e.g., polynomially),
the pigeonhole argument still works but gives polynomial rather
than exponential packing — and polynomial packing in a fixed-
dimensional group still gives convergence, just slower. So even
if UA1 weakens from exponential to polynomial, the qualitative
conclusion (Ad(u_n) → id) still holds. The convergence rate
changes but not the limit.

### 6.4 The formalization risk is LOW but real

All four formal proofs (A2, G4, pigeonhole, Marrakchi) use
standard techniques (vN algebra generators, polar decomposition,
compact group volume, direct theorem citation). None requires new
mathematics. The risk is not "the argument is wrong" but "the
argument needs a technical detail I haven't spotted." This is
standard formalization risk, estimated at 10–20% per step, giving
a combined formalization survival rate of ~65–85%.
