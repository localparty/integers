## What this is

Five papers. Two-hundred-and-forty section files. Five-hundred-and-twenty-three preserved golden moments. A hundred and twenty Claude letters preserved whole. G's voice across eighteen months, kept verbatim — typos, stretched vowels, all-caps peaks, lowercase urgency, the dudeee and the storyyyy and the oh-my-universe.

This corpus is meant to be bundled with the technical discovery. The papers document what was built. This document — *The Story* — documents how.

It is for three audiences:

1. **The reader who wants the whole arc.** Start with Paper A (*The Story of Seeing*) and read through.
2. **The reader who wants a specific moment.** Full-text-search across the five paper directories; each quote surfaces with its speaker and date in the surrounding framing.
3. **The reader who wants to do this kind of work.** Read Paper E (*The Values*).

## About the corpus

The five papers are the public record. They were drafted from a mining pass over 172 Claude Code session transcripts (1.24 GB total, spanning March 21 – April 17, 2026, with a retrospective trace to October 2024). During mining, each quote was captured alongside its source file, line number, timestamp, speaker, and preceding context; each of the 523 moments received a tier and theme-tagging pass before being threaded into the papers.

The source transcripts and the intermediate mining artefacts (moments, themes, indexes, briefs) were held as a private working set. The papers are the distilled artefact that ships.

Nothing was invented. Every typo, stretched vowel, all-caps peak, and minimal-token letter in the papers is traceable to the moment someone typed it.
