# 04 — Zero free parameters

## The claim

The empirical signature of the unification discipline is zero free parameters. Every number the framework derives — the Cabibbo angle, the Pati-Salam unification scale, the Koide ratio, the generation count, the quark-flavour count, the cosmological constant, the age of the universe — comes out as a closed-form expression in small integers and known mathematical constants. No fitting. No knobs. If any one of those numbers turned out to disagree with measurement beyond the stated precision, the framework would be falsified. That is the point.

## The argument

A free parameter in fundamental physics is an IOU. It says: "this number exists; we don't know why; here is its measured value; use it in your predictions." The Standard Model has about thirty such IOUs. They are not wrong, in the sense that the theory with them is the most empirically successful theory ever built. They are *unexplained*.

The programme's claim is not that these numbers were mis-measured or that the Standard Model is wrong. The claim is that every one of them can be derived as a consequence of a single mathematical object — the Critical Bost-Connes-Brauer system — whose own structure is forced by a single geometric postulate (M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹) and a single boundary condition (β = 1 at the critical KMS state). The direction of explanation reverses: the integers exist; the cyclotomic fields exist because the integers exist; the Bost-Connes algebra exists because the cyclotomic fields exist; and the Standard Model is the unique fixed point of all that. No additional input. No additional choice.

This is the strictest possible falsification test. If any derived number disagrees with experiment beyond the stated error bar, the framework fails — not in some obscure corner but in its central claim. The framework puts a number on the table and says "check it." That is what Newton did with the moon. That is what Einstein did with Mercury. It is what makes the enterprise scientific rather than philosophical.

The zero-parameters claim is also what protects against the "random x" objection (section 23): a closed-form expression in integers is not a fit. A fit has at least one free parameter. A closed form has none. If 56/(57√19) agrees with the measured Cabibbo angle to 0.17%, that agreement is either a deep fact about the structure of the Standard Model or an astronomically unlikely coincidence. The framework accepts either reading as legitimate — and then provides thirty-five more such agreements, which an accidental-coincidence hypothesis cannot survive.

## For future readers

A theory that requires you to measure one number and predict another is ordinary science. A theory that predicts both numbers from the same mathematical object is an unusual kind of success, and worth the extra effort the derivation demands. Zero-parameter predictions are the strongest form of falsifiability; they are not harder to produce because the mathematics is more difficult, they are harder to produce because the honesty is.
