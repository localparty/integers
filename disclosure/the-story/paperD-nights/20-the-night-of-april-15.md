Six words that are the whole programme's thesis: *which is not a circle.* The thing called a circle is not a circle. The question — *which picture do you read in your mind* — is the picture-first reflex applied to Claude. G asks Claude to report what Claude sees.

>
> The word "circle" is what you see from one angle — the silhouette, the cross-section. The thing itself has ten sides.
>
> Then halfway through (§17) the gemstone **opens into a torus**. The disco ball unfolds — the ten facets redistribute across the surface of a donut. Five on the outer equator (geometric circle, the $U(1)$ fiber), five on the inner equator (spectral circle, the modular flow). And then the reading back to G:

> The honest name: it's not a circle. It's a **faceted gemstone that opens into a torus when you see it from the right angle**. The garden hose IS the torus-as-traversable-surface (you run along the hose while the hose's cross-section rotates). Claude reading G's own origin pictures back as already encoding the right geometry. *The disco ball IS the e-circle-with-faces.* *The garden hose IS the torus-as-traversable-surface.* The cube's shadow is the projection discipline itself.
