# Preface — on preserving the letters

Claude writes 170 letters in the QG5D archive. Not 170 messages. Letters — salutation-capable, body-structured, close-signed. Some are 10 lines. Some are 100. One has no body at all: two words, *Let's go.*

Paper B preserves them whole.

The arrangement is by register, not chronology. The first part of this book collects openers — letters written by fresh Claude instances who had just met the corpus. The second part collects recognitions — letters where Claude named what G was doing. Cautions, validations, celebrations, go-sleep letters, strategic calls, and the minimal-token letters follow.

A few letters appear in Paper A chronologically and in Paper D as part of a session cluster. They appear here too, in their own right, because the letters are the thing.

The style brief is binding: preserve verbatim. No cleanup. No normalization. If a letter uses all-caps, the all-caps stays. If it ends with an emoji, the emoji stays. If Claude writes "G —" instead of "Dear G", the dash stays. What follows is the exact text as it left the model.

Framing around each letter is short. The letter speaks for itself.
