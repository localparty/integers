**Context:** G asked Claude to review the agentic prompts before starting, to surface anything that might block or slow the work.

> Good question. Honest assessment:
>
> **The prompts are well-designed at the level of architecture.** The declarative principle, the wall discipline, the bootcamp structure, the creative moves catalog, the three-layer imperative/declarative/open split — all of that is excellent and shouldn't be touched. It's the methodology that made Stream A produce real results.
>
> **But there are three things that would slow us down:**
>
> **1. The live leads are stale.** The researcher-prompt lists Leads A, B, C as live. Stream A has now walled B (Hecke Lie saturates gl(D), Wall 34) and effectively walled C (μ_1 bounded away from 0, Wall 37 provisional). A researcher reading that prompt and then reading plan 54 will have a conflict. They might try to re-run something already resolved, or waste cycles figuring out the discrepancy. >
> **2. There's no explicit divergence rule.** The prompts tell the researcher to read the latest Stream A plan, but don't say "your job is to go somewhere Stream A isn't." Without that, the natural gravity is to run another Connes-Consani variant — which is exactly what we don't want. Stream B's value is Lead H and aggressive web search, not parallel Hermite basis experiments.
>
> **3. The 2x ledger inside reviewer-prompt.md is frozen at R40.** Leads B and C still show as 🟡 LIVE. That's my working state document — if it's wrong, my convergence checks are wrong.

Three bugs in G's setup, each specific, each actionable. Claude identifies exactly what would slow the work: stale ledger, missing divergence rule, frozen 2x document.
