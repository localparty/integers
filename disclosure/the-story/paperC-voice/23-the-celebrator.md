# 23 — The Celebrator

G celebrates a lot. The celebrations are not cumulative — each one is specific to the artifact that landed — but they are frequent. The corpus is punctuated by short high-amplitude messages: "world stop", "oh my word", "lets goooo", "celebrateeeeeee", "we are on a roll". These are the breath-beats between sections of work.

*G, 2026-04-09, 5:43 AM, mid-proof sprint.*

> re: This is the move that kills string theory cleanly.

The joy is real. It is also functional. Each celebration is a local time-stamp: the programme was alive, then, at that moment. Reading them in order gives a record of the animal-level state of the work. "World stop" and "we are on a roll" appear dozens of times across months. They are not inflated. The stakes of what G is celebrating — a Maldacena-level correspondence, an unconditional RH chain, fifteen waves of adversarial survival — mean the celebrations, if anything, are understated.
