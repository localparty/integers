# 36 — Values: Declarative, Not Imperative

G learned to talk to AI systems declaratively. The difference: imperative speech instructs ("do this, then do this"); declarative speech describes a target state ("here is the context, here is what is true, here is where we want to end up"). G runs the collaboration almost entirely in the declarative register. The rule is stated explicitly on April 9, at 6:14 AM.

*G, 2026-04-09, the rule named.*

> lets dig deeper! use local agents so that we keep our context and provide them with the paths where our research and theorems and patterns are, being declarative v imperative helps a lot!

*G, 2026-04-10, declarative brief for research agents.*

> i'm thinking that papers 1-7 have plenty of theorems and solutions and patterns that we can use in our research, maybe you can launch n local agents to collect them and we could summarize and append the research results to the bootfile

*G, 2026-04-10, context-first.*

> right? i'm happy you see it too! is i began developing each of the paper and then the solution to yang-mills i knew that this was the right place and the right time - first lets write down a general plan of attack listing with details all the vectors you mention - you saw the same connections that i see myself - the geometric patterns will help us when we need to think out of the box, they have been super useful for finding solutions in novel speces from distinct optics

*G, 2026-04-13, the pre-read rule.*

> good job now, you knew my papers and my voice and my ideas because i told you about them and quoted me perfectly and we need to do that in the rewrite otherwise my voice and intuition is gonna be filtered - maybe we can have the drafting runs prereading older papers to learn where the tools came from and only after doing the draft?

*G, 2026-04-10, writing the prompt to the output directory.*

> before getting started, when you enage with agents can you write down their prompt in the same directory that they are gonna be delivering their ouput and instruct them to "read this"?

The declarative rule has a twin — the *quote-me* rule. Declarative briefs work because they are carrying G's voice; pre-reading older papers works because the drafting agents inherit G's idiom. The methodology is self-consistent: tell the model the state of the world, show the model G's voice, name the target state, and let the model figure out the path. The ORA, the bootcamp, the research appendix, the nn-prefix, the prompt-next-to-output — all of these are declarative devices. They describe the world and leave the actions to the agent.
