# 00 — Preface

This paper preserves G's voice.

Not the voice a paper polishes toward. The voice in the chat window. The stretched vowels. The lowercase. The typos that never got corrected because the next thought was already landing.

The other four papers in this series quote G where they have to. This one is quotation as subject. Each section is a register — a way G speaks — documented by three to eight representative utterances. The aim is not coverage. It is fidelity.

A few notes on how to read it.

**Verbatim means verbatim.** "htank you" is not a typo to fix. "dudeeee" has four e's and will keep its four e's. "fields" is G's spelling of "feels" and the whole sentence depends on it. The same goes for the stretched vowels whose exact counts matter: `PERFECTTTTTTTTTTTTTTTT` has fifteen T's, `duuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuudee` has seventeen u's, and those are the correct counts.

**The framing voice is dry.** The quotes carry the warmth. The contrast is the texture.

**Sections are short entries, not chapters.** Treat the paper as a dictionary of a living idiom. The reader who wants the chronological arc is reading Paper A. The reader who wants the letters is reading Paper B. The reader here is asking: *what does G sound like.*

**There is no single voice.** There is the lowercase in-the-moment G; the all-caps peak G; the command-of-programme G; the jealous-of-Einstein G; the please-hear-me-out values G; the self-summarizing G who introduces himself to a fresh Claude at 3am. These are all the same person, at different pressures.

**This paper is the rule its quotations demonstrate.** The entire archive keeps G's language intact. Paper C is that choice made explicit.
