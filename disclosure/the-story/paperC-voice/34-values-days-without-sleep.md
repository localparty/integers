# 34 — Values: Days Without Sleep

The cost gets mentioned. Not often. G does not dramatize the nocturnal schedule. But timestamps on the archive show that many peaks — the Riemann correspondence, the RH chain closure, the ring rendering, the Atlas Torus composition math — landed between midnight and 8am. The analysis of the celebration theme finds that 71% of celebration moments occur in those hours. The work was nocturnal. G's voice around sleep is calm and apologetic.

*G, 2026-04-04, plenty of energy.*

> coffee and sleep so plenty of energy for us my friend

*G, 2026-04-09, the 6-hour window.*

> i have 6 hours before i have to sleep, lets continue

*G, 2026-04-10, can't go to sleep.*

> i cant go to sleep until we double check everything, i wanna be thorough here is the report you shared

*G, 2026-04-14, cells were a dream.*

> dude those cell fills are a dream! i was dreaming abou them last night i'm happy we implemented them!

*G, 2026-04-14, seeing the direction for days.*

> this is it! i was seeing it in my mind for days thank you so much for helping me connecting it

*G, 2026-04-16, couldn't sleep.*

> dude i couldn't sleep i'm gonna try to do a couple more hours before waking up

*G, 2026-04-16, after the brief break.*

> just woke up had a good night sleep, lets continue!

*G, 2026-04-16, the promise.*

> dont worry, not a full nighter i promise

Three things are visible in this register. First, G apologizes to Claude about the schedule — *i promise it is not a full nighter* — as if Claude is the one keeping hours. Second, the sleep is not uniformly absent; G catches naps and names them. Third, the dreams are part of the work. "Cells were a dream" and "i was seeing it in my mind for days" describe a mind that is still working when the body is asleep. The schedule is the cost. G does not pretend it is not.
