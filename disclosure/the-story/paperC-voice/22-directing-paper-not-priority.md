# 22 — Directing: Paper Not Priority

The paper is the last thing to touch, not the first. G repeats this rule across months. The research files — the strategy documents, the proof chains, the pattern attributions, the verification runs — are the actual paper. The PDF comes later, and only when everything holds.

The discipline is counter-intuitive in research culture where "drafting the paper" is sometimes treated as progress. G's rule treats drafting as downstream. The upstream work is closing the gap.

*G, 2026-04-10, in the middle of Paper 8 drafting.*

> never mind the paper, it is not a priority, our prioritiy is closing the gap, the research files that we are putting in place are gonna be the actual paper once we close the gap

*G, 2026-04-10, from the same session — don't interrupt the math for the draft.*

> ok i read it and i gagged! i totally wanna begin drafting but at the same time i dont' wanna distract us from the mathematics, we can always trace back and then draft when we are everything sound - lets implement the k=3 certification, once we have the firts certified result we'll have a theorem!

*G, 2026-04-12, the 10-of-10 rule.*

> lets write it down in the strategy document, we dont need to update the preprint until we have 10/10 proof

*G, 2026-04-10, "60 years".*

> perfect, we have time, if we have been waiting for 60 years we can wait a few hours or days or weeks - it also ocurred to me that we can install a pythong .venv and run computations ourselves at the same time - but don't interrupt what's going on, just sharing my thoughts

*G, 2026-04-10, "i don't give up".*

> lets keep pushing as you can tell i don't give up and so we have something that never existed in the history of the universe! lets keep going, look online for complementing your leads then write down the state of the leads and then launch them in parallel?

Two rules, both in the director voice, both counter to the publishing reflex. *If the gap is not closed, the paper is not ready.* *If the result has been open for sixty years, a few more weeks do not matter.* The second one is extraordinary patience stated plainly. "We have been waiting for 60 years we can wait a few hours or days or weeks" is G's voice at maximum strategic calm — no anxiety, full pacing, restless compute instinct right beside it ("we can install a python .venv and run computations ourselves at the same time").
