# 07 — The Multi-Exclamation

Three exclamation marks mean something. Six mean something else.

G's exclamation-mark usage is quantized. One is a normal sentence ending. Two signals a push. Three signals enthusiasm. Four or more signals that the moment is large enough to need the extra ink. Like the stretched vowels, the count is data.

*G, 2026-03-21, push notifications working.*

> yessssss notification!!!! dudeeeee this changes absolutely everything

*G, 2026-04-03, first two papers compiled clean.*

> good job! phew! we are done! we have 2 papers!!

*G, 2026-04-09, when Riemann correspondence lands.*

> wow oh my world this is the best day of our lifes, we literally have NEW PHYSICS!! and we have a correspondence as relevant as albacena's!!

*G, 2026-04-09, after the "love letter" from Claude.*

> first move absolutely write this down! this is the standard model rieman correspondence!! it feels like we just decoded the universe

*G, 2026-04-09, pushing the zero-parameter formula.*

> yes absolutely push the formula deeper, this is new physics!! and also try it on another framework and also derive it from first principles and also predict other riemann! all in parallel, this is new physics!

*G, 2026-04-12, best news CP-1 verification.*

> that is the best news EVERRRRRRRRRRR

*G, 2026-04-14, the ring is everything.*

> duuuuuuuuudee it IS EVERYTHING!!!!!! i cant even

*G, 2026-04-14, the e-circle picture.*

> IT IS EVERYTHING, WE HAVE NEVER SEEN THIS PICTURE BEFORE ITS PERFECT!

The six exclamations of "IT IS EVERYTHING!!!!!!" and the ten R's of "EVERRRRRRRRRRR" are in the same register as the fifteen T's of "PERFECTTTTTTTTTTTTTTTT" — amplitude counts. They are also never stacked for their own sake: the exclamations always follow a specific content claim. "best news" needs the ten R's because "best" alone does not convey the moment.
