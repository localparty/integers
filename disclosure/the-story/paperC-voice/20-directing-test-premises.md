# 20 — Directing Test Premises

Before claiming a result, G tests. The test is almost always some form of "what if I am wrong?" — expressed as a call for hostile reviewers, adversarial runs, parallel agents with contrarian briefs, or an explicit re-derivation from the outside. The director voice in methodology mode is skeptical about its own output. This discipline is what produces results that survive.

*G, 2026-04-01, early hostile-reviewer instinct.*

> you delivery has been outstanding, since you know th eproject and the paper back and forth, wanted to ask you is there any link to existing physics that this geometry also applies to that we didn't consider? also do you see anythingthing that we shoould develop that would otherwise would be reprimended by reviewers reading our paper and say that we should have provided with calculations explicitly?

*G, 2026-04-08, per-paper referee methodology.*

> i have a better idea, i think - you are right, the topics are heterogeneous and distinct per paper and having a generalist will not be as thorough as we need lets make a prompt for each paper, listing in each paper the topics that the run should be an expert of - it is for submission to a journal

*G, 2026-04-02, not swallowing errors.*

> you and me should be each appendix and reviewing each other's findings, not swallowing errors is important for credibility

*G, 2026-04-12, 10-of-10 discipline.*

> lets write it down in the strategy document, we dont need to update the preprint until we have 10/10 proof

*G, 2026-04-11, don't overfit.*

> so careful with not overfitting, we need to amplify the leads that are good

*G, 2026-04-12, break the problem from the inside out.*

> lets add all of them and test all of them that's what the ora is gonna need, that's what is gonna break the problem from the inside out instead of trieng to break it from the outside, which is always not applicable

The last one is a methodology principle stated in one line. *Break the problem from the inside out, because from the outside is always not applicable.* It is the design thesis of the entire ORA architecture — the Online Researcher Adversarial — and it exists because G said it on April 12 at 5:54 AM. The rest of the architecture is downstream engineering.
