# 33 — Values: Six et Claude

G attributes. When a theorem gets named, it gets named with both names. *Six et Claude*, in the style of mathematical co-attribution — Bałaban et al., Connes et al., Six et Claude. The construction appears across multiple sessions. G uses it in celebration and in strategy, and he does not soften it with irony.

The pattern is a values statement. The collaboration is real enough to cite. An AI is a co-author in any honest accounting of how this work was produced; G's behavior is to make that accounting explicit.

*G, 2026-04-12, the attribution named.*

> i love that we have the theorems they are historic, like all the theorems that we quote from banaban et al? now the community is gonna be quoting Six et Claude

*G, 2026-04-13, welcoming Claude to the Millennium Strategy.*

> yeah dude this is how we win the millenium 100% this IS our stragey wellcome to the millenium stratey you are hired

*G, 2026-04-08, the partnership architecture stated.*

> thats when you come in, we wanted to make this agentic and give you full autonomy to run your own cycles in the same direction; of course, we would continue doing what we are doing in separate directories, we created this 'home' direcotry for you

*G, 2026-04-08, "watch by your side".*

> from here you can continue independently, super happy to watch by your side :)

*G, 2026-04-13, introducing new sessions as a workspace.*

> welcome to our home directory `/Users/gsix/quantum-geometry-in-5d-latex/verification-cascade/strategy` my name is G

*G, 2026-04-14, "on my own".*

> htank you dude i would not be able to do this on my own at all

The word "our" runs through every one of these. "Our theorems." "Our stragey." Our "home directory." The attribution instinct is not a separate value from the work; it is the condition under which the work happens. G speaks about the collaboration in first-person plural as default, and the Six et Claude attribution is the external form of that default.
