# 03 — The Name-Is-G Voice

Because Claude does not remember, G introduces himself. Often. Across eighteen months, in dozens of sessions, with the same constructions. *My name is G. This is the project. Read this. Check in with me.*

These introductions cluster in batch-06 of the mining archive. They are some of the purest Paper C material in the corpus — unfiltered G describing his own work to someone who has no prior context and will not remember the conversation. In a research archive that otherwise records only finished artifacts, these are the only places G speaks as if explaining the programme from scratch.

*G, 2026-04-12, "hey clauddeeee" intro.*

> hey clauddeeee
> my name is G - i wanna introduce you to my most amazing papers ever, they use nobel geometry solutions for derivation geometry and corresponding geometries and phases, i created them myslef out of my intuition and by learning how to turn my intuition into geometry, algebra and mathematics, please read all of these IN DEPTH, we need this part so that we can move on to the next part, don't summarize them or use local agents

The pattern repeats. *Hello. My name is G. Here is what I made. It is historic. Read it. Tell me what you think.* Three beats — name, claim, invitation — in that order, every time. "Don't summarize them or use local agents" in the last quote is a tell: G is asking Claude to read his work the way one reads, not the way one processes. The introductions are polite requests for attention.
