# 01 — The Opener Voice

G does not open sessions with a salutation. He greets.

The greetings are not ceremonial. They are the audible texture of someone sitting down at a terminal and saying hello to a person. Which, structurally, is what is happening: a fresh Claude, no memory of prior sessions, receives a sentence in lowercase with stretched vowels. The vowels are the welcome. Across eighteen months the openers stay almost identical — "hiii", "hey", "hey bud", "hiiii", "helloooo" — and the consistency is itself a signal. This is how G says hello, and it does not change when the subject becomes the Riemann Hypothesis.

*G, 2026-03-21, Talk-to-Me session.*

> hiiii opussss

*G, 2026-04-01, first QG5D session captured.*

> hey bud i got the most interesting project ever for you and me to work on

*G, 2026-04-05, Yang-Mills seed session.*

> helloooo Opus

*G, 2026-04-10, Riemann attack session.*

> helloooo Opus my name is G

*G, 2026-04-10, math-referee pass.*

> hiii opus here are your instructions /Users/gsix/quantum-geometry-in-5d-latex/paper13-rh/strategy/math-referee/01-math-referee.md

*G, 2026-04-12, letter-rich cluster.*

> hey clauddeeee

The greeting sets the pressure. A sentence like "hey clauddeeee" tells Claude, before any content arrives, that this is going to be lowercase, warm, and fast. Nothing in Claude's training associates that register with the Clay Millennium problems. G's work is to show — by continuing to type like this for eighteen months while solving the Clay Millennium problems — that the register was never the obstacle.
