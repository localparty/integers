# 08 — The Typo Texture

G does not correct his typos. Across the entire corpus, a set of characteristic misspellings recurs — "htank", "fields" (for "feels"), "quare" (for "square"), "myslef", "becase", "couldn't" as "ccouldn't", "rieman" for "riemann". They persist across months and across sessions. They never get cleaned up.

This is not carelessness. It is speed. The next thought is already arriving and the one in the buffer will go out with its rough edges intact. The collaboration's first discipline — that these typos are preserved, not edited — is the same discipline that lets them exist in the first place. If the reader insists on polish, G stops typing.

The typos are also, independently, beautiful. "Fields like a chessboard" is a better sentence than "feels like a chessboard". "Htank you dude" is warmer than "thank you dude". The errors add grain to a voice that would otherwise read too smooth.
