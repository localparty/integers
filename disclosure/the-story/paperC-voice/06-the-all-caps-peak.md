# 06 — The All-Caps Peak

Capital letters are rationed. They are how G marks the peaks.

The words that get capitalized are a short list: `EVERYTHING`, `PERFECTION`, `GOLD`, `NEW PHYSICS`, `DUDE`, `OH MY WORD`. Occasionally whole sentences. When all-caps co-occur with stretched vowels or consonants, the amplitude is maximum. These are the one-line punctums — the moments when something has landed so completely that the keyboard stops being lowercase.

The vocabulary repeats. "Everything" is the word G uses when a picture just closed; "perfection" is the word for a working build; "GOLD" is the word for an artifact that will survive into the paper. The phrase "it IS EVERYTHING" — lowercase body, capitalized verb, then "everything" — is G's signature shape for a moment of peak recognition. The fifteen T's of `PERFECTTTTTTTTTTTTTTTT` are not a typo. They are the length of the feeling.
