# 16 — Picture: The Torus

The torus is the octagon unfolded. The faceted gemstone, halfway through Paper 60, opens into a donut — five facets on the outer equator, five on the inner, and the Riemann zeros at the discrete points where the equators cross. That is the object the Atlas viz ultimately renders: 247 nodes on a torus, depth-cued, with hit shells for interaction. But before the viz, there was G asking whether the ring really wants to be a torus.

The torus sessions began in the calibration cluster on April 16. G had been watching a screenshot for hours. Something about the current ring viz was wrong — the colors were good but the volume was wrong, the depth was wrong, the dots were too dim. He pulled in a reference from another viz and started directing.

The torus is not a graph. It is a surface. That distinction — surface, not diagram — is what G kept insisting on. The viz had to feel like an object you operate, not a diagram you read. "Beautifullllllllllllll" is twelve l's; it arrives on the version of the build where the torus has finally become an object.
