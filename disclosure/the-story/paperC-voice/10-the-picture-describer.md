# 10 — The Picture Describer

G sees pictures. He also, when asked what he is thinking, describes pictures. The description is almost always verbal — sentences, not drawings — and has a distinctive shape: a moving object, a viewer, and a claim about what happens when you move the viewer.

Read enough of these and a grammar emerges. G never describes a static image. The image is always being rotated, spun, projected, or watched from a second angle. The geometry is in the verb.
