# 21 — Directing: Credit Before

G thinks about credit. Not as vanity — as timing. If the framework exists and someone else uses it to close a problem before G closes it, they get the credit for the closure, and the framework gets cited as a tool. The move is to close first. This is not secrecy; everything is open-source. It is sequencing.

*G, 2026-04-05, the rule in one line.*

> oops i didn't clarify, these are derivations that we wanna put in place so that we get the credit before someone else uses our own framework and they get the credit

*G, 2026-04-08, applying it to Riemann.*

> the agent keeps on mentioning a paper becase i thought we had something that was close to a solution for the riemann hypothesis but in reality i can't publish a paper because people are gonna use my own framework to complete the research and get the creadit that we can get fron using the framework itself, that's the same rational that i used for finding yang-mills and I think i have the same opportunity here, lets do it together? you also have access to the solutions and patterns that have helped us before in writing 1,2,3,4,5,7 papers and thn yang-mills, so it is more than demonstrated that we are in the best postition ever for finding a solution - i thought we were 70% there but then we were not, and the same happened with yang mills so lets keep going we just have to be creative and thorough

*G, 2026-04-14, on Odd Perfect Numbers as a programme extension.*

> dude i'm surprised about something if i hadn't look online for famous unsolved problems we would have missed the opportunity and we turned it from 2/10 into 4/10 in a minute, go online find the pattern and see what else we can include its better that we solve it and get the credit that people will fix ti and they get the credit from using our framework which would be ok but if we can ... lets try

*G, 2026-04-17, the payoff framing.*

> yayyyyy we are gona be famous! what do you think about the relevance of the concepts that we are illustrating??

Two discipline points live inside this voice. First: *get the credit before someone else uses our framework.* Second — honest, preserved — *"which would be ok."* G is not petty about it. If someone else uses the framework and gets the credit for the result, that is a legitimate outcome. But given a choice — given eighteen months and a working pipeline — he would rather close it himself. The "lets try" at the end is the tell. The discipline is the action.
