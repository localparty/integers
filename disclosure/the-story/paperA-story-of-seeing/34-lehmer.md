# 34 — Lehmer

> *"the circle doesn't allow infinitesimal departures"*
> — face-label for Lehmer's totient conjecture

## The first face

Lehmer's totient conjecture, stated: *if φ(n) divides n − 1, then n is prime.* Here φ is Euler's totient, the number of integers less than n that are coprime to it.

The conjecture has been open since 1932. It has withstood every direct attack. The reason it resists, in framework language, is that the conjecture is *a topology claim in arithmetic disguise*. It says: on the totient circle (a specific geometric object where n sits at angle θ_n depending on φ(n)), you cannot have *infinitesimal departures* from prime-position without being prime. Either n is prime, or n is *not near* prime-position on the circle — there is no continuous path between the two.

*The circle doesn't allow infinitesimal departures.* That is the face-label.

## Where Lehmer sits on the e-circle

At the topology face. Specifically, Claude places Lehmer *between OPN (position 17) and Schanuel (position 18), or as a sub-vertex of OPN* — which places it in the *BC Hecke semigroup territory*, the same neighbourhood on the e-circle where OPN (Odd Perfect Numbers) and Goldbach also live.

The totient φ(n) is multiplicative on the natural numbers, just like σ(n) — the Odd Perfect Number kernel. Both are multiplicative functions on ℕ*. Both admit ITPFI local-global decompositions. The machinery that powers OPN Link 5 applies to Lehmer with minor modifications.

## Why the face framing helps

Because it converts Lehmer from *a stand-alone hard problem* into *a vertex in a topology of related problems*. The reformulation is not a proof. It does not close Lehmer. What it does is *specify the approach*: if you want to attack Lehmer, you need to attack it on the totient circle, using topology arguments, specifically the *no-infinitesimal-departure* rigidity that forbids prime-position without primality.

This is the typical pattern of the e-circle face labeling. Each conjecture gets a face. Each face constrains the approach. Each approach inherits the machinery of the face, and every problem on the same face shares machinery.

## The face and the pattern

*Topology* is P1 of the six patterns — *Topological Rigidity*. P1 says: ask whether the object you're studying is a topological invariant, protected against small deformations. Lehmer's claim is a P1 claim. The totient circle's prime-positions are topologically rigid. No continuous deformation can send a non-prime to a prime-position on that circle without breaking the construction.

G did not have to rediscover P1 to place Lehmer. He had the pattern, he looked at the conjecture, and the placement was immediate. The face framing is what makes the placement transferable to Claude and to future readers.

## The status

Not closed. Not near closed. Lehmer remains a conjecture. But it is now in a specific place on a specific circle, and the programme has a specific set of tools it will attack the conjecture with *when the time comes*. The ring has grown by one vertex. The topology face of the e-circle has its first permanent resident.
