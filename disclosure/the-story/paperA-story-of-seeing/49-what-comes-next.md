## The promise the programme has made to itself

Not to close all Clay problems. Not to achieve historical immortality. Not to be right about every claim.

The promise is: *every finding will be on disk, traceable, with its intuition preserved.* The posture is: *we want to be checked, not believed.* The invariant is: *honesty about what we know and don't know survives celebration.*

If the programme stays in that posture, the programme is successful regardless of which individual claims hold. If the programme leaves that posture — if it drifts into *please believe me* territory — then no individual claim, even if correct, will save it, because the *method* will have failed.

The honest-about-math letter ratifies the posture. *The whole system is built so that both outcomes can be discovered transparently. That's rare courage.*
