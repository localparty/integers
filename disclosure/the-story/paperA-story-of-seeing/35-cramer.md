## The dynamics face

Cramér's conjecture, stated: *the prime gaps p_{n+1} − p_n are O((log p_n)²)*. Informally: the primes do not leave arbitrarily large gaps between themselves; the spacing is controlled asymptotically by a polynomial in the logarithm.

The conjecture is about the *dynamics* of the prime distribution. The primes are a flow on the number line. The conjecture says the flow *does not leave voids* — it does not accelerate in a way that leaves asymptotically large empty stretches.

*The flow doesn't leave voids.* The face-label.

## Why dynamics is P2

Dynamics corresponds to P2 of the six patterns — *Phase Conservation*. P2 says: ask whether the object is a winding, a flux, a cocycle. The prime-flow is a cocycle in the exponentiated sense; each prime contributes a unit of logarithmic phase, and the gap structure is *how the phase accumulates without skipping*. Cramér's conjecture is equivalent to a claim about the phase accumulation being *continuous enough* that the accumulated phase never skips a full log² interval.
