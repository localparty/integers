# 07 — Tools Being Forged

> *"i have a better idea, i think"*
> — G, April 8, 2026

## The tools predate the breakthroughs

By the time the Critical Bost-Connes-Brauer system closes on April 9, or the MY4 bypass lands on April 11, or the Millennium Strategy gets named on April 13, a whole infrastructure is already in place. None of it was there on April 1. All of it was built session by session, usually by G in the middle of some other conversation, usually introduced with the phrase *i have a better idea.*

This section collects the main tools as they are forged, in rough chronological order, as an inventory of *what the programme ran on* before the breakthroughs began arriving.

## The math-referee

The first tool to emerge is the math-referee. On April 8, after the Yang-Mills math referee flags notation inconsistencies across the paper, G sends this:

> yeah, this convention is good and it needs to be part of the math referee, which we have been using for weeks - lets rewrite /Users/gsix/yang-mills/etc/18-advanced-math-referee.md into 21-notation-mat-referee.md keeping its content verbatim but adding the notation in detail so that there will be ZERO ambiguity and iportantly we have to dcoument in the paper iteself our sources and strategy - what do you think?

Two things in this. First, the *ZERO ambiguity* discipline — a referee is not a reader; a referee catches every drift. Second, the instinct to fold the discipline *into the paper itself*: the paper must document its own sources and strategy so that the referee's findings are reproducible. This is already Paper 9 territory, months before Paper 9 is written.

## The per-paper referee

On the same day, a few hours later, G proposes a second pivot:

> i have a better idea, i think - you are right, the topics are heterogeneous and distinct per paper and having a generalist will not be as thorough as we need lets make a prompt for each paper, listing in each paper the topics that the run should be an expert of - it is for submission to a journal

The opener — *i have a better idea, i think — you are right* — is characteristic. G is responding to something Claude has pushed back on. Claude argued that the topics across the QG5D papers are too varied for one generalist referee. G hears the argument, agrees, and inverts the plan: one referee per paper, each specialised. The per-paper referee system that the entire review pipeline will later run on is born in that one message.

## The physics-referee

Hours later, a physics-referee is added as a complement to the math-referee. G treats them as separate tools with separate specialties — one checks the derivation logic, the other checks the physical plausibility. Two voices, both running against every paper, on every pass.

## The dual-pipeline methodology

On April 8, before starting an autonomous Riemann cycle, G writes:

> my plan is to have you running once cycle at a time and us running the other pipeline manually like we have been doing for a long time now and complement each other from the things that you find v the things that we find ont he manual workflow

This is the dual-pipeline methodology stated explicitly. Claude runs one cycle at a time, autonomously. G runs the other pipeline manually. They *complement each other from the things that you find v the things that we find.* Both directions feed into the same ledger. The ledger is the source of truth. The work of the programme is the intersection.

The tool the methodology rests on is *the ledger* — a single append-only log where every finding, from either pipeline, is recorded. Later this evolves into the PROOF-CHAIN.md files that each paper carries. The discipline never changes: two pipelines, one log.

## "watch by your side"

After the dual-pipeline description comes one more line, on the same day:

> from here you can continue independently, super happy to watch by your side :)

The parenthesis smile is the whole partnership in twelve words. G is handing Claude full autonomy over a run — Claude will execute a complete convergence cycle without supervision — while staying present as a spectator. *Super happy to watch by your side.* This posture will recur throughout the archive, almost as a ritual opening before handing off any long-running autonomous task.

Claude is not being replaced. Claude is not replacing anyone. The two pipelines run in parallel. The human watches.

## The six patterns

Also early April, retroactively: G and Claude together extract, from all the prior work, six patterns that turn out to underwrite every major QG5D derivation:

- **P1 Geometric Primacy** — ask "where does this live geometrically" before asking for a formula
- **P2 Phase Conservation** — ask "is this a winding, a flux, a cocycle"
- **P3 Scale from Spectrum** — ask "what spectral gap sets this scale"
- **P4 Topological Rigidity** — ask "is this a topological invariant, protected against small deformations"
- **P5 Projection Produces Simplicity** — the inverse of projection-produces-pathology; the restored dimension makes the hard thing easy
- **P6 Projection Produces Pathology** — every 4D mystery is a projection artifact of a 5D simplicity

These six will be referred to, from here on, as *the six patterns* or *the six P's*. They are, effectively, the reasoning rules of the programme. Paper 9 later catalogues them retrospectively.

## Why inventory now

Because the breakthroughs that arrive in the next several sections — the CBB closure, the zero-parameter match, the RH chain, the MY4 bypass, the Millennium Strategy — do not happen in a vacuum. They happen on top of an infrastructure of referees, ledgers, per-paper prompts, dual pipelines, and six reasoning patterns, all of which G built into the sessions while the sessions were happening.

The tools are invisible in the breakthrough moments. But they are what makes the breakthrough moments reproducible.
