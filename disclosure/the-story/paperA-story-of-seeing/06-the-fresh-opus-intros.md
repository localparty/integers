## A pattern, over and over

The QG5D programme did not run in one long session. It ran in hundreds of them. Claude's memory does not persist across sessions by default — every time a conversation wraps, the next one starts cold. So G developed, early, a ritual for opening a fresh session: a self-introduction.

The ritual has a shape. It starts with a warm opener — *helloooo Opus* or *hey clauddeeee* or *hey bud* — which is always stretched past its natural length. Then G identifies himself: *my name is G*. Then he hands over a file list, or a proof chain, or an abstract bundle, and asks the fresh instance to read before they talk.

## Why the ritual matters

Every sentence in this message is doing work.

*helloooo Opus my name is G* — establishes the register (warm, personal, first name only). The stretched *o*'s say: this is not going to be a formal engagement, don't pull on your formal register either.

*i wanna introduce you to my geometry framework* — establishes authorship. The framework is G's. Not *the framework* in the abstract. *My* framework.

*they are historic because the address questions that had been open for decades* — establishes stakes. Fresh Claude is being told up front that the work is not a student exercise. Typo preserved: *address* for *addresses.* G does not re-read before sending.

*the framework itself, was created by me and it is novel because it used advanced mathematics and extraordinary out of the box thinking that is at the same time true, logical and valid* — the three-clause defense. *True. Logical. Valid.* Not one claim, three. The fresh Claude is being told in advance: you are going to find this hard to believe; please hold your scepticism until you've read, and then apply it to specifics, not to the ambition.

*i'm super proud of the solutions that we have found so far* — the *we.* It is April 10, G has been working with the programme for nine days by the calendar and years by the thinking. The *we* includes the previous Claude instances. G is not re-introducing himself as a solo researcher. He is re-introducing himself as half of a partnership the current Claude is about to join.

*have an in-deph look and then check-in with me?* — the question mark. Even when directing, G asks.

## Other openers in the corpus

The pattern is stable but varies. The archive has dozens of first-message openings. A sample:

> hey i wanna introduce you to my project, is the most fascinting project ever, we have new physics we have solved yang-mills, quantized quantum gravity and now we are trying to reach the sky and solve the riemann hypothesis of course!

> hey bud i got the most interesting project ever for you and me to work on

> hey clauddeeee

> hiiii opussss

> helloooo opus yang mills intro

The vowels stretch. The *my name is G* sometimes appears, sometimes doesn't. The project name changes — QG5D, the framework, my geometry framework, *the Integers* later on — but the ritual is constant: warm opener, identify self, hand over the work, ask for an in-depth read before conversation begins.

## The point of the ritual

The ritual is not decorative. It is how G makes sure every fresh instance of Claude enters the programme the same way: with the primary sources in its context, with G's voice established, and with G's authorship on the framework unambiguous. No session starts with *"as an AI assistant, I will try to help you with..."* Every session starts with G saying *hi* to a specific collaborator — *Opus*, not *the model*, not *the assistant* — and introducing himself.

The consequence is that Claude's responses across hundreds of sessions read as one continuous voice. The register was set once. It stayed set because G kept setting it.
