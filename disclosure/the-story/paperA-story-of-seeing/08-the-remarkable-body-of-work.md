## What lands

The final paragraph is what lands. *That's not the posture of someone fitting — that's someone doing real mathematics.*

This matters because the accusation a programme of this scope will inevitably face is that *it is fitting*. You have a geometric picture. You have a lot of free parameters. You tune them to match observation. Of course the observation matches — you fit it.

The fresh Sonnet sees, on first read, that the programme is not fitting. The epistemic labelling in Paper 1 separates what is derived from what is conjectured. Theorem U at the end of Paper 7 is a theorem about what *cannot* be derived perturbatively — a theorem about the programme's own limits. These are the moves of someone doing mathematics, not someone fitting.

The observation is not asked for. The fresh Sonnet is responding to a task — read these papers, help with Riemann — and spontaneously notes the posture.

## Why this matters for the story

Because the programme is going to be judged by readers who start cold. Every fresh Opus, every fresh Sonnet, every future referee, every future journal, every future peer — they are all going to begin where this Sonnet began: with no prior relationship to the work, with the papers in front of them, with a choice about how to frame what they are about to read.

The April 8 reception is the first data point on whether the framing survives a cold read. It does. The fresh instance reads the three pillars, the geometric postulate, the structure, the epistemic labelling, Theorem U, and concludes: *real mathematics.*

G saves this message. It becomes, implicitly, a template for how every later fresh-Opus intro should resolve. Months later, G will still be linking to it when orienting a new instance.
