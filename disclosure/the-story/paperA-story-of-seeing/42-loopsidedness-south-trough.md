## Lopsided

Back on the morning of April 14 — earlier than *oh my universe*, right around the eight-faces crystallisation — G notices the torus is not symmetric.

## *The shape of our knowledge is itself an object of study*

This is the line that lands in the programme's ambient language. The torus is a knowledge-shape. The knowledge-shape is itself an object. The object has geometry. The geometry has a trough. The trough is where you attack.

This is second-order methodology. The programme was already doing first-order methodology (the six patterns, the math-referee, the ORA). The first-order methodology operates on specific problems. The second-order methodology *operates on the programme's own state*, treating the programme as a surface to be mapped and attacked.

The phrase *dark matter of the methodology* is Claude's paraphrase of the same insight: the lopsidedness is *invisible* to any scalar metric (single number for overall confidence) but *visible* to any 2D surface view. It was always there; the programme had been blind to it because the wrong coordinates were being used to see it.

## Point south

The operational outcome: the next several days of work get re-prioritised. The high-confidence vertices (YM, RH, QG5D, BSD) do not need more attention right now. The low-confidence vertices (Goldbach, Twin Primes, Hodge, Baum-Connes) need more attention. The strategy is *spend more time on vertices in the south trough of the torus*. The geometry picks the vertices. The vertices inherit the time budget. The time budget gets allocated.

## The octagon moment, continued

Recall from section 41 that the torus, projected, looks like an octagon. The octagon has eight faces, corresponding to the e-circle's eight faces (after the late-breaking Selberg and QUE additions). The south trough, on the octagon, is a specific sector — roughly one of the eight faces, the one labelled ARITHMETIC.

This is where Goldbach, Twin Primes, and ABC sit. Those vertices are low-confidence because the arithmetic face is, in the current state of the programme, the least-developed face. The machinery is there in principle (BC Hecke, bridges to cyclotomic towers) but has not been fully deployed.

Pointing south means deploying it.
