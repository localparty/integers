## The symmetry face

The Katz-Sarnak philosophy, stated informally: *the low-lying zeros of L-functions are distributed according to the eigenvalue statistics of random matrices from specific classical symmetry groups — unitary, orthogonal, or symplectic, depending on the family of L-functions*.

The philosophy has been confirmed on many families and remains open in general. It is not a single conjecture but a *framework* for asking *which group acts on this family*. The answer determines the zero-spacing statistics. Unitary gives GUE. Orthogonal gives GOE. Symplectic gives GSE.

*Which group acts.* The face-label.

## Where Katz-Sarnak sits on the e-circle

At the symmetry face. Adjacent on the ring to the BGS vertex (Bohr-Grunsky-Schur, which is the spectral-statistics vertex), and downstream of both RH and GRH.

The framework has something specific to say here. Each family of L-functions — zeta, Dirichlet, elliptic-curve L-functions, automorphic L-functions — corresponds in the CBB account to a specific *geometric object*, and the symmetry group of the geometric object *is* the Katz-Sarnak group for the family. The group is not an empirical observation; it is a derivable fact about the geometry.

For the Riemann zeta function, the relevant object is the S¹ factor of the 5D geometry. The symmetry group of S¹ (as a Riemannian manifold with a natural flow) is U(1). Expanded to the GUE prediction for zero-spacings, the group is the unitary group U(∞) — which is exactly Katz-Sarnak's prediction for zeta.

For the family of Dirichlet L-functions with conductor q, the relevant object is the cyclotomic tower, and the symmetry group is Sp(∞) or O(∞) depending on the character type — again matching Katz-Sarnak.
