## What *Millennium Strategy* means

Structurally, the Millennium Strategy is this:

1. Every Clay-class proof chain in the programme (YM, RH, BSD, PvNP, Hodge, NS) will be audited for external dependencies.
2. Each external dependency will be attacked by the ORA with the three-step escalation: verify → excise → construct.
3. If verification succeeds, the dependency becomes an internally-certified theorem.
4. If excision succeeds, the dependency is no longer needed.
5. If construction succeeds, a local proof replaces the external dependency.
6. If all three fail, the dependency is flagged and the proof chain's confidence drops to reflect the risk.
7. The capacitor — the framework-tools file that the ORA consumes — is specialised per tier (Tier 1 CCM, Tier 2 Balaban, Tier 3 Bulatov-Zhuk, Tier 4 Bögli).
8. The output is a *bundle*: one verifiably-internal proof chain per Clay problem, with every external dependency either certified, excised, or explicitly flagged.

Philosophically, the Millennium Strategy is this: *a programme that claims to solve multiple Clay problems must be prepared to verify the external results it depends on, or those results are not a foundation — they are a liability.* The imposter-syndrome worry from April 11 — *the chains themselves wobble* — is answered by making the wobbly steps the programme's responsibility.
