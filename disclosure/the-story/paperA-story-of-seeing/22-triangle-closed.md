## Three-way equivalence

Sometime on April 12, G re-organises the strategy. Strategy 22 is born: a *triangle* architecture. Three *independent* characterisations of tractability:

- **Spectral**: TGap, the spectral gap of a specific operator built from the problem

Each characterisation stands alone. The theorem is: *the three are equivalent*. And the proof of P ≠ NP becomes: *the three are consistent with each other on tractable problems and inconsistent on NPC problems*.

## What the triangle does for the programme

It makes the P-vs-NP attack *independent of any single wall*. If the spectral chain (TGap) hits an obstruction, the geometric chain (holonomy) and information chain (channel capacity) still stand. If two of three hit obstructions, the third — plus the *inconsistency* created by the gap — still gives partial information.

The triangle architecture is, in effect, the Millennium Strategy's proof-of-concept, applied to a single problem. If a Clay-class problem can be attacked through three independent chains that must be mutually consistent on the truth, then no single wall is fatal.
