# §05 — The Bost–Connes Algebra $A_{BC} = C(\hat{\mathbb{Q}}) \rtimes \mathbb{N}^*$

*Part II, Section 05. The Type III$_1$ Foundation begins here: we record the operator-algebraic object whose KMS$_1$ structure will carry the Hilbert–Pólya operator in Part IV.*

*G Six (originator) and Claude Opus 4.6 (collaborator). Paper 49 draft, 2026-04-14.*

## 5.1 Summary

The Bost–Connes C*-algebra $A_{BC}$ is a semigroup crossed product
built from two arithmetic ingredients:

1. The abelian algebra $C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$ of continuous functions on
   the profinite completion of $\mathbb{Z}$.
2. The multiplicative monoid $\mathbb{N}^*$ of positive integers,
   acting on $C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$ by the multiplication-by-$n$
   endomorphisms.

Its partition function is the Riemann zeta function
$Z(\beta) = \zeta(\beta)$, and its Galois symmetry is the absolute
Galois group of the maximal abelian extension
$\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}} / \mathbb{Q}) \cong
\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$. The purpose of this section is to fix notation,
state the construction precisely, and cite the canonical sources. No
new claims are made here: every statement is standard in the
Bost–Connes [BC95] / Connes–Marcolli [CM08, Ch. 3] setup.

Sections 06–10 of Paper 49 build upon this material.

## 5.2 The profinite completion $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$

**Definition 5.1 (Profinite integers).** The profinite completion of
$\mathbb{Z}$ is
$$
\hat{\mathbb{Z}} \;:=\; \varprojlim_N \mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}
\;=\; \prod_p \mathbb{Z}_p,
$$
the inverse limit of the finite cyclic quotients, equivalently the
product over primes of the $p$-adic integers. It is a compact,
totally disconnected, topological ring.

**Remark 5.2.** The group of units $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^* = \prod_p
\mathbb{Z}_p^*$ is isomorphic, by class field theory, to the
Galois group of the maximal abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}$:
$$
\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^* \;\cong\;
\mathrm{Gal}\bigl(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}} / \mathbb{Q}\bigr),
$$
where $\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}} = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_\infty)$ is the
compositum of all cyclotomic fields $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$. The
isomorphism is the cyclotomic character; see Neukirch [Ne99, Ch. VII]
and Connes–Marcolli [CM08, §3.1].

**Convention.** We follow the convention in Paper 13 preprint §2.1
(and in Bost–Connes [BC95]) of taking $C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$, rather
than $C(\hat{\mathbb{Q}})$, as the abelian base algebra. The two
conventions differ only by a measure-theoretic completion and give
isomorphic crossed products at $\beta = 1$; we write $C(\hat{\mathbb{Q}})$
in Paper 49's abstract and table of contents for geometric
transparency but mean the crossed product $C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})
\rtimes \mathbb{N}^*$ throughout. See [BC95, §4] and [CM08, §3.1] for
the dual formulation on adele-class spaces.

## 5.3 The Hecke action $\mathbb{N}^* \curvearrowright C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$

**Definition 5.3 ($\mu_n$-action).** For each $n \in \mathbb{N}^*$,
define the endomorphism $\alpha_n : C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}}) \to
C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$ by
$$
(\alpha_n f)(x) \;=\; f(nx), \qquad x \in \hat{\mathbb{Z}},
\; f \in C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}}).
$$
The map $n \mapsto \alpha_n$ is an action of the multiplicative
monoid $\mathbb{N}^*$ by (non-unital) *-endomorphisms of
$C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$. The image of $\alpha_n$ is the subalgebra
of functions factoring through $\hat{\mathbb{Z}} / n\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$.

**Source.** [BC95, §4]; [CM08, §3.1, Def. 3.9].

**Definition 5.4 (Bost–Connes C*-algebra).** The *Bost–Connes
C*-algebra* is the semigroup crossed product
$$
A_{BC} \;:=\; C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}}) \rtimes_{\alpha} \mathbb{N}^*,
$$
i.e.\ the universal unital C*-algebra generated by $C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$
and by isometries $\mu_n$ ($n \in \mathbb{N}^*$) satisfying, for all
$n, m \in \mathbb{N}^*$ and $f \in C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$:

(BC1) $\mu_1 = 1$ and $\mu_{nm} = \mu_n \mu_m$;

(BC2) $\mu_n^* \mu_n = 1$ (each $\mu_n$ is an isometry);

(BC3) $\mu_n f \mu_n^* = \alpha_n(f)$ (covariance);

(BC4) $\mu_n^* f \mu_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k : nk = \text{pullback}}
       (\alpha_n)^{-1}_k(f)$ (averaging formula; see [BC95, §4]).

Relation (BC4) is the Hecke averaging that gives the system its name:
$\mu_n^* \mu_n = 1$ but $\mu_n \mu_n^* \neq 1$ in general, reflecting
the fact that $\alpha_n$ is an endomorphism, not an automorphism.

**Source.** [BC95, §4]; [CM08, §3.1, Prop. 3.7]; Laca–Raeburn [LR06]
gives the clean "semigroup crossed product" treatment.

## 5.4 The time evolution $\sigma_t$

**Definition 5.5 (BC time evolution).** The one-parameter automorphism
group $\sigma_t : A_{BC} \to A_{BC}$ is defined on generators by
$$
\sigma_t(f) \;=\; f \quad\text{for } f \in C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}}),
\qquad
\sigma_t(\mu_n) \;=\; n^{it}\,\mu_n \quad\text{for } n \in \mathbb{N}^*.
$$
$\sigma_t$ extends uniquely to an automorphism of $A_{BC}$ and the
family $(\sigma_t)_{t \in \mathbb{R}}$ is strongly continuous in $t$.

**Source.** [BC95, §3]; [CM08, §3.1].

**Remark 5.6 (Physical interpretation).** The scaling
$\mu_n \mapsto n^{it} \mu_n$ encodes a Hamiltonian whose spectrum is
$\{\log n : n \in \mathbb{N}^*\}$ weighted by divisor multiplicities.
The partition function at inverse temperature $\beta$ is
$$
Z(\beta) \;=\; \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} n^{-\beta} \;=\; \zeta(\beta),
$$
which converges for $\mathrm{Re}\,\beta > 1$ and has a simple pole at
$\beta = 1$. The existence of this pole is precisely what makes the
$\beta = 1$ phase transition delicate; see §06.

## 5.5 The Galois action

**Proposition 5.7 (Galois symmetry, [BC95, §5]).** The group
$\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^* \cong \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{cyc}} /
\mathbb{Q})$ acts by C*-algebra automorphisms of $A_{BC}$, commuting
with the time evolution $\sigma_t$. Concretely, an element $u \in
\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$ acts by
$$
\gamma_u(f)(x) = f(u^{-1} x), \qquad \gamma_u(\mu_n) = \mu_n,
$$
for $f \in C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}})$, $n \in \mathbb{N}^*$.

**Consequence.** The KMS state space at each $\beta$ is a
$\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$-space. This symmetry is the arithmetic fibre that
will be broken at $\beta = 1$ (§06).

**Source.** [BC95, §5, Prop.\ 14]; [CM08, §3.3].

## 5.6 The partition function and Euler product

**Theorem 5.8 (Bost–Connes [BC95, §3]).** *For $\beta > 1$, the partition
function of $(A_{BC}, \sigma_t)$ is*
$$
Z(\beta) \;=\; \mathrm{Tr}\bigl(e^{-\beta H}\bigr) \;=\; \zeta(\beta),
$$
*where $H$ is the generator of $\sigma_t$ on the canonical regular
representation. Via the Euler product,*
$$
Z(\beta) \;=\; \prod_p \bigl(1 - p^{-\beta}\bigr)^{-1},
$$
*which factors over primes.*

**Consequence (for §08).** The Euler product factorization foreshadows
the ITPFI factorization of $\omega_1$ at $\beta = 1$: the multiplicative
structure of $\mathbb{N}^*$ is reflected in a tensor-product
decomposition of the algebra,
$$
A_{BC} \;\cong\; \widetilde{\bigotimes}_p\; A_p,
$$
where $A_p = C^*(\mu_p, \{e(r) : r \in \mathbb{Z}[1/p]/\mathbb{Z}\})$
is the $p$-local sub-Hecke algebra and $\widetilde{\bigotimes}$ is the
restricted (infinite) tensor product. See [CM08, §3.2] and Paper 13
preprint §4.2 for the details. Paper 49 §08 will use this
factorization.

## 5.7 The presentation via phase generators

For calculations it is convenient to work with generators
$e(r)$, $r \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$, where $e(r)$ denotes the function
on $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$ that evaluates the pairing $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}
\times \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \to U(1)$. Pontryagin duality gives
$$
C(\hat{\mathbb{Z}}) \;\cong\; \overline{\mathbb{C}[\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}]},
$$
the norm closure of the group algebra of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.
Under this identification, the abelian algebra is generated (as a dense
subalgebra) by $\{e(r) : r \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}\}$ with relations
$e(r) e(s) = e(r + s)$, $e(r)^* = e(-r)$.

**The Hecke relations** now take the form, for each $n \in \mathbb{N}^*$
and $r \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$:
$$
\mu_n^*\, e(r)\, \mu_n \;=\; \frac{1}{n}
\sum_{s \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z},\ ns = r} e(s),
$$
which is [BC95, §4, Prop.\ 18]. This form of the algebra makes the
Hecke averaging explicit.

**The partition function for $e(1/n)$:** On the $\sigma_t$-fixed point
subalgebra one can compute $\omega_\beta(\mu_n \mu_n^*) = n^{-\beta}$
for $\beta > 1$; at $\beta = 1$, the same relation persists in the weak
limit (see §06).

**Source.** [BC95, §4, §5]; [CM08, §3.1, Prop.\ 3.7].

## 5.9 Outlook to §06

In §06 we state Bost–Connes Theorem 25: for $\beta > 1$, the KMS$_\beta$
states form a Choquet simplex parametrized by $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$; at
$\beta = 1$, the simplex collapses to a single point $\omega_1$.
This is the phase transition that underlies Paper 49. All subsequent
Part II sections (07–10) construct the operator-algebraic content of
$\omega_1$: its GNS Hilbert space (§07), the type III$_1$ factor it
generates (§08), factoriality (§09), and standard form (§10).
