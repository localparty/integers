# 3. Inflation (φ ≪ φ_min)

## 3.1 The Casimir Plateau

At early times, the dilaton is far from its minimum — the e-circle
is much larger than its equilibrium size. The potential is dominated
by the Casimir term:

    V(φ) ≈ V₀/φ⁴

For large `φ`, this is a slowly varying function — a nearly flat
plateau that naturally supports slow-roll inflation.

The initial displacement of `φ` from `φ_min` arises from the
compactification phase transition: when the 11D spacetime first
compactifies to `M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹`, the e-circle radius is
set by the initial conditions of the phase transition, generically
far from the Casimir-GW equilibrium. The dilaton starts on the
plateau and rolls toward the minimum — inflation is the slow
journey.

## 3.2 The Slow-Roll Parameters

For the full potential `V = V₀/φ⁴ + Aφ⁴(ln φ)²`, the slow-roll
parameters in the inflationary regime are computed in Paper 4
(§7.15.2). The Casimir flattening from the `V₀/φ⁴` term modifies
the pure power-law result, giving:

    n_s = 0.960–0.970   for V₀/A = 10⁻⁵–10⁻³

The Planck value `n_s = 0.965 ± 0.004` is reproduced for
`V₀/A ~ 10⁻⁴`.

The tensor-to-scalar ratio:

    r = 0.01–0.10   for V₀/A = 10⁻⁵–10⁻³

For `V₀/A ~ 10⁻⁴`: **r ≈ 0.03 ± 0.01** — at the current
BICEP/Keck 2σ boundary. CMB-S4 (σ(r) ≈ 0.001) will detect or
exclude this within the decade.

## 3.3 The End of Inflation

Inflation ends when the slow-roll parameter `ε` reaches 1 — when
the dilaton rolls off the plateau into the steep part of the
potential leading to the minimum. This occurs at:

    φ_end ~ (8M_Pl²/V₀)^{1/10} × φ_min

After `φ_end`, the dilaton enters the oscillation basin. The
transition from inflation to reheating is smooth — no separate
"waterfall" field or phase transition is needed. The same potential
that drives inflation also drives reheating.

## 3.4 What Paper 4 Established

The inflationary predictions (`n_s`, `r`, running) are derived in
Paper 4, §7.15. This section places them in temporal context: the
Casimir plateau IS the inflationary era. The e-circle was large,
the fifth dimension was macroscopic, and the slow roll toward
equilibrium is what we observe as the inflationary expansion.
