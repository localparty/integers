# 3. The Inflaton — Identification and Status

## 3.1 The Dilaton Is Not the Inflaton

The dilaton (e-circle modulus R) is **not** the inflaton. The
canonical slow-roll parameter for the Casimir potential V = c/R⁴
is ε = 32/3 ≈ 10.7 ≫ 1 (Appendix A, eq. A.2). The potential is
too steep in canonical field space to support slow-roll inflation.
This is a firm result, not a numerical accident.

The canonical kinetic term for the radion R from the 5D KK
reduction is:

    L_kin = (3M_Pl²)/(4R²) (∂R)²

Defining the canonical field σ = (√3 M_Pl/2) ln(R/R₀):

    V(σ) = c R₀⁻⁴ exp(−8σ/(√3 M_Pl))

This is an exponential potential in the canonical field. The
slow-roll parameter:

    ε = (M_Pl²/2)(V'/V)² = (M_Pl²/2)(8/(√3 M_Pl))² = 32/3 ≈ 10.7

**ε = 10.7 ≫ 1.** The Casimir potential V = c/R⁴ is far too steep
in canonical field space for slow-roll inflation. The dilaton cannot
be the inflaton. The confusion arose because V ∝ 1/φ⁴ looks like a
power-law potential in the non-canonical field φ, but the canonical
field transformation converts the power law into an exponential,
destroying the flatness.

## 3.2 The G₄ Flux Axion as Inflaton Candidate

The framework contains two additional moduli with independent
potentials: the S² radius r₂ and the CP² Kähler modulus r₃. These
moduli are stabilized at sub-Planckian values by G₄ flux — their
potentials are steep in the geometric direction (the moduli are
sub-Planckian: r₃/l₁₁ ≈ 0.003, placing them in the M-theory
strong-coupling regime where G₄ flux dominates). However, the
*phase* of the GVW superpotential — the G₄ flux axion a_G — is a
flat direction at tree level, protected by the shift symmetry
a_G → a_G + const.

The inflationary trajectory is along a_G (the axion monodromy
direction, Kim–Nilles–Peloso type), where V ~ μ³ a_G for large
field values. The inflationary scale μ is set by M₁₁ and the flux
quantum number. Slow-roll parameters in this scenario satisfy
ε ≪ 1 for a_G ≫ M_Pl, supporting inflation. Qualitative estimates
suggest n_s ~ 0.965–0.968 and r ~ 10⁻³ (consistent with Planck 2018
and BK18 constraints), but these values are not yet derived and are
not cited as predictions of this paper.

## 3.3 Status and Deferral to Paper 7

The computation of the G₄ axion potential (from the GVW superpotential
on CP² × S²), its canonical kinetic term (from the 11D → 4D
reduction), and the resulting slow-roll parameters (ε, η, n_s, r) is
in progress and will be presented in Paper 7 of this series.

No inflationary predictions (n_s, r values) are made in this paper.
The abstract and timeline (§12) do not cite quantitative inflationary
predictions. Either the G₄ axion inflation calculation will confirm
consistency with Planck 2018 constraints (Paper 7), or the inflaton
identification requires revision.

## 3.4 What This Paper Contributes on Inflation

Despite the inflaton identification being deferred to Paper 7, the
following are firmly established here:

- **The definitive elimination of the dilaton as inflaton**, with
  explicit calculation showing ε = 32/3 (§3.1 and Appendix A).
- **The identification of the G₄ flux axion** as the most natural
  inflaton candidate within the framework (§3.2).
- **The reheating and leptogenesis analysis** (§§4–5), which is
  independent of whether the inflaton is the dilaton or the G₄ axion,
  because both couple to the SM through gravitational (Planck-suppressed)
  channels of the same parametric form (see §4.0).
- **The dark energy prediction** w₀ = −1 (exact, from frozen dilaton),
  which is independent of the inflation model.
- **The Casimir potential V = +c/R⁴ is exact** to all perturbative
  orders (Epstein zeta zeros, Paper 1 Appendix K).
- **The S² and CP² moduli are dynamically stabilized** by their
  nonvanishing spectral zeta values (Paper 4, §9.1, Appendix C).

## 3.5 What Remains Open

The primary open problem for the inflationary sector is: what are
the precise slow-roll parameters for the G₄ flux axion potential
on CP² × S²? Specifically: (1) the canonical kinetic term from the
full 11D → 4D reduction; (2) the GVW superpotential evaluated on the
compactification; (3) the resulting ε, η, n_s, r. This is the
subject of Paper 7.
