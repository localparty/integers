# 8. The Electroweak Phase Transition

*This section places Paper 4 §7.12 in its cosmological context.*

## 8.1 The Event

At `T_c ~ 1 TeV` (`t ~ 10⁻¹² s`), the universe undergoes the
electroweak phase transition. In the Standard Model, this is a
smooth crossover — no drama, no gravitational waves.

In the e-dimension framework, it is a **first-order phase
transition** — the most violent event between inflation and the
present day.

## 8.2 What Happens Geometrically

The Higgs field is the Wilson line angle `θ_H` — the tilt between
the `S²` (weak) and `S¹` (electromagnetic) compact dimensions
(Paper 4, §6.2).

Before the transition (`T > T_c`): `θ_H = 0`. The two compact
dimensions are orthogonal. The full `SU(2) × U(1)` gauge symmetry
is unbroken. All fermions are massless.

During the transition (`T ≈ T_c`): The Casimir potential `V(θ_H)`
develops a second minimum at `θ_H = θ₀ ≠ 0` (Paper 4, §6.5). The
universe supercools in the symmetric phase. Bubbles of the broken
phase nucleate. The bubble walls expand at nearly the speed of
light, colliding and merging.

After the transition (`T < T_c`): `θ_H = θ₀`. The `S²` and `S¹`
are tilted. The W and Z bosons are massive. Fermions acquire mass
from their coupling to the Wilson line. Electroweak symmetry is
broken.

**Electroweak symmetry breaking is a cosmological event — the
moment when two compact dimensions tilted relative to each other.**

## 8.3 The Gravitational Wave Signal

The first-order transition produces gravitational waves through
three mechanisms: bubble wall collisions, sound waves in the
plasma, and turbulence.

From Paper 4, §7.12:

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Critical temperature | `T_c ~ 1 TeV` |
| Transition strength | `α ~ 0.1–1` |
| Peak frequency (today) | `f ~ 1–10 mHz` |
| Peak amplitude | `h²Ω_GW ~ 10⁻¹³–10⁻¹¹` |

**This is squarely in the LISA band** (0.1 mHz – 100 mHz). LISA
(launch ~2035) will search for exactly this signal.

The Standard Model predicts NO gravitational wave signal from the
EWPT (because the SM transition is a crossover). The framework
predicts a DETECTABLE signal. This is a binary test.

## 8.4 Simultaneous Events on the Hidden Brane

At the same cosmic time, the hidden brane undergoes its OWN
electroweak phase transition at `T'_c = ξ T_c ~ 490 GeV`. The
mirror W' and Z' bosons acquire mass. Mirror fermions become
massive.

The mirror EWPT also produces gravitational waves — at a slightly
lower frequency (because `T'_c < T_c`) and a similar amplitude.
The combined signal from BOTH branes may produce a distinctive
double-peak in the LISA band — a signature unique to the two-brane
framework.
