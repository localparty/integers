# 9. Matter-Radiation Equality and Structure Formation

## 9.1 The Dark Matter Takeover

At `T ~ 0.8 eV` (`t ~ 50,000 yr`), the matter energy density
overtakes radiation. The dark matter — mirror protons and neutrons
on the hidden brane (Paper 4, §7.10) — begins to dominate the
cosmic expansion.

The dark matter density:

    Ω_DM = 1/ξ² × Ω_b = 1/(0.49)² × 0.049 = 4.16 × 0.049 = 0.204

(Paper 2: `Ω_DM h² = 0.117`, giving `Ω_DM ≈ 0.26` for `h = 0.674`)

## 9.2 Structure Formation

Mirror dark matter behaves as cold dark matter for structure
formation: the mirror protons and neutrons are non-relativistic at
matter-radiation equality and cluster gravitationally.

The framework's structure formation predictions (Paper 2):
- `S8 = 0.753` (from the CAMB computation with ΔN_eff and evolving w(z))
- This matches all weak lensing surveys (DES Y3: 0.776 ± 0.017,
  KiDS-1000: 0.759 ± 0.024, HSC Y3: 0.763 ± 0.020)
- Resolves the 4σ S8 tension with Planck ΛCDM (S8 = 0.832)

The dilaton is still frozen at `φ_min` during this epoch — dark
energy is negligible, and the expansion is matter-dominated. The
e-circle sits quietly at its equilibrium size.

## 9.3 Recombination

At `T ~ 0.3 eV` (`t ~ 380,000 yr`), hydrogen atoms form on the
visible brane. The CMB is released. The angular scale `θ*` is
matched to within 0.5–6.6 arcseconds of Planck's measurement
(Paper 2, all three scenarios).

On the hidden brane, mirror recombination occurs at
`T' = ξ × 0.3 eV ≈ 0.15 eV` — slightly later. Mirror hydrogen
forms. The mirror CMB is released — invisible to us, but
gravitationally present.
