**Paper 6 of the 5D e-Dimension Framework**

## Abstract

We present the complete thermal history of the universe within
the 11D e-dimension framework — from inflation at `10⁻³⁶` seconds
to dark energy domination at `10¹⁰` years — driven by a single
geometric object: the dilaton `φ`, the modulus of the e-circle
radius established in Paper 1.

The dilaton potential `V(R) = +c/R⁴` (c > 0) — the Casimir energy
of bulk fields on the e-circle, positive because the fermionic KK
contribution dominates the bosonic contribution (§2.1a), exact to
all perturbative orders by the Epstein zeta zero theorem (Paper 1,
Appendix K; §2.1) — has no stationary point. It is not stabilized
by a Goldberger-Wise term: all perturbative corrections beyond the
one-loop Casimir sum vanish identically. The dilaton is kinematically
frozen at its post-inflationary value by Hubble friction, making the
Casimir energy a true cosmological constant (`w = −1` to precision
`ΔR/R₀ ~ 3×10⁻³⁰` per Hubble time).

The inflaton is identified as a modulus of the CP² × S² compactification
(the G₄ flux axion); the complete slow-roll calculation is presented in
Paper 7. The dilaton (e-circle modulus) is explicitly shown to be
incompatible with slow-roll inflation (`ε = 32/3 ≫ 1`), resolving an
earlier misidentification in the series.

This paper makes four contributions unique to the series.

First, we compute the reheating temperature `T_reh ≈ 5 × 10⁹ GeV`
from the inflaton's gravitational decay width (§4). The inflaton
couples to SM fields gravitationally, with the same parametric form
regardless of whether it is the dilaton or the G₄ axion. This is
below the bulk neutrino mass `M_N ~ 10¹⁴ GeV`, requiring a
non-thermal leptogenesis pathway: the inflaton decays to bulk
neutrino pairs through off-shell production, which then decay to
leptons on both branes, producing `η_B ≈ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰` through the
Z₃ CP phases of Paper 4 (§5).

Second, we explain the origin of the brane temperature asymmetry
`ξ = T_hidden/T_visible ~ O(0.5)`. During reheating, the inflaton
couples to both branes through gravitational channels. This paper
identifies the mechanism and demonstrates why `ξ ~ O(0.3–0.9)` is
a natural geometric prediction (§6). The precise value `ξ = 0.49`
is determined in Paper 2 from the Ω_DM/Ω_b observational ratio,
not derived here. The intermediate step — from ξ ~ 0.79 (bulk
neutrino decays) to ξ = 0.49 (after phase transitions) — requires
a full two-sector Boltzmann simulation that is deferred to future
work.

Third, we place the electroweak phase transition (Paper 4, §7.12)
in its cosmological context: at `T_c ~ 1 TeV`, the gauge-Higgs
Wilson line transitions from `θ_H = 0` (unbroken) to `θ_H = θ₀`
(broken) through first-order bubble nucleation driven by KK gauge
boson thermal loops (§8), producing a gravitational wave signal
in the LISA band at 1–10 mHz.

Fourth, we trace the dilaton's far future: the e-circle radius is
kinematically frozen for approximately 50 Hubble times (~700 Gyr),
after which the growing mode of the linear instability analysis
becomes significant and decompactification begins. On any
observationally accessible timescale, the universe is in a
quasi-stable de Sitter phase with `H → H_∞ = H₀√Ω_Λ ~ 0.83 H₀`
and `w = −1` (§11).

The complete timeline — inflation, reheating, leptogenesis,
electroweak symmetry breaking, BBN, mirror BBN, recombination,
structure formation, dark energy — is one geometric story. One
compact dimension, one field, one potential, every epoch.
