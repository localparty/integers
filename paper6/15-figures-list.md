# Paper 6 — Section 15: Figures List

## Figure 1: The One-Page Timeline (diagram version)

A horizontal timeline spanning from `10⁻³⁶ s` to `10¹⁰ yr`, with
logarithmic time axis. Each epoch is marked by its temperature, the
dilaton state (`φ` on plateau, oscillating, frozen), and the key
physics event. Epochs shown:

- Inflation (`10⁻³⁶ s`, `10¹⁵ GeV`, Casimir plateau)
- Reheating (`10⁻³² s`, `5 × 10⁹ GeV`, dilaton decay)
- Leptogenesis (`10⁻³¹ s`, `10⁹ GeV`, non-thermal `φ → N_i N_i`)
- EWPT (`10⁻¹² s`, `1 TeV`, first-order, LISA signal)
- QCD confinement (`10⁻⁵ s`, `155 MeV`)
- BBN (`1 s`, `1 MeV`, standard nucleosynthesis)
- Mirror BBN (`3 s`, `0.49 MeV`, hidden brane)
- Recombination (`3.8 × 10⁵ yr`, `0.3 eV`)
- Dark energy domination (`10¹⁰ yr`, frozen Casimir tail)

This is the visual version of the table in §12. A secondary axis
shows the dilaton potential `V(φ)`, indicating where each epoch
sits on the potential curve.

## Figure 2: The Dilaton Potential

Plot of `V(φ) = V₀/φ⁴` in the physical field `σ`, showing three
regimes:

- **Plateau** (large `φ`): the Casimir plateau region where slow-roll
  inflation occurs. Label: `ε ≪ 1`, `n_s = 0.967 (G₄ axion hilltop, Paper 7 §5)`.
- **Steep slope** (intermediate `φ`): the region where the dilaton
  oscillates and decays, driving reheating. Label: `T_reh ≈ 5 × 10⁹ GeV`.
- **Frozen tail** (small `φ`, post-inflationary): the dilaton is
  frozen by Hubble friction at `ε ≈ 10⁻⁵²`, giving `w = −1`. Label:
  dark energy (Casimir cosmological constant).

Annotate the transition points between regimes. Show that the
potential is bounded below — no Big Rip.

## Figure 3: Mirror Sector Thermal History

Two parallel vertical temperature axes — visible brane temperature
`T` (left) and hidden brane temperature `T' = ξT` with `ξ = 0.49`
(right) — plotted against cosmic time (horizontal, logarithmic).

Key features shown:

- **Reheating**: both branes heated, but hidden brane receives
  suppressed energy (`ξ = 0.49` from warp factor).
- **BBN** (`T ~ 1 MeV`): standard nucleosynthesis on visible brane.
  Hidden brane at `T' ~ 0.49 MeV`, below its BBN threshold.
- **Mirror BBN** (`T' ~ 0.49 MeV`): nucleosynthesis on hidden brane
  proceeds at lower temperature.
- **Mirror e± annihilation** (`T' ~ 0.43 MeV`): mirror electron-
  positron pairs annihilate, heating the mirror photon bath.
  Show the entropy transfer and the resulting `ΔN_eff` contribution.
- **Recombination**: visible brane at `T ~ 0.3 eV`, hidden brane
  at `T' ~ 0.15 eV`.

Shade the region where `ΔN_eff` builds up from `0` to the final
value `N_eff = 3.34`. Label the Planck constraint `N_eff = 3.04 ± 0.18`
for comparison.
