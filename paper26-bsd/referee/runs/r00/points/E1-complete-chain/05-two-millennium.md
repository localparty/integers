## Point E1(e): The "Two Millennium Problems" Claim

**The question:**
Is the "two Millennium Problems from one bridge family" framing honest or marketing?

**Finding:**
The claim is that the same bridge family construction proves both RH (Paper 13) and BSD for CM curves (Paper 26). This is accurate in the following sense:
- The bridge family (four cocycles at k = 2, 3, 4, 6) is the same structural tool.
- The proof pattern (BC -> Nelson -> Meyer -> bridges -> transcendence -> delta = 0) is the same.
- The transcendence step uses the same class of theorems (Gelfond-Schneider/Baker).

The claim is misleading in the following sense:
- BSD is proved only for CM curves of rank 0 (and possibly vacuously for rank 1), not for all elliptic curves. The full BSD Millennium Problem requires all curves over Q and all ranks.
- Under the Clay rules, a partial result "may receive a small prize from other CMI funds" (section 5(c)(ii)) but does NOT qualify for the full $1M prize.
- The preprint is honest about this limitation (Section 15, Section 17.3 Negative 4).

Assessment: the "two Millennium Problems" framing is accurate at the level of methodology (one tool applied to two problems) but overstates the scope of the BSD result (a partial result, not the full conjecture). The preprint's own Section 15 provides honest scope limits.

**Verdict for this sub-question:**
CLOSABLE GAP -- The framing is technically accurate but risks overstatement. The preprint should consider replacing "two Millennium Problems" with "two Millennium Problem domains" or similar, to avoid implying the full BSD is solved. The honest scope limits in Section 15 partially mitigate this.
