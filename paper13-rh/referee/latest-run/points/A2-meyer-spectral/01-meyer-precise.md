# A2.01: Meyer's Precise Statement

Meyer (2005, Duke Math. J. 127) constructs a nuclear Frechet space S adapted to the BC system and proves that the non-trivial zeros of zeta appear as DISTRIBUTIONAL eigenvalues of the scaling operator acting on the Gel'fand triple S subset H_1 subset S'. The eigenstates phi_n are elements of S' (tempered distributions), not elements of H_1.

The paper correctly states this in Appendix B.3: "for each gamma_n, there exists a generalised eigenvector phi_n in S' satisfying T_BC phi_n = gamma_n phi_n in the distributional sense."

**What Meyer does NOT prove:**
1. That phi_n is in H_1 (it is not -- it is a distribution)
2. That gamma_n is an eigenvalue of a self-adjoint operator on H_1
3. That T_BC has compact resolvent
4. That the spectrum of any self-adjoint extension of T_BC equals {gamma_n}

**Verdict: Meyer's theorem is correctly cited.** The distributional nature is acknowledged.
