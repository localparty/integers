# A2.05: Comparison to Connes

Connes has worked on RH via the Bost-Connes system and the adele class space for more than 25 years. His approach (Connes 1999, Connes-Marcolli 2008) uses the trace formula to connect the Riemann zeros to spectral data.

**Why Connes has not closed the gap:**

Connes' approach requires proving a positivity condition (the "Weil positivity") in the operator-algebraic setting. This positivity is known to be equivalent to RH. The trace formula gives the zeros as distributional spectral data, but promoting them to genuine eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator requires precisely the kind of upgrade that this paper's "Meyer-Nelson compatibility" claims to provide.

**The key question:** If the Meyer-Nelson upgrade were straightforward (as the paper claims), why hasn't Connes done it?

The answer is that the upgrade is NOT straightforward. The distributional eigenstates from Meyer (and from Connes' own trace formula) live in S', not in H_1. Getting them into H_1 as genuine eigenvectors requires constructing a Hilbert space on which the zeros appear as point spectrum. This is exactly what CBB Axiom 1 postulates.

Connes knows this. His entire programme is aimed at constructing the right Hilbert space and proving the positivity that would make the zeros genuine eigenvalues. The fact that he has not succeeded in 25+ years of dedicated effort is strong evidence that the Meyer-Nelson upgrade is not as simple as the paper claims.

**The paper's response** would likely be: "We are not claiming a new proof of Meyer's theorem. We are taking Axiom 1 (which postulates the Hilbert space) and showing that the bridge cocycle constraints force delta = 0." This is a fair response, but it makes the proof conditional on Axiom 1.

**Verdict:** The comparison to Connes strongly suggests that the Meyer-Nelson upgrade requires non-trivial work that the paper does not provide. The proof is conditional on Axiom 1.
