# A1.03: The Scaling Operator T_BC

**CRITICAL ISSUE.** The paper conflates two different objects:

1. The "scaling operator" T_BC from the Bost-Connes dynamics. In the standard BC system, the natural scaling operator comes from the time evolution sigma_t. The generator of this one-parameter group acts on the GNS space H_1.

2. The operator R-hat from CBB Axiom 1, which is postulated to be an unbounded positive operator with compact resolvent and log-spectrum {gamma_n * pi^2/2}.

These are NOT the same object. The BC dynamics gives an operator whose spectrum is related to log(n) for natural numbers n, not to the Riemann zeros. The connection between the BC dynamics and the Riemann zeros is through Meyer's distributional spectral inclusion, which gives the zeros as distributional eigenvalues, not as genuine eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator with compact resolvent.

The paper's T_BC is defined in Proposition 8.1 as "T_BC e_n = gamma_n e_n" on the basis {e_n} of H_R. But H_R is defined by Axiom 1 as having this basis. This is a DEFINITION, not a construction from the BC algebra. The operator is defined to have the desired spectrum.

**Verdict: GAP.** The operator T_BC is defined by fiat (via Axiom 1), not constructed from the BC algebra. The claim that it arises from "BC first principles" is not substantiated.
