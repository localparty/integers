## Overall

**Paper 13 is honest about depending on CCM, but over-states
the independence of Layers 2-6 and under-frames the theorem
statement's conditionality.**

Specifically:

1. **Theorem 1.1 should be conditional.** As written, it claims
   RH unconditionally. The correct statement is "assuming CCM
   2025, RH holds". This is a substantive framing change, not
   cosmetic.

2. **Layers 2-6 are not independently 9-10/10.** Several have
   concrete rigor gaps (Section 10.4 exposition, Theorem 7.1
   proof, KLMN closability argument, Slepian-limit heuristic,
   Teschl Galerkin applicability). The paper's self-rating
   underestimates these.

3. **Scenario analysis is missing.** A mathematically careful
   paper would spell out what happens if specific CCM results
   fail (my scenarios A-E in D2.02). Paper 13 does not.

4. **The Connes comparison is accurate.** Paper 13 correctly
   identifies its work as building on Connes' 2025 CCM phase,
   not an independent route. The "25-year obstacle" framing is
   slightly dramatized but not wrong.

## What would close this point

1. Restate Theorem 1.1 as conditional on CCM. Mention in abstract.

2. Add a section on "what happens if CCM has an error" with
   scenario analysis.

3. Re-rate Layers 2-6 with the concrete gaps acknowledged.

4. If possible, independently reconstruct the CCM results on
   E_N^+ with the even-sector modification — this would close
   scenario B.

**Confidence on the honesty of the honest-accounting section:
7/10** (honest in direction, over-rated in specifics).
