# HZ3 — Zeros of ξ̂_N = eigenvalues of D_N

**Claim:** CCM Theorem 5.10(iii).

**Pass criterion:** CCM 5.10(iii) correctly cited; even-sector restriction preserves identification.

**Finding:**
- CCM 5.10(iii) is correctly cited.
- The explicit formula ξ̂_N(z) = 2L^{−1/2} sin(zL/2) · Σ_j
  ξ_j/(z − 2π j/L) has structured real zeros (sine factor) and
  rational-function zeros. The identification with eigenvalues
  refers to the rational-factor roots inside the principal
  strip.
- **The "real-zero property" of ξ̂_N in ℂ** — that every
  complex zero of ξ̂_N is real — follows from this structure
  plus self-adjointness of D_N. **Paper 13 never states this as
  a named lemma**, even though it is the critical property for
  the Hurwitz argument to exclude off-line zeros.
- Even-sector compatibility of CCM 5.10(iii) is asserted (CCM1)
  but not proved.

**Status:** PASS on CCM authority, with the caveat that the
real-zero property must be explicitly stated.

**Confidence:** 7/10. See points/C1-hurwitz/02-holomorphicity.md.
