# IT3 — Form convergence from state convergence

**Claim:** ω_1^{≤P_N} → ω_1 weak-* implies entry-by-entry
convergence of Weil quadratic form.

**Pass criterion:** implication is proved, not assumed.

**Finding:** Paper 13 Proposition 4.3 gives entry-by-entry
stabilization of matrix elements ⟨e_m, Q_N e_n⟩ once N is large
enough that P_N exceeds the max prime factor of mn. This is
trivially true at the algebraic level (stabilization in the
direct limit).

**BUT:** entry-by-entry stabilization is **not** form convergence
in any analytically useful sense. It gives Teschl Lemma 2.7
hypothesis (i) on the algebraic direct limit (pointwise
convergence on a dense subspace). It does **not** give the
relative form bound (hypothesis (ii)), which must come separately
from CF decay.

Paper 13's exposition blurs this: it writes "form convergence
from state convergence" in Section 4.7 as if ITPFI alone
delivers form convergence, when in reality ITPFI delivers one
piece and CF decay delivers another.

**Status:** WEAKENED as stated (exposition issue, not fatal).

**Confidence:** 7/10.
