This is L^2 on the multiplicative group R_+^*, truncated to [lambda^{-1}, lambda].

The BC GNS space: H_1 with basis {|n>}_{n=1}^{infty} (one vector per positive integer).

The Mellin transform M: L^2(R_+^*, d*u) -> L^2(R) maps
D_log = -iu d/du to multiplication by t.

**The connection map E (CCM eq 7.2):**

    E(f)(u) = u^{1/2} sum_{n=1}^{infty} f(nu)

This is exactly the Poisson-type summation that converts L^2(R_+^*) functions
into arithmetic objects. CCM use it to define k_lambda = E(h_lambda).

**Crucial observation:** The map E involves u^{1/2} * sum_n f(nu), which
samples f at integer multiples of u. The factor u^{1/2} is the Delta^{1/2}
shift (eq 3.6) that converts between the Mellin and Fourier pictures.
The sum over integers IS the arithmetic content.

## 8. The honest obstruction

**The fundamental problem:** CCM's xi_lambda is determined by a VARIATIONAL
principle (minimize QW_lambda on E_N). The ITPFI vector is determined by
a STATE-THEORETIC principle (unique KMS at beta=1). These are different
optimization problems.

The Weil quadratic form QW_lambda involves:
- W_{0,2}: the rank-two "global" contribution
- W_R: the archimedean contribution (digamma functions, hypergeometrics)
- sum_p W_p: the non-archimedean contributions

The ITPFI state omega_1 encodes ONLY the Euler product (the sum_p W_p part).
It does NOT encode W_{0,2} or W_R. So the ITPFI vector cannot be the
eigenvector of T, because T includes archimedean and global terms that the
ITPFI structure does not see.

**More precisely:** The Weil matrix tau_{n,m} decomposes as (Section 4):

    tau = tau^{(0,2)} + tau^{(R)} + sum_p tau^{(p)}

The ITPFI contributes to sum_p tau^{(p)} only. The eigenvector of the full
matrix tau is NOT the eigenvector of the sub-matrix sum_p tau^{(p)}.

## 9. What DOES work: the asymptotic regime

In the limit lambda -> infinity:
- The archimedean term W_R(V_n, V_m) contributes O(log L) to diagonal entries
  (eq 4.4, the digamma growth).
- The prime contributions sum_p W_p grow as the Euler product.
- The W_{0,2} term is rank-two and bounded.

The minimal eigenvector xi_lambda is dominated by the prime contributions
for large lambda. In this regime, the ITPFI structure becomes the leading
contribution, and the identification

    xi_lambda ~ c * P_{E_N}(Omega_1^{<=P})  +  archimedean correction

may hold asymptotically, with the correction vanishing relative to the
main term.

**This would suffice for Step B** if the correction can be bounded:

    ||xi_lambda - c * P_{E_N}(Omega_1^{<=P})||_{E_N} = O(something -> 0)

## 10. Assessment of the Steps

### Step A (even-simple): ITPFI can contribute but does not close it

KMS uniqueness of omega_1 gives uniqueness of the ITPFI vector.
But the eigenvector of QW_lambda is NOT pure ITPFI -- it includes
archimedean contributions. KMS uniqueness alone does not force the
even-simple property of the FULL Weil eigenvector.

**However:** The functional equation symmetry zeta(s) = chi(s) zeta(1-s)

symmetry, combined with the expected simplicity of the ground state,
IS the even-simple condition. The ITPFI does inherit this symmetry
(omega_1^p is invariant under the involution p^k -> p^{-k} in the
modular sense). So the even property is natural but the simplicity
is harder.

Step A status: **PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. Even property natural; simplicity
requires a Perron-Frobenius type argument on the full Weil matrix.**

The ITPFI convergence omega_1^{<=P} -> omega_1 gives:

**The gap:** This gives k_lambda ~ ITPFI projection and ITPFI projection ~ xi_lambda

Step B status: **ARCHITECTURE PRESENT. Rate control missing.**

## 11. Verdict

### Is xi_lambda = truncated zeta?

**No, not literally.** xi_lambda is the minimal eigenvector of the Weil
quadratic form, which includes archimedean and global contributions beyond
the pure Euler product. The truncated zeta (ITPFI vector) is the Euler
product part only.

**But asymptotically:** as lambda -> infinity, the eigenvector is increasingly
dominated by the prime contributions, and the ITPFI vector provides the
correct leading-order approximation. The identification holds in the limit,
not at finite truncation.

### Does the identification hold?

**As an asymptotic statement:** plausibly yes, but PROVING it requires
bounding the archimedean correction. This is a non-trivial analytic
estimate involving the interplay of W_R with sum_p W_p in the
eigenvector perturbation theory.

### Do Steps A+B close?

**Not yet.** The ITPFI framework provides:
- The correct Euler product structure for Step B
- The KMS uniqueness that motivates Step A
- The convergence architecture

But it does not provide:
- A proof that QW_lambda is even-simple (Step A)
- A quantitative bound on ||xi_lambda - c * k_lambda|| (Step B)

### Is RH proved?

**No.** The ITPFI identification is a promising structural insight that
illuminates WHY the CCM construction works (the Euler product IS the
ITPFI factorization), but it does not close either missing step rigorously.

The honest path forward:
1. Prove even-simplicity of QW_lambda via Perron-Frobenius + functional
   equation symmetry (Step A).
2. Bound the archimedean correction to xi_lambda using ITPFI + the
   explicit formulas for W_R in Section 4.3 (Step B).
3. Both require new analytic estimates, not just structural identification.
