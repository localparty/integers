# research/03-dir3 -- What Does Integers Add That Connes Doesn't Have?

*Date: 2026-04-10. Spectral Realisation Cycle 3, Direction 3.*

## 1. The question

Connes has the trace formula and the BC system but cannot close
spectral realisation (25 years). What SPECIFIC tools from Integers
might bridge the gap?

## 2. Candidate (a): Bridge family's Frobenius structure

**What it gives:** Explicit spectral data at four specific primes.
The bridge at (p, N, k) provides:

- k=2 at (2,7): CP structure (trivial)
- k=3 at (5,13): Koide Q = 2/3, three generations
- k=4 at (3,13): alpha_PS^{-1} = 17 exactly
- k=6 at (7,19): Cabibbo angle = 56/(57*sqrt(19))

**How it constrains the spectral measure beyond Connes:**

The bridge family gives FINITE algebraic relations among specific
gamma_n values. Connes' trace formula is a distributional identity;
the bridge family gives POINTWISE identities.

For example: the Koide relation Q = 2/3 constrains the masses
(gamma_a + gamma_b + gamma_c)^2 / (gamma_a^2 + gamma_b^2 + gamma_c^2)
= 2/3 for the specific gamma_n entering the lepton formulas.

**Premise check:** Does this distinguish spec = {gamma_n} from
spec = {gamma_n} union {extra}?

PARTIALLY. An extra eigenvalue lambda enters the subfactor index
calculation. The Jones index [M:N] = k is an INTEGER. If lambda
contributes to the subfactor, [M:N] would shift from an integer.
However: the bridge theorem at k=3 is proved for the specific
Hecke-Brauer structure, not as a constraint on ALL eigenvalues.
An extra eigenvalue could in principle decouple from the bridge
and leave the Jones index unchanged.

**Verdict:** The bridge family constrains the ALGEBRAIC structure
(which eigenvalues participate in which relations) but does not
exclude phantom eigenvalues that decouple from all bridges.
PARTIAL constraint, not a proof.

## 3. Candidate (b): ITPFI factorization

**What it gives:** omega_1 = tensor product over primes p of
omega_1^(p), the infinite tensor product of type I factors
(Araki-Woods construction).

M = pi_1(A_BC)" = ITPFI factor of type III_1

The spectral measure decomposes:
    mu = convolution of mu_p over all primes p

Each mu_p is the spectral measure of T_BC restricted to the
p-primary component.

**How it constrains beyond Connes:**

Connes knows the ITPFI structure abstractly. Integers adds:
- The SPECIFIC weights lambda_p at each prime (from the BC
  Euler factors)
- The prime-by-prime matching to bridge data
- The 36-formula consistency as a rigidity check

The ITPFI decomposition says: the spectral measure is a PRODUCT
measure. Each factor mu_p has a specific form determined by the
p-adic valuation. If an extra eigenvalue lambda exists, it must
appear in EVERY mu_p factor simultaneously (because the product
is over ALL primes). This is a strong constraint.

**Premise check:** Does this distinguish spec = {gamma_n} from
spec = {gamma_n} union {extra}?

MAYBE. If the product structure forces each mu_p to have a specific
discrete form, the product could be forced to be discrete. But:
the product of discrete measures can have continuous support
(central limit theorem). The ITPFI product of type I factors
gives type III_1, which HAS continuous Connes spectrum.

**Verdict:** The ITPFI structure is the RIGHT framework, but the
product-of-discrete-giving-continuous phenomenon means it doesn't
automatically force discrete spectrum. It constrains the spectral
measure prime-by-prime but does not exclude continuous spectrum in
the product.

## 4. Candidate (c): The 36 predictions as finite rigidity

**What it gives:** 36 closed-form expressions in {gamma_n, pi,
gamma_E, zeta(2), ...} matching measured values to high precision.

**How it constrains beyond Connes:**

Connes has NO predictions. He has the trace formula (distributional
identity) but no closed-form expressions for physical constants.
Integers has 36 such expressions, of which 33 are within
experimental error (P < 10^{-34} combined).

Each prediction is a RELATION: f_i(gamma_{n_1}, ..., gamma_{n_k}) = m_i.
An extra eigenvalue lambda does NOT enter these relations because
the formulas use SPECIFIC gamma_n, not sums over ALL eigenvalues.

**Premise check:** Does this distinguish spec = {gamma_n} from
spec = {gamma_n} union {extra}?

NO (for individual-gamma formulas). The 36 predictions use matrix
elements <n|L_hat|n> = gamma_n * pi^2/2 for SPECIFIC n. An extra
eigenvalue at lambda adds a state |lambda> but does NOT change
<n|L_hat|n> for any existing n. The predictions are robust to
extra spectrum -- they don't detect it.

The only predictions that could detect extra spectrum are TRACE
formulas (sum over ALL eigenvalues). But the 36 predictions are
NOT trace formulas -- they are individual matrix elements.

**Counter-argument (weak):** If the operator dictionary is COMPLETE
(all matrix elements of L_hat are accounted for by the 36 formulas),
then an extra state |lambda> would need to appear SOMEWHERE in the
dictionary. If it appears nowhere, maybe it doesn't exist?

This is the anti-fine-tuning argument (P < 10^{-34}) rephrased.
It's evidence, not proof. An extra eigenvalue could exist in a
sector that the 36 formulas don't probe.

**Verdict:** The 36 predictions provide overwhelming EVIDENCE
against extra spectrum (via anti-fine-tuning) but NOT PROOF.
They cannot distinguish spec = {gamma_n} from spec = {gamma_n}
union {lambda} where lambda decouples from all 36 formulas.

## 5. Candidate (d): Bridge cocycles as algebraic constraints

**What it gives:** The cocycles beta_k in H^2(Z/kZ, U(1)) = Z/kZ
are discrete algebraic invariants attached to the BC algebra at
specific (p, N) pairs.

**How it constrains beyond Connes:**

The cocycles are STRUCTURE of the BC algebra that Connes doesn't
use (he uses the trace formula, not the Brauer group). The cocycle
values 1/k mod Z are integer-rational and constrained by:
- The Frobenius element Frob_p in Gal(Q(zeta_N)/Q)
- The carry damping 1 - 1/(k*N)
- The level-jump rigidity (Conjecture 3, now PROVED)

An off-line zero perturbs the Euler factor at each prime, which
perturbs the Frobenius data. But (Cycle 1 lesson) the cohomology
CLASS is topologically protected and cannot detect continuous
perturbation. The cocycles constrain the algebraic structure of
T_BC but not its spectral type.

**Premise check:** Same coboundary gap as v1. DEAD.

**Verdict:** DEAD. The coboundary gap (Cycle 1) kills this
direction. Bridge cocycles constrain algebra, not spectrum.

## 7. The honest assessment

Integers provides three things Connes doesn't have:

1. **Statistical evidence** (P < 10^{-34}) -- but evidence is not proof
2. **Algebraic relations** (bridge family) -- but they don't exclude
   decoupled phantom eigenvalues
3. **Product structure** (ITPFI) -- the most promising, but the
   product-giving-continuous issue is unresolved

The STRUCTURAL gap is this: Integers works with INDIVIDUAL matrix
elements of L_hat, while spectral realisation requires a statement
about the TOTALITY of the spectrum. No finite number of individual
matrix elements can determine the spectral type.

## 8. What WOULD close the gap

A tool that constrains the TOTAL spectrum, not just individual
eigenvalues. Candidates:
- A trace identity that is BOTH computable from the BC side AND
  sensitive to extra spectrum
- A completeness relation (resolution of identity) from the
  Meyer eigenstates
- A RAGE-type dynamical argument that uses the BC time evolution
  to characterize the spectral type

These are Angles 1-5 from Cycle 2. Integers does not directly
provide them, but the ITPFI structure (Candidate b) might be
the right framework for a RAGE argument.
