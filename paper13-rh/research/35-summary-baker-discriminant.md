# Research 35 Summary — Lead E: Baker + Discriminant

*Status: COMPLETED (scoping note)*
*Type: Non-lead for general N; possible for N=2,3*
*Feasibility: 3/10 general, 5/10 small N*
*Date: 2026-04-10*

---

## Result

The correct object is the resultant Res(det(B−λI), det(M_a−λI)),
not the discriminant. Vanishes iff spectra overlap. Baker controls
linear forms in logarithms; the resultant is a polynomial — Baker
doesn't directly apply. Need algebraic independence (Nesterenko).

For N=2,3: the resultant can be computed explicitly in
{log 2, log 3, π, γ_E}. Lindemann-Weierstrass or Nesterenko
might give nonvanishing. Worth trying as proof of concept.
