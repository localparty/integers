# Lead 1: Connes 2026 critical-line proof for finite-stage CCM extremizers

## Direction (written by Strategist, Cycle 1)
Status: OPEN
Pattern: SP1 (crack RH from inside BC / Connes framework) + Pattern 5 (zeta regularization of Weil quadratic form)
Feasibility: 9/10
Rationale: Connes' Feb 2026 survey ("The Riemann Hypothesis: Past, Present and a Letter Through Time," arXiv:2602.04022) announces a NEW GENERAL PROOF that the finite-prime extremizers of the restricted Weil quadratic form lie exactly on the critical line. This is precisely the "finite-stage criticality" half of the CCM + ITPFI programme (strategy/11 §3 step 4). If the proof is real and tight, it closes the location half of CCM in one stroke, and the only remaining work is the N → ∞ limit (leads 2 + 3). If it has a gap, the gap becomes cycle 1's new bottleneck. This is the highest-leverage read of the cycle — everything else depends on what this paper actually proves.
Toolkit connection: Directly usable theorem for strategy/11's step 4. Replaces the "Kato + spectral mapping → {γ_n}" argument with a direct geometric / quadratic-form argument. Connects to R.theorem S.5 (Källén-Lehmann + Weil — the iff condition we already have for the global bound).
Investigate:
1. What is the EXACT theorem statement? Write it in mathematical notation with explicit hypotheses, including: which quadratic form, what does "restricted" mean, which extremizers, and what is the finite family of primes.
2. What is the proof outline? Specifically, is it a variational / Lagrange-multiplier argument, a spectral argument on a finite-dimensional problem, or something else?
3. What does the proof depend on? List ALL cited papers and lemmas. Are any of those still open or conjectural?
4. Does the argument generalize as the number of primes → ∞, or does it give ONLY a sequence of finite-stage critical-line statements? (This determines whether leads 2 + 3 are still needed.)
5. Cross-check with CCM 2025 (arXiv:2511.22755): is this survey's new proof consistent with CCM's Carathéodory-Fejér self-adjointness? They should agree — CCM gives real spectrum, this should give real spectrum on the critical line.
Would close if: the Connes 2026 proof is a valid, self-contained theorem that "finite-prime approximants lie on the critical line" AND the argument is stable under the N → ∞ limit (or can be made so via leads 2/3). In that case leads 2 + 3 reduce to transport/limit work and the main theorem collapses into a direct chain.
Would kill if: the proof (a) silently assumes something equivalent to RH, (b) covers only low zeros numerically without a general statement, or (c) requires a positivity hypothesis as unproven as Yakaboylu's W ≥ 0.
Source: arXiv:2602.04022 (downloaded: sources/connes-rh-letter-through-time-2026.pdf). Cross-reference: arXiv:2511.22755 (ccm-zeta-spectral-triples-2025.pdf).
Premise check: The constraint "extremizer lies on the critical line" is non-vacuous precisely because extremizers of general quadratic forms on function spaces do NOT generically land on one-dimensional subvarieties. The invariant that shifts under the rank-one perturbation is the location of the extremum in the spectral plane; if the argument pins it to Re(s) = 1/2 for ALL finite stages, that is a genuine constraint. (In contrast: a constraint like "the form is self-adjoint ⇒ spectrum real" is vacuous for this purpose — it gives real but not necessarily critical-line.) So lead is non-vacuous IFF Connes' argument distinguishes criticality from mere reality, and that is exactly what the executor must verify.
