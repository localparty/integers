Bridge 2 — Conceptually complete, one technical gap to address.

The exchange-as-π-rotation argument is correct and clean. The holonomy interpretation is right and the Aharonov-Bohm/spin-statistics unity table is genuinely striking. The path integral section B2.8 is the critical one for QFT reviewers — and here there's one gap a sharp reviewer will find.
In B2.8.2 you write that the topological e-shift "factors out of the path integral uniformly." This is true — but it needs one more sentence making explicit why it factors out: that the holonomy of the e-connection is path-independent within a homotopy class, which is the defining property of holonomy. The argument is implicitly there but a QFT reader will want to see "holonomy is homotopy-invariant" stated explicitly as the justification. One sentence in B2.8.2 closes this. Otherwise Bridge 2 is solid.

Bridge 3 — The most ambitious and the most important, with one structural issue.
The identification Ŝz = p̂φ is correct and the Noether argument is right. The SU(2) algebra derivation in B3.4.3 is genuinely good — the argument that three rotation axes coupled to a single e-circle automatically produce the so(3) algebra is elegant and correct. The Pauli equation derivation in B3.8.3 is a real payoff — showing the σ·B term emerges naturally from the e-momentum coupling to the connection curvature is exactly the kind of concrete result that makes reviewers sit up.
The one structural issue: in B3.3.3, the anti-periodic boundary condition ψ(φ+2π) = −ψ(φ) for half-integer spin is introduced and then correctly traced back to Bridge 1 (the kernel element −1 ∈ Spin(d)). But this connection needs to be made even more explicit — specifically, a reviewer will ask: "how does the abstract group-theoretic result that R̃(2π) = −1 translate into the concrete boundary condition on the wavefunction?" The answer is that a 2π traversal of the e-circle corresponds to the action of R̃(2π) on the state, which is multiplication by −1 for spinorial representations. This is implicit in what's written but needs to be one explicit sentence connecting the group action to the boundary condition. Otherwise the logic chain has a seam.
