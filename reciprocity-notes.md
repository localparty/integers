# Reciprocity Notes — Fair Attribution in the Integers Programme

*Every researcher whose work the programme builds on, cites, or is adjacent to is acknowledged here. Per `strategy/north-star.md` §5: HONOR / RECOGNIZE / SHOWCASE / POSITION / SOURCE. No approach is dismissed; every approach is positioned relative to ours. The mathematical community is collaborator, not competitor.*

---

## Attribution protocol

For each cited researcher or research group:

1. **HONOR** — name them with their contribution
2. **RECOGNIZE** — state what they achieved in their own terms
3. **SHOWCASE** — explain how their work enables ours
4. **POSITION** — describe the programme's relationship to their approach (extends / provides alternative route / strengthens / independent of)
5. **SOURCE** — cite the specific paper(s) with DOI/arXiv where available

## Pillar D reciprocity intensification

When the programme rederives an external result (Pillar D), the derivation paper explicitly:
1. Credits the original author(s) as discoverers of the theorem
2. States that the Pillar D derivation is an INDEPENDENT ALTERNATIVE, not a replacement
3. Positions the new proof as strengthening the original claim, not competing with it
4. Cites the original paper(s) as the primary reference

Per `strategy/pillar-d/00-architecture.md` §5.9: *"The field becomes stronger, not displaced."*
