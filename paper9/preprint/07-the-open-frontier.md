# Section 7 — The Open Frontier

The framework is not complete. Honest accounting requires stating what remains
open, what is established, and what the open questions tell us about where
the physics is still missing.

## 7.1 The One Remaining Number

The framework has one free parameter: R, the e-circle circumference. It is
fixed observationally via ρ_Λ = ℏc·π²/(240R⁴), giving R ≈ 12 μm (bare
Casimir, mirror sector absent; the corrected value including the mirror sector's
ξ⁴ contribution is R₀ = 10.1 μm, §4c.3 — the value used in §§4b–4d). This is
not a problem — one parameter is extraordinarily few for a framework that
covers quantum mechanics, the Standard Model, cosmology, and black hole
physics. But it is a parameter, and the framework does not currently derive R
from first principles.

Theorem U (Paper 7) proves that algebraic functions of the framework's O(1)
geometric inputs — Kähler weights, Euler characteristics, flux quanta — produce
O(1) outputs. Any derivation of R from geometry must produce a number of
order 10^30 in Planck units. Theorem U* shows this is structurally unreachable
by perturbative means: the ratio R_obs/l_P ~ 10^30 is a type error, not a
fine-tuning problem. No algebraic combination of integers and O(1) topological
numbers reaches 10^30.

This is not a failure of the framework. It is a precise statement about what
kind of mechanism would be needed: non-perturbative, non-algebraic, and
specifically able to generate exponentially large hierarchies from O(1) inputs.
The cosmological constant problem has been sharpened from "why is Λ small?"
to "what non-perturbative mechanism produces R_obs from l_P?" That is a
more tractable question.

**Dimensionless vs. dimensionful predictions.** The framework's claim that
"every other quantity is a consequence of R and the geometry" requires one
refinement (Paper 7 gap-response, Finding D1b). Dimensionless predictions —
sin²θ_W, α₃/α₂, n_s, N_eff, Ω_DM/Ω_b, the generation count, θ_QCD — are
genuinely parameter-free consequences of the geometry, independent of R's
specific value. Dimensionful predictions — M_GUT ∝ R^{1/2}, m_H ∝ R^{1/2},
τ_p ∝ R² — are consequences of R at R_obs. They are not independently
parameter-free: they are consistency relations at the observationally fixed
R. The distinction matters for falsifiability: if R were measured to be 11 μm
rather than 10.1 μm, the dimensionless predictions would be unchanged; the
dimensionful predictions would shift. The framework's strongest predictions
are therefore the dimensionless ones.

## 7.2 Three Open Threads

**Thread 1 — The neutrino mass coincidence**

The framework predicts Σm_ν ~ 0.06 eV via the bulk seesaw mechanism on the
S¹/Z₂ orbifold. Separately, there is a numerical coincidence:

    5/2 = χ(CP²) − c₂^{eff}(V_vis)|_{CP²} = 3 − 1/2

where 3 is the spin^c Dirac index on CP² (computed by HRR; equals χ(CP²) for
this specific manifold and twist) and 1/2 is the fractional instanton number
established by the HW anomaly-cancellation argument (Paper 7 §B.10.1). Note:
the eta invariant η(D_{CP²}) = 0 identically for all standard spin^c structures
on CP², computed explicitly from the Cahen-Franc-Gutt spectrum (Paper 7
§B.10.3a); the APS boundary interpretation of the −1/2 is therefore ruled out.
The −1/2 arises from the physics of M-theory flux at the orbifold fixed plane,
not from APS corrections. The ratio 5/2 connects a manifold invariant (χ = 3)
and a gauge-bundle invariant (c₂^{eff} = 1/2) in different cohomological
contexts. The mechanism that makes their difference equal the mass ratio is
unknown — this is the open coincidence. It may be Pattern 4 (Topological
Rigidity) operating at a deeper level, or it may be a numerical coincidence
of topologically rigid components. This distinction needs to be established.

**Thread 2 — The E₈ embedding and tadpole integrality**

M-theory requires the Standard Model gauge group to embed in E₈. The framework
identifies SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) from the isometries of CP² × S² × S¹, but
the explicit embedding SM ↪ E₈ and the resulting tadpole integrality condition
∫ G₄ ∧ G₄/2 + I₈ = 0 have not been fully worked out. This is a technical
gap — the ingredients are present (G₄ flux, CP², tadpole condition in Paper 7)
but the explicit embedding is not yet constructed.

**Thread 3 — Higher-loop Epstein zeta factorization**

Theorem K.1 (Paper 1) proves that E_L(−j; Q_L) = 0 for all L and j ≥ 1.
Theorem K.3 extends this to BPHZ subtractions at all orders. But at L ≥ 3
loops with overlapping subdivergences, the factorization of the amplitude as
(4D finite part) × E_L(−j; Q_L) relies on the joint holomorphicity of the
Epstein zeta in loop-momentum and Schwinger parameters. Whether this
holomorphicity always holds for the specific momentum routings that arise
from L ≥ 3 overlapping diagrams — and whether the Q_L always reduces to an
Epstein zeta in those cases — is not yet established at full generality.

The framework distinguishes three levels, consistently across all ten papers:

**Established within the framework** — follows directly from the geometric
postulate with no additional assumptions, and is consistent with all known
experimental data. Includes: the spin-statistics theorem, the Aharonov-Bohm
effect, UV finiteness to all loop orders (Theorem K.1), the three-phase
holonomy correspondence, three fermion generations, θ_QCD = 0, the information
paradox resolution, the Wheeler-DeWitt problem of time, color confinement,
the Yang-Mills mass gap (Paper 8), all quantitative predictions in the
consequences table.

**Proposed as a research program** — specific, falsifiable approaches that
emerge naturally from the framework but are not yet fully derived. Includes:
the SM ↪ E₈ embedding, the non-perturbative mechanism for R, the full
nonlinear OS3 positivity.

**Speculative but quantitative** — flagged explicitly throughout the series.
The N_eff = 3.31–3.39 prediction (testable by CMB-S4), the dark photon signal
at ε ~ 5 × 10⁻⁴ (testable by LDMX and LHCb), short-range gravitational
deviations at ~12 μm (testable by next-generation Cavendish experiments),
normal neutrino mass ordering (testable by JUNO within 6 years).

## 7.5 The Type Error

The most philosophically important result in the series may be Theorem U*.

The cosmological constant problem asks why Λ is so small — why the vacuum
energy is 10^120 times below its natural value. This has motivated five
decades of fine-tuning arguments, anthropic reasoning, landscape statistics,
and supersymmetric cancellations.

Theorem U* says: the question is malformed.

The geometric inputs to the framework — flux quanta n₁, n₂; Euler
characteristics χ; the Planck mass M_P — are O(1) integers or small
dimensionless numbers. Any algebraic function of O(1) inputs produces an
O(1) output. The observed radius R_obs requires a ratio R_obs/l_P ~ 10^30.
That ratio is unreachable by algebraic means — not difficult to reach, not
reachable with more clever algebra, but structurally unreachable. It is a
type error: you are asking an O(1) computation to produce a 10^30 output.

This is a sharper statement about the cosmological constant than any prior
framework has made. It does not solve the problem. It identifies the problem
as a type error in the question, and points to the kind of answer that could
be right: a non-perturbative mechanism that generates exponential hierarchies
from O(1) inputs — precisely the kind of mechanism that exists in string
theory (exponential suppression of instanton effects) but has not yet been
connected to the specific geometric context of M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹.

The problem is unsolved. The framework has made it precise.

## 7.6 Beyond Linearized, Flat Background

Paper 10 (Scheme-Independence) closes the one-loop and two-loop UV finiteness
of 5D linearized gravity on flat M⁴ × S¹/Z₂ unconditionally: Theorem 1 with
Lemmas A1–A3, two independent proofs of a₄ = 0 (Seeley-DeWitt and Weyl anomaly
cohomology), and an algebraic Z₂ parity mechanism that holds in every
Z₂-preserving regularization scheme. This closes the open problem stated in
Paper 1 §U.2c.

What remains open beyond Paper 10 is the natural next layer: extension of the
Seeley-DeWitt vanishing to all a_{2k} via a Gel'fand-Yaglom generating function;
curved backgrounds (the proofs in Paper 10 are flat-background); the full
non-linear theory beyond linearized fluctuations; and the curious orbifold
observation a_grand = 19/240 ≠ 0 for the combined h_{μν} + A_μ + φ KK tower
in curved backgrounds — orthogonal to Theorem 1 but a real, non-zero effect
that deserves its own investigation.

These are not gaps in the finiteness claim. They are the next frontier for
the same machinery that closed it.
