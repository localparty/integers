## 4.2 What "Consequence" Means

A fitted parameter is a number chosen to match an observation. It has no
predictive power for that observation — by construction it agrees.

A consequence is a number that falls out of the geometry before the
observation is consulted. The geometry either agrees or it doesn't. There is
no adjustment available.

The Weinberg angle sin²θ_W = 0.232 comes from the ratio of spectral zeta
values of S² and CP². The spectral zeta of a Riemannian manifold is fixed by
its curvature, which is fixed by its topology. There is no dial. The number
is 0.232, and the measurement is 0.2312. That is a 0.3% match with zero free
parameters.

The Lüscher coefficient π/6 comes from counting vibrational modes of the
confining string. The string threads through CP²'s non-contractible 2-cycle.
That cycle contributes exactly two additional internal modes, doubling the
naive Nambu-Goto prediction from π/12 to π/6. The lattice QCD value is
0.502 ± 0.020. The prediction is 0.524. That is a 4% match, and the only
inputs are the dimension of spacetime and the topology of CP².

Three generations comes from χ(CP²) = 3. The Euler characteristic of CP²
is a topological integer — it cannot be adjusted, approximated, or tuned.
It is 3. The number of fermion generations is 3. The Atiyah-Singer index
theorem connects them. This is not a coincidence. It is a topological fact.
