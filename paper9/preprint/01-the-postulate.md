# Section 1 — The Postulate

The framework rests on one statement:

**Spacetime is M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹.**

Four-dimensional Minkowski space, crossed with the complex projective plane,
crossed with the two-sphere, crossed with the circle. That is the complete
postulate. Everything in ten papers follows from it.

## 1.1 What Each Factor Carries

Each factor in the product is not assigned a role — the role is forced by the
geometry of the factor itself. The isometries of a compact space determine
the gauge group it produces. The topology of a compact space determines how
that gauge field behaves. The Casimir energy of fields on a compact space
sets a physical energy scale. These are not choices; they are consequences of
which shape is there.

| Factor | Dim | What it carries |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| M⁴     | 4   | Spacetime: gravity, causality, the four coordinates we navigate |
| S¹     | 1   | The e-circle: quantum mechanics, electromagnetism, UV finiteness, dark energy, the clock |
| S²     | 2   | The weak force: SU(2) from 3 Killing vectors, Higgs as Wilson line, EWSB as geometry tilting |
| CP²    | 4   | The strong force: SU(3) from 8 Killing vectors, confinement from holonomy, string tension from flux tubes |

The Z₂ orbifold of S¹ adds two fixed-point branes: visible matter at φ = 0,
mirror dark matter at φ = π. G₄ flux on the non-contractible 4-cycles of
CP² × S² stabilizes the moduli and fixes gauge coupling unification at the
topological ratio n₂/n₁ = −17/9 — three interlocking discrete constraints,
no continuous freedom.

The total internal dimension is 1 + 2 + 4 = 7. Adding M⁴ gives 11. This is
M-theory's eleven-dimensional spacetime, reached from below by asking: what
compact geometry is required to produce SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1)? The answer
is CP² × S² × S¹. The eleven dimensions are not assumed. They are derived.

## 1.2 The One Free Parameter

The framework has one free parameter: R, the circumference of the e-circle.

It is not fitted to any quantum mechanical measurement. It is fixed by a
single cosmological observation — the dark energy density ρ_Λ — via the
Casimir energy of bulk fields on the S¹/Z₂ orbifold:

    ρ_Λ = ℏc · π² / (240 R⁴)   →   R ≈ 12 μm (bare Casimir, mirror sector absent)

The corrected value including the mirror sector's ξ⁴ contribution to ΔN_eff
is R₀ = 10.1 μm (§4c.3), which is the value used in §§4b–4d. The bare 12 μm
and corrected 10.1 μm refer to the same physical radius at successive levels
of approximation.

Everything else — every mass, every coupling, every cosmological observable,
every topological count — is a consequence of R and the geometry.

## 1.3 The Architecture in One Sentence Per Factor

**S¹:** A particle winding around the e-circle accumulates a phase. That
phase, and the geometry of winding, is what we call quantum mechanics.

**S²:** A gauge field winding around S² develops a vacuum expectation value.
That VEV breaks SU(2) × U(1) to U(1). That breaking is what we call the
Higgs mechanism.

**CP²:** A color flux tube threading through CP²'s non-contractible 2-cycle
cannot be continuously unwound. That topological obstruction is what we call
color confinement.

**Z₂ orbifold:** The orbifold has two boundaries. We live on one. The other
gravitates normally but carries no Standard Model charge. That invisible
brane is what we call dark matter.

One geometry. Four sentences. All four known forces accounted for.

## 1.4 Why This and Not Something Else

Five independent lines of reasoning converge on CP² × S² × S¹:

1. **Gauge group:** The isometry group of CP² × S² × S¹ is
   SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) — the Standard Model gauge group exactly.

2. **Dimension:** M⁴ × CP² × S² × S¹ has dimension 11 — the unique
   dimension in which supergravity is consistent and anomaly-free.

3. **Generations:** χ(CP²) = 3. The Atiyah-Singer index theorem then forces
   three generations of fermions. Not two. Not four. Three.

4. **Entanglement structure:** The Szangolies result shows that three-qubit
   entanglement in (9+1)-dimensional spacetime reduces to 3+1 dimensions with
   residual gauge group SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1)/Z₆. The gauge group is selected
   by the entanglement geometry of three fermionic generations.

5. **Unification:** The flux ratio n₂/n₁ = −17/9 follows from three
   interlocking topological constraints and gives gauge coupling unification
   at the GUT scale — not from tuning, from topology.

None of these arguments was constructed to force the Standard Model. Each
was derived independently from the geometry. They all agree.
