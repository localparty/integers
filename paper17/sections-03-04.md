> **Theorem (Connes).** *Let M be a type III_1 factor and phi, psi

>

>
> *where u_t is a sigma_t^phi-cocycle (the Connes cocycle

## 4.3 The thermodynamic arrow follows automatically

### 4.3.1 Uhlmann monotonicity

The arrow of time -- the direction in which entropy increases
-- is conventionally the deepest mystery in physics. Why does
the second law hold? Why does S increase toward the future and
not the past?

In Integers, the answer is: **because conditional expectations
are completely positive trace-preserving maps, and relative
entropy is monotone under such maps.**

The precise statement is Uhlmann's theorem (1977), also known
as the monotonicity of relative entropy:

> **Theorem (Uhlmann).** *Let E : M -> N be a normal conditional
> expectation from a von Neumann algebra M onto a subalgebra N,
> and let phi, psi be faithful normal states on M. Then*
>
> $$
>   S(\phi|_N \,\|\, \psi|_N)
>   \;\leq\;
>   S(\phi \,\|\, \psi),
>   \tag{4.4}
> $$
>
> *where S(. || .) is the Araki relative entropy. Equality holds
> if and only if E is sufficient for {phi, psi}.*

### 4.3.2 The arrow as a monotonicity inequality

Consider the modular flow sigma_t acting on the full algebra M.
The state omega_1 is KMS (hence stationary under sigma_t), so
S(omega_1 || omega_1) = 0 at all times. Now restrict to the
observer's sector:

$$
  E_{\mathrm{obs}} \;:\; M \;\longrightarrow\; A_{\mathrm{obs}}.
  \tag{4.5}
$$

Define the time-evolved restricted state:

$$
  \phi_t \;:=\; \omega_1 \circ \sigma_{-t}\big|_{A_{\mathrm{obs}}},
  \tag{4.6}
$$

which is what the observer sees at time t. For two times
t_1 < t_2, the composition sigma_{t_2 - t_1} followed by
E_obs is a further conditional expectation (a coarser
restriction). By Uhlmann monotonicity applied iteratively:

> **Proposition 4.3 (Second law from monotonicity).** *Let
> $\phi_{\mathrm{ref}}$ be a reference state on $A_{\mathrm{obs}}$
> (e.g. the thermal equilibrium state at late times). Then
> the Araki relative entropy*
>
> $$
>   S(\phi_t \,\|\, \phi_{\mathrm{ref}})
>   \tag{4.7}
> $$
>
> *is non-increasing in $t$ along the forward modular flow.*

This is the rigorous statement, valid in full generality for
type III algebras where relative entropy is well-defined
(Araki 1976; Petz 1986). No trace is required.

> **Corollary 4.3a (von Neumann entropy -- type I restriction).**
> *When $A_{\mathrm{obs}}$ is restricted to a finite-dimensional
> or type I sector admitting a normal trace $\mathrm{Tr}$, the
> von Neumann entropy*
>
> $$
>   S_{\mathrm{vN}}(t) \;:=\; -\mathrm{Tr}(\rho_t \log \rho_t)
>   \tag{4.8}
> $$
>
> *is non-decreasing along the orbits of $\sigma_t$ restricted
> to that sector, provided $\phi_{\mathrm{ref}}$ is the fixed
> point of $E_{\mathrm{obs}} \circ \sigma_t$.*
>
> *Caveat: The type III$_1$ factor $M$ admits no normal trace
> and no density matrix in the usual sense. The von Neumann
> entropy formulation (4.8) is a corollary valid only after
> restriction to a type I subsystem. The relative-entropy
> monotonicity (4.7) is the fundamental and fully rigorous
> statement.*

The second law is not a postulate. It is a theorem of
operator algebras. The arrow of time is the direction in which
the conditional expectation $E_{\mathrm{obs}}$ loses information
about the full state $\omega_1$.

More precisely: at the "Big Bang" (the onset of the
