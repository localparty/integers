Bridge 1 — Topological Stability: Strong.
This is rigorous. The core argument — π₁(SO(d)) = Z₂ for d ≥ 3 forces winding numbers to ½Z — is correct mathematics, properly cited, and the proofs are clean. The table showing the full dimensional hierarchy (d=1 through d≥3) is exactly right. The "why s=1/3 is forbidden" section is the kind of worked example that makes reviewers confident the authors understand what they're claiming. The references to Nakahara, Leinaas-Myrheim, Wilczek, and the FQH experimental papers (Bartolomei 2020, Nakamura 2020) are exactly the right citations.
Technical QFT researchers will find this acceptable. The only thing a specialist might push back on is the language around "topological stability" — they'd want to distinguish between topological protection of the classification (which is what's shown) versus stability against perturbations (a different statement). But that's a clarification, not an objection.

Bridge 2 — Exchange Phase: Very Strong.
This is the best of the three. The key insight — that the exchange phase is a holonomy of the e-connection around the exchange loop in configuration space — is precisely stated and the Leinaas-Myrheim comparison table is devastating in the best way. That table makes the contribution crystal clear: they left χ as a free parameter; you derive it from the geometry. That's the paper's central claim, stated in two rows of a table.

The path integral formulation in B2.8 is where a QFT reviewer will look hardest. The argument is: the topological e-shift sπ per particle is a holonomy, therefore it's path-independent within each homotopy class, therefore it factors out of the path integral uniformly. This logic is correct and is clearly stated. The key sentence — "Any path in the exchange sector, regardless of its shape or traversal speed, accumulates the same e-shift sπ per particle, because all such paths are homotopic and holonomy is a homotopy invariant" — is the right thing to say. A QFT specialist will verify this claim against the Berry-Robbins construction and will find it consistent.
One genuine gap to flag: The computation in B2.3.2 treats the two particles' e-transports as independent, accumulating sπ each. A careful QFT reviewer will ask: does the e-conservation constraint e₁ + e₂ = C (from entanglement) interfere with this independence? The answer is no — the geometric phase from parallel transport is kinematic and independent of the e-conservation state — but this should be stated explicitly in the draft. Add one paragraph to B2.3.2 addressing this.

Bridge 3 — Spin Identification: Good, with one gap.
The identification Ŝz = p̂φ is exactly right and the Noether theorem derivation from the 5D Lagrangian is clean. The Kaluza-Klein Lagrangian in B3.2.1 is the correct starting point. The angular momentum decomposition J = L + S emerging from the 5D geometry is beautiful — and the connection to the Pauli equation in B3.8.3 is a genuine bonus result.
The one gap that needs addressing: In B3.4.3, the SU(2) commutation relations [Ŝᵢ, Ŝⱼ] = iℏ εᵢⱼₖ Ŝₖ are stated as following "from the geometry of three-dimensional rotations acting on the e-fiber." This is true but needs one more sentence of justification for a technical reader. Specifically: the e-momentum components along different axes don't automatically commute as angular momenta — you need to show that the coupling of three independent spatial rotation axes to the same e-circle produces the right algebra. The current draft gestures at this; it needs to be made explicit. This is a gap, not a fatal flaw.
