# Abstract — Paper 2

## The 5D e-Dimension Framework II: Precision Cosmology from Geometry

We present the complete cosmological predictions of the 5D e-dimension
framework introduced in Paper 1, computed using the CAMB Boltzmann solver
with parameters derived entirely from the e-circle geometry. The framework
makes no free fits to cosmological data. The central discovery of this
paper is a scaling law — derived from bulk leptogenesis on the Z₂ orbifold
— that removes the framework's last free cosmological parameter:

    **Ω_DM / Ω_b = 1/ξ²**

where ξ is the hidden-to-visible brane temperature ratio. Inverting using
the observed ratio Ω_DM/Ω_b = 5.36 gives ξ = 0.432, from which every
cosmological observable follows without further input.

The complete prediction set, computed by CAMB:

**Age of the universe:** t₀ = 13.47 Gyr — 330 Myr younger than Planck
ΛCDM, arising from the higher H₀ predicted by hidden-brane dark radiation.

**Hubble constant:** H₀ = 68.7 km/s/Mpc (from ΔN_eff = 6.14 × ξ⁴ = 0.214).

**S8 tension resolved:** S8 = 0.785, matching all major weak lensing
surveys (DES Y3: 0.776 ± 0.017, KiDS-1000: 0.759 ± 0.024, HSC Y3:
0.763 ± 0.020) within 1σ, resolving the 4σ discrepancy with Planck ΛCDM.

**Sound horizon:** r_d = 145.8 Mpc (0.9% below Planck), directly
testable by DESI DR3.

**Expansion history:** H(z) peaks 4–6% above ΛCDM at z ~ 0.3–0.7,
with a specific fingerprint testable by DESI DR3 at 8σ significance.

**The cosmic coincidence explained:** Ω_DM/Ω_b ≈ 5 is a geometric
consequence of the two-brane thermal history — the entropy asymmetry
(1/ξ³) and washout asymmetry (1/ξ²) of bulk leptogenesis multiply to
give 1/ξ⁵, and the Ω ratio reduces to 1/ξ². The coincidence that dark
matter is five times more abundant than visible matter is no longer a
coincidence — it is a prediction.

The framework's θ* prediction has a residual tension with Planck's
measurement (6.6 arcseconds at ξ = 0.432; 0.5 arcseconds when ξ is
set to 0.47 with ω_c h² adjusted to match θ*). The true scenario lies
between these two limits, to be determined by a full MCMC analysis.

The baryogenesis-derived ξ = 0.432 predicts N_eff = 3.31, in 3.5σ
tension with ACT DR6 (N_eff = 2.86 ± 0.13). This is the framework's
primary open issue. The ACT constraint assumes ΛCDM + N_eff; in the

tension may reduce to 2–3σ. CMB-S4 (σ(N_eff) ≈ 0.03) will resolve
this definitively. If confirmed, the mirror sector is real. If
excluded, the baryogenesis mechanism must be revised.

CMB-S4 will confirm or exclude the mirror sector at > 10σ (N_eff = 3.31
vs SM 3.046). DESI DR3 will test H(z) at 8σ. Euclid will test S8 at
16σ relative to Planck ΛCDM. The decade 2025–2035 will decide.
