We compute the cosmological predictions of the 5D e-dimension framework using CAMB v1.6.6, fitting zero parameters to CMB data. Two observational inputs ($\rho_\Lambda$, $\Omega_{\rm DM}/\Omega_b$) plus a thawing dilaton ($w_0 = -0.85$, $w_a = -0.23$) determine every observable. The central result: $\Omega_{\rm DM}/\Omega_b = 1/\xi^2$ from bulk leptogenesis on the $Z_2$ orbifold, where $\xi = T_{\rm hidden}/T_{\rm visible}$. The washout correction shifts $\xi$ from 0.432 to $\approx 0.49$, converging with the independently $\theta^*$-matched value. Three scenarios bracket the prediction ($\xi = 0.432$--$0.47$): $t_0 = 13.47$--$13.60$ Gyr; $S_8 = 0.753$--$0.785$ (resolving the $4\sigma$ clustering tension); $H_0 = 68.7$--$69.5$ km/s/Mpc; $r_d = 145.8$--$148.0$ Mpc. The bulk seesaw predicts $\Sigma m_\nu = 0.06$ eV with normal ordering (JUNO). $H(z)$ peaks 4--6\% above $\Lambda$CDM at $z \sim 0.3$--$0.7$, testable by DESI DR3 at $8\sigma$. $N_{\rm eff} = 3.31$--$3.39$, the framework's most falsifiable near-term prediction, in $3$--$4\sigma$ tension with ACT DR6 but consistent with the CMB+SH0ES joint fit ($N_{\rm eff} = 3.43 \pm 0.13$); CMB-S4 will confirm or exclude at $> 9\sigma$.
