We propose that spacetime is five-dimensional, with a compact circular fifth dimension $e$ corresponding to the U(1) fiber bundle over 4D spacetime. Quantum phenomena are geometric consequences of the projection from 5D to 4D: superposition is extension in $e$, entanglement is $e$-coordinate conservation, and the spin-statistics theorem follows from winding numbers in the circular $e$-dimension. The Aharonov-Bohm effect, Born rule, and Bell inequality violation ($|S| = 2\sqrt{2}$) are derived geometrically. The same compact circle resolves quantum gravity: under zeta regularization of Kaluza-Klein mode sums, the Goroff-Sagnotti $R^3$ counterterm vanishes identically at two loops via Epstein zeta functions of the Eisenstein lattice, and is conjectured to vanish at all orders. A $Z_2$ orbifold yields geometric dark matter, Casimir dark energy ($R \approx 12\,\mu$m), and neutrino masses via the bulk seesaw. Twenty-two phenomena are addressed at three levels of rigor: eight derived from the basic circle, eight from the orbifold, six conjectured. Seven testable predictions include short-range gravitational deviations at $\sim 12\,\mu$m, a dark photon at $\varepsilon \sim 5 \times 10^{-4}$, and normal neutrino mass ordering (JUNO). A companion paper (Paper 2) computes precision cosmological predictions via CAMB: $\Omega_{\rm DM}/\Omega_b = 1/\xi^2$ from bulk leptogenesis determines $\xi \approx 0.43$--$0.49$, giving $t_0 = 13.47$--$13.60$ Gyr, $H_0 = 68.7$--$69.5$ km/s/Mpc, $S_8 = 0.753$--$0.785$ (resolving the $4\sigma$ weak lensing tension), and $N_{\rm eff} = 3.31$--$3.39$ (testable by CMB-S4 at $>9\sigma$).
